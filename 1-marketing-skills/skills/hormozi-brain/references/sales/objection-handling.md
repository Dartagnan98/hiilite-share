# Objection Handling
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## The 3A Reframing Framework

The skill that separates the best from the rest. Used across $100 to $1M+ deals, in person, phone, Zoom, stage.

### Why Reframing Matters
"The most productive salespeople ask for the sale more times than anyone else."

Problem: asking too many times the wrong way makes prospects hate you.

Solution: Reframe between each ask so rapport is maintained, allowing unlimited asks.

### The 3 A's:

#### A1 -- Acknowledge
Say what they said back to them.
- Shows you're actively listening
- Buys you time to think
- Example: "So you're curious about what our certifications are..."

#### A2 -- Associate
Connect their question/objection to the behavior of successful customers.

Three association strategies:
1. **Recency association:** "It's so funny you ask that -- I had someone just earlier today who asked the same question. Can I share what I shared with them?" (Now you can be brutal to the "other person" without insulting the prospect)
2. **Successful customer association:** "That actually makes you just like Sarah who had a similar business and crushed it with us."
3. **Appeal to authority:** "That's so funny you ask that -- Alex just told me this thing earlier today. Can I share it with you?" (Messenger, not the authority)

The association gives the prospect a label to live up to. Later in the close, bring back that label: "You said you were a family man... because you're a family man, this makes sense."

#### A3 -- Ask
Ask your next question after reframing. Redirect back to the sale.

### Five Rules for Using Reframing

**Rule 1: A prospect believes almost nothing you say and almost everything they say.** Goal is to get THEM to say "I'm a good fit" through leading questions.

NEVER ask "Do you have any questions?" -- This is literally asking them to have objections and take control.

**Rule 2: No one can disagree with a question.** "Be like smoke." You can't catch smoke. Always sidestep with questions. Never disagree with a prospect. "You never win a sale by winning an argument."

**Rule 3: Tell them what their question means.** When they take a step away from buying, associate their question with successful behavior: "90% of our best clients asked that same question."

**Rule 4: Use Straw Men for tough truths.** Three types:
1. "I had someone earlier today who asked the same question..." (attack the straw man, not the prospect)
2. Reference a successful past customer who was similar
3. Appeal to the CEO's authority: "Alex told me this earlier..."

**Rule 5: Retain childlike curiosity.** Physical posture affects tone. Say "Huh" -- it buys 2-3 seconds and resets your frame. "Seek to understand, not to win."

Sticky note above camera: **"Keep the human #1"**

### Universal Response
"What are you afraid of?" / "What are you most afraid of having happen?"

Cuts to the heart of the issue fastest.

- First objection: "What's your main concern?" / "What are the variables you're using to make the decision?"
- Deeper in the sale: "What are you afraid of, man?"
- If they need more time: "What would make this a no?"

## The Three Core Objections

### Price Objection
Core belief to instill in sales team: "If we were giving away Ferraris for $5K, do you think they'd find the money? Yes. So they don't understand the value."

It is ALWAYS the salesperson's fault if someone can't afford the program. This reframe gets the team to take ownership.

### Decision Maker Objection
Three-step process: Circumvent -> Past Agreements -> Ask Forgiveness

**Step 1 - Circumvent:** "What if they say no?" (1 out of 3 times they say "I'd probably do it anyway" -- close immediately)

**Step 2 - If they say they wouldn't do it:** "Well, what do you think their biggest objection would be?" Now they're using the absent partner as a foil and you can attack the actual obstacle.

**Step 3 - Past Agreements:** "Does your partner know that you're struggling with [problem]? Do they approve of that? No? Then why would they be against something that remedies a problem they already don't approve of?"

**Step 4 - Ask Forgiveness:** "Sometimes it's better to ask for forgiveness than permission."

**Step 5 - Delayed Close:** "Let's get your card down. We'll delay the payment to Friday. If between now and then your partner says 'Baby, I want you to keep struggling and never make money,' then call me back and I'll tear up the contract."

### Stall Objection ("I need to think about it")
People are afraid of making mistakes. They don't like making decisions because it's easier to let life decide for them.

**Response:** "Totally. What's your main concern? Let's think about it together."

Walk them through a decision-making framework:
1. "Do you feel like what we're doing can meet your needs and solve the problem? Yes or no?"
2. "Do you want to work with us as a company? Do you like our values?"
3. "Do you have access to funds or know someone who does?"

"Those are the only three things you need to make this decision. Do you like the product, do you like us, do you have the money?"

**Fear-based close:** "What are you most afraid of happening? You're afraid I'm just going to take your money and you get nothing? Let me tell you how that's not going to happen."

## ADDITIONAL DETAILS (Deep Pass)

### "What Are You Most Afraid Of?" Escalation
- Universal escalation for deep objections: main concern -> variables -> what would make this a no -> what would it take to say yes
- Walked through sales floor, heard weak close attempt, grabbed phone: "Dude, what are you afraid of?"
- Gets to the heart of the issue faster than any other question

### 3A Framework Usage Examples (Expanded)
- "I need to think about it" -> "Totally understand, what are the main things you're considering?"
- "I need to talk to my spouse" -> "Totally understand, what parts do you think they wouldn't like?"
- "Timing is off" -> "Totally understand. Our biggest success stories are people who felt the same way. What would make it a good time?"

### Labels Created During Association Can Be Reused at Close
- "You said you're a family man... family men buy this because..."
- Pro tip: bring back the label they accepted in A2 during the final close

### The Mechanic/Surgeon Analogy
- For handling technical questions: "You're asking me what's wrong with your car and it would be unethical for me to answer without looking under the hood first."
- Prevents premature diagnosis that loses the sale

### Three Beliefs to Break in Every Sale
1. **Circumstances**: "I don't have money/time" -> teach resourcefulness/prioritization
2. **Other People**: "Spouse/kids won't let me" -> reframe how it helps them
3. **Self**: "I can't do it / what if I fail?" -> confront decision, show it's not rushed
