# Sales Multipliers
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## Sell 7 Days Per Week
- 52 weeks x 2 extra days = 104 additional selling days/year = 29% increase
- Off-hours (5pm onwards) and weekends are when most consumers are available
- Skipping weekends causes Monday stack-up: high show rate expectations but lower actual shows due to 48+ hour gap

## Speed to Lead
- Call within 5 minutes of lead coming in
- Double dial every lead (call twice back-to-back)
- Morning-of nurture: remind prospects of appointments booked days ago

## Follow-Up Framework
- Work leads same day, next day
- If ghosted at any point: send a meme
- Don't skip follow-up days -- consistency compounds

## Show Rate Optimization
- Book appointments no more than 24-48 hours out when possible
- Morning-of text/call reminders
- Reduce gap between booking and appointment

## Payment Step-Downs

### Order of Presentation
1. **Prepay** (not "paid in full" -- that's a salesman term, not a customer benefit). Say "prepayment discount"
2. **Half down + payments** (in-house financing or credit card)
3. **Third-party financing** (every industry has financing solutions)
4. **Pay-as-you-go** (lowest barrier)

### Price Anchoring Trick
Always present the highest price first. Then step down. Each step down feels like a concession.

"You get to be the good guy for getting them a lower price rather than the bad guy for them not being able to afford it."

## The STAR System (Getting People to Do What You Want)

Five reasons someone doesn't do what you asked:

1. **S -- Set (Did they know?)** Did they know you wanted them to do it? Solution: Write it down. "If it isn't written, it never happened."

2. **T -- Tell (Did they know WHAT?)** Did they know exactly what you wanted? Define in terms of behaviors or outcomes, not vague labels. Don't say "be less creepy" -- specify the exact behavior to change.

3. **A -- Train (Did they know HOW?)** Do they know how to do it? Everything is trainable. Break it down: which specific sub-step don't they know? Document -> Demonstrate -> Duplicate.

4. **R -- Required (Did they know WHEN?)** Did they have a deadline? Default to "end of day" -- an end-of-day organization moves 7x faster than end-of-week, 30x faster than end-of-month. Always ask: "How long will the actual work take?" then "What are you doing between now and then?"

5. **S(tuff) -- Something blocking them?** Are there legitimate obstacles? Go through 1-4 first to eliminate excuses. If nothing else, it's a motivation issue.

**The elegance:** By asking 1-4 first, you give multiple opportunities for it NOT to be a personal attack. Then if you reach 5 (motivation), both parties know it.

## Communication & Speaking Frameworks

### Only Three Things Out of Your Mouth
1. Questions
2. Restatements
3. Short anecdotal stories (30 seconds max)

### The Person Who Speaks Most Loses
"The person who speaks the most in the sale loses."
"Sales is in your ears, not from your mouth."

### Closers Ask Hard Questions
"Questions that challenge their current paradigm. If they're on the phone to prove nothing will work: 'It sounds like there's no way for you to succeed. Does that sound about right?' Then they sell YOU on why you're wrong -- and by doing so, take the contrarian stance that proves there IS a way."

### State Shifts
If you can tell within 5-10 minutes you're not going to close, you need a state shift. Ask challenging questions to shift the frame.

### Advanced Salesmen = Never Skip Basics
"Advanced salesmen are advanced because they never don't do the basics. People are defined as advanced because of their outcomes, not because of their tactics. Advanced lifters aren't doing different exercises. They just lift more weight consistently."
