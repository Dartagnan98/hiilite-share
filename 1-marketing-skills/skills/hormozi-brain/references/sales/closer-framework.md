# The CLOSER Framework
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## C.L.O.S.E.R. (Core Sales Script Structure)

### C -- Clarify Why They Are There
Questions to ask:
- "What made you come in today?"
- "What made you reach out?"
- "What's your goal right now?"
- "Why is that important to you?"
- "Why that number specifically? What does it resonate for you?"
- "What would it look like 12 months from now if this ideal outcome were achieved?"

If prospect says "I just wanted to find out more information":
- "Cool, but why? What did you want the information to solve?"
- "I'm sure you don't just go around collecting information all day. There's something you're trying to get out of this. What's the problem?"

Key rule: You MUST hit this milestone. If you don't know why they're there, do NOT move forward.

### L -- Label Them with a Problem
- "So just so I'm hearing you right, it sounds like [their goal] is where you want to be, correct?"
- "So what I'm hearing is you've done X, Y, and Z, and the missing link has been X. Is that right?"

They must acknowledge they have a problem. They must say it and own it. "I can't cure cancer until you admit that you have it."

### O -- Overview Past Pain (The Pain Cycle)
Four-step cycle, repeated until exhausted:
1. "What have you tried so far to accomplish this?"
2. "How long did you do it for?"
3. "How long ago?"
4. "How did that work for you?"

Then: "What else have you tried?" -- Repeat the cycle.

Continue until: "That's it. I've tried everything under the sun."

Then explain how it's not their fault: "You were so close. You were six inches away. You had this one piece missing, which is what we provide."

Transition: "Do you want to hear about how the program works? I think we might be able to solve that for you."

### S -- Sell the Vacation (The Pitch)

**Critical rule: Pitch must be UNDER 3 MINUTES. Tops.**

The pitch has exactly 3 pillars. Always 3. The human brain can't take more than 3.

For each pillar:
1. State the pillar (5 seconds)
2. Get micro-commitment: "Can you do that? / Does that make sense?"
3. If hesitation: tell a memorized 30-second anecdotal story

**"Sell the vacation, not the plane flight."**

What NOT to sell (the plane flight): modules, meal plans, macros, workouts, support team, URLs, domains, funnels, targeting, ads, periodization, calorie counting, software features.

What TO sell (Maui / the destination): the result, the experience, the transformation.

**Maui Analogy for tiered pricing:**
- Swim to Maui = DIY, sharks, could drown (lowest tier)
- Boat to Maui = slower, no wifi, but safer (mid tier)
- Normal flight to Maui = faster, still smells bad, security (standard tier)
- Private jet to Maui = direct, champagne, suitcase packed on arrival (premium tier)

"Everyone gets to Maui. Variables are the speed and quality of the journey. Likelihood of arrival is assumed."

**Example stories:**

Accountability story: "When you were a kid, did your parents tell you to brush your teeth? You hated it. But you did it because they told you to. Now you're an adult -- you brush your teeth on your own. That's external accountability turning into an internal habit. That's what we do here."

Motivation story: "Do you have a favorite TV show? Game of Thrones? Do you feel like you need to get motivated to watch it? 'Oh man, I wanted to watch it but I just couldn't sit on the couch.' Of course not. You look forward to it. We're going to make [your program] something you look forward to. When you look forward to it, you don't need motivation."

### E -- Explain Away Their Concerns (Objection Handling)

Only 3 real objections exist:
1. **Price** (value discrepancy)
2. **Decision Maker** ("I need to talk to my partner/spouse")
3. **Stall** ("I need to think about it")

(See objection-handling.md for full breakdown of each)

### R -- Reinforce the Decision
Added later to the original framework. Critical for reducing churn, refunds, chargebacks.

Actions within first 48 hours:
- Personalized video from the CEO/founder by name: "Hey Johnny, saw you just signed up. Super pumped to have you."
- Handwritten card
- Welcome gift/swag
- Introduction to their point of contact: "You're going to be meeting with Heather tomorrow."

"People make their decision about whether they believe in you as a company within the first 48 hours after the sale."

"The sale continues throughout the entire customer relationship. You close them at signup, you close them again at onboarding, you close them again at their first check-in."

## The Three Things on a Sales Call

Only three types of statements should ever come out of your mouth:

1. **Questions** -- leading questions that guide prospect to the conclusion they should work with you
2. **Restatements** -- "So what I'm hearing is [X]. Does that sound about right?"
3. **Anecdotal Stories** -- 30-second stories used to break beliefs

NO declarative statements of fact. NO teaching. NO technobabble. NO paragraphs about the product.

"The difference between teaching and coaching: teaching provides tactics (only 10% of people benefit). Coaching breaks beliefs (100% of people benefit)."

"Everyone gets benefits from breaking beliefs. Only 10% of people get benefits from tactics." (attributed to a friend)

## Discovery / The Pull Teeth Technique

### When Prospects Give One-Word Answers
Alternate between two phrases:
- "Can you give me an example of that?"
- "Can you be more specific?"

Bounce back and forth until you get something meaty:
- "Business isn't growing" -> "Can you give me an example?" -> "This year is the same as last year" -> "Can you be specific about what's different?" -> "I have the same amount of leads as last year"

### Chunking Up
Once you get specific information, chunk up to what you sell:
- "So it sounds like you have a marketing issue. Is that fair to say?"
- "So it sounds like you have a pricing and packaging problem."

Then: "So if we solved this marketing and pricing issue, do you think it would help grow the business?"

They say yes. They've already accepted the sale before the pitch.

### Transition to Pitch
"Now that you've agreed [what I'm about to sell] would help, can I tell you about our thing? I think it might help given what you just told me."

Match each problem to a specific solution/expert.

### Rule: Don't Pitch Until They're Ready
"You do not pitch the program until you believe the person will say yes."

"Lawyers do not ask questions they don't already know the answers to. No guy proposes without knowing she's going to say yes."

If you don't feel ready: stay in the question/restate cycle. Don't move to the pitch.

"If someone says 'tell me how the program works' and you're not ready: 'No, I don't think we're ready for that. I need to understand more about your business otherwise it's not going to make sense.'"

### The Mechanic Analogy (When Pressed to Answer Questions Too Early)
"You're asking me what's wrong with your car and it would be unethical for me to answer without looking under the hood first."

"If anyone gives you an answer on the first call without already having looked under the hood... run the other way. It means they're just telling you whatever they can to get you to buy."

## SELLING WITH LOGIC FRAMEWORK (Deep Pass -- Batches 3-4)

### Core Principle
- Charlie Munger: "We have a high moral responsibility to be rational"
- Emotional buyers buy fast, cancel fast. Logical foundation makes decisions stick.
- "When the emotions fade, the logic will continue"

### Three Pillars Rule
- Any solution can only have 3 pillars (brain can't handle more)
- Fitness example: exercise, nutrition, accountability
- Leads example: timely, qualified, exclusive
- Use stories, not jargon to explain each pillar
- Tooth brushing analogy for accountability: "Your parents made you brush your teeth. You hated it. Now you do it on your own."

### Pre-Call Tactics
- Voice memo, video, personalized reach out BEFORE the call
- "What size shirt/boxing gloves do you want?" -- tiny thing, huge effect on show rates
- Follow up before AND after appointments

### Referral Hack at End of Every Call
- Ask for referrals at end of every call (not just closes)
- 1 in 3-4 people refer when asked
- Referral close rate: 80-90%
- Cuts CAC in half

## CLOSER FRAMEWORK EXPANDED (Batch 5-6)

### Detailed CLOSER with Three Decision Points
- **C** = Clarify why they're there: "What made you come in? What's your goal? Why is that important?"
- **L** = Label them with a problem: "So what I'm hearing is you're struggling with X, correct?"
- **O** = Overview past paid attempts: "What have you tried? How long? How'd it work?" (Pain cycle, exhaust all options)
- **S** = Sell the vacation (not the plane flight): 3 pillars max, use anecdotal stories, never jargon. Pitch under 3 minutes.
- **E** = Explain away concerns: Price (past agreements), Decision maker (forgiveness > permission), Stalls ("What's your main concern?")
- **R** = Reinforce decision: Personalized video, handwritten card, gift. "First 48 hours after the sale determines everything."

**Only 3 things come out of your mouth:** Questions, Restatements, Anecdotal stories.

**Three decision points:** Does this meet your needs? Do you want to work with us? Do you have access to funds?
