# Negotiation Tactics
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## 5 Brutally Effective Negotiation Tactics

### Tactic 1: BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)
From Harvard Business School via Chiron Cervata.

"Negotiators who know their alternatives set higher aspirations, make more aggressive first offers, and negotiate better outcomes." (London Business School study)

- Before sitting down: secure other offers/alternatives
- "You only want to sell when you don't want to sell. You want to buy when you don't want to buy."
- Employee: get another job offer before negotiating
- Vendor: get multiple bids
- Deals: have multiple interested parties

### Tactic 2: Anchoring
First number set in a negotiation becomes the anchor. Everyone adjusts from it.

"I'm a big advocate of getting the gasp. Put the big number out there."

**Pro tip -- Anchor the increments too:**
- If house is listed at $20M, offer $15M
- They counter at $17M (moved $3M toward you)
- You counter at $15.25M (moved $250K)
- This signals: "I'm not moving much." They learn to move in small increments too.

**Post-deal comfort:** "You're probably wondering if I would have done it for less. I wouldn't have sold it for a penny less." (Classy move that puts buyer at ease)

### Tactic 3: MESO (Multiple Equivalent Simultaneous Offers)
Present 2-3 offers with different price/term structures that all work for you.

Embeds reciprocity: "I'm being reasonable. Which works best for you?"

Teases out the other party's priorities without them having to reveal them directly.

**Counter-MESO (from Chiron):** "I like the best part of this one and the best part of this one. Let's do option D."

### Tactic 4: Reciprocity in Concessions
Break your offer into as many variables as possible so you can trade more times.

Variables to play with (Value Equation):
- Price
- Speed (faster/slower delivery)
- Risk (who bears more risk)
- Ease (how convenient)
- Financing terms
- Inclusions/exclusions

"Have 80 different things you can change about a deal so you have arrows in your quiver."

Trade small concessions that matter to them but not to you. Keep the reciprocity going while barely moving on price.

**The Front Desk Story:** Contractor nicked a corner. Partner asks: "How much would it cost to replace?" Contractor: "$1,500." Partner: "How about you just discount us $1,500?" Get them to price their own mistake, then flip it.

### Tactic 5: Framing
Position your offer as an investment, not a cost. Position their offer as overhead, not value.

"For a $5,000 investment, you can see XYZ return" vs "This is going to cost you $5,000."

Pool example: "Your house is worth $1M. Houses selling at $1.2M all have pools. Pool costs $100K. You're not spending $100K -- you're adding $200K in value and getting to enjoy the pool."
