# Building & Scaling Sales Teams
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## The 6 C's for Scaling Sales Teams

### C1 -- Closer Sequence
Question-based framework following the CLOSER acronym. Script should be ONLY questions. No paragraphs, no statements.

"If you get lost, you can find your way back again. Just ask the next question."

### C2 -- Consistent Daily Training
**This is the #1 thing besides conviction.**

Schedule: 60 minutes/day, 5 days/week.

Split into two skill sets:
- **Talking (25 min):** Read the script out loud with correct tonality. If they mess up, start again.
- **Obstacle Drilling (5 min):** Flash cards -- "I need to think about it." "I need to talk to my partner." "This is expensive." Draw the objection, deliver the overcome.
- **Listening (30 min):** Listen to a recorded call. Three questions:
  1. What went right?
  2. What went wrong?
  3. What are you going to do next time?

Then drill the specific improvement and move on.

### C3 -- Call Recordings
Record every call. Tools: Gong (for Zoom -- "the f***ing best").

Gong capabilities: talk time ratios, question counts, engagement tracking, AI analysis.

"How are you going to study game film if you don't record? Every NBA team plays the game and watches the footage."

### C4 -- Communication Cycles & Feedback
- **Daily huddles:** Share testimonials, pump up, kiss on forehead good night
- **Weekly one-on-ones** (new reps) or bi-weekly (experienced)
- **Monthly reviews**

One-on-ones: Have them tag 3 calls (best, worst, average). Review where they clarified pain, asked for sale, overcame objections.

**The Golf Swing Rule:** Only give ONE thing to work on at a time. Don't try to fix 8 things at once.

"If he just said 'take another 20 swings and put one thumb under the other,' I could probably get better."

### C5 -- Cut the Fat
"Cut it fast. If guys can't close in the first week to two weeks, they ain't closing."

"It's much easier to take a 6 and get them to a 9 than to take a 2 and get them to a 5."

"If you have a sales person you've been waiting for to turn the corner for 6 months -- they're not going to turn the corner."

Cut bottom 10% on a regular basis. Persistent 30% performance jump from cutting bottom performers.

### C6 -- Competition & Career Path
**Competition cycles:** 6 weeks works best. Long enough to believe they can accomplish something, short enough to feel urgent.

Structure: 3-person teams (not individual). Adjusted draft (best with worst). Creates competitiveness + camaraderie.

Prize: $1,500/person for a 2-3 day weekend (e.g., Vegas). "Not expensive, but worth every dollar."

Must have a published leaderboard updated daily. Bell ring (in person) or GIF thread (remote) for every sale.

**Career path:** Minor milestones every 50 deals closed. Go from $500/deal to $525 to $550. "Just the fact that something is changing on the outside relieves anxiety."

## Hiring Principles
- Never hire seasoned closers
- Find people and educate them about the PROSPECT, not the product
- "If your sales training is focused on your product, you're doing it wrong"
- All closers under 27 years old (one exception at 40)
- Sink or swim in first 1-2 weeks

## Team Structure
- Separate outbound and inbound
- Setters and closers for BOTH teams (4 roles total)
- Outbound setters -> outbound closers
- Inbound setters -> inbound closers
- Different prospect types = different specialists

## Compensation Benchmarks
- 10-15% of deal value works well as group goal
- Per-deal bonus with career path escalation
- Competitions: $1,500/person for 2-3 day trips

## ADDITIONAL INSIGHTS (Deep Pass)

### Train on Prospect, Not Product
- "When you're training sales people, you don't train them on the product. You train them on the prospect."
- "He might know the product perfectly but he just said the guy's problem was not the problem"
- "The product doesn't matter. If you know a niche really well you'll know their pains."

### Duplicating Sales Skill > Having It
- "It's one level of skill to learn to sell. Another level to teach to sell."
- Teaching is "a more valuable skill"
- "If you can duplicate your skill in someone else, that's how you make 10x, 100x more money"

### Sales Comp: Always Off Cash Collected Today
- Aligns salesman interest with business interest
- Back-end payments are success of delivery team, not sales team

### The Wealth Management Reframe (Used ON Hormozi)
- Was getting pitched private wealth management, wanted out fast
- Said "I already have a wealth advisor"
- Guy: "90% of the clients we have that shift over already have a wealth advisor. So it means you already know how a lot of these things work."
- Hormozi: "What just happened? I thought I was getting out of this and now I'm more like the customers that ultimately buy"
- Applied this reframe to all his selling
