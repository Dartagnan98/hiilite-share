# The Conviction Framework
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## Core Principle
"Conviction will correct your tone."

If you believe in what you sell, you will say it the right way. You don't need all the sales skills. You'll do it right because you genuinely want to help the person.

## The Belief Continuum
Belief is NOT binary (believe / don't believe). It's a continuum: "To what extent do you believe?"

- Would you bet $1,000 on this product?
- Would you bet $10,000?
- Would you sell your mother this product?

## The Sales Equation
**Closing Percentage = Skill of Sales (1-10) x Conviction (1-100)**

Conviction counts DOUBLE because:
1. It increases closing percentage
2. It increases work ethic (follow-up speed, persistence, number of attempts)

**Total Deals = (Skill x Conviction) x Work Ethic**

A salesperson with skill level 2 and conviction level 100 will outperform a salesperson with skill level 8 and conviction level 10.

## Proof: Religion
"Every religious movement sells something incredibly hard to sell -- a completely new worldview. They don't train missionaries in the skill of sales. They get them to believe wholeheartedly."

## The Mortgage Leads Story (Conviction in Action)
Alex consulted for a mortgage leads company. Salesman John couldn't sell leads. Instead of training scripts, Alex said:

"If you actually believed the leads were good, you'd say: 'Dude, these leads are unbelievable. I'm studying for my real estate exam right now so I can get in on these leads. My aunt is a realtor and she has more business than she can handle. I'm sending her traffic. I'm trying to get my brother to do it with me. I'm not sure how long I'm going to work here because these leads are killing it.'"

## Why Salespeople Burn Out
"They burn out not because they can't handle 'no' but because they can't handle how they feel about themselves."

If you don't believe in the product, you develop cognitive dissonance: you believe you're a good person but you need to make money, and those two things are at odds.

## Hot Streaks
"When you are hot, those are the videos you should watch. That's the game tape footage you should go over and over again. When you're hot, you say things differently. You pause at different points."

"Great salesmen never get cold. They are always on a hot streak because they know how to recreate success."

## Two Mental Hacks for Conviction
1. "I will never have a perfect product, but I can have perfect intentions."
2. "The prospect will be better served working with me than with anyone else." -- You may not be perfect, but you're the best shot they have.

## Conviction-Building Tactics for Teams
1. **Re-read testimonials out loud daily** in front of your sales team. Fresh ones if possible. "They get beat up every day with nos. They need to remember why they're still doing it."
2. **Fix everything you can about the product.** Never blame a customer for lack of success.
3. **Never stop improving the product** so you always know what's up to date.
4. **Bring head of customer support to sales meeting:** "Erica, tell these guys what you do for our customers." Sales team hears the delivery process and gets re-convicted.
5. **Watch completion/success footage on days you close:** Close rates spike when the team watches success stories from finishing cohorts.

## The "Caring Most" Framework

### Core Principle
"The person who cares the most about the buyer's interests is the one who wins the sale."

A sale is always made -- either they sell you on why they can't, or you sell them on why they should. The winner is whoever cares more.

### Commission Breath
"People can smell whether you're in it for your agenda or their agenda."

### The Gym Owner Story
Went 0 for 25 with the same script. Then realized he was selling for HIS agenda, not theirs. Switched to genuinely caring about the prospect's outcome. Went 5 for 9 immediately. Same script.

### How to Apply
"I care more about your best interest than you do. You're going to throw things in my way, but I care about you enough to get us through them."

"The moment you're able to get someone to be real with you in the sale, you've already won."

### Why "Unskilled" Salespeople Close
"Some of our gyms who honestly aren't the most skilled salespeople, but they kill it. They're genuinely kind-hearted, caring people that you can tell from their vibe really love helping people. Those people get away with so much lack of skill because the people believe they are acting in their best interest."
