# Tone and Voice Techniques
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## The Hidden Dialogue
"Words are only 10% of communication. 90% is how you say the words."

Two dialogues happening on every call:
- Words = talking to the logical brain
- Tone = talking to the emotional brain (the one making decisions)

"How you say what you say IS what you say."

## The Wife Example
"Alex." (neutral) vs "Alex!" (excited) vs "Alexxxx." (seductive/warning) -- Same word, three different meanings, three different afternoons.

## The "I didn't say he hit his wife" Example
- "I didn't say he hit his wife" (someone else said it)
- "I didn't SAY he hit his wife" (I implied it)
- "I didn't say HE hit his wife" (someone else hit her)
- "I didn't say he HIT his wife" (he did something else)
- "I didn't say he hit HIS wife" (he hit someone else's wife)
- "I didn't say he hit his WIFE" (he hit someone else)

Same sentence, 6 different meanings based on emphasis.

## Five Voice Modulation Elements

### Three Constants (Sales Tone):
1. **Volume** -- Speak loud enough they can hear you (especially important in person)
2. **Speed** -- Speak slowly enough they can comprehend you. Sweet spot: ~150 words per minute. Too fast = trust drops. Too slow = you sound unintelligent. Alex speaks at 185 wpm naturally and has to consciously slow down.
3. **Articulation** -- Pronounce each word clearly. Don't mumble. Round out the words. Pro tip: fully pronouncing each word automatically slows someone down who talks too fast when nervous.

### Two Variables:
4. **Pauses** -- Short pause (dot dot dot) draws attention to a point. Full stop (period) expects a response. The close should have the longest pause.
   - Research: Salespeople who wait up to 8 seconds after asking to buy close 30% more sales (three big meta-analyses including Columbia)
   - "Ask them to buy and shut the f*** up."
5. **Frequency/Pitch** -- Voice goes up = question (soliciting response). Voice goes down = statement (not soliciting response). Question mark at end of sentence.

## Script Notation System
Only three punctuation marks needed in a sales script:
- **Period** = full stop, expectation of response
- **Dot dot dot (...)** = brief pause, drawing attention
- **Question mark** = raise voice, solicit response

"The most important time to solicit a response combines both: a long pause AND raised voice. That's when you ask them to buy."

## How to Sound Natural (Not Scripted)
If you sound like you're reading a script, you don't know the script well enough.

**Script Memorization Method (scientifically backed):**
1. Print two copies of the script
2. Read the entire script out loud, enunciating every word
3. After completing, use a marker to cross out ONE word
4. Read the entire script again with the word missing
5. Cross out another word at the end
6. Repeat until every word is blacked out and you're saying the script from a blank page

"By the end, you will have repeated the script as many times as there are words in the script."

Result: "You will be breathing the script." The last thing you should be thinking about is what to say -- you should be thinking about what the prospect is saying.

## Lowering Tone for Emphasis
"I'm lowering my tone right now so you know that this is really important." -- Use deliberately when making critical points. Then go upbeat to bring energy back up.
