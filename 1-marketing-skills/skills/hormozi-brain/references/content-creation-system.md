## 1. HIS CONTENT PHILOSOPHY

### Content Is the Input, Not the Output
- "Content is not the output, it's the input. The output is the audience. The audience is what compounds."
- The audience compounds like Berkshire Hathaway's capital compounds
- "The longer you can wait, the bigger the ask can be at the end. The longer the runway, the bigger the plane that can take off from it."

### Why He Fought It for Years (And Was Wrong)
- Was anti-content. Only ran ads. Wanted to be "rich and unknown"
- Turning point: Kylie Jenner made Forbes as billionaire at age 20. Hormozi was 27, had taken home $17M the year before
- Then saw: Huda Beauty sold for $600M, Conor McGregor's Proper 12 at $600M in 12 months, The Rock's Teremana now worth ~$4B, Prime Water $250M in one quarter
- Read Naval Ravikant's Almanack -- four types of leverage: labor, capital, code, media. Realized he had nothing that compounded
- Had dinner with Dean Graziosi who said "if a couple weird letters every week is the price I have to pay for the impact I want to have, I pay that price any day of the week and twice on Sunday"
- "I've become a significantly better entrepreneur by making content because it distracts me from fucking up my business"
- Leila: "Can you just write another book?" -- because when he writes, the business does exceptionally well because he does so little to it

### The Four Types of Leverage (New Economy)
- Labor (people doing work for you)
- Capital (other people's money invested)
- Code (software -- one-time investment, infinite output)
- Media (content -- one video can reach unlimited people)
- Last two (code + media) = new economy leverage, permissionless
- "Four out of the top five biggest companies in the world are not built on old oil but the new oil" (attention)
- Media has infinite leverage: one video can reach 1 person or 100M people

### Attention Is the New Oil
- Old economy: Rockefellers built on oil (scarce physical resource)
- New economy: attention is the scarce resource
- Platforms (Facebook, YouTube, TikTok) = "digital landscape" buying up collective attention
- People who will become new centi-billionaires must do 3 things: capture, hold, and multiply attention
- Mr. Beast prediction: "He will be a centi-billionaire" (made when Mr. Beast was 24)

### Entertainment vs Education Audience Split
- Entertainment viewers want more entertainment. Education viewers want more education
- They do NOT convert from entertainment to education in any appreciable rate
- "These people do not become these people, not in any appreciable rate that we would build a business around"
- Education viewers are the buyers. Entertainment viewers are vocal in comments but don't buy

### RPM as the Real Metric, Not Views
- All views are NOT created equal
- A video with 100K views can generate more ad revenue (and downstream book sales, applications) than one with 10M views
- RPM (revenue per thousand views) is the true quality metric, not raw view count
- Ad revenue (RPM x views) pairs quality + quantity
- Highest RPM months also had most book sales, opt-ins, and business applications

### Content Creates Multiple Revenue Streams
- 78% of Gym Launch customers consumed at least 2 pieces of long-form content before buying
- Content made ads work, not the other way around
- Content is a massive multiplier on ALL other advertising methods
- Free content retains paying customers -- they don't separate where value comes from
- Content that generates inquiries can be: (a) run as paid ads and (b) compiled into "greatest hits" for sales team to send prospects matching objections

### Making Content as an Entrepreneur
- "I've become a significantly better entrepreneur by making content because it distracts me from fucking up my business"
- Content channels creative ADD productively
- Book writing = same principle: creative energy channeled away from tinkering with the business

## 3. CONTENT STRATEGY

### The Content Unit: Hook -> Retain -> Reward
Every piece of content has three parts:
1. **HOOK**: Give a reason to redirect attention. Get them to notice.
2. **RETAIN**: Keep attention long enough to deliver value. Get them to consume.
3. **REWARD**: Satisfy the reason they consumed it. Make them want more.

Long content = many content units strung together. Short content = fewer units. Start short, build up.

### SPCL Framework (Influence, Not Just Views)
- **S = Status** -- You control scarce resources people want (money, access, knowledge, connections)
- **P = Proof** -- Why should anyone listen to you? Third-party validation, certifications, awards, testimonials
- **C = Content quality** -- The actual material
- **L = Likeability** -- They see themselves in you. Shared values, background, just being you

### Proof-Promise-Plan Introduction Formula
Discovered by rewatching top 35 educational videos:
1. **PROOF**: Prove you know what you're talking about
2. **PROMISE**: Tell them what they'll get/learn
3. **PLAN**: Set expectations for what happens next
- Order flexible -- emphasize whichever is most important
- For business education, usually lead with PROOF
- "Entertainment confirms the thumbnail; education edifies the person"

### Hook Creation: 3 Elements

**Element 1 -- Pick Good Topics (5 Sources):**
- Far Past: Life lessons, behavior changes
- Recent Past: Pull from calendar, conversations, meetings, events
- Present: Real-time capture. Email yourself ideas. Test on Twitter
- Trends: Current events that apply to your expertise
- Manufactured: Create experiences to document

**Element 2 -- Pick Good Headlines (7 Characteristics):**
- Recency, Relevancy, Celebrity, Proximity, Conflict, Unusual, Ongoing
- Combine all 7 = maximum attention

**Element 3 -- Match the Format:**
- Make content look like high-performing content on that platform
- Same video formatted differently for IG Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts

### Retention: 3 Structures
1. **Lists**: Things/facts/tips following a theme. Give the number upfront
2. **Steps**: Actions in order to accomplish a goal. More explicit reward
3. **Stories**: Events (real or imaginary) with lessons. People want to know what happens next
- Best content uses ALL THREE interwoven together

### Reward = Value Per Second
- How good your content is = how often it rewards your audience / the time it takes to consume
- "There's no such thing as too long, only too boring"
- People don't have shorter attention spans. They have higher STANDARDS because competition is fierce
- If your audience grows, you did good. If not, your stuff isn't good enough (not the algorithm)

### Give:Ask Ratio
- **Television** (mature, maximally monetizing): 3.6:1 ratio (47 min content : 13 min ads per hour)
- **Facebook** (mature): 4:1 ratio (4 content posts : 1 ad)
- **TikTok** (growth phase): Nearly zero ads for years. All give
- If you want to GROW: give until they ask. Way beyond 4:1
- "Give in public, ask in private." Your public reputation stays purely on giving
- The moment you start asking publicly is the moment you slow growth
- Cautionary tale: Friend's podcast blew up, asked too frequently, shrank, asked more, got less -- vicious cycle

**Two Ways to Ask:**
1. Integrated: Advertise within each piece. Example: 58.5 min value + 1.5 min CTA = 39:1 ratio
2. Intermittent: Many pure-give pieces, occasionally an ask piece. Example: 10 gives then 1 ask

### Thumbnail/Headline Strategy
- Go from VAGUE to CLEAR
- "If they want that thing they will click on that thing and then you just give them that thing"
- Clarity beats curiosity for educational content
- 70% of screen in best YouTube videos = words/text, not face

### Context > Quality
- "The message is 10% of how people consume it. 90% is the context around the message"
- "Elon Musk can tweet on the toilet and get a million likes... because he's the richest man in the world"
- "Solve for the proof and then the content will take care of itself"
- Podcast went from 3K to 25M downloads -- two things changed: broader name and $46M proof event

### Pre-Work Beats Post-Production
- "An ounce of pre-work is worth a pound of post"
- Spending 25% of the old post-production time on pre-research eliminated 90% of man-hours
- Result: 4x video output at HIGHER quality
- 75% of top-performing reels emphasized LANGUAGE over production
- 75% of bottom-performing reels emphasized visual effects over message
- Text overlay clarifying words on screen = helps. Flames behind speaker = doesn't help
- "If the point is education, production can distract from the objective"

### Repost Your Best Content
- "Look at your number one piece of content from last year. Post it again."
- It will do just as well or better because new audience members haven't seen it
- "We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught"
- Simpsons: longest-running show, same introduction every episode with tiny variations (Bart's chalkboard)
- 1 in 6 of Hormozi's audience didn't know he had a book after 2 years of daily posts

### Make All Content for Strangers
- Never make inside jokes. Never reference past content without context
- Existing audience won't mind the "if you're new" intro
- New audience will feel included, not like an outsider
- Title for strangers, introduce yourself every time, explain all references

### 7 Content Lessons
1. **Switch from "how to" to "how I"**: Less ego, more receptive audience
2. **We need to be reminded more than taught**: Repeat yourself way more than you think
3. **Puddles, Ponds, Lakes, Oceans**: Start narrow. Be king of a small puddle before jumping to the ocean
4. **Content creates sales tools**: Content -> paid ads and "greatest hits" for sales team
5. **Free content retains paying customers**: Customers don't separate where value comes from
6. **No such thing as too long, only too boring**: Streaming platforms prove people binge hours of content
7. **Avoid pre-scheduling posts**: Manual posts outperform pre-scheduled ones -- gives a final quality check moment

## 4. THE 6 LESSONS FROM 40 MONTHS / $4M / 35,000 PIECES OF CONTENT

### Lesson 1: Edutainment -> Education
- Entertainment viewers want entertainment. Education viewers want education. They don't cross over
- Education drives book sales, opt-ins, and applications. Entertainment doesn't
- Ran 90-day experiment: edutainment content -> views 2-3x'd but ad revenue dropped by HALF

### Lesson 2: For Us -> For You
- Make content for your ideal customer, not what's easiest for you
- Tactics: clear packaging (not vague thumbnails), proof-based intros, emphasis on language over production
- Lists/steps/stories over vlogs and razzle-dazzle
- Visualize data, not visual effects

### Lesson 3: Wide -> Narrow
- Doubling down on business models, leverage, selling
- Narrow topic focus attracts the right people

### Lesson 4: Views -> Revenue (RPM)
- Track ad revenue as primary metric, not views
- Ad revenue = views x RPM. Higher RPMs = right audience
- In the 90-day experiment, entertainment content doubled views but halved ad revenue

### Lesson 5: Shorts -> Longs
- Shorts viewers watch more shorts. Long viewers watch more longs
- Longs drive far more conversions
- Exception: someone finds you on TikTok (shorts) then watches your longs on YouTube (cross-platform)

### Lesson 6: Assume More -> Assume Nothing
- Title like they don't know you
- Introduce yourself every time
- Explain all references
- Proof-Promise-Plan for every intro

## 5. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC INSIGHTS

### Shorts vs Longs Data
- Prevailing theory: shorts viewers -> long viewers -> customers (funnel model)
- Reality: shorts viewers watch more shorts, long viewers watch more longs, customers buy more
- "These are different audiences"
- Longs drive significantly more conversions than shorts
- Exception: cross-platform discovery (TikTok shorts -> YouTube longs)

### YouTube Specific
- Commitment on first call: "I'd like to have this channel be something in 5 to 10 years"
- Vendor said no client had ever said that -- they all wanted leads in 90 days
- 70% of screen in best educational YouTube videos = words/text, not face
- Thumbnails: clarity over curiosity for educational content
- Clear thumbnails > vague/mysterious thumbnails

### Podcast Metrics
- Top 1% of podcasters = only 21 episodes uploaded
- Hormozi: 4.5 years of 2 episodes/week = only 2,000-3,000 downloads/month
- First 150 episodes were gym-only content
- Took 6 years to hit top 10 in entrepreneurship category
- Two things changed the trajectory: broader name + massive proof event ($46M exit)

### Social Media Posting Cadence
- 35,000 pieces per year = ~96 pieces per day
- $1-2M businesses doing 365 pieces/year
- Hormozi doing 100x more = 100x the prospects
- "It's far more linear than you would expect"
- Rule of 100: 100 minutes of content per day minimum (one of four options along with $100/day ads, 100 cold outreaches, or 100 warm outreaches)

### Influence Hierarchy: Live > Long > Short > A-List
From UK vs US soccer celebrity match:
- A-list celebrities: almost no applause
- Shorts creators: slight applause
- Long-form creators: legit applause
- Live streamers: stadium erupted
- Why: Reinforcement cycles. 120 minutes of long-form = 480 shorts needed for same exposure. More time = more trust

### Scaling Content: Two Approaches
1. **Depth then Width**: Master one platform -> maximize quality & quantity -> add next platform. Better for resource-constrained/beginners
2. **Width then Depth**: Post on one platform -> go regular -> add closest similar platform -> continue until all covered -> maximize all. Better for experienced creators

- Get 2-3 ads from each new customer (testimonials, transformations)
- Every customer becomes a content/ad asset
- Builds compounding library of social proof creative
- Customer stories as ads have highest trust signals

### Content Repurposing for Ads

## 8. BRANDING

### Pairing Framework
- Branding = "deliberate pairing of things through an outcome"
- Good branding = deliberate pairing with stuff your ideal customers LIKE
- Bad branding = pairing with stuff they don't
  - Bud Light + Dylan Mulvaney = sales dropped
  - Red Bull = extreme sports (deliberate association)
  - Nike pairs with LeBron/Tiger (winning) -> $5 t-shirt becomes $60 -> 12x premium

### How to Measure Brand
- Three metrics: influence x direction x reach
  - **Influence**: How likely to change behavior when seen
  - **Direction**: Toward you or away from you
  - **Reach**: How many people
- Strong brand improves advertising: 6x higher click-through rate on branded vs generic ads
- Warren Buffett: "The single most important decision in evaluating a business is pricing power"

### When to Invest in Brand vs Direct Response
- Hormozi was anti-brand until he saw the data:
  - The Rock: billion-dollar tequila overnight
  - Conor McGregor: $600M exit on Proper 12 in 12 months
  - Huda Beauty: $600M exit from Instagram
  - Kylie Jenner: billionaire younger than him
  - Prime Water: $250M in one quarter
- Brand is the long game. Direct response is the short game
- Brand makes direct response MORE effective (6x click-through rates)
- Both compound, but brand compounds the person/entity, not just the campaign

### Brand as Bouquet
- Brand elements are like flowers. Individually just flowers. Assembled deliberately = bouquet
- Random pairings hurt. Curated pairings strengthen
- Every association matters -- deliberate curation of what you pair with

### Mega Stars Cross-Pollinate Audiences
- Rogan = UFC + comedy + fitness/biohacking + aliens
- More dimensions = faster compound
- Three things future billionaires must master: capture attention, hold attention, multiply attention

## 9. COPY & CREATIVE FRAMEWORKS

### 12 Copy Hacks

1. **Headlines Come First**: 80 cents of ad dollar is in the headline. A banger headline can 3-5x a control. Formula: "How to [good thing] without [bad thing] even if [biggest fear]"

2. **Say What Only You Can Say**: Proof beats promise. Competitors can copy your offer but not your proof

3. **Always Call Out Who You're Looking For (and Who You're NOT)**: "Only for people trying to lose 30+ lbs." Specificity creates compelling copy

4. **Always Have a Reason Why**: The word "because" is one of the most persuasive words. Any reason > no reason

5. **Damaging Admissions**: Admit flaws upfront. "What you sacrifice in promise, you gain in trust. Trust trumps promise."

6. **Show Don't Tell**: Describe the MOMENTS of the outcome, not abstract benefits

7. **Tie Benefits to Status**: Not just "fast cooking" but "so fast your friends will wonder how you found time"

8. **Use Urgency and Scarcity**: Make it LEGITIMATE. Say no when sold out so next time they buy first

9. **Implied Authority**: "As seen on...", longevity is authority, awards matter even if obscure

10. **Always Have a PS Statement**: 2nd most-read part of any written copy (after headline)

11. **Clear CTA**: Tell them exactly what to do next and what will happen

12. **3rd Grade Reading Level**: Fancy = friction, simple = sales. Short sentences, small words, big promises

13. **Bonus: Use Humor Whenever Possible**: Old Spice = highest-converting ad of all time

### Data-Driven Marketing (Slicing Statistics)
Three wheels to spin for compelling claims:
1. What percentage of clients achieve the outcome
2. What is the outcome (absolute $, %, relative)
3. Over what time duration

Ways to express the same stat:
- Percentage: "87% success rate"
- Fraction: "1 in 5.5 gyms is a million-dollar gym"
- Frequency: "Every 7.7 days we create another seven-figure gym"
- Absolute: "Average gym adds $15,500 in first 2 weeks"
- Relative: "Average gym pays off 9 months of rent in first 14 days"

### Landing Page Optimization
- One split test per week. 52 tests/year = massive compounding improvements
- 90% of all tests should be headline or image (above the fold)
- Monday marketing meeting: "What was last week's test? Did we beat the control? What's this week's test?"
- Neil Patel: "The future of marketing is CRO" because ad prices only go up (auction-based)

### More Better New Framework (Applied to Content/Ads)

**More** (Do More of What Already Works):
- If doing 100 cold calls/day, why not 1,000?
- If spending $1K/day on ads, why not $10K?
- For businesses under $1M profit: just do MORE first

**Better** (Improve What You're Doing):
- Test one thing per week per platform
- Prioritize front to back: headlines, first 5 seconds, CTRs
- 52 improvements per year from weekly testing

**New** (Expand to New Places/Methods):
- Only after exhausting More and Better
- Order of "new" from easiest to hardest:
  1. New placement on same platform
  2. New platform with similar media type
  3. New platform with different media type
  4. New Core Four method entirely
