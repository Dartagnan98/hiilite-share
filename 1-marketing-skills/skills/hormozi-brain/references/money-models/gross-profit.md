# Gross Profit
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Money Models course + 383 video transcripts)

---

## Definition
Price minus Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). What it costs you to DELIVER the thing.

---

## The 80% Gross Margin Rule (Services)
- Service-based businesses (local or national) need at least **80% gross profit margin**
- If delivery costs $100, sell for $500
- 80% gross margin may yield ~30% net margin after rent, admin, utilities, marketing, etc.

---

## Gross Profit vs. Gross Margin
- Gross profit = absolute dollar amount ($80)
- Gross margin = percentage (80%)
- Same thing, different measurement

---

## What Goes Into COGS (and What Doesn't)
- COGS = variable costs that go up per unit sold
- Rent does NOT go into COGS
- Admin costs do NOT go into COGS
- Utilities do NOT go into COGS
- Rep salary split across clients DOES count (e.g., $6K rep / 10 clients = $600/client)

---

## Service Example Calculation
- Clients pay $3,000/mo each
- 1 rep per 10 clients at $6,000/mo salary
- Cost per client: $600
- Gross profit per client: $2,400
- Gross margin: 80%

---

## The Arms Race of Business
"He who makes his customer worth more to his business than do his competition wins."
- The person who makes the most money per customer can spend the most on advertising
- This creates an ethical monopoly -- you can outbid everyone in the attention auction
- CAC between competitors in the same space is often fairly similar
- The HUGE discrepancy is in how much each company makes per customer

---

## The $50,000 Burger Story
- Wall Street guy making $50M/yr bought a $50K burger
- In hours worked, that burger cost him LESS than a regular burger costs a normal person
- You can only go down to zero on cost. You can go infinitely high on lifetime value.

---

## The 56x Consulting Story
- Competitor sold the same number of units per month
- Alex: $17M/yr profit. Competitor: $300K/yr profit
- 56x difference, same volume. Difference = gross profit per customer

---

## Three Ways to Increase Gross Profit
1. **More** -- sell more quantity or longer duration (bulk, rebuy tomorrow)
2. **Better** -- sell a higher-quality version (regular burger vs. organic burger)
3. **New** -- sell a new complementary thing (fries and a Coke, then supersize)

## GROSS MARGIN FIX PLAYBOOK (Deep Pass Batch 1)

### Step-by-Step Process
1. Calculate gross margin (revenue - COGS / revenue)
2. If below 80%, either decrease cost of delivery or increase price
3. For service businesses, COGS = incremental cost of one additional unit (not rent, not front desk, not HR)
4. Each jump (80->90, 90->95) doubles profitability
5. From gross margin, allocate 20% for marketing/acquisition
6. Everything between marketing cost and gross margin covers rent, payroll, software, profit
- "If you do not get this price, you will make no profit and you will not be able to scale"

### Specific Margin Math
- 80% to 90% gross margin = 2x more profitable (half the cost per unit)
- 90% to 95% = 2x more profitable again
- Biggest companies run 99% gross margins
- Going from 75% to 80% gross margin = 33% increase in year-end profit
- At 66% gross margin with 12% net: bumping to 80% gross = 26% net (2.2x take-home)

### Marketing Budget Rule
- Spend up to 20% of gross margin on acquisition
- If selling $60 service with $20 cost = 66% margin. Need to either cut cost to $12 or raise price to $100 to hit 80%

### Coaching Business Example
- Coach paid $4K/month handling 40 clients at $400/month = $16K revenue
- Gross margin: 75% ($12K/$16K)
- Options: charge $800 more/month across clients OR pay coach less
- Going from 75% to 80% = 33% increase in year-end take-home

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP) -- 3-Step Back-of-Napkin Calculation

**Step 1: Figure out gross profit per unit**
- Revenue per unit minus cost to deliver per unit
- Example (products): Sell for $100, costs $20 to make/deliver = $80 GP per unit
- Example (services): $3,000/mo client, $600 delivery cost = $2,400 GP per month

**Step 2: Figure out average number of transactions (products) or churn rate (services)**
- Products: How many times does the average customer buy? Track over customer lifetime.
- Services: What % of customers cancel per month?

**Step 3: Multiply (products) or divide by churn (services)**
- **Product LTGP**: GP per unit x avg # of transactions
  - Example: $80 GP x 4 purchases = **$360 LTGP**
- **Service LTGP**: GP per month / monthly churn rate
  - Example: $2,400 GP / 5% churn = **$48,000 LTGP**

### Gross Profit vs Net Profit (Author Note)
- **Gross Profit** = revenue minus ONLY delivery costs (COGS)
- **Net Profit** = revenue minus ALL costs (rent, admin, marketing, payroll, etc.)
- Common confusion: people mix these up when calculating unit economics
- For all money model calculations, use GROSS PROFIT -- not net profit
- "When I say profit in the context of money models, I always mean gross profit unless I specifically say otherwise"
