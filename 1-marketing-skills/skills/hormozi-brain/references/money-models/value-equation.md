# The Value Equation
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Offers + $100M Money Models course)

---

## THE FORMULA

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)**

---

## FOUR VARIABLES

### Top of Fraction (INCREASE These)

**1. Dream Outcome**
- Is the promise something people actually want?
- The bigger and more specific the dream, the higher the perceived value

**2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement**
- How likely do they believe they'll get the result?
- Social proof, testimonials, case studies, guarantees all increase this
- "70% of chiropractors we work with achieve 100+ patients in their first 60 days without running any ads"

### Bottom of Fraction (DECREASE These)

**3. Time Delay**
- How quickly between buy and get?
- Speed is one of the best all-time competitive advantages
- Netflix beat Blockbuster by making time delay zero
- Amazon: next-day delivery (minimal time)

**4. Effort & Sacrifice**
- What must they start doing? What must they stop doing?
- Netflix beat Blockbuster by making effort zero (no driving to store, no late fees)
- Amazon: one-click buy (zero effort)

---

## KEY INSIGHTS

- Most entrepreneurs only work on the TOP side (bigger promises, more testimonials)
- The FORTUNES are on the BOTTOM side (faster results, less effort)
- Netflix beat Blockbuster on the BOTTOM -- same dream outcome, but zero time delay and zero effort
- Amazon beats everyone on ALL four: great selection (dream outcome), reviews (perceived likelihood), next-day delivery (time), one-click buy (effort)

---

## TTV (Time to Value)

- "Look at what everyone else sells and deliver the same thing in half the time"
- You don't have to deliver the FINAL result first -- deliver A result first
- Book publishing company example: created 7-day mini book boot camp. Not the real book, but proves the concept and gives an early win.
- First win = proof of concept for the customer. Locks in their belief.

---

## VALUE EQUATION AS COPY/HEADLINES

The 4 variables = the 4 components of any marketing claim:
- Dream outcome + number of people helped + time to achieve + conditions/stipulations

Example headline:
"70% of chiropractors we work with achieve 100+ patients in their first 60 days without running any ads"
- Dream outcome: 100+ patients
- Perceived likelihood: 70% success rate
- Time delay: 60 days
- Effort/sacrifice: without running any ads

## TRIM AND STACK METHOD (Building High-Value Offers)

1. List every single micro-problem a customer encounters in their journey
2. List the ideal solution for each problem
3. Rate each: which is MOST valuable to customer? Which costs YOU virtually nothing to deliver?
4. Keep: high value + low cost. Cut everything else.
5. Result: extremely valuable, very high margin product/service

## GUARANTEES (Risk Reversal -- Directly Increases Perceived Likelihood)

### Type 1: Unconditional Guarantee
- "Money back for any reason, no questions asked"
- Strongest type. Most risk reversal.
- Math: even if refunds go from 1% to 5%, if you gain 20% more sales, you net +15%
- "Don't let emotions override math"

### Type 2: Conditional Guarantee
- "If X happens, we'll do Y"
- Reverse-engineer conditions from your most successful customers' behaviors
- "If you show up to every workout and send pics of all meals for 90 days and don't lose the weight, I'll give you 2x your money back"
- Fewer stipulations = stronger guarantee

### Type 3: Anti-Guarantee
- Lean INTO having no guarantee
- "If you need a guarantee, this isn't for you. The success is on you."
- Or: proprietary info ("once you learn the secret, I can't un-teach you")
- Works for premium/exclusive positioning

### Type 4: Implied Guarantee
- Performance-based pricing: rev share, profit share, performance bonuses, kickers
- "We get 20% of your revenue increase" = you only pay when it works
- Aligns incentives completely

### Stacking Guarantees
- 30-day unconditional + 90-day conditional
- "Try for 30 days. If it's not for you, full refund. Stay 90 days, hit these milestones, and if you don't get results, full refund."
- Makes the sale essentially a trial

### The 1% Compliance Hack
- "Write what you're going to do each morning and what you did each evening for 6 weeks"
- 1-2% compliance rate on this simple task
- If they don't do it, guarantee doesn't apply
- You know 99% won't qualify for the refund

### Don't Say "Results Guaranteed"
- Banner blindness. Means nothing.
- Spell out exactly: "If you don't lose 6 inches from your waist by [date], I will write you a check for the difference."

## THE BELIEVABILITY TEST

- Famous marketer ran ad: "Give me $100 and I'll give you $1,000 back. Call this number."
- Nobody called. Too good to be true.
- $500/mo offer gets MORE optins than $5,000/mo offer because more people believe it
- Perceived likelihood must be calibrated -- too high and nobody believes you

## VALUE EQUATION EXPANDED (Deep Pass -- Batch 4)

### Four Components Applied
- Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
- Top: increase both. Bottom: decrease both. If bottom = zero, value is infinite
- Personal training vs Lipo: PT scores 1/4 (dream only). Lipo scores 4/4 (all four)
- "That's why you spend an hour arm wrestling for $1,000 while people buy $40,000 in 15 minutes"
- Amazon, Netflix, Uber all focused on decreasing bottom side
- "Fast beats free" (DMV example: friend charges $50 for what government does free, 14 locations, "murders it")
