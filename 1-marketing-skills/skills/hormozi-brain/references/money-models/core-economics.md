## The Core Business Equation

Three metrics that triangle acquisition:
1. **CAC** (Cost to Acquire a Customer) -- how much it costs to get a customer
2. **Gross Profit (GP)** -- how much you make minus cost of delivery
3. **Payback Period** -- how fast you break even

### The Fundamental Rule
- **LTGP > CAC** at minimum 3:1 ratio
- LTGP = Lifetime Gross Profit
- If you don't have LTGP > CAC, you don't have a business

---

## CAC (Cost to Acquire a Customer)

### Definition
All costs required to sell a new customer: advertising dollars, media buyer payroll, creative team, software, sales commissions, salaries, managers of those teams.

### Calculation Examples

**Outreach model:**
- $3,000/mo emailer + $200/mo software + $800 commissions (8 sales x $100) = $4,000
- $4,000 / 8 sales = **$500 CAC**

**Content model:**
- $10,000/mo media payroll (2 people x $5K) + $1,000 commissions (10 x $100) = $11,000
- $11,000 / 10 sales = **$1,100 CAC**

**Paid ads model:**
- $4,000 media buyer + $20,000 ad spend + $10,000 commissions + $1,000 software = $35,000
- $35,000 / 10 sales = **$3,500 CAC**

### How to Improve CAC
1. Make better offers (higher response rates, higher conversion)
2. Lower CPMs (better offers get cheaper distribution)
3. Cheaper labor (less impactful lever)
4. Cheaper software (least impactful lever)
- Focus on offers and conversion -- orders of magnitude more impactful than cost-cutting

## The Two Most Powerful Words in Advertising

**Free** and **New**

---

## The Penny Gap (Dan Ariely / Hershey Kiss Test)
- 9x more people took a FREE Hershey kiss vs. a 1-cent kiss on a college campus
- "Penny gap" = the massive behavioral difference between free and any price

---

## The Chick-fil-A Model
Either it's **free** or it's **full price**. Never discounted. No in-between.

---

## Free vs. Discount Testing (Gym Launch Data)
- 20 locations tested: 10 used "free" in ads, 10 didn't
- Result: Close rates were IDENTICAL between free and non-free leads
- Only difference: cost per lead was lower for free
- Free group made MORE sales (same close rate, more leads)
- "Free brings broke people" is a myth -- rich people also like free stuff

---

## The Believability Test
- Famous marketer ran ad: "Give me $100 and I'll give you $1,000 back. Call this number."
- Nobody called. Too good to be true.
- $500/mo offer gets MORE optins than $5,000/mo offer because more people believe it

---

## Three Reasons People Don't Want Your Free Thing
1. They don't want IT (change what you're giving or how you describe it)
2. They don't believe you (too good to be true)
3. Wrong people seeing it (targeting issue)

---

## Create Flow, Monetize, Add Friction
- Step 1: **Create flow** -- give the big sexy thing away
- Step 2: **Monetize** -- sell the qualified people
- Step 3: **Add friction** -- optimize with filters

### Ways to Add Friction
- Types of questions
- Number of steps in a process
- Targeting
- Force consumption before next step
- Increase ad length

---

## Four Ways to Display Discounts
1. **Percentage off** -- "87% off the first visit"
2. **Absolute amount off** -- "$87 off"
3. **Relative equivalent** -- "less than going out to lunch"
4. **Discounted price next to retail** -- "$800 ~~$1,200~~"

### Portion Off (Subset of Discount Display)
- 12-month sub at $100/mo = $1,200 total
- 25% off = $300 off = 3 months free = $900
- All different ways of saying the SAME thing. Rotate monthly for ad freshness.

### Pro Tip on Discount Effectiveness
- For a discount to work, the market must already understand the commodity price
- "$29 chiropractic adjustment" works because people KNOW it costs more
- For intangible/digital products without market-known pricing, use percentages instead

---

## Pros of Discounts
- Compliant for cheap leads
- Collect some money upfront
- Exceptional for **two-step sales** -- get card on file, then upsell with "use the card you have on file?"

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### 30D Cash as Core Metric
- **30D Cash** = gross profit extracted from a new customer in their first 30 days
- This is THE metric that determines scaling speed
- Why 30 days: every business can borrow money interest-free for 30 days via credit cards
- Cycle: charge card -> acquire customer -> collect 30D Cash -> pay card -> recycle -> acquire more

### CFA Levels Summary (from Book)
- **Level 1**: 30D GP < CAC -- floating on savings, slow growth, "where most businesses live"
- **Level 2**: 30D GP = CAC -- credit limit becomes ad budget, self-funding growth
- **Level 3**: 30D GP > 2x CAC -- each customer funds the next, doubling by customer not by month
- "The difference between Level 1 and Level 3 is the difference between hoping to grow and being unable to stop growing"
