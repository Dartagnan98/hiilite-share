## ENTERPRISE VALUE

Based on discount applied to future cash flows = how much money x how likely it will occur
- Lots of likely future money = big multiple
- Little uncertain money = small/no multiple
- Recurring company at $10M/$3M EBITDA: **8-15x multiple** (worth $24M-$45M)
- Transactional company at $10M/$3M EBITDA: **4x multiple** (worth $12M)

## 4 WAYS FORTUNES ARE MADE (Four Types of Leverage)

### 1. Labor (Permission-based)
- Oldest form: kings had servants, pyramids were built with labor
- Rockefeller built oil empire with labor
- Hardest type of leverage to employ
- Requires others' permission (they must agree to work for you)

### 2. Capital (Permission-based)
- Other people's money
- Buy something for $100 with someone else's money, it doubles to $200, pay back $100 + interest, keep the rest
- Infinite returns when you put $0 down
- Buffett, Munger, Carl Icahn = capital leverage
- Still requires permission (people must give you money under terms)

### 3. Code (Permissionless)
- Software: zero marginal cost per additional user
- CRM adding 1,000 users costs nothing incremental
- Tech giants = code leverage
- No one's permission needed

### 4. Media (Permissionless)
- Captured eyeballs you can continually sell
- Zero incremental cost, ~100% gross margins
- Zuckerberg, Google, Bloomberg = media leverage
- YouTube, podcasts, blogs = free to create, permissionless
- Joe Rogan selling podcast = media leverage

### Compounding Leverage Types
- Biggest fortunes: combine 2-3 types
- Take other people's money (capital) to get other people's labor to write code that sells as media
- "Winner takes all" games use multiple leverage types

### Alex's Accidental Fortune
- Switched from done-for-you service (labor) to licensing media
- Sold at zero incremental cost -> margins went through the roof
- Gym Launch: $1K -> $17M in 20 months

### Marginal Returns with Absolute Returns
- First 20 hours learning sales = massive improvement
- Years later, you're shaving fractions of a percent
- BUT those tiny improvements at the top = the difference between first and second place
- Olympic runner spends years to shave 0.25 seconds -- that's the gold medal difference

## TAX STRATEGIES

### Augusta Rule
- Rent your personal home to your business for up to **14 days per year**
- Income from this rental is TAX-FREE to you personally
- Business gets a TAX DEDUCTION for the rental expense
- Double benefit: tax-free income + business deduction
- Fair market rate: $500-$10,000/day depending on home/studio
- If you built a recording studio in your home ($30K-$80K), professional studio day rate is $5-10K/day
- 14 days x $10K = $140K tax-free income

### Where You Live Matters Most
- Zero tax states vs. California (15-16%)
- Puerto Rico: 4% federal if you live there 6+ months and export services
- Expatriation: renounce citizenship, become citizen by investment ($1M)

### The Ultra-Wealthy Tax Mindset
- Vast majority of net worth comes from GROWTH, not contributions
- $500/mo at age 20 > $5,000/mo at age 45 (compounding)
- Live on absolutely nothing for as long as possible

### Buy Low = Tax-Free Gains
- Buy something worth $100 for $30 = instant 3x gain with NO tax
- The gain compounds tax-free until you sell
- Warren Buffett model: buy and HOLD (See's Candy for 30+ years, Coca-Cola, GEICO)

### Loans Against Assets (How the Ultra-Wealthy Live)
- Loans are NEVER treated as income but ARE spendable money
- Plow money into assets that grow tax-free
- Take loans against growth to live on
- As long as loan payments < asset growth, you can do this indefinitely
- When you die, they rectify the account
- This is how every public company CEO lives -- loans against equity, stocks grow tax-free

### The "Little Hacks" Are Distractions
- Conservation easements, captive insurance = "BBs that don't make a significant difference"
- IRS can change rules retroactively and back-charge with interest and fees
- Focus on the big rocks: where you live, buy low, don't sell, live off loans

### Debt Strategy for Tax-Free Business Income
- Don't sell your business for an exit -- take loans against it
- $1M business grows to $2M: take $700K loan = tax-free cash, still own the business
- Business keeps growing to $2M more = total net worth $3.3M vs. $2M if you just held
- Vs. selling: after earnouts, transition, taxes = much less than headline number
- "The wealthiest people in the world buy and build. Most times they don't sell."
- Refinance at regular intervals as business value grows

## INVESTMENT PHILOSOPHY

### 4-Part Investment Lens
Evaluate every deal through:
1. **Yield** -- how much cash flow comes in monthly?
2. **Capital Preservation** -- how likely is my money coming back?
3. **Tax Advantage** -- will I get taxed at income or capital gains rates?
4. **Equity Growth** -- does the thing I'm buying become more valuable?

### Bad Deal Example (Wealth Advisor Pitch)
- $450K to a startup, 10% return at end of 12 months ($45K)
- Capital preservation: startup = probably not getting money back
- Yield: 10% (below S&P at ~20%)
- Tax: treated as income, effective return after taxes = 5.5%
- Equity growth: zero participation in upside
- Verdict: "Borderline unethical"

### Good Deal Example (Bridge Loan / Hard Money)
- $2.5M note to secure building out of foreclosure
- Building worth $5.1-5.6M (4 offers on it already, top buyer had $300K earnest money down)
- Personal guarantees from both buyers
- Yield: 4%/month = $100K/month = 48-96% annualized
- Capital preservation: building worth 2x+ the loan, personal guarantees, multiple backup buyers
- Minimum carry: 2 months = $200K minimum even if deal closes in 24 hours
- Tax: treated as income (bad), but 27% after-tax return still makes sense

### When to Start Investing
- Don't invest until money is flowing in FASTER than you can spend it on skill acquisition
- $25K, $50K, $250K, $500K in the bank? Spend it ALL on acquiring skills
- "Not going to Starbucks to become a millionaire = 40 years of enjoying nothing"
- Only invest when there's EXCESS after aggressively spending on skill acquisition
- "Somebody who has fewer skills needs more money."

### Opportunity Cost Thinking
- Every dollar you invest in the stock market is a dollar NOT invested in your skills/business
- Your business (where you have expertise) will almost always outperform passive investments
- Be "petrified" of investing in areas where people with deeper talent stacks will crush you

## WEALTH BUILDING ADDITIONS (Batch 5-6)

### Six Ways to Structure Money Trades (Pyramid of Leverage)
1. **I work, then you pay** (W-2): Lowest risk, avg tenure 3.9 years
2. **You pay as we go** (Contractor): 5x annual turnover vs employees. 3-12 month engagements
3. **You pay, then I work** (Surgeon model): Includes layaway. "People will pay for speed"
4. **When X happens, you pay me** (Performance): Rev share, profit share, equity. Divorces compensation from time
5. **When nothing happens, you still get paid** (Insurance): "Predates the tax code. Some of the oldest companies in the world." Reverse lottery model
6. **No matter what, you get paid** (Government/Tax): Monopoly on violence enforces collection
- Through-line: "You will be compensated in proportion to the risk you're willing to take"
- "The people who do this well have it be very risky for other people and not risky at all for them"

### Royalties and Risk Positioning
- Royalties called "royalties" because paid to the royals -- comes off the top
- Rev share always better than profit share: "somebody can play around with their profit. Top line is topline."
- Apple Care: "a gazillion dollar insurance play where they just say screen cracks and get paid the whole time basically for air"
- Franchisors who control money flow: "get paid and then remit payment the remainder"
- Payment processors: "always get paid. They control the money flow."

### Risk Assessment (Bezos/Lynch Applied to Wealth)
- Bezos: "Humans overestimate the downside and underestimate the upside"
- Lynch: compounded 30% for 15+ years
- Bezos: "Given a 10% chance of a 100x payoff, you should take that bet every time"
- "Baseball has truncated outcomes. In business, you can score a thousand runs."
