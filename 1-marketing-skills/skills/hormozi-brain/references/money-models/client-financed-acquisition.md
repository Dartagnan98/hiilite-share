# Client Financed Acquisition (CFA)
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Money Models course + 383 video transcripts)

---

## Definition
Making enough gross profit from a new customer within 30 days that advertising pays for itself.

---

## Three Levels of Advertising

### Level 1: LTGP > CAC
- Lifetime gross profit exceeds cost to acquire customer
- Minimum 3x ratio
- "The dull level" -- bare minimum to have a business

### Level 2: 30-Day GP > CAC
- Make more than acquisition cost in FIRST 30 DAYS
- Result: Never spend your own money to grow
- Why 30 days? Every business can borrow money interest-free for 30 days via credit cards/credit lines
- Use credit card to get customers, pay card back, recycle cash, get more customers
- Limited only by credit card limit and operational capacity

### Level 3: 30-Day GP > 2x CAC
- Make more than TWICE acquisition cost in first 30 days
- Result: Never limited by money at all
- One customer pays back debt AND funds the next customer
- Doubling happens by CUSTOMER, not by month (can happen daily)

---

## Month-by-Month Compounding Example (Level 3)
- Reinvest all profit: 1 -> 2 -> 4 -> 8 -> ... -> 4,000+ customers by month 12

- **Level 1**: LTV > CAC (3x minimum) -- bare minimum
- **Level 2**: 30-day GP > CAC -- use credit card float, never spend own money

- School.com currently using Level 3: doubling rate of acquisition by customer, not by month
- Level 3 math: Month 1=1, Month 2=2, Month 3=4... Month 12=4,096 total customers

### LTV:CAC by Automation Level
- All 3 automated (lead gen, sales, delivery): **3:1 minimum**
- 2 of 3 automated: **6:1**
- 1 of 3 automated: **9:1**
- None automated (all people-driven): **12:1+**
- Back of napkin LTV calc: Total revenue / total customers x gross margin %
- Back of napkin CAC calc: Total marketing + sales spend / new customers acquired
- "Optimize for returns, not for cheapest leads"

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### CFA Levels (Detailed from Book with Lemonade Stand Example)

**Level 1: GP < CAC in 30 days (Floating)**
- You spend more to get a customer than you make back in 30 days
- Must float the difference from savings or credit
- Example: Spend $20 CAC, make $8 GP in 30 days = down $12, floating on your own money
- "This is where most businesses live. They're spending money to get customers and HOPING to make it back over time."

**Level 2: 30D GP = CAC (Credit Limit = Ad Budget)**
- You make back exactly what you spent within 30 days
- Your credit card limit becomes your advertising budget
- Cycle: charge card -> acquire customer -> collect GP -> pay card -> repeat
- "Every business can borrow money interest-free for 30 days via credit cards"

**Level 3: 30D GP > 2x CAC (Doubling by Customer)**
- Each customer pays back the acquisition cost AND funds the next customer
- Doubling happens by CUSTOMER, not by month (can happen daily)
- One customer's profit pays for two more customers
