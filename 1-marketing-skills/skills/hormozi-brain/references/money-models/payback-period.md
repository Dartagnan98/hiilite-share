# Payback Period
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Money Models course + 383 video transcripts)

---

## Definition
How long it takes until gross profit is greater than CAC. How long to break even.
- Also called "cash conversion cycle"

---

## Why It Matters
- Shorter payback = easier to scale advertising
- Break even in a day vs. 5 years = massive difference in scaling speed
- Eliminates the need for a "marketing budget"

---

## Calculation Example
- CAC: $160
- Monthly revenue: $100
- COGS: $20/mo
- Month 1 GP: $80 (still down $80)
- Month 2 GP: $80 (now at $0)
- **Payback period: 2 months**
- IMPORTANT: Calculate off GROSS PROFIT, not revenue

---

## The Hyperactive Buying Cycle
- When someone makes a new purchase decision, they enter a hyper buying mode
- Example: Friend buys a bike, then immediately buys helmet, shoes, pants, gloves, water bottles, signs up for a race -- all within a day or two
- If you don't offer those products, someone else will
- Making offers available is NOT being pushy

---

## Five Upsell Timing Windows

### 1. Immediately After First Sale
- Works when solving one problem naturally creates the next

### 2. Next Step (24-72 Hours)
- Schedule a follow-up that creates awareness of a new problem

### 3. After a Big Win (Milestone)
- Fully solved problem creates new problems
- Example: made $10K/mo? Now you have tax problems

### 4. Halfway Point
- Historically very high conversion window

### 5. Last Chance (Exit Interview)
- If you haven't upsold by now, this is your last shot

**Key insight:** 4 out of 5 windows are BEFORE the halfway point. Most people try to upsell at the end -- that's too late.

---

## The Supplement Sale Timing Lesson
- Selling supplements right after the gym signup FAILED (customer already scratched their weight loss itch)
- Selling supplements at a nutrition orientation 48 hours later WORKED (created a NEW problem: "you're not eating these things")
- "You sell at the point of greatest deprivation, not at the point of greatest satisfaction"

---

## Quick Payback Improvements
- **First and last month today** -- collect 2 months upfront
- **Initiation/onboarding/enrollment/activation fee** -- made-up fee, all gross profit
- Example: $160 CAC, add first-and-last = $160 collected upfront = 100% offset before 30-day recurring even hits

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### The Lemonade Stand Comparison (Normal vs Wonderful Business)

**Normal Lemonade Business:**
- CAC: $20 (cost to get a customer)
- Revenue: $10/month
- COGS: $2/month
- Gross Profit: $8/month
- **Payback Period: 2.5 months** ($20 / $8 per month)
- Must float $20 per customer for 2.5 months before breaking even

**Wonderful Lemonade Business:**
- CAC: $1 (cost to get a customer)
- Revenue: $10/month
- COGS: $2/month
- Gross Profit: $8/month
- **Payback Period: ~7 days** ($1 / $8 per month)
- Same product, same margins -- only difference is acquisition cost

**Key insight:** The wonderful business compounds 4x faster than the normal business. Same product, same price, same margins -- the ONLY difference is how quickly they get their money back. "Speed of compounding is the real advantage."

### 30-Day Cash (30D Cash)

**Definition:** The gross profit extracted from a new customer in their first 30 days.

**Why 30 days specifically:**
- Every business can borrow money interest-free for 30 days via credit cards and credit lines
- Use credit card to acquire customers, collect enough GP in 30 days to pay the card back, recycle cash, acquire more

**5-Step Walkthrough:**
1. Spend money on credit card to acquire customer (Day 0)
2. Customer pays you (Day 0-30)
3. Deliver product/service, subtract delivery cost (Day 0-30)
4. Remaining = 30-Day Gross Profit (Day 30)
5. Compare 30D GP to original credit card charge -- if GP > charge, you never needed your own money

**30D Cash is the single most important metric for scaling speed.** It determines which CFA level you're operating at.
