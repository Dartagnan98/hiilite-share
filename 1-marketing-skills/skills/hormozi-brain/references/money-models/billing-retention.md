## BILLING HACKS

### Bill Every 4 Weeks (Not Monthly)
- 4.3 weeks per month = 13 billing cycles per year (not 12)
- **8.3% more revenue** just by changing billing language
- Present as weekly ($50/week), bill every 4 weeks ($200)
- If you run 20% profit, this is a ~41% increase in profit
- No difference in take rates between "every 4 weeks" vs "monthly"

### 3% Processing Fee Hack
- Add "X + 3% processing fee" to every transaction
- Nobody declines over 3% processing
- Goes straight to bottom line
- If you run 10% profit, 3% added = 30% more profit

### Get Two Forms of Payment
- Offer to WAIVE the 3% processing fee if they provide a second form of payment
- Positions as benefit: "We pass those savings on to you"
- Reduces involuntary churn (expired cards, insufficient funds)
- 20% churn reduction from involuntary churn alone more than makes up the 3%

### Get ACH as Second Payment
- ACH = Automated Clearing House (bank-to-bank)
- Cheapest way to transact besides cash
- People rarely change bank accounts vs. credit cards
- Almost never declines
- Saves 1-2% on processing costs

## QUICK PAYBACK IMPROVEMENTS

### First and Last Month Today
- Collect 2 months upfront instead of 1
- Example: $160 CAC, add first-and-last = $160 collected upfront = 100% offset before 30-day recurring even hits

### Initiation/Onboarding/Enrollment/Activation Fee
- Made-up fee that is all gross profit
- Call it whatever sounds most natural for your business
- Pure margin, offsets CAC immediately

## CANCELLATION POLICIES

### Four Approaches
1. **Allow cancel during trial** -- increases sales, decreases stick
2. **Zero cancellations** -- decreases sales, increases stick (only works with premium customers)
3. **Prorated fee** -- bill them the difference of savings accrued
4. **One-time breakup fee** -- easiest to explain, increases both sales AND stick

### Best Practice: Make Cancellation Fee = Discount Given
- If they got $600 in discount, cancellation fee is $600
- Simple, everyone understands it
- Legally clean, ethically defensible

### Fee or Feedback
- Script: "I'll waive your cancellation fee if you sit down and tell me what we could do better"
- Let them vent. Be MORE upset than them about the problem.
- Try to solve it. If you can, offer a rollover upsell.
- Exit interview process: let them vent -> validate -> be more upset -> ask to make it up -> offer rollover/do-over

### Make It Easy to Cancel
- If customers have nowhere to complain inside your business, they WILL complain outside (one-star reviews)
- Clear cancellation process = fewer reviews, more save opportunities
- "Small businesses do not get rich by making stuff hard for their customers"

## INVOLUNTARY CHURN REDUCTION

### The Two-Payment Method
- Get card + ACH (or two cards) on file at signup
- When primary payment fails, auto-charge backup
- 20% of all churn is involuntary (expired cards, insufficient funds, bank holds)
- Fixing involuntary churn alone can reduce total churn by 20%
- This is the easiest churn fix because it requires zero product improvement

### ACH Benefits
- Bank accounts almost never change (vs. credit cards that expire every 3-4 years)
- Lower processing fees (saves 1-2%)
- Near-zero decline rates

## RETENTION PRICING TACTICS

### Lifetime Discount at Churn Point
- Amazon model: 5% off with continuity, 15% off if you stay past 5 months
- Place the incentive right AFTER your average churn month
- Two benefits: (1) people pay more upfront knowing it'll go down, (2) people stay longer to earn the discount
- Reminder cadence as they approach: "2 months left! 1 month left!"

### Big Head Long Tail
- Big upfront payment + small ongoing payments
- $5,000 down, $200/mo or $1,000 down then $19.99/mo
- Sunk cost fallacy prevents cancellation
- Very low cancellation rates
- People called before card expired to save their $19.99/mo rate

### Waived Fee for Commitment
- Charge a setup/initiation fee that's 3-5x monthly rate
- Waive it if they commit to continuity
- Creates urgency and perceived savings
- If they leave early, fee applies (= built-in cancellation penalty)

## CONTINUITY & RETENTION (Deep Pass)

### Four Ways to Apply "3 Months Free" Discount
1. **Upfront**: Free time first, paid term starts after. Pro tip: 3 free + 12 paid = 15 months total (NOT 3 free + 9 paid = 12)
2. **At the End**: Pay full year first, earn 3 free months. Encourages adherence
3. **Spread Over Term**: Divide discount across all payments (25% off/month)
4. **First + Last + Free**: Pay first and last month, free period between. Covers acquisition cost

### Gift Card Discount Hack
- Give discount as physical gift card redeemable after 3 payments
- Three outcomes: forget (full price), gift to friend (free referral), use it (still got 3 paid months first). All wins.

### Lifetime Discount at Churn Point
- Average drop-off at month 4? Offer lifetime discount at month 5
- Remind at months 2 and 3. Gets people to buy at higher rates AND stay longer

### Cancellation Framework
- Preferred: cancellation fee = discount they received
- "Fee or Feedback" -- waive fee for exit interview
- In exit interview: be MORE upset than them about their issue -> they calm you down -> you've won
- Exit interviews: over half get resold into a higher-level package
- "Small businesses do not get rich by making stuff hard for customers"

### Five Retention Strategies (Top 2% Gyms)
1. **Consumption accountability:** If they miss by Wednesday, escalate -- repeatedly contact to get them scheduled
2. **Exit interviews:** In-person conversation required to cancel. Collect data + resell
3. **Member events:** Quarterly community events minimum
4. **Personalized handwritten cards:** Must reference specific progress ("You went from black band to yellow band")
5. **Personal check-ins:** Text every 14-21 days, independent of service. Just "How's life?" NOT automated

### Automating What You Should Systematize Destroys Goodwill
- "As soon as someone finds out that communications are automated, they stop paying attention"
- Automated outreach WITHDRAWS goodwill rather than depositing it
- "Better to say nothing at all than to automate"

### Proactive Price DECREASE on Existing Customers Increases LTV
- Portfolio data: second highest LTV segment was at a FAR LOWER price
- Ultra-low churn because they only paid for what they used
- Proactively reaching out: "You're only using 4 of 6 features. Want to drop to a lower rate?"
- Those people NEVER leave

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### Discount + One-Time Fee (Owen Parking Lot Story)
- Owen told Hormozi in a parking lot: "Charge a one-time fee alongside your discount"
- Changed everything about how Gym Launch structured front-end offers

**Four objectives of the one-time fee:**
1. **Liquidate acquisition cost**: The fee covers what you spent to get them in the door
2. **Pay salesman commission**: Commission comes out of the fee, not monthly revenue
3. **Increase buy-in of customer**: Higher upfront investment = higher commitment = lower churn
4. **Cover higher onboarding cost**: First month always costs more to deliver (setup, orientation, etc.)

**Examples:**
- 95% off first month + $1,900 setup fee
- 88% off first month + $1,000 setup fee
- $5K setup fee + $267/month ongoing
- The discount attracts attention, the fee captures cash

**4-Step Creation Process:**
1. **Pick fee name**: Setup fee, initiation fee, onboarding fee, activation fee, enrollment fee -- whatever sounds natural for your industry
2. **Pick fee price**: Should be 3-5x your monthly rate minimum. Higher = more liquidation.
3. **Pick reason why**: "Covers your personalized onboarding," "Includes your starter kit," "Funds your dedicated account setup"
4. **Start charging/discounting/waiving**: Use the fee as a lever -- charge it standard, discount it for urgency, waive it for commitment

### Initiation Fee Expanded Mechanics
- The initiation fee is pure gross profit (costs nothing incremental to collect)
- Even if waived as a promotion, its existence creates perceived value
- Can be paired with any discount strategy:
  - "First month 50% off + waived initiation fee" (double incentive)
  - "No initiation fee if you commit to 6 months" (trades fee for commitment)
  - "Initiation fee reduced from $500 to $200 this week" (partial waiver = still captures cash)
- Track: what % of customers would have bought WITHOUT the fee waiver? That's your real cost.
