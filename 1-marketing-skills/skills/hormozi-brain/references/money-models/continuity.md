# Continuity & Recurring Revenue
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Money Models course + 383 video transcripts)

## WHY CONTINUITY MATTERS

### The Math
- $1,000 one-time to 100 people = $10,000 from 10 buyers
- $50/mo to 100 people = $40,000 from 40 buyers who stay 20 months
- 4x the customers to upsell later
- Pros: Attract more customers at lower price
- Cons: Less money NOW (which is why continuity goes LAST in your money model)

### The Core Principle
"The way to create a fortune in business is to only sell things that people do not stop buying."

### Customer Surplus
- Customer surplus = value received far exceeds price paid
- When surplus is high enough, people call YOU when their card expires to make sure billing continues
- Examples: Wi-Fi, phone service, Netflix -- you immediately update your card

### The Starbucks/Coca-Cola Test
If you've been buying Starbucks for years and won't stop, that's the model you want.

### The Thought Experiment
- Write down total number of people you've ever sold
- Multiply by your current monthly price
- That's what your revenue WOULD be if nobody ever left
- How much could you spend on acquisition with that revenue?

## RECURRING VS. REOCCURRING REVENUE

- **Recurring** = Netflix (fixed subscription, automatic billing)
- **Reoccurring** = Coca-Cola (repeated purchases, no subscription)
- Both are great. Key: demonstrate that average buyer purchases X more times.

### Enterprise Value Impact
- Recurring company at $10M/$3M EBITDA: **8-15x multiple** (worth $24M-$45M)
- Transactional company at $10M/$3M EBITDA: **4x multiple** (worth $12M)
- Subscription just existing triples LTV -- 3x even with no added value

## THE "DOWNSELL THE UPSELL" TECHNIQUE

- Sell the expensive thing first, then offer MORE VALUE for LESS per month
- Gym Launch: $16K for 16 weeks ($1K/week) then offer 3-year deal at $3,200/mo (half the weekly rate)
- Script: "Have you enjoyed everything so far? Mind if I tell you a way to save a little and get a lot more?"
- People think about monthly cash flow more than duration

---

## BIG HEAD LONG TAIL

"You can shear a sheep for a lifetime, but you can only skin it once."

- Big upfront payment + small ongoing payments
- $5,000 down, $200/mo
- Sunk cost fallacy prevents cancellation (people don't want to "lose" their $5K)
- Very low cancellation rates
- Helps with CFA (big upfront offsets acquisition cost)
- Example: $1,000 down then $19.99/mo -- people called before card expired to save that rate
- "The bigger the head, the longer the tail"

---

## BACK-OF-NAPKIN LTV CALCULATION

- **Lifetime Revenue** = Price / Monthly Churn Rate
- **Lifetime Gross Profit** = Gross Margin x Lifetime Revenue
- Example: $100/mo, 10% churn = $1,000 LTV. At 80% margin = $800 LTGP
- Example: $100/mo, 3% churn = $3,333 LTV. At 80% margin = $2,667 LTGP

## CONTINUITY SUB-TYPES

### Continuity Bonus Offers ("Stay and you get MORE")
- Pricing data: at **1.33x** standalone price = 50/50 split between continuity and standalone
- At **2x** standalone price = 70% choose continuity
- At **3x** standalone price = 90% choose continuity
- Use this data to set your continuity pricing

### Continuity Discounts (4 Ways to Apply)

**Way 1: Upfront discount, push out term**
- "3 months free, then 12 months paid" (15-month total)
- Works best with enforceable contracts (cell phones, storage, leases)
- PRO TIP: Don't eat into the term. 3 free + 12 paid = 15 months. NOT 3 free + 9 paid = 12 months.

**Way 2: Discount at the end**
- "3 months free AFTER you complete your 12-month commitment"
- Encourages payment adherence
- They "earn" their free time

**Way 3: Spread over time**
- 3 months free on a 12-month at $200/mo = $600 discount spread = $150/mo for 12 months
- After term ends, reverts to $200/mo full price

**Way 4: First payments + free period + contract**
- Get 1-2 payments upfront, then give free period, then contract kicks in
- "First and last month" or "setup fee" + free months
- Liquidates acquisition cost while still advertising "3 months free"

### Lifetime Discount at Churn Point
- Amazon does this: 5% off with continuity, 15% off if you stay past 5 months
- Place the incentive right AFTER your average churn month
- Two benefits: (1) people pay more upfront knowing it'll go down, (2) people stay longer to earn the discount
- Remind them as they approach the milestone: "2 months left! 1 month left!"

### Waived Fee Offer
- Charge a setup/initiation fee that's **3-5x monthly rate**
- Then waive it if they commit to continuity
- Creates urgency and perceived savings

## EDUCATION/INFO PRODUCTS CONTINUITY FIX

- Problem: once you learn arithmetic, the course is worth nothing (methods = one-and-done)
- Solution: separate one-time value (install the sales system) from consumable value (monthly coaching calls, new ad templates, new plays)
- Price continuity based on consumable value alone, not blended
- Or sell annual memberships (buy time to create next offer)

### Models vs. Methods
- Methods = one-and-done (buy the course, learned it, done)
- Models = evolving frameworks that always need updating (franchises, systems)
- Sell models, not methods -- models have inherent recurring value

## THE THREE-STEP FORTUNE PROCESS (from Mark Roberge / HubSpot)

1. **Promotion** (0 to $1M) -- just get customers. Don't optimize pricing. Get feedback.
2. **Product** ($1M to $10M) -- iterate until people don't leave. Survey, interview, improve.
3. **Scale** ($10M+) -- once people don't leave, pour gas on marketing. Everything compounds.

### The Promotion-to-Product Sequence
- Most internet marketers stop at promotion. That's why they can't build fortunes.
- "You can become a millionaire learning to market and sell. You can't build a fortune."
- The BORING work of product improvement is where fortunes are built.

## 8 C's OF RECURRING REVENUE (Stickiness Framework)

Think of each as a CONTINUUM (not a checkbox). To what extent do you do each?

### 1. Consumption
- How well do people consume/use your product?
- Better onboarding, more addictive product, easier to use
- Netflix, Spotify = pure consumption-based retention
- Getting people to consume the product = #1 factor in retention

### 2. Collateral
- Do you hold something the customer needs?
- Physical: storage has your stuff
- Digital: Dropbox has your files, CRM has your contacts
- "Holding hostage" = sticky

### 3. Cost of Switching
- How arduous/expensive is it to switch providers?
- Time cost, money cost, sunk cost fallacy
- Bigger upfront payment = harder to leave
- "The bigger the head, the longer the tail"

### 4. Choice (Lack of Alternatives)
- Are you the only option?
- Patents, trade secrets, unique competitive process
- Niching down = fewer alternatives for your specific customer
- Warren Buffett's "competitive moat"

### 5. Control of Money Flow
- Who gets paid first?
- Uber takes fee before paying driver
- Government takes taxes before paycheck
- Less overt charges = stickier (employees gripe less about taxes than business owners because they never see the gross)

### 6. Cause
- Are people aligned with your mission?
- Churches = 10% tithe based on identity/cause
- Political donations, brand loyalty (buy shoes for the cause, not just the shoes)
- Tom's Shoes model

### 7. Community
- Would leaving mean losing their identity/tribe?
- People don't leave communities -- they leave businesses
- Strengthen community ties = stickier than any contract

### 8. Contracts (Enforceable)
- 6-month contract at $2K/mo vs. month-to-month
- Even if month-to-month averages 4 months, 6-month contract gets 5-6 months
- Sell to better prospects for more enforceable contracts
- Positioning as "6 months then month-to-month" increases stick without scaring buyers

## PI EQUATION EXPANDED (Deep Pass -- Batches 3-4)

### Three Applications
1. **Max Customers**: Inflow / Outflow = Max. 5/month at 10% churn = 50 cap
2. **LTV**: Revenue per client / churn rate. $1K/month at 20% churn = $5K LTV
3. **Revenue Cap**: New biz $/month / revenue churn. $10K at 10% = $100K/month
4. **Golden Ratio**: Referral % > Churn % = infinite growth (net negative churn)

### Three Variables
- Price, churn, inflow -- "First three questions I ask any business owner"
- Growth curve: fast at start, slows as churn eats bigger percentage, caps at ~80-90% of theoretical max

### Virtuous Cycle of High Prices
- Higher price -> higher margins -> better talent -> better service -> better reputation -> more demand -> higher price
- "Pricing is two-way communication"

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### Lifetime Discounts -- "The Opener" Story
- Billion-dollar gym franchise opens every single location with 400+ recurring members on day one
- Method: **Founding member lifetime discount**
- Before the gym even opens, sell founding memberships at a locked-in rate
- "Pay $29/month forever" (regular rate will be $49/month)
- People sign up before the doors open because the deal will never be available again
- Creates immediate recurring revenue base before a single class is taught

### Three Ways to Display Lifetime Discounts
1. **Percentage off retail**: "Save 40% for life" -- $49 becomes $29.40
2. **Dollar amount off retail**: "Save $20/month forever" -- $49 becomes $29
3. **Fixed price (Price Protection)**: "Lock in $29/month -- price will never increase for you"
- Price protection is the most powerful framing: people fear price increases more than they value discounts
- "Locking in today's price feels like insurance against future pain"

### Price Protection for Fixed Periods
- Variant: "Your price is locked for 24 months, then reverts to current rate"
- Creates urgency to sign up NOW (current rate is lowest it'll ever be)
- After lock period, natural price increase feels expected (not punitive)

### Sunk Cost Mechanics
- Large upfront payment + locked rate = two retention forces working together
- The upfront payment creates sunk cost ("I already put $500 in, I can't leave now")
- The locked rate creates fear of loss ("If I leave, I lose my $29/month rate forever")
- "The bigger the head, the longer the tail" applies to discounts too

### Initiation Fee Waiver Trick
- Charge a real initiation/setup fee ($300-$500)
- Standard offer: full fee + monthly rate
- Promotional offer: "We're waiving the initiation fee this week only"
- Creates urgency (save $300-$500 by acting now)
- Even if you always waive it, the EXISTENCE of the fee makes the waiver feel valuable
- Pro tip: occasionally DON'T waive it, so the waiver promotion remains credible

### Lifetime Upgrades for Retention
- Use upgrades as retention bonuses: "Stay 12 months and your account permanently upgrades to Gold"
- Gold gets: priority scheduling, exclusive content, VIP community access
- The upgrade is permanent -- even if they pause and come back, they keep Gold status
- Creates loyalty loop: the longer they stay, the more they'd lose by leaving
