## THE FOUR OFFER TYPES

### 1. Attraction Offers -- Liquidate CAC
Get people in the door. Create demand. Purpose: minimize cost to acquire.

### 2. Upsell Offers -- Maximize Profit
Get people to spend more money. Purpose: maximize gross profit per customer.

### 3. Downsell Offers -- Maximize Conversion
Get people who said "no" to say "yes" to something. Purpose: capture revenue from objectors.

### 4. Continuity Offers -- Stabilize Cash Flow
Get people to keep paying. Purpose: recurring revenue, predictable income.

### How They Work Together
Strangers -> [Attraction] -> Customers -> [Upsell] -> More spend
If "no" -> [Downsell] -> Still convert -> [Continuity] -> Recurring payments

### Key Principles
- Not every business needs all four. Some need only one or two.
- Start with ONE. Add layers like paint.
- "Simple scales, fancy fails"
- As few offers as possible to maximize GP in shortest time
- You CAN use attraction offers as upsells, continuity offers as front-ends, etc. No rules.
- Affiliate products can fill money model gaps (zero operational cost)
- Any attraction offer becomes a continuity offer by adding a clause that rolls into continuity
- A business model is NOT patentable. It's a speed game -- innovate, extract, repeat.

## 6 ATTRACTION OFFER TYPES

### 8a. Win Your Money Back
- Customer pays full price
- Gets the chance to "win" their money back (via referrals, completion, results)
- Creates engagement and gamification
- You keep full price, they feel like they earned something

### 8b. Free Giveaways
- Give something valuable away for free
- Purpose: generate maximum leads, lower CAC
- Use friction to filter quality after generating flow
- "If I had only one offer to make to convert or my family would be killed, it would be a free offer."

### 8c. Decoy Offers
- Offer something that makes the real offer look like a better deal
- Strategy call that's "free" = decoy for the $16K package
- The decoy exists solely to make the real offer more attractive by comparison

### 8d. Buy X Get Y Free
- Bulk purchase incentive
- "Buy 6 months, get 6 months free" = doubles LTV, gets cash upfront
- Only do if average stick < total term offered (otherwise you lose money)

### 8e. Pay Less Now, Pay More Later
- Entry price lower than eventual price
- Lock in continuity at introductory rate
- Combined with lifetime lower rate = powerful continuity play
- Newsletter example: Free trial, start $3.99/mo, skip trial for 2 years of past content + discount

### 8f. Free with Consumption
- Give product/service free IF they consume/use it
- Creates engagement loop
- Ties retention to usage, which is the #1 factor in reducing churn

## THE RENTAL CAR STORY (Money Model in Action)
Started at $19/day reservation. Left paying $400 for 3 days.

Sequence of offers:
1. **Car upgrade** -- "We don't have your reserved car. Is a Tundra okay?" ($19/day -> $69/day)
2. **Late checkout** -- half-day fee to avoid late return penalty (fee they invented)
3. **Insurance upgrade** -- "Do you want to upgrade your insurance?" (implied you already have minimum)
4. **Minimum insurance** -- "So just the minimum then?" (implied you must get it because it's "minimum")
5. **Prepaid gas** -- $3.75/gal vs. market $3.50 (buy the FULL tank upfront; whatever you don't use = pure profit)
6. **Damages** -- implicit upsell (scratch it, you pay)
7. **Late return fee** -- yet another invented penalty

Key lessons:
- "Minimum package" naming = masterful. 90% of people take it because it sounds required
- Rental car companies invented the fees, then sell avoidance of those fees
- Making 5+ offers in one conversation is normal. 19/20 customers don't mind.

## HOW TO BUILD YOUR OWN MONEY MODEL (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Start with an attraction offer
- Get leads to engage with you

### Step 2: Pick an upsell offer
- Menu upsell, anchor upsell, rollover upsell, or classic

### Step 3: Pick a downsell offer
- For people who say no: payment plan -> feature downsell -> free trial

### Step 4: Pick a continuity offer
- Get cash back in 30 days, more customers, more money

### Rules
1. Perfect ONE at a time. Adding all four at once will break your business.
2. Start with a lower price, get yeses, raise prices in stages.
3. Simple scales, fancy fails. Fewest offers possible.
4. Affiliate products can fill gaps without operational cost.
5. Any attraction offer becomes continuity by adding a rollover clause.

## ADDITIONAL OFFER STRUCTURES (Deep Pass)

### Buy X Get Y Playbook
- Reframe discounts as free offers. "Buy one get two free" > "33% off"
- Three versions of 18-month service:
  - Buy 12 months, get 6 free ($1,800) -- good
  - Buy 9 months, get 9 free ($1,800) -- better
  - Buy 6 months, get 12 free ($1,800) -- crazy compelling
- Give MORE free than paid. Buy 1 get 2 > Buy 2 get 1
- Cap prepays at 10% of recurring customer base
- People who prepay are most likely to buy MORE later -- "Their wallets replenish"

### Rollover Upsell (Most-Used Upsell)
- Credit previous purchase toward next offer
- Four who's: (1) Old customers (win-back): "I owe you money" subject line, (2) Upset customers: redo > refund, (3) Competitor's upset customers: scrape 1-star reviews, (4) Current customers: roll initial purchase into longer commitment
- Price next offer 4-5x higher than rollover credit (25% discount max)
- Made personalized videos for 200 past customers offering $4,000 credit to return

### Gift Card Play
- Sell $200 gift cards for $20 (90% off), limit 2 per person, can only use on someone else
- Customer pays $20 = you get paid for a hot referral lead
- Roll $200 into $1,000-2,000 product. Works for Christmas, Black Friday, birthday gifts

### Decoy Offer
- Strategy call / free with consumption as front end
- Gets prospects into ecosystem with no risk

### Win Your Money Back (Full Model)
- Learned from Danny in gym mastermind
- Client bet $X they'd lose 20 lbs in 8 weeks
- Three versions: results-based, action-based, or both
- 78% success rate of people hitting their goal
- Real money from upselling winners at week 3 into annual memberships
- Apply $600 credit over 12 months ($50/mo discount) rather than giving 3 months free
- Make everyone a winner at halfway point
- Over 4,000+ sales: only 2 unconditional refund requests
- Upsell should be 5x more expensive so credit = max 20% discount

### Seven Free Offer Types
1. **Free Bribe**: Free high-value thing with purchase
2. **Limited Free**: AB close -- free basic vs paid intensive. 3/4 upsell to paid
3. **Free Trial + Penalty**: Card on file, charge for missed sessions ($50 no-show)
4. **Free with Deposit**: 6-week challenge. Money back if goal hit or credit toward program
5. **Free Forever**: Free service, monetize supplements/products. 95% revenue from non-service stream
6. **Free Giveaway**: "Meals for month" -- all entrants are qualified leads
7. **Free with Commitment**: First month free + 9-month contract. Higher average values
- "If you can't explain the offer in one or two sentences, it is not clear enough"

### Downselling 6-Step Sequence
1. Primary offer at full price
2. First step-down: half price, longer term + "Fair enough?"
3. Second: same price, remove commitment
4. Third: small discount + leave testimonial
5. Fourth: bigger discount + bring a friend
6. Temperature check: if they say no to 6 step-downs, "It's not a money thing. You lost them earlier in the sale."
- Exchange principle: each step-down asks them to do MORE
- Never remove bonuses -- add requirements instead

### Feature Downsell System
- Take something away, lower the price, ask "how about now?"
- Remove something VALUABLE first with tiny price decrease -> forces re-upsell to original
- Then: put guarantee back, remove something they DON'T care about, BIG decrease -> better deal
- Name combinations: First Class, Business Class, Economy
- Name cheapest: "The Minimum" (implies they have to get at least that)
- Temperature check after 2 rejections: "1-10, how bad do you want this?"

### Payment Plan Downsell (7-Step Process)
1. Anchor higher (baked-in payment plan price), then offer prepayment discount
2. Third-party financing (increases revenue 35% on average), credit card, layaway
3. Half now, half later (aligned to paychecks)
4. Temperature check: 1-10 how bad you want this?
5. Split into 3 payments
6. Evenly spread over service duration
7. Free trial

### Continuity Offer Assembly
- "Downsell the Upsell": $16K for 16 weeks -> back end at $200/month (half the weekly rate)
- "Give 5x the value for less" -> extends term, higher total LTV
- Big Head, Long Tail: $5,000 down + $200/month
- High upfront payment -> sunk cost fallacy reduces cancellation

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### The Value Grid Framework
- Visual tool showing ALL offers mapped across the customer journey
- Rows = offer types (attraction, upsell, downsell, continuity)
- Columns = stages of customer lifecycle
- **Simple grid** (one offer per type) = **$75 30D Cash per prospect**
- **Stacked grid** (multiple offers per type) = **$1,763 30D Cash per prospect** -- same business type
- **23x difference** from stacking offers vs having one of each
- "The guys I was competing against sold the same product as me. I won because I had a better money model."

### Advanced Offer Stacking Process (4-Step Loop)
1. **Attract** with free/discount offer -> get them in the door
2. **Liquidate** with upfront cash collection -> offset acquisition cost immediately
3. **Maximize** with upsells/downsells -> extract maximum GP from each customer
4. **Stabilize** with continuity -> lock in recurring revenue
- **Repeatable**: After step 4, loop back to step 2 with the next adjacent need
- Can run this loop multiple times per customer across different "wallets"

### Adjacent Needs Stacking
- Once someone buys, ask: what adjacent problems do they now have?
- Gym member -> needs supplements (different wallet), meal prep (different wallet), apparel (different wallet)
- Each adjacent need is a separate buying decision with separate budget
- "People have different wallets" -- hitting multiple wallets from one customer

### Affiliate Stacking ($3M+ Example)
- If you can't fulfill an adjacent need yourself, find an affiliate partner who can
- Gym Launch made $3M+ in affiliate revenue from supplement/nutrition partners
- Zero operational cost -- partners fulfill, you earn commission
- Affiliate products fill money model gaps without adding delivery complexity

### Weight Loss 4-Sale Flow (Full Example)
- **Sale #1 (Attraction)**: Free 6-week challenge -- gets 100 leads at low cost
- **Sale #2 (Liquidation)**: $500 upfront at signup -- 15 of 100 buy = $7,500 Day 1
- **Sale #3 (Maximize)**: Supplement upsell $200 avg -- 12 of 15 buy = $2,400 Day 1-2
- **Sale #4 (Stabilize)**: Credit $500 toward annual at $200/mo -- 70% take rate = 10 customers x 5 months x $200 = $10,000
- **Prepay bonus at week 6**: 20% of remaining buy annual prepay = ~$5,000
- **Total from 100 leads: $24,900** -- almost all collected in first 30 days

### Freemium (Full Chapter)

**Definition:** Freemium is an ACQUISITION STRATEGY, not a business model.

**Three requirements for freemium to work:**
1. Must come from word-of-mouth or organic (can't pay to acquire free users profitably)
2. Must cost $0 (or near-zero) to serve free users
3. Must provide continuous value to keep free users engaged until they convert

**Examples that work:** Dropbox, Spotify, Wistia, Gmail -- all software with near-zero marginal cost per user

**Three roadblocks:**
1. **Conversion problem**: Not enough free users upgrade to paid
2. **Value problem**: Free tier is too good (no reason to upgrade) or too bad (users leave)
3. **Cost problem**: Serving free users costs too much

**Freemium CAC math:**
- $.05/month to service each free user
- 1% of free users upgrade to paid per month
- Effective CAC per paid user = $5/month ($0.05 / 1% conversion)
- Compare $5 CAC to LTGP of paid plan -- if ratio is 3:1+, freemium works

### Free Pick Your Price (Full Chapter)

**Two versions:**

**Version 1 (Value Ladder):**
- Add more value/features as price increases
- Include guarantee at each level
- Let customer self-select based on perceived value
- Example tiers: $0 (basic), $50 (standard + guarantee), $200 (premium + VIP + guarantee)

**Version 2 (Supporter Labels):**
- Same product at every level, different "supporter" labels
- Example: $0 (freeloader), $10 (supporter), $50 (champion), $100 (hero)
- Appeals to identity and social status

**COVID Car Wash Story:**
- During COVID, car wash let customers pay whatever they wanted
- Average ticket: **$99** (normal price was much lower)
- Generated massive goodwill and customer loyalty
- "People paid MORE when given the choice, not less"

**5 Industry Examples:**
1. Lemonade stand: pick your price with suggested amounts
2. Car wash: pay what you think it's worth
3. Weight loss: free challenge, pay what you got in value at the end
4. Dentist: cleaning for whatever you can afford (new patient acquisition)
5. Coaching: first session free, you decide the value after

### Free With Alternate Revenue Stream (Full Chapter)

**Two types:**
1. **Paired upsell**: Product A is free IF they buy Product B
   - Example: Free gym membership if you buy the supplement package
2. **Independent upsell**: Product A is free, then encourage (not require) Product B
   - Example: Free gym membership, supplements available but optional

**"No Sale Sale" Gym Story:**
- People who said NO to the gym membership were offered free nutrition orientation
- At orientation, they bought supplements
- Those "no" customers spent **50% more on supplements** than members
- "I hadn't even started servicing them yet and was already cash flow positive"

**Free Book Upsell Chain:**
1. Free book (pay shipping ~$7)
2. Upsell: audiobook version ($37)
3. Upsell: templates/worksheets ($97)
4. Upsell: video training ($297)
5. Upsell: financing for premium program ($2,000+)
- Each step captures more of the hyperactive buyer

**$300 Upsell Math:**
- Give away Product A for free
- Upsell Product B at $300
- 80% of free users take the $300 upsell
- 80% gross margin on Product B
- Net GP per free giveaway: $300 x 80% take x 80% margin = **$192 per free thing given away**
- "You MAKE money giving things away for free"
