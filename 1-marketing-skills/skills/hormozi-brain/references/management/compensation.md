# Compensation & Equity Frameworks
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## Phantom Equity

- Equity that doesn't vest unless triggered (usually by a sale/liquidity event)
- Employee doesn't have to pay tax until vesting (unlike real equity transfer)
- If employee leaves, they lose phantom equity (protects owner)
- Employee isn't personally liable (protects employee)

### Typical Structures

- Senior leadership: 0.5% to 2% phantom equity
- True executive leading half the company: 3-5%
- Vesting schedule: e.g., 5% over 5 years (1%/year)
- Cliff: first year vests entirely at 12 months, then quarterly after

### Phantom Equity vs Profit Sharing

- These are separate and independent
- Can be equity owner without profit distributions
- Can participate in profit distributions without equity
- Hormozi prefers to keep profit sharing separate

### When to Use

- "Winners win and they want to participate in the upside"
- "The more money I make other people, the more money I make"
- Sam Walton: wished he'd done employee stock options sooner
- Align employee life outcomes with owner life outcomes

## When NOT to Give Equity

- "Just because you have a need doesn't mean they have to be equity owner"
- Accountant analogy: "I just pay the accountant. He's not a partner."
- Only two reasons someone wants equity: control or exit participation
- If you're not selling and they don't want control, just do performance-based pay

## Value-Based Compensation Philosophy

- "The goal of the company is to pay as little as possible for the highest amount of value"
- Value = what you get, Price = what you pay
- Bigger discrepancy = better deal (same as investing)
- Only raise compensation if: can't attract talent OR can't retain talent
- If neither is a problem, current comp is fine

## Star Employee Economics

- Superstar salesperson outselling 3x normal reps: worth paying 10-20% more
- Losing them = losing half your sales = far worse than the raise
- "Make the economic argument"
- Conversely: amazing customer service person leaves tomorrow -- how much less revenue? "Probably not a lot."

## Specific Benchmarks

- Phantom equity for senior leadership: 0.5-2%
- True executives: 3-5%
- Standard vesting: 5 years with 1-year cliff
- Pay 25% above market for A players, get 5x output
- Star salesperson worth 3-5 regular salespeople

## SUPERSTAR CEO STRUCTURE (Deep Pass)

### Billionaire's Model (Private Call)
- Man doing $1B+/year, 50 privately held companies, mid-70s
- CEO profit sharing: 5% of profits + opportunity to buy increasing equity chunks
- One CEO now owns ~30% of largest company, started at 5-10%
- "I don't believe businesses should make a profit. Profits are unnatural."
- Broke company into 100 pieces, gave equity to superstars, checked out. 5 years later: $1 billion
- "Not being greedy" grows the pie faster than keeping a bigger slice

### A-Player Compensation Rule
- 25% above market pay, 5x the output
- "One A-player is worth five B-players"
- Look for endurance/grit first, teach the rest
- 3 weeks below expectations = cut

## EMPLOYEE VS BUSINESS OWNER MINDSET (Deep Pass -- Batch 4)

- Same $2M income, same business:
  - Owner who does everything = net worth is cash (high-paying job)
  - Owner with team = cash + $10M+ equity (sellable asset)
- Wrong: "If you want it done right, do it yourself"
- Right: "If you want it done right, get someone to spend all their time doing it"
- "100% of humans are replaced every 100 years"
