# Talent Assessment & A Players
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## The Talent Grid (What-How-Who for People)

### Three Levels of Employee Development

| Level | Focus | Title | Activity | Business Stage |
|-------|-------|-------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | WHAT to do | Individual Contributor | Doing | $0-$1M |
| 2 | HOW to do it | Manager | Teaching | $1M-$3M |
| 3 | WHO can do it | Leader | Systematizing | $3M-$10M+ |

### Level 1: Individual Contributor

- Must learn the skills (the "what")
- All time spent doing
- Proficiency doesn't take long: "2 hours of consuming information, then you try"
- "You learn more in your first 20 sales than in the first 20 hours of learning about sales"

### Level 2: Manager

- Understands the sequence (the "how")
- Can teach others to replicate the skill
- Holds people accountable to consistent execution
- "Managers don't need to be better than individual contributors -- they need to understand HOW it works"

### Level 3: Leader

- Finds and develops the right people (the "who")
- "Building the machine that builds the machine"
- Very different from managers -- this is what most people fail to recognize
- Most managers (despite tenure) aren't leaders
- Good individual contributors are often terrible leaders and vice versa

## A Players vs B Players

### How to Identify A Players

- A players take ownership ("I'm not sending that to MY customers")
- Go beyond presenting problems -- they bring solutions
- Do more research, more diligence, higher quality work
- Ask questions you haven't thought of
- Have lower tolerance for anything but excellence than B players

### The Apparel Story

- Brand manager tasked with finding new revenue
- B player: Google 3 companies, pick cheapest, slap logo on
- A player: emailed 20 manufacturers, tried all samples, rejected crap quality, found alternative solution, came with implementation plan
- "None of what I described was earth shattering -- it was just a willingness to do more work"

### A Player Economics

- Harvard study: A players worth 5x B players
- Pay 25% above market, get 5x output
- "Still the biggest arbitrage value creation opportunity every business owner has"

### Chick-fil-A Quote

- "A lot of companies want to compete against us in the championship, but the reality is they lost in the draft"
- Some people come with batteries included -- worth the extra 5-20 interviews to find them

## Measuring Talent

- Measure on RATE OF IMPROVEMENT, not starting skill level
- "Intelligence = rate of learning"
- Someone who starts lower but improves faster will pass the experienced-but-slow person
- "I'd rather have a more intelligent employee who learns faster than a more experienced employee who learns slower"

## The Intolerance Principle

- "The higher the person who runs the division, the lower amount of tolerance for anything but excellence"
- Intolerance trickles down and gets diluted at each level
- If someone below a supervisor has lower tolerance than the supervisor, reverse those roles
- "The person who cares the most should be the one in charge"

## TALENT INSIGHTS (Deep Pass Batch 2)

### The Talent Gap is Everything
- "The difference between small and big businesses is how many A-players they have"
- Most small business owners hire a lot, get lucky with one person
- Big business owners maintain lower tolerance for mediocrity
- "They keep looking until they find six Andreas. Then it becomes too easy."

### The Person You Thought About This Morning
- "If they're not making you money and you're not sleeping with them, don't think about them first thing in the morning"
- That person takes 25% of your bandwidth
- "The biggest lever of growth is on the other side of a few hard conversations"

### Performance Improvement Plan (PIP Script)
- 14-day PIP: "You got 14 days to fix your s***"
- The script: say TO THEM what you say ABOUT THEM behind their back
- 1/5 have genuine turnaround
- 4/5 either refuse or don't change -> exit them

### Confronting Discomfort
- "People who will win vs. won't is based on ability to confront discomfort"
- "Guys cold plunge and lift heavy but can't say 'I think you suck a little bit'"
- "When you're qualifying that someone's heart is in the right place, it's because it's obvious they suck"

### Owner vs. Team-Run Business Value
- Owner-dependent $5M = worth the income only
- Team-run $5M ($2M take-home, doesn't require owner) = $10-20M asset
- Going from $2M to $3M annual income = adding $10M to net worth (at 10x multiple)
- "You become wealthy from the things you own, not the things you do"
- "Right now, many businesses are not valuable. No one would buy them because it's just you with helpers."

### Volume x Leverage = Output
- If you're lazy, you need massive leverage
- "If you want big things, you need a lot of other people's hours working for you"
