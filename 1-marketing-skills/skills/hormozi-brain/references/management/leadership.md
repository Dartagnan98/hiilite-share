# Leadership & Culture
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## Department Grading System

- Apply the Talent Grid (What-How-Who) to each department: marketing, sales, product, shared services
- Grade yourself: "Where am I involved? What level am I at in each department?"
- Your STRENGTHS often become the bottleneck (you're comfortable delegating what you don't know)
- Goal: all departments at Level 3 so you can operate above as CEO/owner

### Key Insight: Strengths as Bottleneck

- "It's actually your strengths which almost always become the bottleneck in the business"
- You delegate weaknesses easily but hold onto strengths too long

## Training Framework (Document-Demonstrate-Duplicate)

### Three Steps

1. **Document**: Make a checklist of what you do
   - Pro tip: video yourself doing it, then make checklist from the recording
   - "You don't think about checklisting while doing it"
2. **Demonstrate**: Do it in front of them (they watch you)
3. **Duplicate**: They do it in front of you (you give feedback)
   - Cycle until they can give themselves feedback = mastered the skill

### Short Windows for Proving

- Entry-level advertising jobs aren't complex -- they require traits (grit, endurance)
- Meta-skill of grit takes longer to train than the skill itself
- If below expectations after 3 weeks of proper training: cut them

### Shadow Train Method (for leadership roles)

1. They watch you do it
2. They do it in front of you
3. Support their independence (available but not involved)
- Initially more ad hoc calls, over time decisions get automated down to them
- Full handoff = they own it completely

## Culture Building

### Smarts vs Hearts

- Most entrepreneurs overestimate smarts, underestimate hearts
- "Business strategy overarchingly is not that complex"
- Lost in execution, not in strategy
- Two companies with same strategy: the one with better culture wins EVERY time

### Championship Team Mentality

- Everyone showed up to practice, tried hardest, aligned
- Rules are the same for everyone -- execution separates winners
- Competitors have access to same talent pool and can see your strategy
- Difference: ability to execute and have people execute on your behalf

### John Wooden Principle

- "Advanced people never don't do the basics"
- Every winning gym/business: they follow up leads within 5 minutes, follow a clear sales framework, have good onboarding
- "Nothing they say is revolutionary. It's just that they actually do it."

### Culture at Scale

- "When you scale, what you scale is culture and training"
- Culture = telling people what to do and WHY
- Training = making sure they know HOW
- Strong core team self-selects: new hires either assimilate or get rejected
- "Having that culture allows you to spit people out quickly but also retain"

### The Bar-Setting Principle

- You are the most distilled version of the culture
- Your level of intolerance sets the ceiling
- Gets diluted at every level below you
- "If someone cares more about my customers than I do, they should rightfully run the company"

## Buyback Your Time (Control Framework)

### The Entrepreneurial Control Journey

1. Start: shift from lack of control (job/parents) to complete control
2. Problem: the complete control that got you here stifles growth (there's only one of you)
3. Must progressively relinquish control

### Sequence of Buying Back Time

1. Lower-skilled support tasks (assistant)
2. Higher-level admin (billing, contracts, collections)
3. Sales ("No one can sell like me" -- but you can't outsell 12 people)
4. Sales management (recruiting, hiring, training, compensating a sales team)
5. Leadership (too many departments to lead everyone directly)

### The Identity Issue

- Each level requires giving up part of your identity
- "I used to think I was such a great salesman"
- The thing you're holding on to IS the thing you need to give up

### Rule of Thumb: "Know Where the Bodies Are Buried"

- If you can't name problems in a department, you're too far away
- If you only hear good news from a department, you're too far away
- Need more transparency, not more control
- "Every department has issues -- it's a business made of humans"

### The Trust Paradox

- "How can you be free if you control everything?"
- You relinquish control but keep full accountability for results
- "I'm going to give this over to you, I'm losing control, and yet I'm still responsible"
- Trust issues from childhood = the reason most entrepreneurs can't scale

- "Familiarity, though comforting, is not attractive"

## Charlie Munger's 8 Laws (As Applied by Hormozi)

### 1. Invert, Always Invert

- From Johnny Carson: try to solve problems in reverse
- Start at what you DON'T want and solve from that direction
- Fresh perspective that forward-thinking doesn't give

### 2. Don't Ingest Chemicals to Alter Your State

- "I can't imagine anyone thinking 'my life is so much better because I do drugs and drink'"
- One of the principles Munger found consistent in successful people

### 3. Don't Be Envious

- Buffett and Munger: "We have never met anyone who's done well in life who was envious of other people"
- "The more money I make, the poorer I feel" -- measuring stick changes
- $100K felt wealthiest because measuring unit was still meals
- Now measuring unit is millions -- someone always has more
- "Simply an imaginary way to make yourself suffer"

### 4. Don't Resent People

- King who wrote names in a drawer, later read them to see how life dealt with those people
- "People who do bad things eventually get caught up to"
- Someone existing in that state is already suffering

### 5. Be Reliable

- "If you just get this one right, it's very difficult to be unsuccessful"
- Hormozi's high school rival story: kid wasn't athletic, wasn't quick, but showed up every time
- Did all extra credit, all homework, went to every teacher event
- Got into Duke (Hormozi went to Vanderbilt)
- "He was more reliable than I was. More long-suffering. More consistent."

### 6. Don't Overspend Your Income

- "Wealth is a ratio between income and expenses, not a number"
- If you live on 1% of income, you're infinitely wealthy (100 years of living saved in one year)
- Hormozi tries to live on 10% of income
- When he had $1M in bank, still lived on $30K/year (3%)
- "I felt so free because I had way more money than I would ever need"
- Two ways to increase wealth ratio: increase income OR decrease expenses

### 7. Don't Learn Only from Your Own Mistakes

- Learning sources evolve with stage:
  1. Starting out: trade TIME for mentorship (work for free for small business owner)
  2. Growing: buy courses/coaching
  3. Established: join masterminds (peers at your level)
  4. $8 figures+: networking + advisers (minority equity in exchange for teaching)
  5. All levels: heroes (consumed content from people you'll never meet)
- "I've been directly mentored by Charlie despite never meeting the man"

### 8. Don't Quit After 3-4 Failures

- "You will fail many times. If you quit early, you guarantee misery."
- Don't even count failures -- the number means nothing

### Hormozi's Addition: Don't Crack Under Pressure

**Three Tactics for Resilience:**

1. **Negative Visualization** (from Epictetus via Trevor)
   - Imagine the bad thing happening 1,000 times in a row
   - How would you feel on the 1,000th time? You wouldn't care.
   - "You can rapidly force acclamation to stress by visualizing it happening thousands of times"

2. **Expect the Worst Before Starting New Behaviors**
   - Going to work out? Expect to be tired, unmotivated, busy. EVERY time.
   - If that's your baseline, you're never surprised or derailed
   - Any positive deviation is a bonus

3. **Everything Becomes Fine Mist** (from Elon Musk)
   - Universe will expand until everything is a fine mist
   - Everything you create will eventually disappear
   - "Nothing matters, which means we get to choose what we create meaning with"
   - Our brains are "meaning making machines" -- no inherent meaning, we assign it
   - "That has been so calming and has relieved so much pressure for me mentally"

## System for Outlearning Everyone

### 5 Levels of Competence (Student Mindset)

| Level | Type | Belief |
|-------|------|--------|
| 1 | Positive Active | "No one has proven it can't be done. I will be the best student ever no matter what." |
| 2 | Positive Passive | "He did it, so I can do it." (Replicate before iterate) |
| 3 | Neutral Passive | "Let's see if it works for me." (Where most people sit) |
| 4 | Negative Passive | "It might work for other people, but probably won't work for me." (Half-asses) |
| 5 | Negative Active | "I can prove this won't work for me. I'm special. Nothing works for me." |

### Key Insight on Level 5

- Highest potential to flip to Level 1
- "They have the active -- they're just proving the wrong thing"
- Once they switch direction, they become absolute brand champions
- Ask them: "Do you want to be right or do you want [the outcome]?"

### Why Unequal Outcomes Exist (Bottom-Up)

- Number of skills going in predicts success
- Each education/program provides "bricks" building a bridge
- We attribute success to the last brick (most recent) when it was cumulative
- "Skills and education build on themselves"
- More meta-skills (reading, learning, grit) = better ability to bridge gaps where instructions are vague

### Replicate Before You Iterate

- "I will do everything they said to the tea to replicate what they've done"
- First get the same result, THEN iterate with your other skills
- "Unless you've learned to replicate, you don't know why it worked to begin with"

### ROI Every Experience

- Even terrible experiences teach what NOT to do (inversion)
- "You can always benefit from it or benefit from the inverse of it"
- "I will win whether it's with you, the next guy, or the guy after that"

### Morpheus/Yoda Principle

- "Stop trying to hit me and hit me" (The Matrix)
- "There is no try" (Star Wars)
- Replace: "Stop trying to make your marketing work and MAKE your marketing work"
- "Try" allows the possibility of failure -- remove it

## Leveling Up: Why We Get Stuck

### The Progression

1. **Skills** (early stage): Stack skills together to become proficient, fill the holes
2. **Amplification** (growth stage): Deepen skills through repetition ("willing to get punched in the face over and over")
3. **Character Traits** (scaling stage): Focus, determination, patience, saying no, persistence

### The Woman in the Red Dress (Matrix Analogy)

- Opportunities = seductive distractions
- The higher you go, the more attractive the distractions become
- At $2M/year, the temptation to start a supplement company
- At $10M/year, the temptation to start a software company
- "The more talented you are, the more opportunities you know you could crush, which just means more things you have to say no to"

### Grit (Angela Duckworth)

- "Stick-to-itness" -- how likely are you to abandon a path for another
- "You can be successful at anything you want, just not everything you want"

### The Fitness Analogy

- Minimum effective dose vs threshold for new adaptation
- Huge "no man's land" in between where you work enough to maintain but not enough to improve
- If income stayed same for years, your skill set stayed the same
- "If your skill set has stayed the same, it's usually because you lack the character traits to deepen those skills"

### The Nature of Change Changes

- Early: level up skills
- Later: level up character traits
- "The actual thing that you need to improve changes -- not just skills, but the nature of the change itself changes"
- "Sometimes the skill itself is to not do other things"

## CULTURE BUILDING FRAMEWORK (Batch 7 -- Expanded)

### Observable Culture Definition Process
1. Define culture into observable terms (not vague values)
2. Demonstrate it yourself (Gandhi sugar story)
3. Get them to do it, watch what they did
4. Immediately correct failures, publicly praise successes
5. Next stage: they start giving kudos to EACH OTHER
6. Final stage: they start correcting EACH OTHER in private ("Hey man, you're cutting corners. It's going to get us all in trouble")
- "Every business you think does an amazing job has standards. Every business you think is shit, the owner didn't have standards."

### Paired Metrics for Team Incentives (Specific Example)
- Speed incentive without quality metric = guaranteed corner-cutting
- Paired system example (lawn care):
  - Pay: performance-based per job (speed incentive)
  - Quality: complaint = redo on own time (zero pay for redo)
  - Escalation: 2-3 complaints in a month = lose bonus for month
  - Third offense: lose job
  - Documentation: video of every completed lawn

### Why Small Businesses Stay Small
- "The reason most businesses stay small is because they think small and they act small"
- "You have to have the beliefs before you get the outcomes of a bigger business"
- "If everyone's responsible for everything, no one's responsible for anything"
- First hire should always be strong operational second
- Time study: write down what you do every 15 minutes for a week to understand where time goes

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### Maker or Manager (from Paul Graham, Applied by Hormozi)
- Paul Graham concept: there are only two modes of working -- making or managing
- **Makers** need long uninterrupted blocks (4+ hours) to produce their best work
- **Managers** work in 30-60 minute meeting blocks, context-switching all day
- A single meeting in the middle of a maker's day destroys the entire block
- "Time is the only resource. People who make the most money trade it for the highest return."

### The Wealth Equation
- **Overworked** (80 hrs/wk): $2M income, net worth = cash in bank
- **Wealthy** (10 hrs/wk): $2M income, net worth = cash + $10M in equity
- Same income, wildly different wealth
- "You get rich from what you make. You become wealthy from what you own."
- The transition from rich to wealthy requires building systems that run without you

### "Everything I Thought About Employees Was Wrong"
- Early Hormozi mindset: employees are a cost, minimize headcount, do everything yourself
- Reframe: employees are the mechanism through which you multiply your output
- The cold outreach bottleneck story: tried to scale by doing more outreach himself, hit a ceiling
- Solution: hired employees to do the Core Four on his behalf -- output multiplied
- "The way you scale is by getting other people to do the Core Four on your behalf"
- Employees aren't an expense -- they're an investment with measurable ROI (total payroll / engaged leads = cost per lead)
