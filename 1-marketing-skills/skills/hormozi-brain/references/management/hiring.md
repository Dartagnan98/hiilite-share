# Hiring & Recruiting Framework
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## The Internal 5 Core Functions (Mirror of External)

The hiring/recruiting process mirrors the customer acquisition process exactly:

| External (Customers) | Internal (Employees) |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| Lead Generation | Application Generation |
| Lead Nurture | Application Nurture |
| Sales | Interviewing (selling them on why to work for you) |
| Fulfillment | Training |
| Retention + Ascension | Retention + Ascension of talent |

## 6 Ways to Get Employee Leads (Same as Customer Leads)

1. **Paid ads** (promoted job postings)
2. **Earned media** (employer brand)
3. **Owned media** (your lists, CRM, followers)
4. **Manual outbound** (cold outreach, headhunting)
5. **Affiliates** (communities of skilled people)
6. **Word of mouth** (employee referrals)

## Critical Stat

- "10 out of 10 high-quality prospects will find a job within 8 days"
- If it takes you 6 days to make first contact, you've lost them

## Application Nurture

- Should have just as structured a process as lead nurture
- Treat applications with same urgency as sales leads

## Interview Process

- Have a script just like a sales script
- Disqualify people who aren't a good fit (same as sales)
- Should feel like you're being sold TO by great candidates (especially leadership+)
- "If you aren't learning from the person in the interview, waste of time"

## Volume Matters

- Interview MORE people than you think you should
- Use group interviews for frontline to manager roles (saves time, reads vibes)
- Sam (School) interviewed 600 developers to find CTO Daniel
- Pattern recognition = biggest asset (you tune your "picker" over time)
- "To figure out which sales manager was good, I had to hire five and waste six months on each"

## Key Hiring Beliefs

- "One amazing hire away from all the growth you could ever want"
- "A lot of times you don't need a what, you need a who"
- Look for people who come "batteries included" -- already ran and won the Olympic gold

## Who to Hire First

### The Time Stack Method

1. List everything you do
2. Calculate % of time per activity
3. Ascribe value to each activity (what makes you the most money?)
4. Hire for: highest % of time + lowest cost activity

### Objective

- "The objective of the hire should be to give you time"
- "How can I get the most time back for the least amount of money?"

### Typical First Hires (bootstrap founder)

1. Admin/helper (jack-of-all-trades assistant) -- often a spouse or first follower
2. Customer fulfillment person (once you've productized your service)

### The Climb

- Continue outsourcing: lowest value first, working up to highest value
- At the top: you're only making decisions + providing vision
- "The process of entrepreneurship is just buying back time and increasing your level of skill"

### Critical Mistake

- People try to outsource CEO too soon
- "Don't jump out of the CEO seat too soon"
- At $100K/month: "Dude, you're just getting into the game"

## Buy Talent vs Build Talent

- Like buying companies vs building companies
- Buying costs more but speed to money is faster
- As company scales, tend to buy more than build
- "Great lawyers are expensive. Cheap lawyers cost a fortune."
- "Buy nice or buy twice"

## Employee ROI Calculation

### Formula

- Total payroll / Total engaged leads = Cost per engaged lead
- Example: $100K payroll, 1,000 leads = $100/engaged lead
- If 1/10 engaged leads become customer: CAC = $1,000
- Compare against LTV for ROI (target 3:1 to 4:1 LTGP)

### Diagnostic Tool (When CAC > 3x Industry Average)

- Ask: "Do my engaged leads have the problem I solve AND the money to spend?"
  - If NO: advertising problem (wrong audience)
  - If YES but not enough: advertising problem (not enough volume)
  - If YES but not buying: sales problem

## HIRING DETAILS (Deep Pass)

### Don't Hire Entrepreneurs -- Hire Intrapreneurs
- Entrepreneurs in your company have only two outcomes:
  1. Lost their juju, sit in your business, complain about not making enough
  2. Regain confidence, start doing well, then take as much of your business with them as possible
- "Either way the outcome's poor"
- "I had to learn that one the hard way multiple times"
- Intrapreneurs: love driving growth and innovation but don't have appetite for risk
- Jeff Bezos calls his directors "CEOs" of sub-businesses within Amazon

### Group Interview Solution
- Stop doing 1-on-1 interviews for frontline positions (too slow)
- Set up group interviews: 5 candidates per hour
- Sales manager picks 1-2 from each group
- Can process 5x more candidates per week

### Smallest Skilled Deficiency Hiring
- When hiring, look for the smallest skill gap
- If a CFO is a "dick" but exceptional: 2 days to train sales vs 2 months to train kindness
- Hire the person who's kind, train them on the technical skill
- Chick-fil-A head of people: "A lot of people are trying to fix process when they really lost the championship in the draft"

### Quad Marketing Calendar (Hiring as Marketing)
- 4 quadrants: Internal Employees, Internal Customers, External Prospects, External Talent
- Most businesses only do External Prospects (marketing to get customers)
- Most missed quadrant: External Talent
- "Run ads for employees, generate leads, work leads, set interviews, make job offers, onboard"
- Parallel to customer journey: you make "offers" to candidates just like you do to customers

- Grit/endurance harder to train than skills
- Look for endurance first, teach the rest
- 3 weeks below expectations = cut
- A-player: 25% above market, 5x output
- "One A-player is worth five B-players"

- Employee Lead Getters parallel customer lead getters:

### 5 Employee Lead Stages
1. **Uncontacted** -- Application received, no outreach yet
2. **Contacted** -- First touch made (email, call, message)
3. **Engaged** -- They responded and showed interest
4. **Interviewed** -- Completed interview process
5. **Hired** -- Offer accepted, onboarding begun

"Treat employee leads with the same urgency as customer leads. 10 out of 10 high-quality prospects will find a job within 8 days."

### Document-Demonstrate-Duplicate (3Ds) -- Expanded from Book

**Step 1: Document**
- Make a checklist of every step in the process
- Pro tip: video yourself doing it FIRST, then make checklist from the recording
- "You don't think about checklisting while you're doing it -- video captures what you'd forget"

**Step 2: Demonstrate**
- Do the task in front of them while they watch
- They observe your pace, your decisions, your standards
- Let them ask questions during/after

**Step 3: Duplicate**
- They do it in front of you
- You give feedback
- Cycle until they can self-correct (give themselves feedback without you)
- "When they can spot their own mistakes, they've mastered the skill"

**Training notes from book:**
- "If they get it wrong, YOU got it wrong" -- the trainer owns the outcome
- Praise following directions even if the result is wrong -- reinforce compliance first
- One piece of feedback at a time -- don't overwhelm with corrections
- No punishment during training -- punishment is for AFTER training is complete and they know the standard

### Performance Diamond (4 Reasons Performance Drops)

When someone's performance drops, diagnose which of 4 causes:

**1. Communication -- They don't know THAT**
- They weren't told the expectation or standard
- Fix: Communicate clearly what's expected

**2. Training -- They don't know HOW**
- They were told the standard but weren't taught the method
- Fix: Run the 3Ds again (Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate)

**3. Motivation -- They don't WANT to**
- Three sub-reasons:
  - (a) Wrong reward: compensation/incentive doesn't match their motivation
  - (b) Aversion: they dislike the work, environment, or people they work with
  - (c) Outside life events: divorce, death in family, health crisis -- temporary performance hit
- Fix varies by sub-reason: restructure incentives, change role/team, or provide support

**4. Circumstances -- Something is STOPPING them**
- External blockers: broken tools, missing software access, another team blocking them
- Fix: Remove the obstacle

**Pro Tip feedback question:** "Over the last [time period], your [specific task] has changed from the norm. What do you think has gotten in the way and how can I help?"

### Daily Huddles Framework
- Meet with lead-getting employees **6-11 times per week**
- One 30-45 minute coaching session per week (deep dive)
- Daily short group huddles at start AND end of shift
- Start of shift: what are you doing today, what's your target
- End of shift: what did you accomplish, what blocked you
- "The cadence of accountability determines the speed of improvement"

### Employee ROI Calculation (from Book)
- **Formula**: Total Payroll / Total Engaged Leads = Cost per Engaged Lead
- Example: $100K payroll / 1,000 engaged leads = $100 per lead
- If 1 in 10 convert: $100 x 10 = $1,000 CAC
- Compare to LTGP: target 3:1+ ratio
- **Acquisition.com numbers**: ~$100K/month content team payroll / 30,000 leads = **$3.33/lead** with zero paid ads

### CAC Diagnostic (from Book)
- If CAC is within 3x industry average = good enough, focus on increasing LTGP instead
- If CAC > 3x industry average, ask: "Do my engaged leads have the problem I solve AND the money to spend?"
  - **No** = advertising problem (wrong audience, wrong targeting, wrong message)
  - **Yes but not buying** = sales problem (script, closer skill, offer positioning)
- "Most people try to fix CAC when the real lever is LTGP"
