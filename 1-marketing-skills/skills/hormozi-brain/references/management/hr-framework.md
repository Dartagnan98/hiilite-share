# HR Framework (What HR Should Actually Do)
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## False Beliefs to Destroy

1. "My job is to make employees happy" -- NO. Employees make themselves happy.
2. "Employees are my customer" -- NO. The COMPANY is your customer.
3. "I'm a middleman between employees and ownership" -- NO. You protect the company.

## Company Has Three Stakeholders

1. Shareholders (people who own it)
2. Customers (people who pay it)
3. Employees (people who run it)
- All three must be balanced, but company comes first

## The $600K HR Disaster Story

- HR director with 6 people (for 100-person company) did 90-day salary analysis
- Result: 20% overpaid (stay), 50% fair (stay), 25% underpaid (raise with no explanation)
- No problem was being solved -- no attraction issue, no retention issue
- Cost ~$600K/year in unnecessary profit reduction
- "The single stupidest thing I have ever seen someone do in my entire life"

## What Good HR Actually Does

1. **Protect the company** -- first answer to "can I hire?" should be "no, show me your utilization"
2. **Recruit best talent** -- active recruiting, not passive
3. **Develop talent** (with departments)
4. **Retain talent** (compensation packages + department heads)

## Operations vs Core Value

- Marketing, Sales, Product = core value drivers (top line revenue)
- HR, Legal, IT, Finance = operations (must happen but not primary value drivers)
- "Operations should support and always enable sales, marketing, and product"
- When Hormozi put operations first, company started declining

## "Think Like an Owner"

- If you think like an owner, you'll move up
- If you live in "bean bag and employee happiness" land, you'll be sidelined

## HR Team Sizing

- 6 HR people for a 100-person company was excessive in Hormozi's case
- The $600K mistake came from an overstaffed HR team solving problems that didn't exist
- HR should be lean and focused on protection + recruitment, not empire-building

## Key Numbers

- 25 support reps replaced by 4 (Prestige Labs) -- shows what happens when you measure utilization
- HR director's salary analysis mistake cost ~$600K/year in pure profit destruction
