# Partnership Frameworks
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## Only Three Reasons to Have a Partner

1. They have skills you do not have
2. They have money you don't have
3. They have time you don't have
- "If both of you are 'cool' and love the same thing, one of you is unnecessary"

## Structure Recommendations

- Prefer performance-based relationships over equity partnerships
- Use phantom equity with profit/revenue sharing instead of real equity
- Standard arrangement: percentage of revenue or profit based on performance
- Example: e-commerce companies that give 10% of top-line from attributed marketing

## Talk About the Ugly First

- "Right now you guys are in lust"
- Define: exit strategy, roles and responsibilities, what happens if someone leaves
- "Have the alimony conversation before the marriage"

## How to Exit a Partnership

- If business isn't making money: both walk away
- Two pieces to resolve: equity (who owns what) and debt (who owes what)
- Transfer equity via simple document, create promissory note for debt
- "I've done million dollar loans on one page"
- Promissory note: borrower, lender, no interest, amount, date, payment increments, default terms
- Have the conversation directly: "You said you'd do sales, you're not doing sales. What do you think is fair?"
- "The biggest hack in all relationships: the way you talk about someone behind their back is what you should tell them"

## Hormozi's Partnership Failures

- Had 8 failed partnerships
- Early career: took on partners left and right because he didn't know what he was doing
- "I was so stingy with equity early on... then swung back to not wanting to give anyone equity... now fallen in the middle path"

## PARTNERSHIP DETAILS (Deep Pass)

### Three Pillars of Every Business
1. **Acquisition** (rainmaker -- marketing and sales)
2. **Product** (fulfillment -- what you deliver)
3. **Operations** (everything else -- payroll, taxes, legal, IT, finance, HR)
- If both partners like the same things, one is unnecessary
- Operators can be hired for less; rainmakers and product people are more valuable

### Nine Failed Partnerships
- Had 9 failed partnerships, nearly all concurrent
- Made all money in life POST-partnerships (after stopping 4+ years ago)
- Most failed because both partners liked the same things (both acquisition-oriented)
- No contracts, no terms, no agreements -- "afraid of messing up the vibe"
- Biggest wealth dilution: giving away 50% equity on day one before the business grows
- "Most expensive equity is given in the beginning"

### Billionaire's Three Superstars Rule
- Private call with man doing $1B+/year revenue, 50 privately held companies
- Three superstars per business: Acquisition, Delivery, Operations
- "If you have three superstars, that business can grow on its own"
- "I've never NOT thought someone was a superstar and later realized they were"

### Selecting Trusted Advisors (Two-Trait Framework)
1. **Integrity:** Do they have your best interest at heart? Test by aligning incentives
2. **Efficacy:** Do they have the skill? Test by interviewing as many as possible
3. If forced to choose: 8/10 efficacy + trusted intention beats 10/10 efficacy + uncertain intention
4. Red flags: new entities (spinning up fresh LLCs), principal been indicted, can't explain simply
5. "Explain it to a golden retriever who speaks Spanish"

## PARTNERSHIP PLAYBOOK (Batch 7 -- Expanded)

### Time Study Method for Role Division
- Write down every 15 minutes what you do for a week
- Divide roles based on actual time spent and strengths
- Everything on this side you own, everything on that side I own
- Draw the line clearly

### Married Couples as Partners
- Often work well because they naturally specialize (front-end/back-end)
- Gym Launch: Alex = acquisition + product, Leila = operations
- Operators can be hired for less; rainmakers and product people are more valuable
- Skill stacking across 2-3 pillars makes you exponentially more valuable

### Solo Founder First Hire
- First hire for solo founder should always be a strong operational second
- Not equally weighted: operators can be hired for less than rainmakers/product people
