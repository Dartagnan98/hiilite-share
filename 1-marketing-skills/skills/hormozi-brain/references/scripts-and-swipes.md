# 1. SALES SCRIPTS

## Opening / Discovery

### CLOSER Framework Opener
**Context:** Opening any sales call or consult. Must hit milestone before moving forward.

"What made you come in today?"
"What made you reach out?"
"What's your goal right now?"
"Why is that important to you?"
"Why that number specifically? What does it resonate for you?"
"What would it look like 12 months from now if this ideal outcome were achieved?"

If they say "I just wanted to find out more information":
"Cool, but why? What did you want the information to solve?"
"I'm sure you don't just go around collecting information all day. There's something you're trying to get out of this. What's the problem?"

### Label (L in CLOSER)
**Context:** After clarifying why they're there. They must acknowledge the problem.

"So just so I'm hearing you right, it sounds like [their goal] is where you want to be, correct?"
"So what I'm hearing is you've done X, Y, and Z, and the missing link has been X. Is that right?"

"I can't cure cancer until you admit that you have it."

### Pain Cycle (O in CLOSER)
**Context:** Cycling through past failures. Repeat until exhausted.

1. "What have you tried so far to accomplish this?"
2. "How long did you do it for?"
3. "How long ago?"
4. "How did that work for you?"

Then: "What else have you tried?" -- repeat cycle.

Continue until: "That's it. I've tried everything under the sun."

Then: "You were so close. You were six inches away. You had this one piece missing, which is what we provide."

Transition: "Do you want to hear about how the program works? I think we might be able to solve that for you."

### Pull Teeth Technique
**Context:** When prospects give one-word answers.

Alternate between:
- "Can you give me an example of that?"
- "Can you be more specific?"

Bounce back and forth until meaty:
"Business isn't growing" -> "Can you give me an example?" -> "This year same as last year" -> "Can you be specific about what's different?" -> "Same amount of leads as last year"

Then chunk up to what you sell:
"So it sounds like you have a marketing issue. Is that fair to say?"
"So if we solved this marketing and pricing issue, do you think it would help grow the business?"

### Mechanic Analogy
**Context:** When pressed to answer technical questions before discovery is done.

"You're asking me what's wrong with your car and it would be unethical for me to answer without looking under the hood first."

"If anyone gives you an answer on the first call without already having looked under the hood... run the other way. It means they're just telling you whatever they can to get you to buy."

### BANT Qualification Script
**Context:** Set calls to qualify before closing call.

- **Budget:** "This is a four-figure+ investment. Just want to make sure this isn't a shock."
- **Authority:** "Are you the person making the financial decision, or do you need someone else's permission?"
- **Need:** "How important is this to solve, 1-10?" Then: "Why isn't it a 1?" (gets them to sell themselves)
- **Timing:** "Would you want this solved today or when?"

## Objection Handling

### 3A Framework
**Context:** The skill that "separates the best from the rest." Use for ANY objection.

**A1 -- Acknowledge:** Repeat what they said back.
"So you're curious about what our certifications are..."

**A2 -- Associate:** Connect their concern to behavior of successful customers.
"It's so funny you ask that because our biggest success stories actually asked the same question."

Three association strategies:
1. **Recency:** "I had someone just earlier today who asked the same question. Can I share what I shared with them?"
2. **Successful customer:** "That actually makes you just like Sarah who had a similar business and crushed it with us."
3. **Appeal to authority:** "That's so funny you ask that -- Alex just told me this thing earlier today. Can I share it with you?"

**A3 -- Ask:** Ask a question about their question. Never answer directly.
"Which certifications are you looking for specifically?"

### Five Rules for 3A
1. A prospect believes almost nothing you say and almost everything they say. Get THEM to conclude they're a good fit.
2. Never disagree with a prospect. "You never win a sale by winning an argument." Be like smoke.
3. Tell them what their question means: "You must be a really smart decision-maker."
4. Use straw men for tough truths.
5. Retain childlike curiosity. Say "Huh" to yourself to reset.

### Price Objection Scripts
**Context:** "It's too expensive" / "I can't afford it"

Ferrari test: "If I was selling you a brand new Ferrari for $10K, would you find the money? Of course. The reason someone is not buying is simply because they do not understand the value."

To sales team: "If we were giving away Ferraris for $5K, do you think they'd find the money? Yes. So they don't understand the value. It is ALWAYS the salesperson's fault."

### "I Need to Think About It" Scripts
**Context:** Stall objection.

"Totally. What's your main concern? Let's think about it together."

Walk them through:
1. "Do you feel like what we're doing can meet your needs and solve the problem? Yes or no?"
2. "Do you want to work with us as a company? Do you like our values?"
3. "Do you have access to funds or know someone who does?"

"Those are the only three things you need to make this decision. Do you like the product, do you like us, do you have the money?"

### Decision Maker Scripts
**Context:** "I need to talk to my spouse/partner"

Step 1 - Circumvent: "What if they say no?" (1 in 3 say "I'd probably do it anyway" -- close immediately)

Step 2: "What do you think their biggest objection would be?"

Step 3 - Past Agreements: "Does your partner know that you're struggling with [problem]? Do they approve of that? No? Then why would they be against something that remedies a problem they already don't approve of?"

Step 4: "Sometimes it's better to ask for forgiveness than permission."

Step 5 - Delayed Close: "Let's get your card down. We'll delay the payment to Friday. If between now and then your partner says 'Baby, I want you to keep struggling and never make money,' then call me back and I'll tear up the contract."

### "Can't Afford It" Scripts

"Are you on a budget?"
"Would you like me to order these in order of importance?"
Remove lowest-impact item: "This would be the lowest one I'd remove. Does this work?"
If still no: "Really getting into bare bones here. I could take this out, but you're gonna have to eat a pound of broccoli."
Find money: "Are you going to stop going out? Drink less? How much do you spend on that? We just found some money."

### Timing Objection
"Totally understand. Our biggest success stories are people who felt the same way. What would make it a good time?"

### Universal Deep Objection Question
**Context:** When you're stuck and need to cut to the heart of the issue.

"What are you afraid of?"
"What are you most afraid of having happen?"

Escalation sequence: main concern -> variables -> "What would make this a no?" -> "What would it take to say yes?"

### Three Beliefs to Break in Every Sale
1. **Circumstances:** "I don't have money/time" -> teach resourcefulness/prioritization
2. **Other People:** "Spouse/kids won't let me" -> reframe how it helps them
3. **Self:** "I can't do it / what if I fail?" -> confront decision, show it's not rushed

## Closing Scripts

### Assumed Close
**Context:** After primary sale, for upsells. Origin: supplement sales at gym.

Instead of pitching benefits: "Do you want chocolate or vanilla for your protein?"
"For the pre-workout, do you want kiwi or strawberry?"
"Do you want to just use the card you have on file?"

Key: "I didn't ask her WHETHER she wanted them. I asked her WHICH ONE she wanted."
Result: 9-9.5 out of 10 vs standard 1 in 3-4.

### No-Based Questions
**Context:** End of sale when prospect has decision fatigue.

Instead of: "Are you excited to move forward today?"
Use: "Would you be opposed to moving forward today?"

More phrases:
- "Would you be completely unreasonable for us to..."
- "Would it be completely out of left field for me to say..."
- "Would you be against..."
- "You don't want anything else, do you?"

### "Fair Enough?" Close
**Context:** After making any concession during downselling.

Make concession, then: "Fair enough?"

"So many times they're like, 'well they made a concession and I'm a reciprocal human being, it's very hard for me to deny this request.' Then: 'Great, let's move forward.'"

### Temperature Check
**Context:** After 2 rejections during downselling.

"Real quick, 1-10, how bad do you want this?"
- 8-10: keep going with downsells
- Below 8: pivot to "Why not a 10?" then switch to different product

### Prescriptive Close
**Context:** After Ghost Products technique, pairing with assumed close.

Tell them HOW to use each product:
"What do you do every morning no matter what? Brush your teeth? Put this next to your toothbrush."
"Put a piece of tape on it with a '2' -- take two every morning."

Go through each product with usage instructions, then:
"Did you just want to use the card you have on file?"

### Wealth Management Reframe
**Context:** When prospect says "I already have [competitor]."

"90% of the clients we have that shift over already have a wealth advisor. So it means you already know how a lot of these things work, which means you'll be up the learning curve."

### Comparison Close
**Context:** When prospect says competitor is cheaper.

"Let's pretend price isn't an option. Let's say we're both priced the exact same amount. Which one would you rather do?"
They say you. Ask: "Why?"
Let them spend 3 minutes selling themselves on why you're better.
"Right. You can understand why we're not $200."

"Amazon is more expensive than Bank of America. Price is what you pay, value is what you get."
"Buy nice or buy twice."

### Underdog Positioning Script
**Context:** When competing against a bigger company.

"You don't want to work with [big company] because to them, you're just a number."
"You're never going to meet [founder]. You'll get assigned some account rep who doesn't care."
"Who you're really comparing is not my company versus [leader]. It's me versus their rep."

### Pricing Delivery Hack
**Context:** Right before stating your price.

"Before I tell you the price, it's super expensive."
- Rich: think their version of expensive, your number seems fine
- Poor: brace themselves, less sticker shock

Alternative: Write price on paper, slide it. Or use calculator, turn screen to them.

### The Gasp Test
"If you didn't get a gasp from the price, you didn't go high enough."

### The "That's the Reason" Close
**Context:** Universal close for any objection.

Whatever reason they give, flip it:
"I don't have time." -> "The fact that you don't have time is probably the reason you need this more than anyone else."
"I don't have money." -> "The fact that you don't have money is exactly why you need to do this -- because this is exactly what our product helps you solve."

"All objection overcomes exist literally just to give you an excuse to ask again."

## Downsell Scripts

### Step-Down Cards
**Context:** When prospect says no to primary offer. Each step adds a requirement from them.

1. "$299 for 3 weeks. Fair enough?" -- No.
2. "$49/week for 12 weeks with a commitment. Fair enough?" -- No.
3. "Don't worry about the commitment. Just get started today for $49. Pay as you go. Fair enough?" -- No.
4. "$39/week if you leave me a testimonial at the end. Fair enough?" -- No.
5. "$29/week. Pay as you go. Just bring a friend in the first 30 days so I can at least cover my costs. Fair enough?" -- No.

If no after 5-6 step-downs: "It's not a money thing. You lost them earlier in the sale."

### Feature Downsell
**Context:** Standardized process for reducing scope to close.

1. First cut: Remove something VALUABLE, tiny decrease in price -> forces re-upsell to original
2. If no: Put guarantee back, remove something they DON'T care about, BIG decrease in price -> better deal
3. Name combinations: First Class, Business Class, Economy
4. Name cheapest: "The Minimum" (implies they have to get at least that)

"Deal?" / "Fair enough?" after each:
"If I do this, would that work for you? Fair enough?"

### Payment Plan 7-Step Process
**Context:** When they want the product but can't pay in full.

1. Anchor higher (show payment plan price), then offer prepayment discount: "Want to save money? Prepay for $10K"
2. Third-party financing, credit card, layaway: "Would you rather I decide your payment terms or you decide?" -> "Great, use your credit card"
3. Half now, half later: "What's the most you can put down today?" Biweekly payments aligned to paychecks
4. Temperature check: "1-10, how bad you want this?"
5. Split into 3 payments
6. Evenly spread over service duration
7. Free trial

### No Sale Salvage
**Context:** When customer declines everything.

Give free at-home program (thumb drive), invite back for free nutrition consult.
At consult: close on supplements. Same close rate, higher ticket ($300 vs $200).
"We tried to solve their problem not in a way they wanted to solve it. They wanted a magic pill."

## Upsell Scripts

### "Chocolate or Vanilla?" Assumed Close
**Context:** After primary sale, for add-on products.

"Do you want chocolate or vanilla?"
"Do you want to just use the card you have on file?"
Never ask IF they want it; ask WHICH ONE they want.

### Classic Upsell
**Context:** Making add-ons feel required.

"You can't have X without Y."
Must feel as required as possible without being required.
Seamless process = 80%+ take rate.
"If getting 30% on upsells, you're not making it assumed enough."
Benchmark: McDonald's fries+coke at ~80%.

### Richard Schwarz Furrier
**Context:** Stacking upsells using no-based questions.

"What we're going to do is store and condition your coat. You don't want anything else, do you?"
Customer says "No" -- confirming the conditioning upsell.
Then: free ear muffs -> "you need to store and condition these too"
Then: inspect coat -> "want us to fix this loose button?" -> "you don't want anything else, do you?"
Result: 90%+ uptake vs 20-30% standard.

### Rollover Upsell
**Context:** Crediting previous purchase toward next offer.

"I owe you money" (subject line for win-back emails)
"I'll credit what you paid toward what I should have sold you originally"
For competitor's upset customers: scrape 1-star reviews, offer to credit what they spent.
Price next offer 4-5x higher than rollover credit (25% discount max).

### Menu Upsell
**Context:** After initial close, present options.

Script for continuity conversion at halfway point:
"Have you enjoyed everything so far? Mind if I tell you about a way to save and get more?"

### Anchor Upsell
**Context:** Show expensive option first to make main offer look reasonable.

Present anchor -> Get the gasp -> Come to rescue -> Present main offer -> Ask for payment.
If they DON'T gasp: close the whale.
Anchor must be 5-10x the price of main offer.
"One out of 10 buying the 10x product = doubles your business."

# 2. COLD OUTREACH SCRIPTS

### Cold Call Opener
**Context:** First words on a cold call. Don't say your company name.

Don't say: "This is [first name]..." (too formal, too salesy)
Don't mention your company name.
DO pause after your name (implies they should search memory for you).

### Voicemail Script
**Context:** Leaving voicemails on cold calls.

"Hey John, it's Alex. I'm calling in reference to [their competitor/neighbor]. Please give me a call back at [number]."

Short. Relevant. Opens a curiosity loop.

### Cold Email Template
**Context:** Outbound email. Write at below 3rd grade reading level.

- Personalize: act like you know them, reference something about them/their business
- Give BIG FAST value in 2-3 sentences
- Lead magnet must be exceptional: "So good people feel stupid saying no"
- Biggest breakthrough: offer what competitors CHARGE for, for free (3x response rate)

### Follow-Up Cadence
- Contact each person 2-3x/day for first few days
- Then 1x/day for 7 days
- Multiple channels: phone + email + DM + text + voicemail
- Wait 3-6 months, restart from top of list

### Team Management Script
"Here are your 70 leads. I need 7 appointments back. That's the quota."
New reps: "Copy the best rep exactly, do TWICE the volume. Don't change anything until you beat their numbers."

# 3. PRICING & MONEY SCRIPTS

### Price Raise Letter (Existing Customers)
**Context:** When raising prices on current customers.

1. "You've noticed we've been growing..."
2. Re-sell them on forgotten value: what you're currently doing for them
3. What you want to do in the future (ambitious improvements)
4. "With integrity, I cannot maintain my promise unless we do this."
5. Communicate price in smallest unit (daily or weekly, not annual)
6. PS: "If this will materially affect your life, reach out."

Execution: video AND direct mail. Turn comments off: "Out of respect, let's handle face to face."

### Skip-and-Anchor (New Customer Price Raise)
**Context:** Raising prices without losing close rate.

Want to go from $2K to $3K? Anchor at $4K.
Sales team presents $4K as new price.
"But you can prepay today for $3K."
Sales team still has $2K as emotional home base.
Always clear the pipeline first: announce price increase to create urgency.

### Payment Plan Presentation
**Context:** Anchoring on payment plan price.

Present $12K (payment plan price), not $10K.
"Want to save money? Prepay for $10K."
Savings frame, not penalty frame.

### Win Your Money Back Pitch
**Context:** Front-end attraction offer.

"You bet $600 you'll lose 20 lbs in 8 weeks. Hit your goal, you get it back. Miss it, you still got the transformation."
At week 3: "You realize this isn't about 6 weeks, right? What would it look like to keep going?"
At halfway: make everyone a winner -- credit toward annual.
Apply credit over 12 months ($50/mo discount on $200/mo) rather than giving 3 months free.

### Guarantee Language
**Context:** Reducing risk perception.

"If [result] doesn't happen, [consequence for you]."
"Over 4,000+ sales and 5 years: only 2 unconditional refund requests."

### Supply Control Pricing Script
**Context:** When demand is high, use scarcity.

"Being at full capacity is completely up to you -- you can say today that you are full."
Create waiting list even if you're not at capacity.
"When demand increases, you cut supply."

# 4. HIRING SCRIPTS

### Job Posting Language
**Context:** Use same offer structure as customer acquisition.

Run ads for employees the same way: "generate leads, work leads, set interviews, make job offers, onboard."
"10 out of 10 high-quality prospects will find a job within 8 days."

### Interview Questions
"If you aren't learning from the person in the interview, waste of time."
Look for smallest skill gap -- hire the kind person, train the technical skill.

### 3-Week PIP Conversation
**Context:** When trained employee is underperforming after 3 weeks.

Say TO THEM what you say ABOUT THEM behind their back.
"You got 14 days to fix your s***."
1/5 have genuine turnaround. 4/5 either refuse or don't change -> exit them.

### Firing Language
**Context:** The partner who locked the door.

Locked door at meeting time, told assistant to fire everyone banging on the door.
"He reset the bar. This is how we perform."

### Partnership Dissolution
"You said you'd do sales, you're not doing sales. What do you think is fair?"
"Only partner for money or skills you don't have -- nothing else."
"If both partners like the same things, one is unnecessary."

# 5. RETENTION / SAVE SCRIPTS

### Fee or Feedback
**Context:** Customer wants to cancel.

"I'll waive your cancellation fee if you sit down and tell me what we could do better."
Let them vent. Be MORE upset than them about the problem.
"I'm so sorry that happened. That's not okay. Let me make this right."
Over half get resold into a higher-level package from exit interviews.

### Consumption Accountability
**Context:** When members stop showing up (by Wednesday each week).

"Hey [name], I noticed you haven't been in this week. When can we get you scheduled?"
Escalate: text -> call -> personal visit.
"If they don't consume the stuff, they cancel at a much faster rate."

### Personal Check-In
**Context:** Text every 14-21 days, independent of service.

Just: "How's life?"
NOT automated. "As soon as someone finds out communications are automated, they stop paying attention."
"The best way to get people to think you give a shit is to actually give a shit."

### Handwritten Card
**Context:** Must reference specific progress.

"You went from black band to yellow band on pull-ups, and you're down 10 pounds."
Can include referral gift card.

### Proactive Price Decrease
**Context:** When data shows they're not using all features.

"You're only using 4 of 6 features. Want to drop to a lower rate?"
"Those people NEVER leave."
"These guys were ethical and honest. They proactively asked me to pay less. I love these guys."

# 6. CONTENT SCRIPTS

### Proof-Promise-Plan (Video/Content Intro Formula)
**Context:** Opening for any educational content.

- **Proof:** Prove you know what you're talking about (credentials, results)
- **Promise:** Tell them what they'll get/learn
- **Plan:** Set expectations for what happens next

Order flexible -- emphasize whichever is most important. For business education, lead with proof.
"Entertainment confirms the thumbnail; education edifies the person."

### Hook Formulas
"If you can't explain the offer in one or two sentences, it is not clear enough."
Go from VAGUE to CLEAR in thumbnails/headlines.
"If they want that thing they will click on that thing and then you just give them that thing."

### CTA Language
"Look at your number one piece of content from last year. Post it again."
"We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught."

### Damaging Admission
**Context:** Copy and presentations near CTAs.

Wrong order: "You're going to make a ton of money BUT it's going to be hundreds of hours of work."
Right order: "You're going to have to go through hundreds of hours of videos, take 5 hours a day to execute BUT you're going to make more money than ever in your life."

"WARNING: When you sign up with GymLaunch, you're going to massively increase your tax burden. We are not liable for the amount of taxes you'll have to pay as a result of the money we're going to make."

# 7. MANAGEMENT SCRIPTS

### Culture Definition (Gandhi Framework)
**Context:** Setting standards.

Woman asks Gandhi to help her kid stop eating sugar. He says come back in a month. Returns, he tells kid "stop eating sugar." Why a month? "I had to stop eating sugar first."

Steps:
1. Define culture into observable terms (not vague values)
2. Demonstrate it yourself
3. Get them to do it, watch what they did
4. Immediately correct failures, publicly praise successes
5. They start giving kudos to EACH OTHER
6. They start correcting EACH OTHER in private

### The Vince Story
**Context:** Identifying managers who undermine culture.

Owner Dave trained Hormozi on standards. Manager Vince said on first shift: "We don't do any of that stuff."
"Do you have a Vince in your business? Are YOU the Vince?"

### Stop / Start / Keep
**Context:** Giving direct feedback to employees.

"Sarah, I need you to STOP doing this."
"I need you to START doing this."
"I need you to KEEP doing this."

Don't say "stop being lazy" -- break into behaviors:
"This week, focus on responding to Slack. Turn on notifications."

### Delegation Script
"If you want it done right, get someone to spend ALL their time on it."
"100% of humans are replaced every 100 years."

### "The Person You Thought About" Script
**Context:** Identifying the biggest management lever.

"If they're not making you money and you're not sleeping with them, don't think about them first thing in the morning."
That person takes 25% of your bandwidth.
"The biggest lever of growth is on the other side of a few hard conversations."

### Paired Metrics
**Context:** Preventing one-sided incentives.

- Sales: total sales vs refunds
- Customer Success: tickets handled vs NPS
- Marketing: total apps vs qualified apps
- Speed: performance-based pay + quality metric (complaint = redo on own time)

### Three Objectives Filter
**Context:** Deciding what to work on.

Everything must map to:
1. How does this get us more customers?
2. How does this make customers worth more?
3. How does this decrease the risk that 1 and 2 stop happening?

If it doesn't map, cut it.

### Hard Conversation Script
"Guys cold plunge and lift heavy but can't say 'I think you suck a little bit.'"
"When you're qualifying that someone's heart is in the right place, it's because it's obvious they suck."

**Context:** Ask at end of every call (not just closes).

"Who do you know that's also dealing with [problem]?"
1 in 3-4 refer when asked. Referral close rate: 80-90%. Cuts CAC in half.

### BAMFAM
**Context:** Book a meeting from a meeting. End of every session.

"Everybody pull up calendars, when are you showing up again?"

### Gift Card Play
**Context:** Generating referral leads.

Sell $200 gift cards for $20 (90% off), limit 2 per person, can only use on someone else.
Customer pays $20 = you get paid for a hot referral lead.
Roll $200 into $1,000-2,000 product.

# 10. ONBOARDING SCRIPTS

### Low Consumption Playbook
**Context:** Preventing churn from poor onboarding.

1. Closer books the onboarding call (BAMFAM)
2. 4-6 one-on-one sessions before group
3. Each session ends with BAMFAM: pull up calendars, book next session
4. Group sessions positioned as graduation: "You're not ready for that yet, you got to earn it"
5. AB close: "Would you rather start one-on-one or in a group?" If they choose group, gift them 1-2 one-on-one sessions anyway

### Reinforce Decision (First 48 Hours)
**Context:** R in CLOSER. Critical for reducing churn/refunds.

- Personalized video from CEO by name: "Hey Johnny, saw you just signed up. Super pumped to have you."
- Handwritten card
- Welcome gift/swag
- Introduction to point of contact: "You're going to be meeting with Heather tomorrow."

"People make their decision about whether they believe in you as a company within the first 48 hours after the sale."

# 11. TONALITY & DELIVERY

### Script Memorization Method
**Context:** How to sound natural, not scripted.

1. Print two copies of the script
2. Read entire script out loud, enunciating every word
3. After completing, cross out ONE word with marker
4. Read entire script again with word missing
5. Cross out another word
6. Repeat until every word is blacked out and you're saying it from a blank page

"By the end, you will have repeated the script as many times as there are words in the script."

### Close Pause
"Ask them to buy and shut the f*** up."
Research: Salespeople who wait up to 8 seconds after asking close 30% more sales.

### Sales Training Schedule
**Context:** Daily practice for sales teams.

- 60 min/day, 5 days/week
- Text manager before waking up, text when done
- First 25 min: read script out loud
- 5 min: draw obstacle overcomes
- Review recordings: what went right, wrong, next time
