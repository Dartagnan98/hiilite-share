# Growth Stages
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## The 4 Stages of Entrepreneurship (Business Growth Stages)

### Stage 1: $0 to $1M/year
- **Formula**: One product, one channel
- Reliable system of acquiring customers -- that's literally it
- People get stuck here because they still haven't nailed repeatable acquisition
- Also get stuck due to personal/interpersonal problems (sleeping with employees/customers, drama)
- Channel = cold email, Facebook ads, Instagram ads, YouTube ads, referral partners, organic/earned media
- "You gotta sell one product and one channel. That's it."

### Stage 2: $1M to $10M/year
- **Formula**: Multiple products (cross-sells + upsells), potentially multiple channels
- NOT starting multiple businesses -- it's creating a value ladder / backend
- "What's the next level of service? What do they naturally want/need after buying our initial product?"
- You're selling the same number of units but increasing LTV (e.g., 4-5x your LTV)
- Higher LTV opens up traffic platforms you couldn't afford before
- Superior backend allows broader acquisition channels

### Stage 3: $10M to $30M/year
- **Formula**: Professionalizing the business
- This is painful -- your "special sauce" starts getting diluted
- Still running on Google Sheets and Trello boards -- need real infrastructure
- Must hire corporate people: HR, legal, accounting, IT systems
- Become a target for lawsuits -- contracts and legal protection matter
- Pendulum risk: going too far toward corporate stifles innovation and creative energy

### Stage 4: $30M to $100M+
- **Formula**: Intrapreneurs running profit centers
- Need more "souls" -- more people with entrepreneurial drive who can take initiative
- Structure: Core operations hub (HR, legal, IT, finance) with profit center spokes
- Each profit center has: Product + Acquisition (marketing/sales)
- **Critical distinction**: Hire intrapreneurs, NOT entrepreneurs
  - Entrepreneurs either lost their juju and complain, OR regain confidence and leave taking business with them
  - Intrapreneurs love driving growth but don't have same appetite for risk
  - They like the "safe place" to experiment while getting a paycheck
- Jeff Bezos calls his directors "CEOs" of sub-businesses within Amazon
- Use existing resources/capital/infrastructure to create similar-but-different product lines
- Each new product line needs a TRUE leader (as smart as you) before launching

## Scale Zero Framework
- From $500M mastermind with 8 entrepreneurs
- "For me to get bigger, I must get smaller within the company"
- Must become less and less important to the company's success
- If you're doing 20 hours/week and you double, you'll be at 40 -- you become the bottleneck
- Goal: Get to literally ZERO requirement of you
- "Only then will I be able to scale, otherwise I'll be the bottleneck"

## Business Growth Simple Math
- $1M/year = $83,333/month = ~$21K/week = ~$3K/day
- Pick your LTV, work backwards to units needed
- At $1K LTV: need 21 units/week = ~5 deals/day
- At 1-in-3 close rate: 15 calls/day showing up
- At 66% show rate: 22 set appointments/day
- **Key insight**: Easier to start with premium ($5K-$15K) than high-volume low-ticket
- Below $1K/unit is brutal operationally -- need high volume, need operations skill
- "The operations behind it -- you have to support so much volume"
- LTV must be high: unique, expensive, sticky, habit-forming

## FOUR CHAPTERS EXPANDED (Deep Pass -- Batch 3)

### Hub-and-Spoke Model for $30M+
- Central ops: HR, legal, IT, finance (keeps everyone out of jail)
- Profit centers spin off the hub, each with Product + Acquisition
- Each run by an intrapreneur who's a "sub-CEO"
- Don't launch new ones without a leader "as smart as you are"

### Your Spirit Gets Diluted at Scale
- At $30M+, your vision/care/drive gets diluted across the organization
- "You need heads that can reinvigorate the business and drive innovations"
- Need multiple "souls" -- not just one founder driving everything
- "We did 28, 37, we've just really been stuck at this level" -- reason is founder spirit dilution

## PERFECT BUSINESS MODEL (Deep Pass -- Batch 4)

### U.E.S.A. Framework
- **U**nique -- can't be comparison-shopped
- **E**xpensive -- premium pricing
- **S**ticky -- habit-forming, high switching cost
- **A**ir (digital/weightless) -- zero marginal cost
- Buffett: "Costs a penny, sells for a buck, habit-forming"
- Margin math: 90% -> 95% margin = **doubling profitability** (costs halved)

## INSURANCE AS ULTIMATE BUSINESS MODEL (Deep Pass -- Batch 4)

- Gets paid far before ever having to do anything
- May never have to fulfill at all
- Reverse of depreciation: collects money that appreciates over time
- Oldest companies (180-300+ years) are insurance companies
- Predate the tax code

### The Money Ladder -- Who Gets Paid When
- Employee: work first, paid 2-4 weeks later
- Self-employed: split (half now, half on delivery)
- Professional (doctor): paid first, then work
- Banks: preferred creditor, paid first if things go wrong
- Insurance: paid the whole time, may never deliver
- God/Franchisors: 10% off the top, forever
- Lesson: restructure your business to be higher on the ladder
