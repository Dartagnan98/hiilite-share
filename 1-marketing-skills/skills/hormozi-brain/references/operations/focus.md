# Focus
## Source: Alex Hormozi (383 video transcripts)

## Stop Starting New Businesses
- Hormozi had 6 gyms, 2 agencies, a turnaround business -- barely making money
- DUI/head-on collision was the catalyst to consolidate
- Ended 3 partnerships, sold/gave away gyms, focused entirely on Gym Launch
- Result: went from scattered millions to $3M profit in 12 months

## Attention > Time
- "The most valuable thing we have as entrepreneurs is not our time but our attention"
- Unmade decisions accumulate and consume thinking power
- Each unmade decision allocates a portion of your brain, reducing decision-making power
- "I'm always one decision away from changing my life"

## The Trash Business Neighbor Story
- Neighbor sold trash business for a lot of money, waited out non-compete, started same business again
- "Why would I learn something new? I already know everything about the trash business"
- Most entrepreneurs desire novelty when they should desire depth

## Trade Novelty for Loyalty
- "You become loyal to the business"
- Business is like fine wine -- you understand it deeper over the years
- "You miss out on that opportunity if you're going after one night stands from a business perspective"
- Channel ADD into marketing/content: new hooks, new creative, new ad ideas
- Hormozi puts his ADD into content -- "I've become a significantly better entrepreneur by making content because it distracts me from messing up my business"

## The Decision Etymology
- "Decide" -- root "cide" means "to kill" (suicide, homicide)
- A decision is choosing to kill off all other options
- "You need to decide what your business is going to be and kill off all other options"

## The 3 Evil Es of Entrepreneurship

### Evil E #1: Expectations
- Setting arbitrary timelines on arbitrary numbers
- Leads to shortcuts, hasty high-risk decisions, destroying the business
- **Fix**: Shift from outcome-based to process-based expectations
- "Good process drives good results" (from mentor)
- Keep high expectations but put them on PROCESS, not outcome

### Evil E #2: Emotions
- Best CEOs (Bezos, Munger, Buffett) are devoid of emotion in decisions
- Red flag: flip-flopping on decisions when presented same data
- **Tactics**:
  - When you feel urgency, EXTEND the time horizon (sleep on it)
  - Make big decisions from a state of contentment and gratitude
  - "The best decisions I've made came from a state of contentment"
  - If you don't feel content/grateful, don't make the decision

### Evil E #3: Expenses
- Expenses = liabilities = risk
- "Get the growth first, then growth pays for the people"
- Don't hire in anticipation of growth -- growth will pay the expenses
- Hormozi hired 25 support reps anticipating growth for Prestige Labs, now 4 people handle more
- When you anticipate growth, culture decreases, morale decreases, efficiency decreases
- **Self-reinforcing loop**: Bad expectations (from deficiency) -> emotional decisions -> bad expenses -> worse outcomes -> worse expectations

### Most Deadly: Expectations
- "Call all your beliefs assumptions -- they become far more malleable"
- "This is my assumption, what do you think?" vs "This is my expectation, what do you think?"

## FOCUS FRAMEWORKS (Batch 7 -- Expanded)

### Bucket Analogy for Business Growth
- Business = bucket, traffic = water flowing in
- Step 1: Fix holes in bucket (better: improve what exists)
- Step 2: Increase flow to bucket (more: scale what works)
- Step 3: Add new flows (new: only after exhausting better and more)
- Most entrepreneurs: multiple leaky buckets, no bandwidth to fix any of them
- Diverting resources from core bucket to build new buckets = both buckets deteriorate
- "Media costs go up, talent costs go up. A business that isn't improving is getting worse relative to the market over time"

### "Better, More, New" Priority Framework (Expanded)
- Always ask: Have we maxed out BETTER yet?
- Better = lowest risk opportunity everyone has
- More = riskier than better (scaling what works)
- New = riskiest (innovation, new products)
- Only move to More after Better is exhausted, only New after More

### Cost of Distraction (Half-Built Bridges)
- "Most people learn two, three, four of the skills but there's ten they need to learn, and they say 'this isn't for me' and jump to another one"
- "It is arrogant to believe that you are going to out-compete people who are fully focused on one thing when you're split on five"
- First lesson to unlearn: innovation was reinforced early. "The Innovation already happened when you got product-market fit. As soon as people started buying, NEW needs to end for a while"
- "Mark Zuckerberg didn't have side hustles"

## FOCUS EXPANSION (Deep Pass -- Batches 3-4)

### Do Nothing New = Best Results
- Company events: rule of "zero new things, just execute known good things"
- Four times in a row: best NPS scores ever
- "Every time we change things, NPS goes down"

### Busy But Broke = Too Many Things
- Any change creates **guaranteed 20% decrease** before potential upside
- Need at least 20% upside to justify guaranteed 20% downside
- In 18 months, grabbed only **3 items** from his 200+ idea list
- "The biggest problem in your business is lack of execution, not lack of new ideas"
- "You will feel so much relief when you take your to-do list and say 'this is no longer my to-do list'"

### One Priority Framework
- "Priority" from Latin meaning "first" -- you can only have ONE first
- Test: "If 100% certain you could accomplish ONE thing in a year, which gets you furthest?"
- "People do things faster linearly than in parallel"
- Warren Buffett's punch card: 20 investment decisions for life = much better decisions

### Shorts Editor Story
- One editor split across two platforms -- both suffered
- Focused on one platform: **got MORE views** than both combined when split

### The Stuck Lady at HQ
- Kept innovating sales process (the thing she loved)
- Sales wasn't the constraint -- she was already good at it
- Marketing/lead gen was the constraint, had it outsourced and it showed
- "Some stuff is good enough because it's not the constraint"

- Your potential/intelligence/work ethic = size of magnifying glass (how much sun it captures)
- Doesn't matter how big it is if it's not focused on one point
- "The more talented you are, the more opportunities you know you could crush, which just means more things you have to say no to"

### Only 2-3 Entrepreneurial Seasons Left
- "I had a big realization... deep sadness where I realized I could only do a few things throughout the rest of my life"
- Plans acquisition.com to be his final swing: "Do this until I get too old to do it"

### The Dress Test for Business Ideas
- See dress in store -> decide not to buy -> if still thinking about it a month later, go back and buy it
- Same for business ideas: write it down, sleep on it, if still compelling after 2-4 weeks, it might have legs
