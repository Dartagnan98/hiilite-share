## The Psychology of Guarantees

"Every purchase has risk. The seller's job is to assume as much of the buyer's risk as possible."

"The larger the perceived risk, the smaller the pool of buyers. Remove the risk, and you expand the pool dramatically."

---

## Four Types of Guarantees

### Type 1: Unconditional Guarantee

No questions asked, full refund.

Examples:
- "If you don't love it for any reason, we'll refund every penny"
- "30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked"
- "Satisfaction guaranteed or your money back"

**Outsized refund variant:** "If you're not satisfied, we'll refund you DOUBLE" (the prospect thinks: "If they're willing to lose money on unsatisfied customers, they must be VERY confident")

### Type 2: Conditional Guarantee

Refund contingent on specific criteria.

Examples:
- "Do the work for 90 days. If you don't get [result], we'll refund you." (Must complete the program)
- "Attend every session. Follow the meal plan. If you don't lose 20 lbs, full refund."
- "Implement everything we teach for 6 months. If you don't add $100K in revenue, we refund you."

Advantage: filters out non-serious buyers. Only people who won't do the work can't get a refund.

**Specific conditional guarantee subtypes from the book:**

- **Service guarantee:** "We'll keep working with you for free until you get the result"
- **Modified service guarantee:** "If you don't hit [result] by [date], we'll work with you 1-on-1 until you do"
- **Personal service guarantee:** "I will personally coach you until you achieve [result]"
- **Hotel + airfare guarantee:** "If our event doesn't blow your mind, we'll reimburse your hotel and airfare"
- **Wage-payment guarantee:** "We'll pay you $X per hour for every hour you spent with us if you don't get the result"
- **Release of service guarantee:** "If you're not satisfied after 30 days, you can cancel and keep everything"
- **Delayed second payment guarantee:** "Pay half now, and only pay the second half if you see results in 30 days"
- **Credit-based guarantee:** "If it doesn't work, we'll credit you 100% toward any other program we offer"
- **First outcome guarantee:** "We guarantee you'll get your first [client/sale/result] or you don't pay"

### Type 3: Anti-Guarantee

Explicitly NO refunds, NO guarantees.

"All sales final. No refunds. No exceptions."

Sounds counterintuitive, but works for:
- Ultra-premium offers where exclusivity IS the value
- Offers where the market is hot and demand exceeds supply
- When paired with massive social proof and track record

"All sales are final. We've been in business for 10 years. We've never had to offer a refund because nobody has ever asked for one."

### Type 4: Implied/Performance Guarantee

No explicit guarantee stated, but the pricing structure itself IS the guarantee.

Examples:
- **Performance-based pricing:** "You only pay when you get results"
- **Revenue share:** "We take 20% of the revenue we generate for you" (if you make nothing, they pay nothing)
- **Profit share:** "We take 30% of profit above your current baseline" (even less risk than revenue share)

"The pricing structure IS the guarantee. There's nothing to refund because they never paid for something they didn't get."

## Stacking Guarantees

Use MULTIPLE guarantee types together for maximum impact:

Example stack:
1. "We guarantee you'll get 10 new clients in 90 days" (conditional)
2. "If you don't, we'll keep working with you for free until you do" (service)
3. "And if after 6 months you still haven't hit it, we'll refund every penny" (unconditional backstop)

"Each layer of guarantee removes another layer of risk in the prospect's mind."

## The Math of Guarantees (from the book)

Scenario without guarantee:
- 100 prospects, 10% close rate = 10 customers at $2,000 = $20,000
- 0 refunds (no guarantee offered)
- Net revenue: $20,000

Scenario with guarantee:
- 100 prospects, 13% close rate (30% more people say yes because of reduced risk) = 13 customers at $2,000 = $26,000
- 10% refund rate on 13 customers = 1.3 refunds = $2,600 refunded
- Net revenue: $23,400

**Result: 23% more net revenue by offering a guarantee, even after refunds.**

"A guarantee with a 130% close rate increase and double the refund rate still nets you 23% more money. The math ALWAYS works in your favor."

"The question isn't 'can I afford to offer this guarantee?' The question is 'can I afford NOT to?'"

## The Risk Reversal Hierarchy

From least to most powerful:
1. No guarantee (prospect assumes all risk)
2. Conditional guarantee (shared risk)
3. Unconditional guarantee (you assume all risk)
4. Outsized unconditional guarantee (you assume MORE than all risk)
5. Performance-based pricing (risk is structurally impossible)

"The further down this list you go, the more powerful. Performance-based pricing is the ultimate guarantee because there's literally nothing to refund."

### Win Your Money Back Offer: Full Execution Playbook
- Learned from Danny in gym mastermind -- client bet $X they'd lose 20 lbs in 8 weeks
- Hormozi's iterations: (1) switched from 1-on-1 PT to large group, (2) ran ads for it instead of just telling customers
- Three versions: results-based, action-based, or both
- 78% success rate of people hitting their goal
- Over 4,000+ sales and 5 years: only 2 unconditional refund requests
- Real money not from failures -- from upselling winners at week 3 into annual memberships
- "Toothbrush story": external motivation teaches internal motivation

**Execution steps:**
1. Set a goal for customer
2. Define criteria: actions-based, results-based, or both
3. Criteria must be: easy to track, likely to get results, and advertise the business
4. Include mandatory sales appointments (nutrition orientation, progress check-in, transformation feedback)
5. At week 3: sell annual membership ("you realize it's not about 6 weeks")
6. Make everyone a winner at halfway point (credit toward long-term goal)
7. Apply credit over 12 months ($600/12 = $50/mo discount on $200/mo membership)

**Key mechanics:**
- People who got stuff free up front stopped coming; people who paid at discount kept coming
- Upsell should be 5x more expensive than front-end so credit = max 20% discount
- Bump backend price by 20% to account for known credit
- Advertising through criteria: every customer posts 3x/week for 6 weeks = 18 organic posts x 100 people = 1,800 organic posts with tags and before/after photos
