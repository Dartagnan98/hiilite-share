## Bonuses: The Splinter Stack

### The Core Bonus Framework

"A single offer is less valuable than a single offer broken into its component parts and presented as bonuses."

This is the splinter stack: take what you were already going to include and present each piece as a standalone bonus with its own value.

### 11 Rules for Bonuses

1. **Always offer bonuses** -- they increase conversion rate more reliably than any other tactic
2. **Give each bonus a name** -- unnamed bonuses feel generic; named bonuses feel valuable
3. **Give each bonus its own value** -- "$297 value" next to each bonus
4. **Explain why each bonus is valuable** -- don't assume they know
5. **Make bonuses solve a specific problem** -- each one addresses an objection or obstacle
6. **Bonuses should be things people would buy independently** -- if nobody would buy it standalone, it's not a real bonus
7. **Stack bonuses so total value exceeds the price of the core offer** -- this creates the value discrepancy
8. **Use bonuses from other businesses** -- affiliate/partner products that cost you nothing but add real value
9. **Offer exclusive bonuses that money can't buy elsewhere** -- "only available with this program"
10. **Time-limit some bonuses** -- "first 10 people get this bonus" adds urgency
11. **Present bonuses AFTER the core offer and AFTER the price** -- the reveal sequence matters

### The Bonus Math (Advanced Tactic)

From the book, an example of using bonuses to generate pure profit:

- Core offer: $400
- Bonus 1: Partner supplement company's product (they pay you $150 affiliate commission per sign-up)
- Bonus 2: Partner meal delivery service (they pay you $200 affiliate commission per sign-up)

Result:
- Customer pays you $400 for your program
- You also earn $350 in affiliate commissions from the bonuses
- The bonuses cost you $0 but added massive perceived value to the customer
- Total revenue per customer: $750
- Of which $350 is pure profit from bonuses alone

"Your bonuses can literally make you more money than your core offer."

### Using Bonuses to Justify Price

"If someone objects to your price, don't lower the price. Add a bonus."

Instead of dropping from $2,000 to $1,500, say: "I can't lower the price, but let me throw in [bonus worth $797] -- does that feel fair?"

The customer gets more value. You maintain your price. Everyone wins.

## Scarcity: Creating Real Limits

### Why Scarcity Works

"People want what they can't have. Period. Scarcity is the oldest and most powerful motivator in human psychology."

### Three Types of Scarcity

**1. Limited Supply of Seats/Spots**
- "We only have 20 spots available for this program"
- Must be REAL. If you say 20, only take 20.
- Works because: once spots fill, opportunity is gone
- Best for: coaching programs, group programs, done-for-you services
- Service-based methods:
  - **Total business cap:** "We only take 100 clients at a time across our entire company"
  - **Growth rate cap:** "We only onboard 5 new clients per month"
  - **Cohort cap:** "Each cohort is limited to 20 people"

**2. Limited Bonuses**
- "The first 10 people to sign up get [bonus]"
- Bonus disappears after threshold
- Creates a race to act
- Best for: launches, promotions, webinars

**3. Never Available Again**
- "This is a one-time offer that will never be repeated"
- Must be TRUE. If you bring it back, you destroy credibility forever.
- Best for: seasonal promotions, founding member deals, retirement sales

### Honest Scarcity

"The best scarcity is real scarcity. If you manufacture fake scarcity, people will find out, and you will destroy trust permanently."

Ways to create REAL scarcity:
- Actually limit capacity (fire clients to maintain quality)
- Actually limit time (seasonal businesses)
- Actually limit availability (only sell during launches)
- Physical constraints (only so many seats in a room, only so many hours in a day)

## Urgency: Creating Real Deadlines

### Why Urgency Works

"Scarcity is about quantity (how many). Urgency is about time (how long)."

### Four Types of Urgency

**1. Rolling Cohorts**
- "Next cohort starts Monday. After that, the next one isn't for 8 weeks."
- Creates natural urgency without being salesy
- Example: "Our January group is filling up. If you don't get in now, you'll have to wait until March."

**2. Rolling Seasonal**
- Tied to calendar events, seasons, or external deadlines
- "Summer body program starts April 1st -- last day to register is March 28th"
- "New Year transformation -- enrollment closes January 7th"
- Works because the external event creates a real deadline

**3. Pricing or Bonus-Based**
- "Price goes up $500 on Friday"
- "This bonus is only available until midnight tonight"
- Price increases should be REAL and PERMANENT
- Never drop the price back down after raising it

**4. Exploding Opportunity**
- "This opportunity exists right now because of [specific circumstance] and won't exist later"
- Example: "We just had two clients cancel, so we have two open spots. Once they fill, we're closed."
- Must be tied to a REAL circumstance

### Combining Scarcity + Urgency

"Use both. They are different psychological triggers. Scarcity says 'there aren't many.' Urgency says 'there isn't much time.' Together, they create maximum pressure to act."

### Lifetime Upgrades -- When/What Framework

**Bonus "When" (Timing of Delivery):**
- **Immediate**: Given at point of purchase
- **Delayed**: Given after a time period (e.g., 30 days after signup)
- **Milestone-based**: Given when customer hits a specific achievement (e.g., completes onboarding, hits first goal)

**Bonus "What" (Type of Bonus):**
- **One-time**: Single delivery (e.g., a course, a template pack, a consultation)
- **Variable/recurring**: Delivered periodically (e.g., monthly coaching call, quarterly swag box)
- **Lifetime upgrade**: Permanent enhancement to their service level (e.g., "complete this challenge and your account is permanently upgraded to VIP")

**Bonus Examples by Combination:**
1. **Delayed one-time**: "Complete 90 days and receive a free strategy session"
2. **Delayed recurring**: "After 6 months, you get monthly group coaching calls added permanently"
3. **Milestone one-time**: "Hit $10K/month and get a free advanced ads course"
4. **Recurring consumable**: "Every month you stay, you get a new template/swipe file"
5. **Continuous use**: "Members get permanent access to the private community"

### Status Labels for Retention
- Assign escalating status labels based on tenure or achievement
- Customer -> Committed -> Lifer -> VIP -> Elite
- Each label comes with real perks (not just a title)
- People stay longer to maintain or earn the next status
- "Nobody wants to go from VIP back to regular customer"

### Combine Bonuses Guidance
- Stack "when" and "what" combinations to create layered incentive structure
- Front-load immediate bonuses (reduces buyer's remorse)
- Sprinkle milestone bonuses throughout the journey (creates "next level" anticipation)
- Add lifetime upgrades at key churn points (gives reason to stay past the danger zone)
