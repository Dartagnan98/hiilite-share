## 1. GYMS & FITNESS

### Revenue & Pricing
- Gym Launch first gym: $0 for 6 weeks ("free 6-week challenge"), then $500 upfront at end
- Gym Launch generated $400K cash in first 7 months from one location
- Gym Launch revenue trajectory: $0 → $400K (7 months) → $4.4M (year 2) → $6.8M (year 3) → $28M (year 4) → $36M (year 5, sold for $46.2M)
- Gym membership: $99/month → $200/month after repositioning
- Challenge offer: $500 upfront (free 6-week challenge, pay at end if satisfied)
- Annual membership rollover: credit $500 challenge fee toward annual at $200/month
- Supplement upsell: $200 average per customer, 80% take rate (12 of 15)
- Total revenue from 100 leads (Hormozi model): $24,900 vs competitor's $5,325 (4.7x more)
- Max spend per lead (Hormozi): $249 vs competitor's $53

### Churn & Retention
- Average gym churn: 7-10% monthly
- Top 2% gym churn: 3% monthly
- Churn from 10% to 8% = 25% more LTV
- Churn from 10% to 5% = doubles LTV
- 4-week billing cycle (not monthly): 13 billing periods/year vs 12 = 8.3% revenue increase
- Involuntary churn (failed payments): 20-40% of total churn in most gyms
- Credit card expiry: ~2% of cards expire each month
- Dunning sequence: text → email → call → letter over 7-14 days
- Five retention strategies for top 2% gyms: reactivation offers, pause instead of cancel, milestone rewards, community events, personal check-ins

### Ad Metrics
- Gym leads cost: $5-15/lead on Meta (free challenge offer)
- Competitor's model: $21 for 21-day trial, 25% conversion of leads
- Hormozi's model: free 6-week challenge, 15% conversion at $500
- COVID pivot: $0 ad spend, 4,600+ leads in one weekend using "free daily workout" lead magnet
- Pre-sale model: 500 leads before opening, convert 15-20% on day 1

### Close Rates
- Competitor: 25% close on $21 trial, 35% convert to $99/month membership
- Hormozi: 15% close on $500 challenge (higher ticket = lower % but more revenue)
- Prepay discount at week 6: 20% buy annual

### Margins
- Target gross margin: 80%+ on memberships
- Fulfillment cost should be <20% of revenue
- Supplement margins: 60-80%

### Staffing
- First hire: salesperson (not trainer)
- Gym can run with 2-3 staff at $10-20K/month revenue
- Trainer cost: $15-25/hour or revenue share

## 2. SaaS / SOFTWARE

### Revenue & Pricing
- school.com: $99/month base tier
- Tier structure: $99/mo (School) → $5K (L1) → ~$35K (L2) → $135K (L3) → Portfolio (equity)
- Each tier 5-10x the previous
- Expect 20% take rate per upsell tier
- 1,000 at $10/mo ($10K) + 200 at $100/mo ($20K) + 40 at $1,000/mo ($40K) + 8 at $10,000/mo ($80K) = $150K total from $10K base
- 15x revenue from adding 3 tiers to a base product

### Churn & Retention
- SaaS benchmark churn: 3-5% monthly for SMB, <2% for enterprise
- Net negative churn: expansion revenue from existing customers exceeds lost revenue from churned customers
- Involuntary churn (failed payments): 20-40% of total churn
- Annual billing reduces churn by 2-3x vs monthly

### Margins
- SaaS gross margins: 80-90% target
- Software is highest-leverage business: zero marginal cost of replication

## 3. CONSULTING / COACHING

### Revenue & Pricing
- Hormozi's first consulting: $0 (asked clients to donate $500-$1,000 to charity)
- First 13 clients free, then transitioned to paid
- Career coaching (Hotline example): $30K ask, downsell to $15K down + $15K when kid gets job
- Workshop: $4K, credited toward $30K program
- Coaching call cadence: pitch parents at "halfway call" (week 2-3)
- 56x consulting story: $500 investment in consulting → client made $28,000 return
- Value-based pricing: same service, different price based on client revenue
- If you charge $2K and want to go to $3K: anchor at $4K (skip-and-anchor method)

### Close Rates
- Workshop to high-ticket: 30-50% conversion
- Webinar to call: 5-15% booking rate
- Call to close: 20-40% depending on price point
- "Halfway call" pitch to parents: high conversion because feels like phase 2, not a new sale

### Margins
- Consulting gross margins: 80-90% (time is primary cost)
- Group coaching: 90%+ margins at scale
- 1-on-1 coaching: 70-80% margins

### Ad Metrics
- Webinar registration: $5-20/lead
- Application funnel: $20-50/qualified application
- Book funnel: $5-15/lead, nurture to high-ticket

## 4. AGENCIES

### Revenue & Pricing
- your agency context: Meta ads agency for local businesses
- Agency pricing range: $1K-$5K/month retainer typical for SMB
- Premium positioning: 5-10x market rate once you have proof
- Three-phase migration: Free → Market rate → Premium (each phase funds credibility for next)
- Value-based pricing: charge % of value delivered, not hours worked
- "Never compete on price. If the only difference between you and a competitor is price, you've already lost."

### Churn & Retention
- Agency churn benchmark: 5-10% monthly for SMB clients
- Best agencies: <3% monthly churn
- Contracts: 3-6 month minimum commitments reduce churn
- Results-based retention: clients who see measurable ROI stay 3-5x longer

### Margins
- Agency gross margins: 50-70% typical
- Target: 80%+ by productizing delivery
- Fulfillment cost per client should be <30% of retainer

## 5. E-COMMERCE / PRODUCT

### Revenue & Pricing
- Remove small option: only offer medium and large (power of default) → 20-30% sales increase
- Present highest-margin items first in purchase flow
- Anchor upsell: show item at 5-10x price first, then present main offer
- Different wallets: gym membership wallet, supplement wallet, apparel wallet, food wallet (each separate buying decision)
- Shipping threshold: "free shipping over $X" increases AOV 15-25%

### Ad Metrics
- E-commerce Meta ads: $10-30 CPM typical
- Target ROAS: 3:1 minimum for cold traffic, 5:1+ for retargeting
- Creative testing: 3-5 new creatives per week minimum
- Creative fatigue signals: CTR drops 20%+ over 2-week period, frequency >3

### Margins
- Product margins: 60-80% gross margin target (DTC)
- Below 50% gross margin = very hard to scale profitably with paid ads

## 10. GENERAL BENCHMARKS (All Industries)

### Show Rates
- Webinar show rate: 25-40% of registrants
- Sales call show rate: 60-80% target
- With confirmation sequence (text + email + call): 80%+ show rate
- Same-day booking: highest show rate
- 48+ hours out: show rate drops 20-30%

### Close Rates by Price Point
- Under $500: 40-60% close rate target
- $500-$2,000: 30-50%
- $2,000-$5,000: 20-40%
- $5,000-$10,000: 15-30%
- $10,000+: 10-25%
- Close rate diagnostics: 60-80% = 3-5x price increase available; 50-60% = 2-3x available; 30-50% = appropriately priced; Below 30% = selling skill issue

### Ad Spend Rules
- Rule of 100: <$100/day spend = do 100 manual outreaches/day instead
- Three levels of ad spending: (1) spend your own money, (2) spend revenue from ads, (3) CFA -- Client-Financed Acquisition (spend customer money before ad bill is due)
- CFA: collect payment from customers before your ad bill is due → infinite scaling
- 70/20/10 budget rule: 70% proven campaigns, 20% testing variations, 10% wild experiments
- Kill rule: 3x target CPA with no conversions = kill the ad
- Scale rule: at target CPA for 3+ days = increase budget 20%
- Creative machine: 3-5 new variations/week minimum
- Meta CPMs: $10-30 typical (varies by niche and season)
- Cold email: $0.10-$0.50/lead acquired
- Cold calling: $2-$5/lead acquired via VA ($3-$5/hour VAs)

### LTV:CAC Ratios
- Minimum viable: 3:1 LTV:CAC
- Good: 5:1
- Great: 10:1+
- "The business that can spend the most to acquire a customer wins"
- LTV by automation level: Manual = 1x, Semi-automated = 2-3x, Fully automated = 5-10x
- Back-of-napkin LTV: Revenue per month × Average lifespan in months × Gross margin %

### Margin Targets
- 80% gross margin = healthy business (Hormozi's universal rule)
- Below 60% = "you have a margin problem, not a sales problem"
- Gross Margin Fix Playbook: raise prices, reduce fulfillment cost, change delivery model (1-on-1 → group → digital)
- "If you can't get to 80% gross margins, you don't have a real business, you have a job"
- Net margins: 20-30% is good, 30-40% is great, 40%+ is exceptional

### Churn Benchmarks
- "A 10% monthly churn means you replace your entire customer base every 10 months"
- Churn from 10% → 5% = doubles LTV
- Churn from 10% → 8% = 25% more LTV
- Annual billing reduces churn 2-3x vs monthly
- 4-week billing: 13 cycles/year vs 12 = 8.3% more revenue
- Involuntary churn: 20-40% of all churn (fix with dunning sequences, card updaters)
- Processing fee hack: charge 3% for credit card, waive for ACH → moves 30-40% to ACH, reduces failed payments

### Content Metrics
- 1.5 million+ impressions/month across platforms (Hormozi's content operation)
- 8-figure content team: 12 people, $100K+/month budget
- Video content: 2-3 long-form/week + 7-14 short-form/week
- Podcast: 160+ episodes before meaningful traction
- LinkedIn organic: highest B2B engagement per impression
- Content ROI timeline: 6-12 months before meaningful attribution
- "Volume negates luck" -- Rule of 100 applied to content

### Team & Hiring Numbers
- First hire: someone who does what you do (not an assistant)
- Employee ROI rule: each employee should generate 3-5x their compensation
- A-player cost: 25-50% more than average, but produce 3-10x more
- A-player arbitrage: "Pay top of market, get 10x output, net 5x more profitable"
- Hiring pipeline: 100 applicants → 10 interviews → 3 trials → 1 hire
- VA cost: $3-$5/hour for cold calling, data entry, appointment setting
- Sales rep ramp: 30-90 days to full productivity
- Salesperson income: top performers $150K-$300K+/year (base + commission)
- Commission structures: 10-20% of cash collected typical
- Manager span of control: 5-7 direct reports max
- "Hire slow, fire fast" -- but actually fire faster than you think

### Pricing Psychology
- Price-to-value ratio target: 10:1 minimum (deliver 10x perceived value of price)
- "If you are not embarrassed by your price, you're not charging enough"
- Gasp test: "If you didn't get a gasp from the price, you didn't go high enough"
- Anchor must be 5-10x the price of main offer (not 20% more)
- Before stating price: "Before I tell you the price, it's super expensive"
- Wine experiment: same wine rated differently based on price label
- 50% price increase on health company → close rates went UP
- "They were so cheap compared to the promise that people didn't even believe it worked"
- 1 out of 10 buying 10x product = doubles your business
- Price tiers should match different buying personas: $100/$200/$1,000 (different people), NOT $100/$110/$120 (same person)
- A certain percentage of population ALWAYS buys the most expensive thing
- Layaway: "How much can you afford?" → unlimited payment plans, people pay for speed
- "People who pay, pay attention" -- higher price = better compliance = better results = more referrals

### Conversion Benchmarks by Format
- VSL (Video Sales Letter): 10-20% opt-in to watch, 2-5% purchase
- Webinar: 25-40% show rate, 5-15% purchase of attendees
- Challenge/bootcamp: 30-50% completion, 15-30% purchase
- Application funnel: 30-50% apply, 60-80% show for call, 20-40% close
- Free + shipping: 5-15% of cold traffic converts
- Tripwire ($1-$47): 3-8% cold traffic conversion

### Revenue Milestones & Scaling
- $0-$100K: founder does everything, prove the offer works
- $100K-$1M: hire first salesperson, systematize fulfillment
- $1M-$3M: build a team, document processes
- $3M-$10M: hire managers, remove yourself from delivery
- $10M+: CEO role only -- strategy, capital allocation, culture
- "Everyone has a $10M business in them. Not everyone has a $100M business."
- Enterprise value multiples: services 3-5x EBITDA, SaaS 8-15x ARR, marketplace/platform 15-30x

### The Ferrari Test
- "If selling a brand new Ferrari for $10K, people would call friends, family, max credit cards, take loans"
- "The reason someone is not buying is simply because they do not understand the value"
- "Everyone can afford it if they believe the value is there"

### Tax & Wealth Building
- S-Corp election: save 15.3% self-employment tax on distributions above reasonable salary
- Reasonable salary: $50-80K for most small business owners
- Tax rate effective: 25-35% for most entrepreneurs
- "Pay yourself first" -- owner's draw before expenses
- Real estate depreciation: write off building value over 27.5 years (residential) or 39 years (commercial)
- Cost segregation study: accelerate depreciation to year 1-5 on components

### Partnership Structure
- 51/49 splits: one person must be the tiebreaker
- Partnerships fail from misaligned expectations, not bad intentions
- Vesting schedules: 4-year vest with 1-year cliff standard
- "The best partnership is one where each person thinks they're getting the better deal"
