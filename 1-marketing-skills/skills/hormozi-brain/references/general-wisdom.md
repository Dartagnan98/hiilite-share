## BUSINESS SELECTION CRITERIA

### The Four Business Shapes
1. **E-commerce** -- Fast early growth, hits breaking points (cash, traffic, distribution). Unlocks from supply chain or distribution breakthroughs.
2. **Service businesses** -- Low startup cost, no brand or product foundation needed. Best for starting out. Trade time for money initially, then build leverage.
3. **Software/SaaS** -- Long build phase, but once built scales without proportional cost increases.
4. **Content/media** -- Years of audience building precede monetization.

### The Perfect Business Model (Buffett/Hormozi Synthesis)
Mnemonic: **UNIQUE EXPENSIVE STICKY AIR**
- **Unique** -- Big competitive moat, can't be replicated easily. Brand = compounding advantage.
- **Expensive** -- Sells for a dollar, costs a penny. 99% gross margin ideal. Difference between 90% and 95% gross margin doubles profitability.
- **Sticky** -- Habit-forming. Customers come back repeatedly. High lifetime value.
- **Air** -- Costs almost nothing to produce/deliver.

The operator should have: Risk aversion ("only have to get rich once"), Long-term thinking (family-owned businesses think in generations), Unimpeachable character (reputation compounds over time).

### The Three Ps for Picking a Business
1. **Pain** -- Something you went through personally and overcame
2. **Profession** -- What you do/did for your day job
3. **Passion** -- The stuff you can't get enough of

Critical belief: You don't need to pick perfectly first time. You're looking for your "best bad idea" that you make less wrong over time.

### Narrowing the Avatar
Use 3+ descriptors: Age, Gender, Profession, Problems/pains, Interests/passions.
"35-year-old male accountants who are bored at their jobs."
**The narrower you go, the more you can charge.** Time management PDF = $19. Time management for outbound sales reps in garden/power tools = $10,000.

### LTV-to-CAC Ratio Rules
- All 3 automated (lead gen, sales, delivery): **3:1 minimum**
- 2 of 3 automated: **6:1 minimum**
- 1 of 3 automated: **9:1 minimum**
- None automated: **12:1 minimum**

The business that can spend more to acquire a customer than competitors wins. Methods expire. Models endure.

### Ways to Increase LTV
1. Raise the price
2. Decrease costs of delivery
3. Add upsells (higher quality/more of same)
4. Add downsells (capture otherwise-lost buyers)
5. Add cross-sells (sell something different)
6. Add financing (pull cash forward)
7. Change payment terms (collect upfront)
8. Add warranties/add-ons

## THE VALUE EQUATION
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)

Increase the top, decrease the bottom. If bottom goes to zero, value is infinite. The biggest companies won by decreasing the bottom (Amazon = instant delivery, Netflix = zero effort, Uber = no wait time).

## STARTING FROM ZERO / BROKE PLAYBOOKS

### The Avante Letter
1. Cut ALL costs. Food and shelter only.
2. Increase income. Apply to better-paying jobs, drive Uber.
3. Learn to sell. Start with selling someone else's stuff.
4. Invest 4 hours/day in learning one skill.
5. Be the best applicant ever. Personalize every application. Volume negates luck.
6. When you get the job, be Kobe. 3x the work of the top performer.

### Foundation Sequence
1. **My fault.** Own everything. Whatever you cast blame to is where you cast power to.
2. **Use what you have.** Not what you wish you had. Anger, shame, pain are fuel.
3. **Cut the information diet.** Stop listening to broke people about getting rich.
4. **Experiment and get XP.** Failed experiments aren't losses -- they're experience points.
5. **Invest excess in making more.** Don't save your way out of poverty.

### First $100K Playbook
1. When poor, barter. Trade what you can offer for what you need.
2. Start with Facebook ads even on tiny budget. $100/day can self-fund growth.
3. Nonprofit/free-first model to build proof.
4. $5K-to-$100K formula: $5K in bank, $100/day ads, close 2/10 at $600 = $1,200/day.

### Acquiring a Business with No Money
- Find businesses doing $250K+/year profit where owner is tired
- Agree on price first, THEN negotiate terms
- Get seller financing for 75% of the deal
- SBA loan for remainder, personal savings as down payment

## BRAND BUILDING FRAMEWORKS

### What Branding Actually Is
**Branding = deliberate pairing of things through an outcome.**
Good branding = pairing your business with good outcomes for ideal customers.

### Brand as Bouquet
Brand elements are like flowers. Individually just flowers. Assembled deliberately = bouquet. Random pairings hurt, curated pairings strengthen.

### Three Brand Metrics
1. **Influence** -- How likely to change behavior when seen?
2. **Direction** -- Toward or away from you?
3. **Reach** -- How many people?

### Brand Makes Money Through
1. Premium pricing (turn $5 commodity into $60 branded product)
2. Better ad performance (6x more clicks on branded content)
3. Customer loyalty (buy more, resist competitors)

## SCALING DECISION FRAMEWORKS

### The Six Problems Under $10M
1. Serve too many avatars -- niche down
2. Undercharging -- raise prices, improve offer
3. No repeatable acquisition -- pick ONE channel, master it
4. Key man risk -- build redundancy
5. No systems/SOPs -- document everything
6. Over-diversification -- cut to the one that works, double down

### One Avatar, One Channel, One Product (Up to $1M)
Spreading across multiple is the fastest way to stay small.

### Supply vs. Demand Constraint
First question: Is this supply constrained or demand constrained?
If demand: new channel (hard), lower CAC (through CRO/offer), spend more (requires lower CAC first).

### "More, Then Better, Then New"
When something works: do MORE first, make it BETTER second, try NEW third.
Most entrepreneurs do the opposite.

### Speed Over Everything
Fast beats free. (DMV example -- people pay $50 to avoid free government service.)

## WHEN TO QUIT VS. PERSIST

### Five Stages of Opportunity Hopping
1. Uninformed Optimism
2. Informed Pessimism
3. Valley of Despair
4. Informed Optimism
5. Achievement

Most cycle 1-3 repeatedly. Never reach stage 4 because they quit at the valley.

### Channel/Model Distinction
Don't quit the business because a METHOD stopped working. Methods expire. Rebuild on a better MODEL. Quit when the fundamental model is broken.

## MINDSET PRINCIPLES

### Volume Negates Luck
Apply to 1,000 jobs. Send 100 emails. You either learn or you win.

### The Rejection Asymmetry
Rejection costs nothing. 100 nos put you no worse off than zero asks, but massively better in skill.

### Sadness, Anxiety, Disappointment
- Sadness = Perceived lack of options. Solve with knowledge.
- Anxiety = Many options, no priorities. Solve with a decision.
- Disappointment = Unmet expectation. Change expectations or reality.

### Confidence Comes from the Past
Domain specific. Built by doing the thing enough times you get bored. "If your plan requires luck, it's a bad plan."

### Identity = Actions
"We are what we repeatedly do." Do what the future version of you does, and you ARE that person. The proof just takes time.

### The Pottery Class Parable
Group A: one perfect pot. Group B: as many pots as possible. Group B produced more AND better pots. Volume produces quality.

## DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORKS

### Inversion (The Loser List)
"What would GUARANTEE I lose?" List everything. Flip each item = your playbook.

### The Hypothetical Extreme
Take any strategy to logical extreme. If the extreme works, the direction is right -- just need volume.

### "If Only One Thing Had to Be True"
Reduces analysis paralysis to a single testable hypothesis.

### Operationalize Everything
"Be patient" is useless. "Figure out what to do in the meantime" is actionable.

### Delta Over Square
Triangles (change) win. Squares (stay the same) lose. Intelligence = rate of learning.

### Winners Focus on Winning, Losers Focus on Winners
Competitors who copy you need you. Be afraid when people STOP copying.

## COUNTER-INTUITIVE PRINCIPLES (Deep Pass)

### Passion = Suffering (Etymology)
Latin "passio" = suffering. First usage: Passion of Christ (crucifixion). 95% of daily work won't be "your passion" even in a passion-based business. "You're using the excuse of a lack of passion to disguise your inability to handle difficulty." Most miserable year of his life: first year owning a gym (the thing he was "passionate" about).

### Joy vs Happiness
"Joy comes from voluntary suffering for a purpose. Happiness comes from the absence of suffering." Casino analogy: house always wins (entropy). Play long enough that variance works in your favor on things you control.

### Happiness Optimization Makes You Unhappy
"People who want to be happy treat it like 'I want to eat so good I'm never hungry again.'" "All the people that I know who obsess about happiness are the least happy people." His alternative: tracked good days vs bad days over 3-4 years, minimized bad-day activities, maximized good-day activities.

### Profits Are Unnatural
Billionaire: "A business left to its own devices will pretty much break even." Everyone has same access to resources, skill, and talent. Profits require willpower and something above and beyond. Creating profits is the sole responsibility of the CEO.

### Insurance = Ultimate Business Model
Gets paid far before ever having to do anything. May never have to fulfill at all. Reverse of depreciation: collects money that appreciates over time. Oldest companies (180-300+ years) are insurance companies. Predate the tax code.

### The Money Ladder (Who Gets Paid When)
- Employee: work first, paid 2-4 weeks later
- Self-employed: split (half now, half on delivery)
- Professional (doctor): paid first, then work
- Banks: preferred creditor, paid first if things go wrong
- Insurance: paid the whole time, may never deliver
- God/Franchisors: 10% off the top, forever
- Lesson: restructure your business to be higher on the ladder

### Volatility Is Not Risk
Insurance company: loses money 1 out of 9 years (catastrophe years), makes a lot the other 8. Sales is highly volatile but LOW risk -- salespeople generate revenue, making them the most secure employees. "If you want to decrease the volatility in your life, decrease your expenses to your salary."

### Wealth Is a Ratio, Not a Number
"Wealth is a ratio between how much you spend versus how much you make." Salesperson making $140K living on $25K = 6x income-to-expense ratio. "A salesperson making $200K/year owns a million-dollar business. It's the same thing." (assuming 20% net margins)

### Spending Is a Skill
Highest earners at his company were living paycheck to paycheck. "The people who were the wealthiest were not the highest earners." People spending $600/month car lease = 6 of 22 working days just for the car. "Do you want to look rich or do you want to be rich?"

### The Favor Perception Gap
Mega meta-analysis: 40,000+ studies. Immediately after a favor: recipient values it highest, doer values it lowest. Over time: recipient's perceived value declines, doer's perceived value increases. Practical: get testimonials IMMEDIATELY after the result.

### Content Is Input, Not Output
"Content is not the output, it's the input. The output is the audience. The audience is what compounds." Must consistently create new "telephone poles" of value delivery.

### Confidence Is a Prediction, Not a Feeling
"Confidence is a prediction -- what you think is going to happen will happen." "How do you increase the likelihood? Have it have happened already." Don't build confidence, build evidence. "Walk it before you talk it" replaces "fake it till you make it."

### Delayed Gratification Compounds
Warren Buffett: "The first $100K is a bitch." $500/month at age 20 > $5,000/month at age 45 (compounding). Every $250/month saved = roughly $1M at retirement. "$200 invested at age 25 = $6,000+ at age 65."

### Balance Is a Myth
"You don't win at marriage. The point is to stay married. You don't win at business. The point is to stay in business." Finite vs Infinite games. Kobe: "I just love playing to the best of my ability."

### Cost of Switching Businesses
If you're in year 3, switching to a "faster" opportunity puts you at year 1 -- still behind at year 4-5. Growth compounds: year 5 growth > year 1 growth. "Sticking with it IS how you get the head start."

### Price vs Cost
"Is it really a cost if you spend $200 and get nothing? Is that better than spending $1,000 and getting 10x back?" "Price is what you pay, value is what you get." Amazon stock is more expensive than Bank of America stock -- "buy nice or buy twice."

### Cheap vs Deals
Rich people price from high to low -- look at most expensive stuff first. "Great lawyers are expensive. Cheap lawyers cost a fortune." Bottom 50% of Americans hold $2 out of every $100 of total wealth. Top 1% holds $30 out of $100.

### Suffering Is a Fixed Cost
Poverty is tough. Growth is tough. Employee is tough. Rich is tough. "What if all three paths have the same amount of suffering? Pick the biggest reward." "The goal is to reframe reality so that bad things are good, not to only experience good things."

## MENTAL TOUGHNESS MODEL

Written after his mother's death:
1. **Tolerance**: withstand current pain
2. **Fortitude**: endure sustained difficulty
3. **Resilience**: bounce back from setbacks
4. **Adaptability**: change approach when needed

### Resilience vs Toughness vs Amplitude
- Toughness = how much before you decrease (threshold)
- Resilience = how fast you return to baseline (recovery speed)
- Third vector = how low do you go (amplitude)
- Ideal: high threshold, low amplitude, fast recovery

## TAX & WEALTH STRATEGY (Deep Pass)

### Ultra-Wealthy Tax Mindset
94% of Forbes 100 growth came from appreciation of equities, not income. At 40x multiples: $2M EBITDA increase = $80M net worth increase. Richest people live in highest-tax states because they understand this.

### Section 280A (Augusta Rule)
Rent personal home to business for up to 14 days/year. Income is TAX-FREE personally + business gets deduction. Fair market rate: $500-$10,000/day. If you built a studio: 14 days x $10K = $140K tax-free.

### Four-Lens Investment Framework
Evaluate every deal through: (1) Yield, (2) Capital Preservation, (3) Tax Advantage, (4) Equity Growth.

### When to Start Investing
Don't invest until money flows in FASTER than you can spend on skill acquisition. $25K, $50K, $250K, $500K in the bank? Spend it ALL on skills. "Somebody who has fewer skills needs more money."

### The 85-Year-Old Self Test
"Probably the most useful decision-making frame I have ever made." Envision yourself at 85, a decabillionaire. Would that person care about saving 30% on taxes? "Don't sacrifice anything for me. I'm old. You're young. Enjoy."

## COUNTER-INTUITIVE INSIGHTS (Batch 7)

### Removing Small Cups Increased Sales 20-30%
- Frozen yogurt stores removed small cups, kept only medium and large
- Power of the default option: people fill the bigger cup because "I guess this is standard"
- Small amount of yogurt in a bucket-sized cup "just looks weird" so people fill more
- Same principle as fast food: "Do you want a medium or a large?" (skip the small option)

### Self-Serve Pricing Makes Customers Blame Themselves
- When customers control their own portion/usage, they blame themselves for overspending, not you
- Same psychology as old mobile minutes/text message billing

### Tax Shelters Cost MORE Than They Save
- Three hidden costs: discovery/conversion, upkeep, unwinding/fines
- Friend in Puerto Rico: 104 travel days/year. "Could you make 30% more if you didn't travel 104 days?" He hadn't thought about it that way
- Friend missed baby's birth for tax residency days
- IRS is 3-5 years behind. Most loopholes get unwound. Then you pay fines + interest + legal costs

### "They Can't Afford It" Is Always Wrong
- Ferrari test: If selling a brand new Ferrari for $10K, people would call friends, family, max credit cards, take loans
- "The reason someone is not buying is simply because they do not understand the value"
- "Everyone can afford it if they believe the value is there"

### Being Advanced Means Never Skipping the Basics
- "There's tons of businesses you can absolutely demolish who are making lots of money just by doing the basics"
- Most entrepreneurs compete against people who don't even think about execution

### Volume Negates Luck AND Skill (Expanded)
- Getting your first deal on day one can MESS YOU UP more than grinding for months, because you don't learn and you've used up one of the easy wins early
- "Eventually it will be unreasonable for you to suck anymore"

- "Beat 99% by being willing to endure pain and uncertainty for longer"

### "Nobody Knows Who You Are" Is an Advantage

### Don't Train Salespeople on the Product
- "When you're training sales people, you don't train them on the product. You train them on the prospect."
- "He might know the product perfectly but he just said the guy's problem was not the problem"

### Insurance Companies Are Best Risk Managers in History
