# 12 Internal Advertising / Copy Hacks
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Leads + 383 video transcripts)

## The 12 Hacks

### 1. Headlines Come First
- 80 cents of your ad dollar is in the headline. Curiosity is king, different is ideal.
- A banger headline can 3-5x a control.
- Test headlines from organic content and competitors. Steal from different industries.
- Formula: "How to [good thing] without [bad thing] even if [biggest fear]"

### 2. Say What Only You Can Say
- Proof beats promise. Competitors can copy your offer but not your proof. Document everything.

### 3. Always Call Out Who You're Looking For (and Who You're NOT)
- "Only for people trying to lose 30+ lbs." Polarize. You advertise to ONE person. Specificity creates compelling copy.
- **Levels of awareness**: Unaware -> Problem Aware -> Solution Aware -> Product Aware -> Most Aware (know you and your product)

### 4. Always Have a Reason Why
- The word "because" is one of the most persuasive words. Any reason > no reason.
- Fraternity party planner: any excuse works.

### 5. Damaging Admissions
- Admit flaws upfront. "Statement A, BUT statement B" -- people believe what comes after "but."
- "Our gym has terrible parking and the AC is on and off, BUT our sessions are more fun than everyone else's." Now they believe you.
- Eminem strategy: own all your flaws so opponents have nothing left.
- "What you sacrifice in promise, you gain in trust. Trust trumps promise."

### 6. Show Don't Tell
- Don't say "get more sales." Describe: "You're running out of order forms because customers are reading credit cards to you over the phone too fast."
- Describe the MOMENTS of the outcome.

### 7. Tie Benefits to Status
- Not just "fast and easy cooking" but "so fast your friends will wonder how you found time to be fit AND cook for your family."
- Think: Who gives them status? Family, friends, colleagues, rivals? Past, present, future.

### 8. Use Urgency and Scarcity
- Scarcity = quantity (only 100 spots). Urgency = time (ends midnight).
- Make it LEGITIMATE. Say no when sold out so next time they buy first.

### 9. Implied Authority
- "As seen on...", "Best [X] in [City]", "We've helped 5,000 gyms over 7 years."
- Longevity itself is authority. Awards matter even if obscure.

### 10. Always Have a PS Statement
- 2nd most-read part of any written copy (after headline). PPS = 3rd most-read.
- Use for: joke/reward, CTA, recap of main points, or "who this is NOT for" disclaimer.

### 11. Clear CTA
- Tell them exactly what to do next and what will happen. "Click the button. On the next page, fill your info. Select a time."
- Make promises, keep promises = gain influence (Martha Stewart effect).
- Hormozi named his company acquisition.com so the name IS the CTA.

### 12. 3rd Grade Reading Level
- Most copy doesn't convert because people pause to understand.
- Fancy = friction, simple = sales. Short sentences, small words, big promises.
- Fewer adverbs, better verbs. Run through a free reading level tool.

### Bonus #13: Use Humor Whenever Possible
- Entertainment goes broad, education goes deep.
- Old Spice = highest-converting ad of all time.
- Test humor like comedians test material.

## Data-Driven Marketing (Slicing Statistics for Campaigns)

### The Three Wheels
1. **What percentage of clients** achieve the outcome (top %, bottom %, median, average, "1 in X", fractions)
2. **What is the outcome** (revenue increase, absolute $, percentage increase, relative increase like "pays off 9 months of rent")
3. **Over what time duration** (first 30 days, first year, every X days/weeks)

### Ways to Express the Same Stat
- Percentage: "87% success rate"
- Fraction: "1 in 5.5 gyms is a million-dollar gym"
- Inverted: "4.5 out of 5.5 gyms hit X"
- Frequency: "Every 7.7 days we create another seven-figure gym"
- Absolute: "Average gym adds $15,500 in first 2 weeks"
- Relative: "Average gym pays off 9 months of rent in first 14 days"

### Why This Works
- Data has absolute conviction (it's just true)
- Most competitors don't track their own data
- You can base ENTIRE campaigns around a single compelling statistic
- Mix and match the three wheels to find the most compelling angle

## Landing Page Optimization

### The One Process
- Every week: one split test. Force prioritization to the single highest-impact change.
- 52 tests/year = massive compounding improvements.

### What to Test (Priority Order)
1. **Headline** (above the fold -- biggest impact)
2. **Image/visual** under the headline
3. **Sub-headline**
- 90% of all tests should be headline or image. That's it.
- Everything above the fold is where you're selling them on staying. Biggest returns happen there.

### Process
- Monday marketing meeting: What was last week's test? What won? Did we beat the control? If not, try another test.
- Google "statistical significance calculator" to validate results.

## More Better New Framework

### More (Do More of What Already Works)
- Ask: "What's stopping us from doing more?" Answer is often: nothing.
- If doing 100 cold calls/day, why not 1,000?
- If spending $1K/day on ads, why not $10K?
- For businesses under $1M profit: just do MORE first. Not enough volume for percentage improvements to matter.

### Better (Improve What You're Doing)
- The only way to get better: TESTING.
- **Test one thing per week per platform.** Four reasons:
  1. Multiple simultaneous tests = you never know what worked
  2. Split tests affect each other (confounding variables)
  3. Constraining to one forces you to prioritize the highest-impact test
  4. Must run long enough for statistical significance
- 52 improvements per year from weekly testing.
- **Prioritize front to back**: Headlines, first 5 seconds, CTRs (front of funnel) have the biggest percentage improvement potential.
- The constraint = the step with the largest return for the smallest improvement.

### Example of Better
- 30% opt-in -> 5% apply -> 50% schedule
- Adding 5% to each: 35% opt-in (16% lift), 10% apply (100% lift!), 55% schedule (10% lift)
- Attack the 5% apply rate first -- doubling it would 2x throughput
- Is it easier to go from 5% to 20% apply rate than to get 4x more traffic? Probably yes.

### New (Expand to New Places/Methods)
- Only after exhausting More and Better.
- Order of "new" from easiest to hardest:
  1. **New placement** on same platform (Stories if already doing Feed)
  2. **New platform** with similar media type (short video on TikTok -> short video on Reels)
  3. **New platform** with different media type (YouTube videos -> Twitter text)
  4. **New Core Four method** entirely (content -> cold outreach team)
- Change as little as possible. Half steps, not pendulum swings.
- For businesses over $1M: flip to Better -> More -> New (improvements have big impact at scale).
