# Referral Systems & Employees as Lead Getters
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Leads + 383 video transcripts)

## Core Principle
If you solve someone's problem well, they become a walking advertisement. Referrals are the highest-leverage lead getting method.

## Getting More Referrals
- Ask at the point of peak satisfaction (right after they get a result)
- Ask specifically: "Who do you know that's also dealing with [problem]?"
- Make it easy to refer (give them a link, a card, a script)
- Reward referrals (discounts, bonuses, cash, recognition)

## Employees as Lead Getters

### Why Employees Make You Wealthy
- Same business, same $2M income:
  - Owner does everything = net worth is cash in bank
  - Owner works 10-20 hrs/week with team = net worth is cash + $10M+ in equity
- A business that runs without you is sellable. One that requires you is a high-paying job.

### Wrong Beliefs to Replace
- "If you want it done right, do it yourself" -> "Get someone to spend ALL their time on it"
- "No one can do it like me" -> "If I can do it, someone can do it BETTER"
- "I'm irreplaceable" -> "Everyone is replaceable, especially me"

### The Internal Core Four (Getting Employee Leads)
Same framework, different nomenclature:
- Warm Outreach = Asking your network for referrals
- Cold Outreach = Headhunting/recruiting
- Posting Content = Job postings on personal/business pages
- Paid Ads = Promoted job postings

### Lead Getters for Employees
- Customer referrals = Employee referrals
- Affiliates = Community owners (editors, salespeople)
- Employees getting leads = In-house recruiters
- Agencies = Staffing firms, paid headhunters

### Training: Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate
1. **Document**: Do the thing yourself, make a checklist (video yourself doing it)
2. **Demonstrate**: Do it in front of new employee
3. **Duplicate**: They do it in front of you, you give feedback
- Short windows to prove themselves. Below expectations at 3 weeks = cut them.
- Give the short window for entry-level; look for GRIT as the meta-skill.

### Calculating Employee ROI
- Total payroll / total engaged leads = cost per engaged lead
- Example: $100K/month payroll, 1,000 engaged leads = $100/lead
- 10 leads per customer = $1,000 CAC
- If LTV is 4:1 on that = profitable
- If CAC is 3x+ above industry average, diagnose: Are engaged leads qualified? (No = advertising problem) Are they qualified but not buying? (Sales problem)

### A-Player Arbitrage
- Pay 25% above market = get 5x the output
- One A-player is worth five B-players
- "Great lawyers are expensive. Cheap lawyers cost a fortune."

## REFERRALS ARE EXPONENTIAL, NOT LINEAR (Deep Pass Batch 2)

- Two bring four, four bring eight -- compounds
- Book example: one post with 20K followers -> still sells more copies every month than the month before
- 25,000 copies/month, ~1,000/day, no marketing spend, 14,000 5-star reviews
- "People obsessed with their product are the ones that get the compounding"
- This is why product quality is the ultimate growth lever: referrals compound naturally when the product is exceptional
