# Lead Magnets
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Leads + 383 video transcripts)

## Definition
A lead magnet is a **complete solution to a narrow problem** given away at a significant discount or free to attract ideal customers.

## Why They Work
- People who pay with time now are more likely to pay with money later.
- Lead magnets get 3-5x more people to engage than pitching core offer alone.
- Costco food samples: giving away free food drives more purchases than no samples.
- The solved narrow problem reveals the next bigger problem (which your core offer solves).

---

## 3 Types of Lead Magnets (How to Solve)

### 1. Reveal a Problem (Diagnosis/Assessment)
- Works great for problems that get worse while you wait (termite inspections, posture analysis, website speed tests, financial audits)
- Creates implied urgency because deprivation increases with every second after they find out
- Example: "4 reasons why you'll never have a million-dollar agency" (Hormozi's highest-converting ad ever for Allen)
- Bonus: not only reveal the problem but show what it COULD look like if solved, and the delta

### 2. Free Trial / Sample (Taste Test)
- Limit by: number of uses, quantity, time, or combination
- The Costco sampler, try-before-you-buy
- Give solution then REMOVE it to create deprivation that drives purchase
- Example: Gym Launch gave agencies services free for 4 months, then charged after they got used to having leads

### 3. One Step of Many (Give away first step of a multi-step process)
- Particularly effective for complex products/services
- Example: First coat of paint (need 3 more), first laser hair removal session (need 6-8), first 2 videos of a comprehensive course
- Example: Hormozi's books solve "what to sell" and "how to get leads" but not the full scaling picture

---

## 4 Delivery Mechanisms

### 1. Software/Tools
- Spreadsheets, calculators, dashboards, assessment tools, small software
- Example: Neil Patel's URL analyzer tool

### 2. Information
- Courses, lessons, interviews, keynotes, tips, templates, guides
- Infinitely scalable, costs nothing, provides tremendous value

### 3. Services
- Do work for free. People SLEEP on this.
- Creates massive goodwill. People know it costs you something.
- High interface time = soft sell opportunities
- Example: Hormozi ran gym owners' ads free for 30 days -> they made money -> wanted to keep paying
- Math: 1 hour of labor ($25 cost), worth $250+ to market, 1 in 4 converts = $100 CAC. Not bad.
- Only give to QUALIFIED prospects (have budget, authority, need, timing)

### 4. Physical Products
- Books, supplements, branded items, t-shirts, samples
- Example: T-shirt that says "CEO" at a conference -> only given to verified CEOs -> qualified lead list

## Naming Your Lead Magnet

**This is the most important part.** How you name it determines engagement more than anything else.

- Hormozi split-tested 6 different headlines for $100M Leads book
- One change (first 5 seconds of a video) took it from 4,000 views to 850,000 (200x)
- David Ogilvy: "After you've written your headline, you've spent 80 cents of your advertising dollar."
- Test names by: running small ad tests, polling audience, posting A/B on stories, DMing contacts

### Naming Formulas That Work
- **Number + Outcome + Timeframe**: "3 emails that turn cold leads into clients in 24 hours"
- **How to YAY without BOO if GREATEST INSECURITY**: "How to build a funnel without hiring a copywriter even if you've never done it before"
- **Adjective + Type + Good Thing**: "The lazy funnel template that converts like crazy"
- **X Mistakes**: "5 ad copy mistakes that are killing your conversion rate"

## Making It Easy to Consume
- Offer multiple formats: ebook, audio, video, physical (Hormozi has nearly perfect split across all 4)
- Software: accessible on phone and computer, multiple devices
- Services: available more times, more ways, more days
- Physical products: simple to open, simple to use

---

## Making It Good
- Zero fluff. Give away the secrets, sell the implementation.
- If you only have one valuable thing, learn another and give the first away free.
- The market judges you on your FREE stuff, not your paid stuff. 80%+ of people will never buy but will have opinions.
- Bad free stuff DISCOURAGES people who otherwise would have bought.
- People make purchasing decisions based on prediction of future value. Best predictor = value already received.

---

## The Call to Action
- Clear, direct language. Not clever. "If you're doing X, go to [URL]."
- Include a reason to act NOW (urgency, scarcity, or any reason at all)
- **Fraternity Party Planner principle**: Any reason is better than no reason. "Timmy got his wisdom teeth out -- kegger!" Your business anniversary, your dog's birthday, National Dog Day -- it doesn't matter.
- Weave in scarcity (limited quantity) and/or urgency (limited time)
- Service businesses inherently have capacity limits -- USE THEM. "I can only take 4 more clients."

## FROM $100M LOST CHAPTERS

### Free Presentations (Full Chapter)

**Three reasons free presentations work:**
1. **Captive audience** -- they chose to attend, they're sitting there, they're paying attention
2. **Long enough time** -- 45-90 minutes gives you enough time to break beliefs and build trust
3. **Offer at peak motivation** -- by the end, they're at maximum desire and minimum skepticism

**Presentation Benchmarks (Conversion Rates):**
| Format | Close Rate | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Dinner seminar | 25% to lower offer / 8% to higher | Most intimate, highest cost per attendee |
| Masterclass (live/virtual) | 10% | Scalable, good for mid-ticket |
| Challenge (multi-day) | 2-5% | Lowest close rate but largest audience, best for low-ticket or list building |
| Summit/conference | 10% | Multiple speakers, social proof environment |
| Strategy call (1-on-1) | 25% | Highest close rate, least scalable |

**Key stat:** Gym Launch found that **78% of customers consumed 2+ long-form content pieces** before buying. Free presentations serve as that long-form content.

### BANT Qualification for Strategy Calls
Use BANT to qualify who gets a strategy call (don't waste calls on unqualified leads):
- **B**udget: Do they have the money or access to it?
- **A**uthority: Are they the decision maker?
- **N**eed: Do they actually have the problem you solve?
- **T**iming: Are they ready to act now (not "someday")?

### Three Core Limiting Beliefs (to Address in Presentations)
Every prospect has three beliefs preventing purchase:
1. **"Results must come easy"** -- If it takes work, the product must be bad
2. **"I must get the best results possible"** -- Perfectionism prevents starting
3. **"Results must lead to attention/approval"** -- Only values externally validated outcomes

**How to break each:**
- Belief 1: Tell stories of people who did the work and got massive results. Reframe effort as investment.
- Belief 2: Show that "good enough" results still transform their life. Perfect is the enemy of done.
- Belief 3: Show internal transformation stories -- confidence, peace, freedom -- not just external status.
