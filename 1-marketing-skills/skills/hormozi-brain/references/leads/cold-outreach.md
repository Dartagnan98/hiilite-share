# Cold Outreach
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Leads + 383 video transcripts)

## Three Problems Strangers Create (and Solutions)

### Problem 1: How do I contact them?
Build a list 3 ways:
1. **Scrape**: Subscribe to lead scraping/outbound softwares. Search criteria, test a sample of a few hundred, buy more if good.
2. **Buy from Brokers**: List brokers buy and sell contact lists. Ask for sample, test, scale winners.
3. **Elbow Grease**: Join groups/communities where your audience hangs out. Check directories for contact info. Reach out outside the group to avoid getting kicked out.
- **Interest groups are the warmest cold audience** -- problem-aware people concentrated in one place.

### Problem 2: What do I say?
Personalize + give big fast value:
- **Personalize**: Act like you know them. Match area code, use their name, reference something about them/their business. "Make cold outreach feel like warm outreach."
- **Give BIG FAST value**: Not tickling interest -- blowing their minds in 30 seconds. Your lead magnet must be EXCEPTIONAL. "So good people feel stupid saying no."
- **Write at below 3rd grade reading level**: 50% response bump from rewriting templates at this level. Run all scripts through a free reading level app.
- Scripts are never more than 1-2 pages (phone/chat) or half a page (email). First message may be 2-3 sentences.

### Problem 3: Not getting enough chances.
Increase volume 3 ways:
1. **Automate Delivery**: Live (highest response, lowest scale) vs Recorded (lower response, highest scale). Voice memos, handwritten cards = high response. Templated emails, pre-recorded voicemails = high scale.
2. **Automate Distribution**: Manual clicking send vs robot blasting thousands at once.
3. **Follow Up More Times in More Ways**: More ways (email + DM + call + text) = more likely to reach them. More times = shows you're serious.

## Voicemail Script (Using Third-Party Trust)
"Hey John, it's Alex. I'm calling in reference to [their competitor/neighbor]. Please give me a call back at [number]."
- Short: no overthinking needed
- Relevant: mentions a company they know
- Opens a loop: curiosity about what it's about

## What NOT to Do on Calls
- Don't say "This is [first name]..." (too formal, too salesy)
- Don't mention your company name
- Don't fail to follow up
- DO pause after your name (implies they should search memory for you)

## Cold Outreach Benchmarks
- 100 cold calls/day -> 25% pickup rate -> 25 pickups -> ~4 become engaged leads = 1 engaged lead per hour
- Cost = $0 (just your time/food)
- Pro tip: Give each rep explicit number of leads per week (~70 if capacity is 100). "No Leads Left Behind."
- 70 leads -> expect 7 appointments back. That's the quota.

## Scaling Example
- One virtual assistant sends 2,000 emails/day using multiple softwares
- Generates 40 engaged leads/day
- 10% close = 4 customers/day
- Customer LTV = $20,000+
- Unlocked cold outreach to pass $1M/month

## Why Cold Outreach Is Worth It (Pros)
1. Don't need lots of content or ads
2. Competition can't see what you're doing (everything private)
3. Incredibly reliable -- numbers work like clockwork
4. Fewer platform changes (cold call laws barely changed in decades)
5. Less compliance hassle (only legal, not platform rules)
6. Creates a more sellable business (team of 20 callers works without owner)
7. Hard to copy (private = no one knows your scripts/offers)

---

## Pro Tips
- New rep? Copy the best rep exactly, do TWICE the volume. Don't change anything until you beat their numbers.
- After contacting list, wait 3-6 months, start from top again (they didn't see it, couldn't respond, or circumstances changed).
- Response rate dropped with automation but volume made up for it -- always calculate: reach-outs x response rate = output.
