## The Content Unit (Hook -> Retain -> Reward)
Every piece of content has three parts:
1. **HOOK**: Give a reason to redirect attention. Objective: get them to notice.
2. **RETAIN**: Keep attention long enough to deliver value. Objective: get them to consume.
3. **REWARD**: Satisfy the reason they consumed it. Objective: make them want more.

Long content = many content units strung together. Short content = fewer units. Start short, build up.

---

## Hooks: 3 Elements

### 1. Pick Good Topics (5 Sources)
- **Far Past**: Life lessons, behavior changes ("I used to think X, then Y happened")
- **Recent Past**: Pull out your calendar, look at conversations, meetings, events
- **Present**: Real-time capture. Email yourself ideas. Test on Twitter.
- **Trends**: Current events that apply to your expertise
- **Manufactured**: Create experiences to document (e.g., "I lived on $100/month")

### 2. Pick Good Headlines (7 Characteristics)
From news media research on high-performing headlines:
- **Recency**: "Just happened" beats "happened 5 years ago"
- **Relevancy**: Personally meaningful to the target audience
- **Celebrity**: Including prominent people/authorities
- **Proximity**: Close to home geographically
- **Conflict**: Opposing ideas (pineapple vs no pineapple, freedom vs security)
- **Unusual**: Odd, rare, bizarre, one-of-a-kind
- **Ongoing**: Stories still in progress, dynamic, evolving

Combine all 7: something that just happened, relevant to the reader, includes a celebrity, right near them, with conflict, that's bizarre, and still unfolding = maximum attention.

### 3. Match the Format
Match the format that has rewarded them before on that platform. Make content look like high-performing content on that platform. Same video formatted differently for IG Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts.

## Retention: 3 Structures
1. **Lists**: Things/facts/tips presented one after another following a theme. Give the number upfront ("7 ways to..."). More flexible, less explicit reward.
2. **Steps**: Actions in order to accomplish a goal. Less flexible, more explicit reward ("Do these 5 steps to sell your house").
3. **Stories**: Events (real or imaginary) with lessons. People want to know what happens next. Fiction works too (Star Wars, Lord of the Rings).
- Best content uses ALL THREE interwoven together.

---

## Reward
- How good your content is = how often it rewards your audience / the time it takes to consume. Think: **value per second**.
- "There's no such thing as too long, only too boring." People binge 900-page books and Netflix series.
- People don't have shorter attention spans. They have higher STANDARDS because competition is fierce.
- If your audience grows, you did good. If it's not growing, your stuff isn't good enough (not the algorithm).

## Content Tip: Make All Content for Strangers
- Never make inside jokes. Never reference past content without context.
- Existing audience won't mind the "if you're new" intro.
- New audience will feel included, not like an outsider.

## Proof-Promise-Plan Introduction Framework
For educational content, start every piece with:
1. **Proof**: Why should they listen to you? Demonstrate credibility.
2. **Promise**: What will they learn/get from this content?
3. **Plan**: How will you deliver on that promise? (Steps, structure)
- Can be in any order; emphasize whichever is most important.

## Monetizing Content: The Give/Ask Ratio

### The Ratio
- **Television** (mature platform, maximally monetizing): 3.6:1 ratio (47 min content : 13 min ads per hour)
- **Facebook** (mature): 4:1 ratio (4 content posts : 1 ad)
- **TikTok** (growth phase): Nearly zero ads for years. All give, give, give.
- These ratios are for platforms that STOPPED growing and are maximally extracting.
- If you want to GROW: give until they ask. Way beyond 4:1.

### Hormozi's Approach
- **"Give in public, ask in private."** Your public reputation stays purely on giving.
- The moment you start asking publicly is the moment you slow growth.
- DM conversations and private sales keep your brand pristine.
- If your product is exceptional, even the "ask" deposits goodwill (book for $2.99 still reinforces brand).

### Two Ways to Ask
1. **Integrated**: Advertise within each piece of content, keeping give:ask ratio high. Example: 58.5 min of value + 1.5 min CTA in a 60-min video = 39:1 ratio.
2. **Intermittent**: Make many pure-give pieces, occasionally make an ask piece. Example: 10 gives then 1 ask.

### Where to Place CTAs
- After a valuable moment or at the end of content
- In a PS statement (the 2nd most-read part of any written content after the headline)
- Make CTAs contextual to the content topic

### Cautionary Tale
- Friend had podcast, it blew up, he asked too frequently -> podcast shrank -> asked more -> got less -> vicious cycle.
- Hold back asks as long as possible. Only ask with hand open, not shoving.

## Scaling Content: Two Approaches
1. **Depth then Width**: Master one platform -> maximize quality & quantity -> add next platform. Better for resource-constrained/beginners.
2. **Width then Depth**: Post on one platform -> go regular -> add closest similar platform -> continue until all covered -> maximize all. Better for experienced creators.

## 7 Content Lessons
1. **Switch from "how to" to "how I"**: Less ego, more receptive audience. "My favorite ways to generate leads" not "The best way to generate leads."
2. **We need to be reminded more than taught**: 1 in 6 of Hormozi's audience didn't know he had a book despite daily posts for 2 years. Repeat yourself way more than you think.
3. **Puddles, Ponds, Lakes, Oceans**: Start narrow. Be king of a small puddle before jumping to the ocean. A plumber in a town -> local business pond -> brick-and-mortar chains lake -> general business ocean.
4. **Content creates sales tools**: Content that generates inquiries can be (a) run as paid ads and (b) compiled into "greatest hits" for sales team to send prospects matching objections.
5. **Free content retains paying customers**: Customers don't separate where value comes from. More amazing free content = paying customers stay longer.
6. **No such thing as too long, only too boring**: Streaming platforms prove people binge hours of content.
7. **Avoid pre-scheduling posts**: Manually posting gives that final quality check moment. Manual posts outperform pre-scheduled ones.

## SPCL Framework (Status, Power, Credibility, Likeness)
The four elements that create influence (= high likelihood of compliance with requests):

1. **Status**: You control scarce resources people want. Money, access, knowledge, connections.
2. **Power**: Say-do correspondence. You give instructions, they follow, good things happen. More reinforcement cycles = more power.
3. **Credibility**: Third-party validation. Guinness records, certifications, awards, testimonials, media appearances.
4. **Likeness**: They see themselves in you. Physical resemblance, shared values, similar background. Just be you.

## 6 Lessons from 40 Months / $4M / 35,000 Pieces of Content

1. **Edutainment -> Education**: Entertainment viewers want more entertainment. Education viewers want more education. They don't cross over. Education drives book sales, opt-ins, and applications. Entertainment doesn't.

2. **For Us -> For You**: Make content for your ideal customer, not what's easiest. Tactics: clear packaging (not vague thumbnails), proof-based introductions, emphasis on language over production, list/steps/stories over vlogs.

3. **Wide -> Narrow**: Narrow topic focus attracts the right people. Business content brings business owners.

4. **Views -> Revenue**: Track ad revenue as primary metric, not views. Ad revenue = views x RPM (revenue per thousand). Higher RPMs = right audience. In a 90-day experiment, entertainment content doubled views but halved ad revenue.

5. **Shorts -> Longs**: Shorts viewers watch more shorts. Long viewers watch more longs. Longs drive far more conversions. Shorts on one platform can lead to longs on another.

6. **Assume More -> Assume Nothing**: Title like they don't know you. Introduce yourself every time. Explain all references. Make content for strangers. Warm people like reminders; cold people need introductions.

## Consistency Over Goals
- "Every winner and every loser have the same goals." (Atomic Habits)
- The goal of the winner: commit to the activities. The goal of the loser: commit to the goal.
- Hormozi's YouTube commitment: "I'm in for a decade."
- His podcast took 6 years (400+ episodes) to hit top 10.
- "You are not successful because whenever you see an outcome, you stop the activities."
- Divorce activities from outcomes. Your self-esteem stays stable because you control inputs.

## CONTENT STRATEGY (Deep Pass)

### Agency Acquisition 3-Phase Framework
- Phase 1: Hire basic agency ($3-5K/month) for 3-6 months -- learn fundamentals, establish cadence
- Phase 2: Hire advanced agency ($15K+/month) for 6 months -- learn nuanced strategies. Explicit agreement: "I want to learn how you do it, then train my team"
- Phase 3: Co-create -> transition -> consulting retainer -> cut
- Ran this exact playbook for YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, podcast, paid ads

### Agency Trap Pattern (10 Agencies, All Failed Same Way)
1. Excited about leads they'll bring
2. Onboarding feels valuable
3. Best senior rep assigned
4. See results
5. Senior rep moved to newest customer
6. Junior rep takes over
7. Results suffer
8. Complain -> senior comes back temporarily
9. Results never recover
10. Cancel -> repeat cycle

### Repost Your Best Content
- "Look at your number one piece of content from last year. Post it again."
- It will do just as well or better because new audience hasn't seen it
- "We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught"
- Simpsons: same introduction every episode with tiny variations

### Thumbnail/Headline: Clarity Over Curiosity
- Go from VAGUE to CLEAR
- "If they want that thing they will click on that thing and then you just give them that thing"
- Clarity beats curiosity for educational content

### Shorts Don't Feed Longs
- Prevailing theory: shorts -> longs -> customers
- Reality: shorts viewers watch more shorts, long viewers watch more longs, customers buy more
- These are different audiences. Longs drive significantly more conversions

### All Views Are Not Created Equal
- A video with 100K views can generate more revenue than one with 10M views
- RPM (revenue per thousand views) is the true quality metric
- Highest RPM months also had most book sales, opt-ins, and business applications

### Pre-Work > Post-Production
- "An ounce of pre-work is worth a pound of post"
- 25% of old post-production time spent on pre-research eliminated 90% of man-hours
- Result: 4x video output at HIGHER quality

### Content Introduction Formula: Proof, Promise, Plan
- PROOF: prove you know what you're talking about
- PROMISE: tell them what they'll get/learn
- PLAN: set expectations for what happens next
- For business education, usually lead with PROOF

### Context > Content Quality
- "The message is 10% of how people consume it. 90% is the context around the message"
- "Elon Musk can tweet on the toilet and get a million likes... because he's the richest man in the world"
- "Solve for the proof and then the content will take care of itself"

## CONTENT STRATEGY INSIGHTS (Deep Pass -- Batches 3-4)

### Edutainment vs Education (90-Day Experiment)
- Ran 90-day experiment doing more edutainment/entertainment content
- Views went 2-3x higher than normal
- But ad revenue DROPPED by half
- "We're actually getting the wrong people"
- Entertainment viewers want more entertainment, education viewers want more education -- they do NOT convert into each other
- Key metric switched from views to ad revenue (RPM) because it pairs quality + quantity
- Month with highest RPMs also created most book sales, opt-ins, and business applications

### Content Decision Framework (SPCL)
- S = Status (control reinforcers in a given environment)
- P = Proof (why should anyone listen to you)
- C = Content quality
- L = Likeability
- Build influence, not just views

### Audience Monetization Framework (4 Ways)
1. Affiliates: paid after sales (lowest risk for brand)
2. Sponsorships/Endorsements: paid upfront to advertise
3. Partnerships: equity in existing business (higher commitment)
4. Start your own brand: promote your own products (highest control + risk)

### Branding Playbook
- Branding = "deliberate pairing of things through an outcome"
- Good branding = deliberate pairing with stuff your ideal customers LIKE
- Bad branding = pairing with stuff they don't (Bud Light + Dylan Mulvaney = sales dropped)
- Brand measurement: influence (likelihood to change behavior) x direction (towards/away) x reach (how many)
- Nike pairs with LeBron/Tiger (winning) -> $5 t-shirt becomes $60 -> 12x premium
- Strong brand improves advertising: 6x higher click-through rate on branded vs generic
- Warren Buffett: "The single most important decision in evaluating a business is pricing power"
