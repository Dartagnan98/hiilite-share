# Paid Ads
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Leads + 383 video transcripts)

## What Matters Most
- Efficiency > creativity. All advertising works. The question is HOW WELL.
- Goal: find ads good enough to scale, then scale the hell out of them.
- **Benchmark: 3:1 LTGP-to-CAC ratio minimum** (lifetime gross profit vs cost to acquire customer). Hormozi targets 10:1 minimum for portfolio companies. Best ones do 20+:1. His personal lifetime average is 36:1.

---

## Three Phases of Ad Spending
1. **Track Money**: Get tracking in place first. Without tracking, you're flying blind at a casino.
2. **Lose Money**: You MUST lose money to make money. Out of 300 ads, maybe 295 won't work, 5 will be ROI-positive, 1 will be a home run. Most people quit at this phase.
3. **Print Money**: Find the winner, scale it. If you put $1 in and get $10 out, your budget = as many dollars as you can fit into the machine.

## "If someone says 'I have a $10K/month marketing budget,' I already know they don't understand paid advertising."
- If ads are working, there is no budget. You spend as much as possible.
- Constraint is never ad budget -- it's delivery capacity.
- Reverse engineer: if you can handle 20 new customers/month and CAC is $100, budget = $2,000/month. Focus on deconstraining delivery, then crank ads back up.

## Key Metrics

### LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit)
- LTGP = (Price - Cost) x Number of purchases. NOT total revenue.
- Example: $15/month product, $5 cost = $10 gross profit. Customer stays 10 months = $100 LTGP.

### CAC (Cost to Acquire Customer)
- CAC = all-in cost including ad spend, commissions, software, team.

### Ratio Targets
- If LTGP/CAC > 3: scale aggressively
- If LTGP/CAC > 1 but < 3: improve either side
- Common mistake: confusing revenue with gross profit. A food company spending $100 to acquire a customer who pays $500 but at 10% margins = spending $100 to make $50.

---

## Client-Financed Acquisition (CFA)
- The goal: break even or profit within 30 days so you can recycle the cash.
- Example: $15/month membership, $10 gross profit, $30 CAC = 3 months to break even. Too slow.
- **Fix**: Add a $100 one-time upsell. 20% take rate = $20 average per customer. $10 (first month) + $20 (upsell) = $30 = breaks even day one.
- Now you can reinvest immediately. Compounding: 2x -> 4x -> 8x -> 16x -> 32x in 5 months.
- Easiest upsells: sell MORE of what they bought, sell BETTER of what they bought, sell NEW/complementary.

---

## The 3 Numbers You Must Know
1. **CAC** (Cost to Acquire Customer) -- include everything
2. **LTV as Gross Profit** (not revenue) -- gross margin x lifetime purchases
3. **30-Day Cash** -- how much gross profit collected in first 30 days per customer
- Target: LTV/CAC > 3:1
- Target: 30-Day Cash/CAC > 1:1 (break even in 30 days = use credit cards to finance growth)

## How to Fix Ads That Aren't Working
1. Don't confuse sales problems with advertising problems. If right people are showing up and not buying = sales problem, not ad problem.
2. Your best free content makes the best ads. Videos not intended to be ads that went viral = murder as ads because the platform already validated them.
3. User-generated content (UGC) creates a flywheel: customers make videos -> run as ads -> more customers -> more videos.
4. Don't say "I'm not techy." Hormozi said it for 4 years, reversed it with 4 hours of effort.

## Ad Creation: The Elements
Every ad has:
1. **Call out**: Who is this for? ("Hey gym owners" or "If you're a mom over 35...")
2. **Value element**: What's in it for them? (The 3 W's: What you get, Who it's for, When/how)
3. **Call to action**: What do you do next? Clear, simple, direct.

---

## Targeting
- **Audience targeting**: Demographics, interests, behaviors, lookalikes
- **Creative targeting**: The content of the ad itself IS the targeting. The algorithm knows what you're talking about and shows it to the right people.
- "The content IS the targeting" -- make content for your avatar and the algorithm finds them.

---

## Paid Ads Checklist
- Who: You or your advertiser
- What: Your offer or lead magnet
- Where: Any platform you can buy access to your audience
- To whom: Target audience + lookalike audiences with filters/interests
- When: Every day, 7 days/week
- How much: Learning budget (track -> lose -> print), or reverse engineer from sales capacity
- How many variations: 30 hooks x 10 valuable content pieces = 300 ad variations to test
- How long: As long as it takes (sometimes 3-6 months to crack)

## META ADS CREATIVE MACHINE (Batch 7)

### Andromeda System
- Meta's new ad delivery system: feed it tons of ads, it picks top ~10% winners
- Makes advertising more efficient but requires massive creative volume
- Need ~100 ads; Andromeda picks top 5-7 winners. Old winners decay -- must constantly feed fresh creative
- Targeting is now embedded in creative itself: avatar in ad determines who sees it
- Demographic targeting less important than creative-based targeting

### Creative Production Playbook
- Life cycle ads: get 2-3 ads from each new customer (testimonials, transformations)
- Repurpose best organic content as ads + 5-second CTA cut-in
- Banner overlays for specific avatar callout (e.g., "Occupational Therapist, Physical Therapist, Speech Therapist")
- Andromeda finds people who match the avatar IN the ad -- make sure creative shows ideal customer
- Top-of-funnel hooks: ask the qualifying question in the ad ("Do you have a green card?")

### Specific Benchmarks from Hotline Calls
- Dental (high-ticket): expect $50/lead on Meta, 1-2% conversion rate at $8K+ ticket = $2,500-$5,000 CAC
- PPC dental: $5K-$8K/month minimum spend
- Creative volume: need ~100 ads, Andromeda picks top 10%, need to constantly feed fresh creative

## PAID ADS ADDITIONS (Batch 5-6)

### Facebook Slap Recovery Story
- Lead cost went up 5x overnight from Facebook algorithm change
- Was losing money on marketing daily
- Solution: attached phone to funnel, sold high-ticket on front end instead of trials
- Went from losing 2.5:1 to making 10:1 return on front end
- Could now spend up to $100/lead profitably vs breaking even at $10/lead before

### Three Levels of Advertising (CFA Framework)
- **Level 1**: Lifetime gross profit > CAC (minimum 3x to be a business)
- **Level 2**: 30-day gross profit > CAC (never spend your own money to grow -- credit card float)
- **Level 3**: 30-day gross profit > 2x CAC (unlimited scale -- one customer funds the next)
- Level 3 math: Month 1=1, Month 2=2, Month 3=4... Month 12=4,096 total customers
- Credit card = 30-day interest-free money -- that's why 30 days is the benchmark

### Core Four Advertising Framework
- 2 audiences (warm/cold) x 2 communication types = 4 methods
- Warm: people who know you (unidirectional -- they know you)
- Cold: people who didn't give permission ("other people's audiences")
