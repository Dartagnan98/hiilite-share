# Benchmarks, Numbers & Key Principles
## Source: Alex Hormozi ($100M Leads + 383 video transcripts)

## Response Rates & Conversion
- Cold calls: 25% pickup rate, ~16% of pickups become engaged leads
- Cold email: 30% open rate, 10% reply rate (with optimization)
- 3rd grade reading level rewrite = 50% response bump
- Switching lead magnet to something others charge for = 3x response rates
- Average 9 follow-up attempts to get response

## Paid Ads
- LTGP:CAC ratio target: 3:1 minimum, 10:1 ideal, 20+:1 exceptional
- Hormozi lifetime average: 36:1
- Top 0.1% of Facebook advertisers test 11x more creative than everyone else
- 30 hooks x 10 content pieces = 300 ad variations to test
- Learning budget: expect to lose money for 3-12 months before it clicks

## Content
- 78% of Gym Launch customers consumed 2+ long-form pieces before buying
- Ebook/audio/video/physical book consumption: nearly perfect 25% split
- First 5 seconds of a video = 85% of whether someone watches
- Editing first 5 seconds took one video from 4,000 views to 850,000 (200x)
- Manual posts outperform pre-scheduled posts

## Email
- ROI: $35-$45 per $1 spent
- 61% check email multiple times/day
- Hormozi open rate: ~35.7%, CTR: 8.5%
- 16.99% of B2B marketing emails never reach inbox
- Segmented lists dramatically outperform unsegmented
- Speed to contact (call within 60 seconds): 391% increase in sales
- Best days: B2C = Monday/Tuesday, B2B = Wednesday
- Best times: 10am-noon and 1pm-3pm

## Business Metrics
- Within same industry, CAC varies only 1-3x between advertisers
- But revenue per customer can vary 10x+ between competitors
- 30-day cash / CAC > 1:1 = can use credit cards to finance growth
- If CFA (Client-Financed Acquisition) achieves 2:1 in first 30 days: 2x -> 4x -> 8x -> 16x -> 32x -> 4,000x in one year of compounding

## Employee Metrics
- Industry average CAC: if you're within 3x, you likely don't have an advertising problem
- Total payroll / engaged leads = true cost per lead (even without paid media)
- A-players: pay 25% above market, get 5x output

## Content Volume
- Hormozi: 35,000 pieces of content in 40 months
- $1-2M businesses doing 365 pieces/year
- Only 9% of US population owns a business
- 95% of those are under $1M revenue
- Only ~1.5M people in US are business owners doing $1M+
- Two businesses doing $1M+/year with fewer than 5,000 followers

## Cold Outreach
- 100 cold calls/day -> 25 pickups -> ~4 engaged leads = 1 engaged lead per hour
- One VA sending 2,000 emails/day = 40 engaged leads/day
- 10% close = 4 customers/day at $20K+ LTV
- 70 leads per rep per week -> expect 7 appointments back

## ADDITIONAL BENCHMARKS (Batch 7)

### Gym Churn Math
- 3% monthly churn = retain 69% of customers per year (0.97^12)
- 9% monthly churn = retain 32% of customers per year (0.91^12)
- $100/month at 3% churn = $3,300 LTV (100/0.03)
- $100/month at 9% churn = $1,100 LTV (100/0.09) -- 3x difference
- Only 20 out of ~1,000 gyms achieved <3% monthly churn for 6 months (top 2%)

### Personal Wealth / Compounding Benchmarks
- 35% of every American paycheck goes to banks/lenders for debt
- $200 invested at age 25 = $6,000+ at age 65 in S&P 500
- $250/month invested at 9% for 40 years = $1 million
- $500/month = ~$2 million (can live on ~$80K/year at 4% withdrawal)
- $2,500/month for 55 years at 9% = $52 million
- Every $250/month saved = roughly $1M at retirement
- First $100K is hardest (Charlie Munger, adjusted to ~$150-200K today)
- 30x12 challenge: work 30 straight days, 12 hours/day, no weekends

### Tax Structure Math
- Business at $10M EBITDA, 5x multiple = $50M valuation
- After-tax dividends at 37%: $6.3M. Grow to $12M EBITDA: valuation $60M, dividends $7.6M
- Net worth increase: $17.6M, effective tax rate only 21% (56% was tax-free appreciation)
- S&P companies at 40x: $2M EBITDA increase = $80M net worth increase

### Pricing Benchmarks (Expanded)
- Close rate pricing guide: Every 10% above 35% close rate = 25-50% pricing lift available
- Medium price placement trick: $5/$6/$10 = most buy $6; $5/$9/$10 = most buy $10
- Pricing should match buying personas: $100/$200/$1,000 (not $100/$110/$120)
- CRO value-based pricing: 10% improvement for $1M store = $100K; for $100M store = $10M (same work)
