## Finding the Funnel Bottleneck

### Step-by-Step Diagnosis
1. Look at ad data: CTR and total click volume
2. Look at EACH step of the funnel, conversion rate per step
3. Calculate step-by-step:
   - 1,000 hit opt-in > 100 opt in = 10% opt-in rate
   - 98 hit webinar > 12 hit order page = 12.2% webinar-to-order
   - 12 on order page > 2 purchase = 16% order-to-purchase
4. Factor in upsells and their conversion rates
5. Identify the choke point
6. Put attention on the choke point until desired KPIs are met
7. Repeat for the next choke point

### The CTR Signal
- Good CTR (2%+) but low conversions = problem is on the landing page/funnel side
- Bad CTR (under 2%) = problem is the ad creative/copy
- If CTR is good, focus optimization on the funnel, not the ads

## Call Funnel CRO Elements

### Order Form / Checkout Page Optimization
1. **Green CTA buttons** -- higher conversion rates on order forms and closing pages
2. **Support info** -- email, phone number, physical address for trust
3. **Short testimonials** -- formatted like tweets, easy to read
4. **FAQ section** -- digital objection handling for common concerns
5. **Product visualization** -- demos, unboxing videos, influencer content
6. **Live chat** -- bottom right corner
7. **Trust icons** -- "Guaranteed SAFE Checkout" + Visa, MC, Stripe, AES-256bit logos
8. **Social proof tools** -- UseProof.com or ClickFunnels "digital table rush" (shows who's buying)
9. **Clear shipping info** -- free shipping typically helps physical product conversions

Each element adds single-digit % increase. Combined = significant lift.

## Confirmation Page Best Practices

### Purpose
The confirmation step serves as the final conversion reinforcer:
- VSL explaining what to expect on the call
- Congratulate them on getting closer to solving their problem
- Add testimonials for further framing
- Gets the person more committed to showing up

### What to Include
- Video Sales Letter (short, focused)
- What to expect on the call/next step
- Client testimonials and results
- Congratulations messaging
- Clear next steps

## Split Testing Framework

### Testing Hierarchy (What to Test First)
1. **Hook/Headline** -- biggest impact on whether anyone engages at all
2. **Offer** -- what you're actually promising
3. **Creative format** -- video vs image vs carousel
4. **Body copy** -- the substance of the message
5. **CTA** -- how you ask them to take action
6. **Landing page** -- where they land after clicking

### Testing Rules
- Only change ONE variable at a time
- Run for minimum 72 hours before making decisions
- Need sufficient data volume to make meaningful conclusions
- At $100-$500/day: full week minimum test window
- At $500+/day: 3-4 days sufficient for trends

### The 72-Hour Window
- Review data day by day for tracking
- Make actual decisions every 72 hours
- Don't kill anything too soon
- Don't get excited and scale after one good day

## Dynamic Product Retargeting

For e-commerce (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.):
- Set up catalog feed with all products and categories
- Run "dynamic product retargeting ads" showing:
  - Products they viewed but didn't buy
  - Products they added to cart but didn't purchase
  - Cross-sell products in same category as previous purchase
- Highest probability to bring them back to the store

## Framing Issues (When Leads Aren't Qualified)

### The Secret Shopper Audit
1. Go through the campaign like a customer
2. Start with ads > enter funnel > watch videos > schedule call
3. Note everything that's off
4. Common problem areas:
   - Ads not setting right expectations
   - No VSLs on pages (huge difference)
   - Generic reminder automation vs well-written follow-up copy
   - Disconnect between ad promise and funnel delivery

### Content-Driven Framing (Venus Fly Trap / Harvester)
- Incorporate content at ALL stages of the ad strategy
- Content for framing BEFORE prospect sees direct response ads
- Content throughout ALL funnel stages
- Each piece contextual and beneficial for that step

## No-Show Rate Optimization

| Rate | Status |
|------|--------|
| Under 20% | Good |
| 30% | OK |
| Above 35% | Unacceptable |

### Reduce No-Shows:
1. Audit marketing automation (testimonials in reminders, resell why they should show)
2. Text reminders (collect phone numbers)
3. Video text immediately after scheduling (from closer, face-to-face, ask for questions)
4. $5-10/day retargeting campaign for scheduled callers
5. Confirmation page with VSL + testimonials
6. Combined approach keeps no-shows under 20%
