# Budget Rules & Kill/Scale Decisions
**Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing -- IFTTT Advertising Rules SOP + Ad Scaling Framework**

---

## The CPA Range Framework

Before making any scaling or killing decision, establish three CPA tiers:

| Tier | Definition | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| GREAT | Lowest acceptable CPA (e.g., $10/lead) | Scale 20-30% daily |
| GOOD / AVERAGE | Acceptable CPA (e.g., $25/lead) | Scale 10% daily |
| POOR / INTOLERABLE | Above threshold (e.g., $50+/lead) | Cut or optimize |

**Critical:** Don't just look at CPA -- look at Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC).
- $200/lead sounds expensive, but if selling a $3,000 product with 50% close rate, CAC = $400
- Some would panic at $200 CPL when it's actually profitable

> "Never turn something off that is profitable."

---

## The 3x Kill Rule

If an ad set doesn't get results after spending 2-3x your target CPA, kill it.

Examples:
- Target CPA: $50/lead --> Kill at $100-$150 spent with zero results
- Target CPA: $20/lead --> Kill at $40-$60 spent with zero results

---

## The 20% Rule (Scaling Budget Increases)

- Scale winning campaigns 10-30% per day
- 10% for "good" performers (2:1 ROAS range)
- 20-30% for "great" performers (5:1+ ROAS)
- NEVER scale more than 30% on the same campaign in one day
- Going beyond 30% kicks campaign back into learning mode

### If You Need Bigger Jumps
- Duplicate the campaign
- Set the duplicate at the higher budget
- Kill the original once the duplicate is approved and active
- This preserves data integrity and avoids learning mode reset

---

## The 72-Hour Decision Window

Make scaling/killing decisions every 72 hours, not daily:
- Day-by-day data is too noisy
- 72-hour windows smooth out variance
- At high spend ($1K+/day): also review yesterday's data alongside the 72-hour window

---

## Problem: Low Conversions -- Find the Bottleneck

1. Look at ad data (CTR, click volume)
2. Look at each funnel step conversion rate
3. Example funnel math:
   - 1,000 hit opt-in page, 100 opted in = 10% opt-in rate
   - 98 hit webinar page, 12 hit order page = 12.2% webinar-to-order
   - 12 on order page, 2 purchase = 16% order-to-purchase
4. Focus on wherever the choke point is
5. Repeat until desired KPIs are met throughout entire funnel

---

## Problem: CTR Below 2%

- Good CTR = above 2%
- Great CTR = above 5%

**If below 2%:**
1. Rotate in new creatives every 72 hours until CTR exceeds 2%
2. Test new headlines, body copy, images/videos, dynamic vs static creative
3. **CAVEAT:** If ads are profitable AND below 2% CTR, don't change that campaign
   - Instead: duplicate campaign, test new audiences with same creative
   - See if higher CTR audience exists
4. Tools for limited creative: Shakr, Canva for quick video/image variations

---

## Problem: Learning Limited

**What it means:** Not enough conversions for the platform's machine learning to work.

**Solutions (in order):**
1. **Relaunch the ad set** if you got close to the needed conversions -- high probability of exiting
2. If way short: find and fix the funnel bottleneck first, then relaunch
3. **Test new audiences** with best-performing creatives -- determines if it's audience or creative problem
4. **Run cyclic campaigns** -- optimize for each step of the funnel using standard events
   - E-commerce: purchase + initiate checkout + add to cart campaigns
   - Lead gen: complete registration + submit application + lead campaigns
   - Gets more data throughout all funnel steps, exits learning phase faster

## Problem: CPA Too High

### Cause 1: Audiences Too Small
- Happens for audiences under 1,000,000
- Fix: Widen the audience being targeted
- If not possible: drop daily budget 20% until CPA lowers, then scale back up 10-20% daily
- Alternative: manual bid campaign to see if desired CPA is even achievable at that audience size

### Cause 2: Hyper Competitive Demographics
- Example: Q4 elections + COVID e-commerce surge
- **Manual bid strategy for competitive periods:**
  1. Switch to manual bid campaigns
  2. Start $200/day with $50 bid cap
  3. Monitor reach every 2 hours
  4. If throttled: bump bid cap $10 every 2 hours
  5. For each conversion: increase daily budget by that conversion's cost
  6. When reach stops: you've cleared out available buyers at that bid cap level

### Cause 3: Scaling Too Fast
- Any automated bidding campaign scaled more than 10-30% in one daily increase can spike CPA
- Fix: Duplicate campaign at higher budget instead of scaling same campaign

### Cause 4: Wrong Audience
- Test new audiences with same creatives
- Compare CPA across audience segments

## Problem: High No-Show Rate on Calls

**Benchmarks:**
- 20% or less: Good
- 30%: OK
- Above 35%: Unacceptable -- costing big money

**Most marketers don't factor no-show cost into their cost per scheduled call.**

### Solutions:
1. **Audit marketing automation** -- generic reminders are not enough. Pepper in testimonials. Treat every reminder as a resell on why they need to show up
2. **Text-based reminders** -- collect phone numbers in scheduler
3. **Video text immediately after application/scheduled call** -- real, custom video from closer saying they're excited to chat, face-to-face the person, ask them to send questions before the call. Best from iPhones (no quality reduction)
4. **Retargeting campaign** ($5-10/day) -- contextual content: "We're excited to help now that you've scheduled a call, here's what to expect." Needs audience of 100+ callers
5. **Confirmation step** with VSL explaining what to expect + testimonials
6. **All in combination keeps no-shows below 20%**

---

## Problem: Lots of Add-to-Carts but Few Sales

### CRO Actions:
1. **Dynamic product retargeting** -- catalog feed showing products they viewed/added to cart
2. **Green CTA buttons** -- higher conversion rates on order forms
3. **Support info on page** -- email, phone number, physical address = trust
4. **Short testimonials** -- formatted like tweets, easy to read
5. **FAQ section** -- digital objection handling
6. **Product visualization** -- unboxing videos, influencer content
7. **Live chat** -- bottom right corner
8. **Clear shipping info** -- free shipping typically helps
9. **Social proof tools** -- UseProof, ClickFunnels "digital table rush" showing who's buying
10. **Trust icons** -- "Guaranteed SAFE Checkout" with Visa, MC, Stripe logos
11. **Cumulative effect** -- each adds single-digit % increase; combined = significant

## Problem: Calls Scheduled but Few Closing

1. **Framing issue** -- audit entire campaign like a customer (secret shopper approach)
   - Start with ads, work through funnel, watch videos, schedule call
   - Find what's off in marketing automation
   - Common areas: ads, funnel, marketing automation, sales conversation
   - Missing VSLs on pages = huge difference
   - Generic reminder automation vs well-written copy
2. **Lead quality** -- wrong hook + right audience, or right hook + wrong audience
   - If right people: test new hooks, gauge quality again
   - If wrong people: test new audiences with same hooks
3. **Lead quantity** -- increase spend on successful campaigns for larger data sample
4. **Closer quality** -- separate sales training issue

---

## Problem: Leads Not Framed Properly

### The Secret Shopper Audit
1. Go through campaign like a customer you want to sell
2. Start with ads, enter funnel, watch videos, schedule call
3. Note everything that's off in the customer experience
4. Most common issues: ads, funnel, marketing automation, sales conversation

### The Fix: Incorporate Framing Content
- Use strategies like Venus Fly Trap and Harvester that incorporate content at ALL stages
- Content used for framing BEFORE prospect sees direct response creatives
- Content leveraged THROUGHOUT all funnel stages
- Each piece contextual and beneficial for that step of the process

## Meta Ads Performance Columns (Quality Signals)

Three reporting columns that replaced the old relevance score:

1. **Quality Ranking** -- how ads perceived quality compares to same-audience competitors
2. **Engagement Rate Ranking** -- expected engagement vs same-audience competitors
3. **Conversion Rate Ranking** -- expected conversion rate vs same-goal, same-audience competitors

When all three are low + high CPA + low reach = update creatives or try new audiences.
Use Facebook Ad Library to research competitor Instagram/Facebook ads and outcompete.
