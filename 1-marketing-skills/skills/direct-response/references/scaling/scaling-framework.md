# Scaling Framework: $1K/Day to $2M+/Month
**Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing -- Ad Scaling Framework SOP + Week 3 CBO/Cyclic Transcripts**

---

## Scaling Mindset

> "ROAS trumps everything. A $130 CPM with 7x ROAS is better than a $30 CPM with 1.5x ROAS."
> "Scale while the iron is hot. When you have profitable campaigns, your first priority should be scaling them, not optimizing them."

---

## The Dog Food Brainstorm Method

Before scaling, map out EVERY reason people would want your product/service:

Dog food example: healthy, frozen, energy, human grade, not highly processed, quality of food, smooth coat, joint health, live longer, liver healthier, gut balance, doesn't smell, no dog farts, taste, fewer doctor visits

This same approach applies to any product. Map ALL buying motivations before creating ads.

## Chapter 1: Early-Stage Scaling ($1K-$5K/day)

### Budget Allocation
| Category | % of Budget | Purpose |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Foundation (proven winners) | 80-90% | Campaigns consistently hitting minimum ROAS targets |
| Testing (new audiences/creatives) | 10% | Most tests fail, but need consistent testing for new winners |
| Optimization | 10-20% | Profitable but not at scaling thresholds -- test improvements |

### Creative Refresh: Every 1-4 weeks
### Key Rule: Be disciplined about allocations. Don't throw entire budget at exciting new tests.

## Chapter 2: Mid-Level Scaling ($10K-$50K/day)

This is where mistakes that were annoying at $1K/day become business-threatening at $20K/day.

### Budget Allocation
| Category | % of Budget | Description |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Foundation | 80-90% | Multiple foundation campaigns (3-5 proven audience/creative combos) |
| Scaling | 10-20% | Performing well, actively trying to scale up. Eventually rolls into foundation |
| Testing | 10% | Percentage decreases but absolute dollar amount increases significantly |
| Optimization | 5-10% | Borderline profitable campaigns worth improving |

### Fatigue Management
- Monitor frequency DAILY at this level
- Creative refresh every 1-2 weeks (not 2-4 like early stage)
- Higher spend accelerates fatigue cycles significantly

### Audience Expansion

**Lookalike Stacking:**
- Lookalikes based on purchasers
- Lookalikes based on high-value customers
- Lookalikes based on email subscribers
- Lookalikes based on video viewers
- Stack in same campaign for broader targeted audience

**Interest Expansion:**
- Combine related interests: real estate + investing + entrepreneurship
- Sales + marketing + business development

**Behavioral Targeting:**
- People who purchased similar products
- Frequent travelers (for travel offers)
- Recent movers (for local services)

### Performance Monitoring Systems

**Daily Review (every morning):**
- Previous 7, 30, 90 day ROAS by campaign
- Frequency, CPMs, link CTR, opt-in rate, show rates, close rates, AOV, cost per result
- Any campaigns needing creative refresh
- Budget allocation adjustments

**Weekly Strategic Review:**
- Which campaigns to scale more aggressively
- Which to pause or optimize
- What new tests to launch
- Creative production needs for next week

**Automated Alerts for:**
- ROAS dropping below minimum threshold
- CPM increases above normal ranges
- Frequency climbing above 3
- Daily spend significantly over/under targets

## Chapter 3: High-Level Scaling ($50K+/day)

At millions per month, you're playing a completely different game. You have a "fire hose of data" -- make decisions faster and test more aggressively.

### Budget Allocation
| Category | % of Budget | At $10K/day |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Foundation | 80-90% | $8K-$9K/day to absolute best performers |
| "Doing Okay" | 10-20% | Technically ROI positive but inconsistent |
| Testing | 5-10% | $500-$1K/day purely for new tests |

**Foundation campaigns have:**
- Proven hooks that appeal to majority audiences
- Consistent ROAS above minimum thresholds
- Handle increased spend without performance degradation
- Long-term stability (running profitably for weeks/months)

**"Doing Okay" campaigns kept running because:**
- Provide additional volume when foundation hits saturation
- Sometimes have breakthrough days
- Give options when foundation needs creative refresh

### Two Testing Approaches

**All-In Testing** (significant budget on new approach):
- Use when: high-confidence hypothesis, foundation showing poor performance, need winners quickly
- Take $3K-$5K/day and test completely new approach

**Segmented Testing** (smaller variations within successful campaigns):
- Use when: optimizing existing campaigns, incremental improvements, maintaining stability

## CBO Scaling Strategy (from Facebook HQ)

### Campaign Structure
- 1 campaign dedicated to 1 objective
- 2-4 ad sets (audiences) -- comes from Facebook directly, backed by tests
- Dynamic creative ON (especially for initial tests)
- With dynamic creative: upload up to 10 images/videos, 5 body copies, 5 headlines

### The Horizontal Scaling Method

1. Launch CBO campaign with 4 ad sets at $100-200/day
2. After learning phase (usually 72 hours), one ad set becomes the clear winner
3. Duplicate the campaign
4. Delete the winning ad set from the new campaign
5. Let the 3 "choked out" ad sets test against each other at $100-200/day
6. After 72 hours, find the winner among those 3
7. Repeat: duplicate, delete winner, test remaining

This finds winners you'd never discover if you just left the original campaign running.

### Vertical Scaling Rules
- Scale 10-30% per day based on ROAS
- 2:1 ROAS = reward with 10% increase
- 10:1 ROAS = reward with 30% increase
- Base decisions on last 3 days of data
- At $1K+/day: also consider yesterday's data alongside 72-hour window

### Why 10-30%?
- Does NOT kick campaign back into learning mode
- Keeps CPAs, CPMs, CAC more consistent
- Going from $100 to $1,000 in one jump = learning mode reset = all metrics become unpredictable

### If You Need Aggressive Jumps
- Don't scale the same campaign from $100 to $1,000
- Duplicate the campaign, set new one at $1,000/day
- Kill the old campaign once new one is active
- Preserves historical data integrity

> "Facebook loves clean consistent data."

## Cyclic Campaigns Strategy

### The Problem with Custom Conversions
- Custom conversions are specific to YOUR URL/pixel data
- Facebook has far fewer data points to leverage
- Higher ad costs, lower reach, more expensive conversions

### Standard Events Advantage
- All 7M+ Facebook advertisers use standard events (purchase, lead, etc.)
- Facebook accumulates massive data on these events across ALL advertisers
- Far higher probability of finding likely converters in your audience
- "Thousands of times higher probability" of finding right people

### How Cyclic Campaigns Work

**E-Commerce Example:**
1. Campaign 1: Optimized for Purchase (standard event)
2. Campaign 2 (duplicate): Optimized for Initiate Checkout
3. Campaign 3 (duplicate): Optimized for Add to Cart

**Lead Gen Example:**
1. Campaign 1: Optimized for Complete Registration
2. Campaign 2: Optimized for Submit Application
3. Campaign 3: Optimized for Lead

### Benefits
- Each campaign should produce positive ROI independently
- Significantly strengthens retargeting lists
- Drives higher total volume than single-objective campaign
- Lower cost per action across all campaigns
- Better reach than just optimizing for the final step

### Pro Tip
Skip optimizing for View Content (that's essentially a traffic campaign). Start at least at the second-to-last step.

## Audience Scaling Progression

1. **Start:** In-market audiences (3-4% who already want what you sell)
2. **Expand:** Needs convinced (30% who want the general outcome)
3. **Then:** Mass market (test angles for the unconvinced)

Don't skip steps. Master in-market first. Exploit it to the maximum. Then expand outward.

### Broad Targeting (Modern Approach)
- Modern platforms heavily favor broad targeting
- Facebook's Advantage Detailed Targeting automatically expands beyond selected audiences
- "Trust the machine" approach:
  1. Start with broad, relevant audiences (10M+ people)
  2. Optimize for actual business outcome (purchases, calls, leads)
  3. Let algorithm learn from conversion patterns
  4. Focus optimization on creative and landing pages

### Exclusion Strategies (Critical at Scale)
**Warm Audience Exclusions (for cold campaigns):**
- Website visitors (180+ days)
- Email subscribers
- Social media engagers
- Video viewers (all thresholds)
- Previous purchasers

**Geographic Exclusions:**
- Areas where you can't legally operate
- Areas with consistently high costs and low conversion
- Regions where product isn't relevant

## Key Principles Summary

1. **ROAS trumps everything** -- never let vanity metrics distract from profitability
2. **Scale while the iron is hot** -- scale first, optimize while scaling
3. **Infrastructure enables scale** -- build redundancy before you need it
4. **Creative is your biggest bottleneck** -- invest in production systems
5. **Audience progression is key** -- in-market > needs convinced > mass market
6. **Fatigue management is critical** -- build systems for predicting and preventing
7. **Data drives decisions** -- systematic approaches over gut feelings
