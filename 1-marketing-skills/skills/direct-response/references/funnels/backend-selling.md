## Overview

Backend selling systems solve the information deficit problem. Leads show up cold, sales calls become education calls, show rates are low, and close rates suffer. These four strategies combined have produced an average 17% lift in show rate across implementations.

> "There is an information deficit that generally happens when leads show up too cold. A lot of problems are all due to information gaps." -- Jeremy Haynes

### The Skin Cancer Analogy
Telling someone "men have 50% higher chance of skin cancer" won't make them act. But a dermatologist on a plane saying "that spot on your arm concerns me, I think it might be early stage melanoma" creates immediate action. Both are the same information -- but the degree to which they inspire action differs substantially. That's what backend selling systems do.

## Part 4 Deep Dive: Sales Team Best Practices

### What Salespeople Should Do Before the Call

**Pre-Call Research:**
- Review lead's survey responses from confirmation page
- Check their social media profiles
- Look at their business/website
- Note any specific pain points or goals mentioned
- Review which content they consumed (email opens, video views)

**Pre-Call Outreach:**
- Send a personalized video text (selfie-style from iPhone)
- Keep it casual: "Hey [Name], it's [Your Name]. Just wanted to put a face to the voice before our call tomorrow. I looked at [something specific about them] and I've got some ideas. See you at [time]!"
- This alone can increase show rates significantly

### SDR Best Practices

**Manual Text Follow-Up:**
- Text every lead manually (not automated)
- Keep texts short and conversational
- Focus on confirming the appointment, not selling
- Ask questions that get them talking: "What made you book the call?"
- Build rapport before the call happens

**Lead Qualification via Text:**
- Gauge their buying temperature through conversation
- Identify their biggest pain point before the call
- Note financial readiness signals
- Flag hot leads for priority scheduling

### Modern Show Rate Best Practices / Build Trust Rapidly

**Trust Building Before the Call:**
- Send value-driven content (not just reminders)
- Share relevant case studies matching their situation
- Provide quick wins they can implement immediately
- Personal touches: voice notes, video texts
- Social media connection requests

**The Goal:** By the time they show up, they should feel like they already know you. The call should feel like a continuation, not a cold start.

## The Educator Sales Role

### The Problem: Two Types of Prospects

**Person 1 (Framing Lovers):**
- Happy to go through all framing material
- Love long-form content
- Watch pre-call email sequence videos
- Research you extensively (YouTube binge, etc.)
- Prefer lengthy funnels like webinars, challenge funnels
- Salespeople love these -- they're "layup deals"

**Person 2 (Time Protectors):**
- Value their time differently
- Will NOT watch all the pre-call videos
- Would never register for a webinar or challenge
- Want information delivered one-on-one on the call
- Get deemed "unqualified" by sales teams -- but they're NOT
- Result in education calls instead of sales calls
- Typically two-call close instead of one-call close

> "Person number two gets deemed as unqualified. But that's just not true. They are at the point where it's typically a two call close, and they're on their first call." -- Jeremy Haynes

### The Educator Role

A new role in the sales team alongside setters and closers.

**How it works:**
1. Lead comes in, goes through normal setter/closer process
2. If Person 2 shows up on the call (hasn't consumed framing material)
3. Salesperson live-transfers to the Educator
4. Educator steps in and leads the call

**Identifying Person 2:**
- Ask: "Hey, did you get a chance to watch any of the videos or emails we sent over?"
- If "No" -- that's Person 2

**What the Educator does:**
- Answers questions one-on-one
- Walks through resources and decks
- Treats it like a custom webinar in real time
- Curates a list of resources for them to consume
- Sets 10-day timeline for a decision
- Sets follow-up call for closer (or closes themselves)

**Compensation:** Same as setters. If they close, pay like closers too.

**ROI:** Generally adds 0.5-1x bump to current ROAS because these leads were coming through anyway but getting wasted.

### Alternative: Train Existing Sales Team

Instead of hiring educators, reframe existing salespeople:
- Teach them about the two person types
- When Person 2 shows up, switch to education mode
- "I'm here to get you as much information as I can to make this decision. Let's target a 10-day window."
- "What do you want to know?" Then curate resources for them
- Don't treat broad questions ("How does this work?") as disqualifying

## Post-Sales Call Follow-Up Tactics

### For Prospects Who Didn't Close
1. Same-day recap email with key points discussed
2. Personalized video follow-up addressing their specific objections
3. Case study that mirrors their situation
4. Deadline or expiring offer to create urgency
5. Multiple touchpoints: email, text, social DM

### For Prospects Who Need More Time
1. Curated resource list (videos, case studies, testimonials)
2. Scheduled follow-up call (don't leave it open-ended)
3. Micro-commitments: "Can you watch this 5-minute video before our next call?"
4. Social proof drip: one success story per day via text/email

### For Prospects Who Said No
1. Graceful exit (don't burn the bridge)
2. Add to long-term nurture sequence
3. Re-engage with new offers, testimonials, or events
4. Check in quarterly with value-driven outreach

## Email Marketing Show Rate Best Practices

### Key Principles
- Every email should deliver value, not just remind
- Vary content types: stories, tips, case studies, proof
- Each email addresses a different objection or builds a different belief
- Always include next steps and call-to-action
- Personalize when possible (use survey data from confirmation page)
