# Book Funnel Mastery

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing - Master Internet Marketing

## The Book Funnel Model

- Offer a physical or digital book for free (customer pays shipping, typically $7-9.95)
- The book serves as a lead magnet and authority builder
- Backend offers (courses, coaching, masterminds) generate the real profit
- The book positions you as THE expert in your space

## Ad Creative Frameworks

### Hook Formulas

1. **The "Free Book" Hook**: "Get my new book FREE (just cover shipping)"
2. **The Curiosity Hook**: "The #1 mistake [audience] makes with [topic]"
3. **The Story Hook**: "How I went from [before] to [after] using this one strategy"
4. **The Authority Hook**: "After helping [number] people [achieve result], I wrote the book on it"
5. **The Contrarian Hook**: "Everything you've been told about [topic] is wrong. Here's why..."

### Video Ad Structure (Recommended)

1. **Hook (0-3 seconds)**: Pattern interrupt. Stop the scroll.
2. **Problem Agitation (3-15 seconds)**: Describe the pain point.
3. **Solution Introduction (15-30 seconds)**: Introduce the book. What's inside and why.
4. **Credibility/Social Proof (30-45 seconds)**: Results, testimonials, authority.
5. **Offer Reveal (45-60 seconds)**: Free + shipping. Exactly what they get.
6. **CTA (final 5-10 seconds)**: "Click the link below to get your free copy."

### Image Ad Best Practices
- Show the physical book (even if digital, create a mockup)
- Bold, readable text overlay
- "FREE" callout prominently displayed
- Show the author (builds trust)
- Contrasting colors that stand out in feed
- Clear value proposition in the image

## Ad Copy Frameworks

### Formula 1 - Problem/Solution
- Start with the problem (2-3 sentences)
- Introduce the book as the solution
- List 3-5 key takeaways
- Social proof element
- CTA with free + shipping offer

### Formula 2 - Story-Based
- Open with personal or client story
- Describe the transformation
- Connect to book content
- Offer the book as the roadmap
- CTA

### Formula 3 - List-Based
- "Inside this book you'll discover:"
- Bullet point 5-7 key benefits/secrets
- Each bullet creates curiosity
- Close with free offer + CTA

### Formula 4 - Testimonial-Led
- Open with powerful customer quote
- Expand on the result
- Connect to the book
- Offer details + CTA

### Headline Formulas
- "FREE Book: [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe]"
- "The [Topic] Book That [Number] [Audience Type] Can't Stop Talking About"
- "Get the [Topic] Playbook - FREE (Just Cover Shipping)"
- "[Number] Secrets to [Desired Result] - FREE Book"
- "Why [Number]+ [Audience] Trust This [Topic] Book"

## Creative Testing Strategy

### Phase 1: Hook Testing
- Create 3-5 different hooks for same offer
- Same body copy and CTA
- Run 3-5 days minimum
- Winner based on CTR and CPL

### Phase 2: Body Copy Testing
- Take winning hook, test 3-4 body copy angles
- Vary between short (<125 words), medium (125-250), long (250+)
- Test different proof elements

### Phase 3: Format Testing
- Test winning copy across video, image, carousel
- Test vertical (9:16), square (1:1), horizontal (16:9)
- Test UGC-style vs. polished production

### Phase 4: Scale Winners
- Top 2-3 performing ads
- Scale budget 20-30% every 2-3 days
- Monitor frequency and cost metrics
- Refresh creative when frequency hits 3.0+

## Targeting Strategy

### Warm Audiences
- Website visitors (30, 60, 90, 180 days)
- Email list
- Social media engagers
- Video viewers (25%, 50%, 75%, 95%)

### Lookalike Audiences
- 1% lookalike of book buyers
- 1% lookalike of backend buyers (most valuable)
- 1% lookalike of email list
- Stack multiple lookalikes in one ad set

### Cold Audiences
- Stack relevant interests, behaviors, demographics
- Target competitor audiences
- Target related authors and thought leaders
- Broad targeting (let algorithm optimize)

## Platform-Specific Tips

### Facebook/Instagram
- Lead with value, not the pitch
- Native-looking content outperforms polished ads
- Use Facebook Ad Library to study competitors
- Carousel ads work well for showing book contents/chapters
- Reels placement can drive cheap traffic

### YouTube
- First 5 seconds must hook (they can skip after 5)
- Longer form (2-5 minutes) works for higher-ticket backend
- Include book cover on screen, end screen with clear CTA

### TikTok
- Raw, authentic content outperforms polished
- Show physical book in hand
- "BookTok" style reviews
- Hook must be immediate (first 1-2 seconds)

## Key Metrics

- **CPC**: Target under $1-2 for cold traffic
- **CTR**: Target 1%+ for link clicks
- **Landing Page Conversion Rate**: Target 20-40% for free + shipping
- **Cost Per Acquisition**: Keep under $15-20 for book buyer
- **Backend Conversion Rate**: This is where profit comes from
- **ROAS**: Track 30, 60, 90-day including backend

## Common Mistakes

1. Focusing too much on the book itself instead of the transformation
2. Not testing enough creative variations
3. Scaling too fast (more than 20-30% budget increase per day)
4. Not tracking backend conversions (the book is a loss leader)
5. Using generic stock images instead of the actual book
6. Writing copy that's too clever instead of clear
7. Not having a strong enough hook in the first 3 seconds
8. Ignoring mobile optimization (60%+ of traffic is mobile)
9. Not refreshing creative before fatigue sets in
10. Targeting too narrow on cold audiences

## Budget Recommendations

- **Testing Phase**: $20-50/day per ad set, test for 3-7 days
- **Optimization Phase**: $50-100/day on winning ad sets
- **Scaling Phase**: Increase 20-30% every 2-3 days on winners
- **Kill Rule**: If no results after spending 2-3x your target CPA, kill it
