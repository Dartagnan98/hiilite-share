# Low-Ticket to High-Ticket Funnel Strategy

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing - Master Internet Marketing (Week 6 Lesson 7, Week 2 Lesson 14)

---

## Core Concept

The low-ticket to high-ticket model uses an affordable front-end offer ($7-$97) to acquire customers profitably, then ascends them through increasingly valuable (and expensive) offers. The front-end product covers ad costs and builds trust, while the real profit comes from the backend.

## Funnel Architecture

### Step 1: Low-Ticket Offer (The Entry Point)
- Price range: $7-$97
- Format: Book, mini-course, templates, toolkit, challenge ticket
- Goal: acquire customers at breakeven or small profit
- The product MUST deliver real value (not just a lead magnet wrapper)

### Step 2: Order Bumps
- Offered on the checkout page
- Price: 30-60% of main offer
- Target take rate: 30-50%
- Must be highly congruent with main purchase
- Example: "Add the done-for-you templates for just $27"

### Step 3: One-Time Offers (OTOs)
- Immediate post-purchase upsells
- OTO 1: Complementary resource ($47-$197)
- OTO 2: Advanced training ($97-$497)
- OTO 3: Done-with-you service ($197-$997)
- If they decline: show downsell (lower price or payment plan)

### Step 4: The Call Funnel Step
- Confirmation page after purchase becomes application/call booking page
- "Now that you have [product], let's make sure you implement it correctly"
- This bridges low-ticket buyers to high-ticket sales conversations
- The person already has trust (they bought something)

### Step 5: High-Ticket Backend
- Price range: $2,000-$25,000+
- Format: Coaching, consulting, mastermind, done-for-you service
- Sold via phone call (not self-checkout)
- Close rate is significantly higher than cold traffic calls because buyer already experienced your value

## Why This Model Works

### Economics
- Low-ticket front end covers or exceeds ad spend
- Every OTO increases average order value (AOV)
- By the time they hit the call funnel step, you're already profitable
- High-ticket backend is pure profit with warm leads

### Psychology
- Small purchase = low risk decision for prospect
- Delivery of value builds trust and credibility
- Progressive commitment: each purchase makes the next easier
- By the call, they've already invested money AND consumed your content
- Objection handling is easier: "You've already seen our quality"

### Comparison to Cold Call Funnels
| Metric | Cold Call Funnel | Low-Ticket to High-Ticket |
|--------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Cost per lead | Lower | Higher (but offset by front-end revenue) |
| Lead quality | Variable | Higher (they paid) |
| Show rate | 25-50% | 50-70%+ |
| Close rate | 10-20% | 20-40%+ |
| Sales cycle | Longer | Shorter (pre-sold) |
| Salesperson effort | More education needed | Less (they consumed content) |

---

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Front-end AOV**: Average of all purchases on checkout page + OTOs
- **Cost per customer**: Ad spend / total front-end buyers
- **Front-end ROAS**: Front-end revenue / ad spend (target 1x or better)
- **Call booking rate**: % of buyers who book calls
- **Show rate**: % of booked calls that show up
- **Close rate**: % of calls that become high-ticket clients
- **Blended ROAS**: Total revenue (front + back) / total ad spend

## Best Practices

1. Front-end product must be genuinely valuable -- if it sucks, the backend dies
2. OTOs must be congruent (related to what they just bought)
3. Call funnel step should feel like a natural next step, not a hard pivot
4. Train sales team to reference front-end purchase: "You already have [product], so you know our quality"
5. Email sequence after purchase should deliver value AND warm them up for the call
6. Track 30/60/90-day ROAS -- the real money comes from backend over time
