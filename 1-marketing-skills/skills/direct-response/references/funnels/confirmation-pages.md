# Confirmation Page Best Practices

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing - Master Internet Marketing + Backend Selling Systems

---

## Core Concept

> "Most marketers think the marketing ends the moment someone opts in. Your confirmation page is the first brushstroke in painting a picture of how legit you are and how badly they need what you're offering." -- Jeremy Haynes

## Show Rate Variables (4 Main Factors)

1. How much your audience cares about your webinar claim
2. Confirmation page framing
3. Email/text reminder and framing sequences
4. Content remarketing (Hammer Them ad strategy)

---

## Confirmation Page Elements

### 1. Short Survey (2-3 questions max)
- "What's your #1 challenge related to [Your Topic]?"
- "How soon are you looking to achieve [Desired Outcome]?"
- Use answers to tailor emails, ad copy, and webinar content
- People are pumped right after registering -- prime time for data
- Their exact language becomes your ad copy gold

### 2. Personalized Video

**For Warm Traffic (people who know you):**
- Warm welcome from you/host
- Sneak peek of main takeaway
- Importance of showing up live
- Mention freebies or big reveals

**For Cold Traffic (people who don't know you):**
- Slightly longer video
- Mini "about me" + proof you're the real deal
- Quick success story, highlight big client wins
- End with clear next step: "Mark your calendar. We're going live at 8pm sharp."

### 3. Bonus Content
- Link to relevant YouTube videos or curated playlist
- Value-packed clips solving small pieces of their problem
- "Here's who I am" content for new audiences
- Keeps your name top of mind, leaves them wanting more

### 4. Calendar/Reminder Prompts
- Mark their calendar with exact date and time
- Bookmark the webinar link
- Whitelist your email
- "Check your email for [Subject Line]"

### 5. Social Proof
- Testimonials on page or referenced in video
- "Hundreds of people have already used this method to [Outcome]"

### 6. Urgency
- Seats might be limited
- Replay only up for short window

## Pre-Webinar Email Sequence (10 Emails)

### Email 1: Welcome + Confirmation
- Congratulate on registering
- When and where it's happening
- Set the tone

### Email 2: Why You Can't Miss This
- Hammer home the #1 outcome/promise
- Spark curiosity -- tease "secret" highlights

### Email 3: Speaker Spotlight / Credibility Boost
- Short background: credentials, track record, major wins
- "I've done X, Y, Z, so I know exactly how to solve your problem"

### Email 4: The Pain Point / Problem Explanation
- Agitate the core problem
- "We'll cover exactly how to fix this on the webinar"

### Email 5: Future-Pacing & Success Stories
- Mini-case study of someone who crushed it
- Big transformation "before vs. after"

### Email 6: Insider Preview of the Content
- Drop a small actionable tip for a quick win
- Promise bigger payoff if they attend

### Email 7: Objection Buster
- Time: "90 minutes that'll shortcut your next 6 months"
- Cost: "Yes, it's free training -- because [reason]"
- Relevance: "Here's how it applies to you specifically"

### Email 8: Reminder #1 (Day Before)
- Big bold "Add to Calendar" link
- Top 3-4 bullet benefits

### Email 9: Reminder #2 (Morning Of)
- Start time, time zones, last-minute instructions
- Freebies, bonuses, Q&A that'll happen live

### Email 10: Last-Call / Starting Soon
- "We're going live in [X hours/minutes]"
- Quick bullet list of biggest benefits
- Link to live room -- make it super obvious

## SMS Sequence for Show Rate

> "SMS sits at the top of the totem pole for open rates. The second that phone buzzes, people check -- and that is exactly where you want your reminder sitting." -- Jeremy Haynes

### Rules for SMS
1. Keep it straightforward and personal -- like a text from a friend
2. First two messages: hype them up, NO link
3. Last two: drop the direct link, make it stupid easy to join

### The Four-Text Sequence

**Text 1: Early Reminder (No Link)**
> Hey [FirstName]! Just a heads-up: [WebinarName] is coming up soon. Mark your calendar for [Date/Time] -- I'll be sharing some major insights you won't want to miss. See you there!

**Text 2: Day-Before Check-In (No Link)**
> Quick Reminder: Tomorrow's the day for [WebinarName] -- we'll cover [short teaser or major benefit]. Get pumped, because this is going to be a game-changer. See you live!

**Text 3: Final Hour Hype (Include Link)**
> [FirstName], it's nearly showtime! We go live in an hour with [WebinarName]. This is your shot to learn [key promise/benefit]. Jump in here when it's time: [Link]

**Text 4: Starting Now (Include Link)**
> We're live! [WebinarName] just kicked off -- don't miss the good stuff. Click here to join us now: [Link] Let's make it happen!

## Content Remarketing ("Hammer Them" Strategy)

Instead of leaving people ice-cold after they register, serve them a high-frequency stream of content that frames them to show up.

### Why It Works

1. **Building Familiarity Fast**: Mere-exposure effect cranks up familiarity bias
2. **Pre-Handling Objections**: Kill "I'm too busy" or "not sure if it's for me" with strategic bite-sized content
3. **Shortening Hesitation Gap**: 15-20 rapid-fire pieces lock in engagement between registration and go-live
4. **Positioning as Go-To Pro**: Shifts from "Eh, we'll see..." to "I'm definitely showing up"

### How to Execute

1. **Segment Webinar Registrants** -- prime candidates for content hammering
2. **Deploy 15-20 Pieces Over ~72 Hours**
   - Short, vertical videos tackling 4 pillars: Questions, 2nd Layer Questions, Objections, Expectations
   - Each piece pre-answers why they should show up
3. **Retarget Via Ads Manager**
   - Only registrants see the content
   - Goal: 15-20+ exposures in 72-hour lead-up
   - Exclude people who watched 3 seconds of each piece so they see new content
4. **Watch Show Rate Jump**
