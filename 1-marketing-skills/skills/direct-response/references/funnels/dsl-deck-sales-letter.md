# DSL (Deck Sales Letter) Funnel Strategy

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing - Master Internet Marketing

---

## What Is a DSL?

A sales letter presented in slide deck format (PowerPoint/Google Slides) used as a landing page. Instead of a traditional long-form text sales letter or VSL, the prospect clicks through slides at their own pace.

## Why Use a DSL

- People are increasingly resistant to watching long videos (VSLs)
- Prospect controls the pace -- they click through at their own speed
- Higher engagement because the prospect is actively participating (clicking)
- Works well for cold traffic -- feels less "salesy" than a video pitch
- Lower production cost than video -- no filming, editing, or on-camera talent
- Easy to test and iterate -- just swap slides
- Can be used for: webinar registrations, lead magnets, call funnels, applications, direct purchases

## DSL vs VSL Comparison

| Factor | DSL | VSL |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Production cost | Low | High |
| Prospect control | High (self-paced) | Low (set pace) |
| Engagement | Active (clicking) | Passive (watching) |
| Time to create | Hours | Days/weeks |
| Easy to A/B test | Yes (swap slides) | Hard (re-film) |
| Cold traffic performance | Strong | Strong |
| Mobile experience | Excellent | Good |

## The DSL Funnel Structure

### Step 1: Ad (Traffic Source)
- Facebook/Instagram, YouTube, Google ads drive traffic to DSL landing page

### Step 2: DSL Landing Page
- Series of slides the user clicks through
- Each slide = one key point, visual, or story element
- Prospect clicks "Next" to advance
- Clear CTA at the end of the deck

### Step 3: CTA / Conversion Event
- Book a call / schedule appointment
- Register for a webinar
- Fill out an application
- Purchase directly
- Opt in for a lead magnet

### Step 4: Follow Up
- Email sequence, retargeting ads, SMS, Messenger

## DSL Slide Structure (Persuasion Sequence)

### Opening Slides (1-3): Hook & Pattern Interrupt
- Grab attention immediately
- Make a bold claim or statement
- Create curiosity that compels them to click "Next"
- Pattern interrupt -- something unexpected

### Problem Slides (4-8): Agitate the Pain
- Identify the prospect's core problem
- Agitate the pain -- make them feel the weight
- Show deep understanding of their situation
- Use specific scenarios they relate to
- Make the cost of inaction clear

### Solution Slides (9-14): Present Your Mechanism
- Introduce your unique mechanism or solution
- Explain WHY your approach is different
- Don't reveal everything -- maintain curiosity
- Position your solution as the logical next step
- Use data, proof, and logic

### Proof Slides (15-20): Build Credibility
- Case studies and results
- Testimonials and screenshots
- Before/after comparisons
- Media mentions, credentials, authority markers
- Social proof (number of customers, students, etc.)

### Offer Slides (21-25): Present the Offer
- Clearly state what they get
- Stack the value
- Show the bonuses
- Present pricing (if applicable)
- Remove risk with guarantees
- Create urgency/scarcity if genuine

### CTA Slides (26-28): Close
- Clear, single call to action
- Remind them of the key benefit
- Restate the guarantee
- Final push to take action

## Key Design Principles

1. **One idea per slide** -- never overcrowd
2. **Large text** -- must be readable on mobile
3. **Use visuals** -- charts, screenshots, images to break up text
4. **Keep slides scannable** -- bullet points over paragraphs
5. **Use contrast and color** -- highlight key phrases, bold strategically
6. **Progress bar** -- show how far they are through the deck
7. **Mobile-first design** -- majority of traffic is mobile
8. **Brand consistency** -- use brand colors, fonts, logo throughout

## Funnel Variations

### DSL to Call Funnel
Ad -> DSL -> Application Page -> Calendar Booking -> Confirmation Page

### DSL to Webinar Funnel
Ad -> DSL -> Webinar Registration -> Webinar -> Sales Page -> Order Page

### DSL to Lead Magnet Funnel
Ad -> DSL -> Opt-In Page -> Lead Magnet Delivery -> Email Sequence -> Offer

### DSL to Direct Purchase Funnel
Ad -> DSL -> Sales Page -> Order Page -> Upsell -> Thank You

## Metrics to Track

- **Click-through rate on slides** -- what % make it to the end
- **Drop-off points** -- which slide do people stop at
- **CTA conversion rate** -- what % who see the CTA take action
- **Cost per lead/acquisition** -- standard ad metrics
- **Time on page** -- longer is generally better

## Best Practices

1. **Test multiple hooks** -- opening slides determine everything
2. **Keep the deck to 25-35 slides** -- enough to persuade, not so many they bounce
3. **Use story format** -- stories are more engaging than bullet points alone
4. **Include a "who this is for" slide** -- self-qualification increases quality
5. **Include a "who this is NOT for" slide** -- creates exclusivity
6. **End every slide with a reason to click next** -- maintain momentum
7. **Use curiosity gaps** -- tease what's coming on the next slide
8. **Test slide order** -- sometimes moving proof earlier increases conversions
9. **Retarget people who made it past slide 10** -- they showed interest
10. **Segment retargeting by drop-off point** -- slide 20 needs different message than slide 5

## When to Use a DSL

- When you don't want to create video content
- When you want to quickly test offers before investing in a VSL
- When your audience prefers reading over watching
- When you want higher engagement from mobile traffic
- When you need fast iteration on messaging
- As a complement to your VSL (some people prefer reading)
- For complex offers that need visual explanation
