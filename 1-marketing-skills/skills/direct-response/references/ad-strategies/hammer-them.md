# Hammer Them Ad Strategy

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing
Type: Content Retargeting Strategy for Post-Lead/Post-Action Audiences
Best For: Personal brands, high ticket product/service businesses, companies that raise capital, ecommerce, lead gen, anyone with high quality content wanting maximum qualified leads and sales per day.

---

## The Problem

Running paid ads and generating leads but struggling with:
- Low show rates
- Long sales cycles
- Low sales conversions

This happens because you fail to frame leads successfully prior to the sales call or purchase decision. Questions unanswered, leads not excited and sold before the call.

---

## What It Does

Leverages high-frequency, contextual content marketing to pre-frame objections, build trust, and prime leads to eagerly hand over their credit card.

By "hammering" prospects with **15-20+ pieces of hyper-relevant content in the critical 72-hour window** before a sales call:
- Show rates skyrocket
- More pay-in-fulls
- Shorter sales cycles
- Better feedback from sales team on lead quality

**Core Philosophy:** Turn sales reps into cashiers -- lay-up deals as easy to close as organic leads, at paid advertising scale and consistency.

---

## Why It Works (4 Reasons)

### Reason 1: Familiarity Bias
We prefer and trust things we are exposed to repeatedly. With each content piece consumed, familiarity and trust grows. Even without conscious engagement, seeing your name, face, and message repeatedly builds subconscious trust.

**Familiarity progression:**
- Low preference (initial contact)
- Neutral (growing comfort at ~5 exposures)
- High preference (strong trust at ~15-20 exposures)

### Reason 2: Mere-Exposure Effect
People develop preference for things simply because they are familiar. Sheer repetition of exposure increases affinity and trust, even without clicking or consciously consuming.

### Reason 3: Selling the Next Step, Not the End Result
Content does not sell the product. It sells the NEXT STEP -- showing up to the sales call. Focus on building trust, providing value, addressing objections related to taking that next step.

### Reason 4: Objection Handling Before the Call
Proactively address common questions, concerns, and objections BEFORE the sales call. Leads arrive more informed, confident, and ready. Sales conversation focuses on deepening relationship instead of overcoming objections.

## The 4 Pillars of High-Converting Content

All pieces: short, direct, vertical videos optimized for reels. Post to IG, cross-post to FB. Do NOT use copyrighted music (blocks paid promotion).

### Pillar 1: Questions
Address common questions about your product/service/offer.

**How to identify:** What would YOU need to know to be sold on showing up to a call?

**Examples:**
- What is the overarching reason you are selling what you sell?
- How does this compare to alternatives?
- What makes this unique or superior?
- Key talking points from your VSL (break into separate pieces)
- Who is the offer best for?
- Any question sales team is frustrated having to answer on calls

### Pillar 2: 2nd Layer Questions
Follow-up questions that arise from your initial answers.

**The Bagel Example:**
- 1st Q: Why do New Yorkers say their bagels are better?
- 1st A: The water is better.
- 2nd Q: What do you mean the water is better?
- 2nd A: Low concentrations of calcium and magnesium make it softer.
Without the 2nd answer, you are left unsatisfied. Same in sales.

**Coaching program follow-ups:**
- What specific strategies or techniques do you teach?
- Walk me through the step-by-step process?
- What level of support or accountability?
- How long until clients see results?

You can handle both 1st and 2nd layer in the same video.

### Pillar 3: Objections
Pre-frame and handle objections BEFORE the call so salespeople become cashiers.

**Common objections:**
- Price too high / cannot afford it
- Not sure I have time to commit
- How do I know this works for me specifically?
- Tried similar solutions, no results
- Competitor offers similar for less

**If price is the sticking point, create content that:**
- Breaks down true cost of NOT solving the problem (financial, emotional, opportunity)
- Reframes price as investment with clear ROI
- Offers flexible payment/financing
- Uses customer success stories showcasing value

Normalize and validate concerns while providing compelling counterargument.

### Pillar 4: Expectations
Shape prospects' expectations for what success looks like.

**Content that:**
- Outlines steps and milestones of your process
- Sets realistic timelines for progress and results
- Specifies effort and commitment required from client
- Shares relatable success stories showing full customer journey arc
- Paints vivid picture of end result and transformation

## Campaign Setup (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Build Campaign Foundation
1. Facebook Ads Manager -> Create
2. Objective: **Engagement**
3. Name: "Hammer Them - [Your Offer/Audience]"
4. **Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO)** on
5. Daily budget: $50/day (small audiences), $100+/day (larger)
6. Budget defined by hitting frequency of 15-20+ in 72 hours
7. Bid strategy: Highest volume

### Step 2: Target Audience
1. Name ad set: "VID 1 - retargeting X audience"
2. Optimization: Video Views -> ThruPlay
3. Target the key tight timeframe audience:
   - Recent leads who booked a call but have not had it yet
   - DMs talking to setters actively
   - People who showed up to call but did not buy
4. **Minimum 100 people** in audience
5. **Disable "Expand Audience"** -- laser-focused targeting only

### Step 3: Choose Content
- Exclude audience network
- 1 piece of content per ad set
- Post to Instagram, cross-post to Facebook
- At ad level: select "existing post"
- No copyrighted music

### Step 4: Exclusion Audiences (The Magic)
Create **3-second video view Custom Audience for each video:**
1. Audiences tab -> Video source
2. "People who viewed at least 3 seconds"
3. Select specific video
4. Retention: 365 days
5. Name: "VID 1 [Name] | 3 Sec View"
6. Repeat for every piece of content

### Step 5: Duplicate and Repeat
1. Duplicate "VID 1" ad set for each content piece
2. Rename (VID 2, VID 3, etc.)
3. Swap video at ad level
4. Each new ad set excludes the 3-second view audience for its video
5. Guarantees each lead gets FRESH content until they have seen your entire arsenal

**Ideal total:** 30-50 pieces of content = 30-50 unique ad sets

### Step 6: Assess and Adapt
Monitor **Frequency** and **Reach**.
**Target:** 15-20+ frequency within 72 hours.
If low: crank budget, tighten audience, evaluate hooks.

### Final State
Campaign with CBO, $100-$200/day, 25-50 ad sets, each with unique content. 3-second exclusions ensure rotation. Content is just content -- no CTAs.

## Long Form Content Addition

### Why Long Form Is Back
Short form alone warms leads to awareness but stops short of giving **complete safety** in buying decisions. Long form provides the deep dive that reduces friction.

**Short Form:** Rapidly builds familiarity (15-20+ impressions in 72 hours)
**Long Form:** Provides safety and depth. Answers complex questions, reveals method in detail.

### 3 Critical Nurture Lines
1. Short form ads
2. Long form content
3. Sales team manual outreach (texts, calls, 100% inbox deliverability)

### Long Form Setup
- **Separate campaign** from short form (prevents short form from dominating)
- One video per ad set
- Same retargeting audience as short form
- Exclude 3-second viewers per video
- ABO recommended ($5-$10/day per ad set)
- Aim for 15-30 pieces of long form

### Value Density Rule
- Open strong: first 5-10 seconds call out big problem or outcome
- Cut fillers: remove "hey guys," "like and subscribe," casual chatter
- Every ~45 seconds: drop a banger insight

### Minimum/Maximum Length
- Shorter long form: 10-15 minutes
- Longer long form: 30 minutes to 2 hours
- 2-hour podcasts cost ~$1.07 for full watch

## Sending Six Emails a Day

As part of Hammer Them, send six targeted emails/day in the critical window between booking and call. Standard "your call is coming up" emails are not enough.

## Measuring Impact

### Primary Metrics
- Show up and close rates (the ultimate measure)
- Frequency: 15-20+ in target window
- Reach: how many unique leads hit
- Relevance scores: 7+ means content is landing

### Qualitative Intel
- Are reps reporting positive difference in lead quality?
- Are new customers referencing specific content pieces?
- Frontline insights are pure gold for optimization

### Big Picture
- Short form (30-50 pieces): familiarity, questions, objections, framing
- Long form (15-30 pieces): safety, depth, "ready to sign" conviction
- Sales team: personalized touch, reference content in outreach
