# Venus Fly Trap Ad Strategy

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing
Type: Sequential Video Content Strategy + Direct Response
Best For: Personal brands, companies, anyone with high quality social media content. Works for any funnel type. Designed for lower to mid budgets.

---

## Core Concept

Get future buyers indoctrinated from not knowing you (cold audience) to knowing about you and having the right biases, beliefs, and objections handled BEFORE presenting offers. You create a video SEQUENCE for ideal future customers to go through, then run direct response and retargeting AFTER they go from not knowing you to knowing you.

**Key Difference from Forester:** VFT is a SEQUENCE (ordered content journey). Forester is a spider web (random content exposure). VFT is more controlled but more complex to set up.

---

## Why It Works

- Increases chances of those being targeted to buy
- Lowers cost of conversions (targeting warm audiences who know who you are)
- Right frame of mind to buy by time you start making pitches
- Builds brand with social proof from views, likes, comments, shares
- Lowers advertising costs significantly
  - Cost per engagement/video views: $0.01-$0.02
  - Conversion campaigns off-platform: $0.50-$2.00 average
- Content cycle bin keeps people warmed up and consistently framed
- Prevents conditioning to ignore your ads from over-pitching

| Type | Count | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| Priming Videos (1, 2, 3) | 3 | Sequential warming, each with specific purpose |

## The Process (Detailed Video Breakdown)

### Video 1: Cold Audience Introduction

**Objective:** Engagement + Video View (ThruPlay)
**Purpose:** Establish authority bias, social proof bias, and something of very broad value. Absolutely NO call to actions.
**Length:** 1-3 minutes
**Target:** Cold audiences -- same you would use for direct response. These people do not know you and do not care about you.
**Goal:** Get them to watch 10 seconds (average time on a post is 1.8 seconds).

### Video 2: Brand Promise + Positioning

**Objective:** Engagement + Video View (ThruPlay)
**Purpose:** Establish the brand promise in a subliminal way. Make the biggest claim or boldest statement your solution caters to. Plant and relate to deep buying motives.
**Method:** Value-driven content leveraging curiosity bias -- be so valuable the person thinks about how they had not known about you before.
**Main intention:** Establish your positioning (big step).

### Video 3 (3.1, 3.2, 3.3): Category Content

**Objective:** Engagement + Video View (ThruPlay)
**Purpose:** Test 3-5 different forms of "category content." Start managing beliefs and introduce the categories your business solutions cater to.

**The Rotisserie Chicken Analogy:**
People do not buy for one reason. A grocery store only marketing to one type of buyer would sell less. The rotisserie chicken buyer could be:
- Family that does not want to cook tonight
- Fitness buyer wanting calories and protein
- Person on lunch break bringing food for co-workers

Your product likely has 20+ different deep buying motives correlating to hooks and angles.

**Minimum 3 categories = Videos 3.1, 3.2, 3.3**

### Conversion Ads 1-3: Direct Response

**Purpose:** Make your pitch. Create 3-5 variations with different hooks and angles.
**Target:** People just taken from cold to warm through the content sequence.

**Ad Types for Warmed Audiences:**

#### 60-Second Video (cold audience)
- 3-4 problems your ideal customer has
- Talk about your free thing or product being available
- Go right into 3-4 results they get by taking action
- CTA to press button or click link

#### 2-Minute Video (cold audience)
- 3-4 problems, free thing or product available, 3-4 results/solutions
- Can also storytell plugging the same format

#### 4 Retargeting Videos
- Bring up different problems the potential customer is experiencing
- Go into detail, relate to solution and results
- Call out actions you know they have taken: viewed offer pages, watched ad videos, seen things several times but have not taken action

#### 2 x 15-Second Vertical (Stories)
- Quick CTAs -- swipe up
- Cover 1-2 problems and solution/results by swiping up

#### 2 Retargeting Videos for Stories (15 sec, vertical)
- Tell them to take action on thing they forgot to act on and why in under 15 seconds

#### "You Didn't Buy, Come Back" Video
- Direct call out that they saw an offer/ad/opportunity but did not take action
- Call them out creatively
- Remind them of the 3-4 results they can get

### Retargeting Ads 1-5
Same approach as conversion ads, targeted at people who saw offer but did not convert.

### Content Bin (Videos 4-11+)

**Objective:** Engagement + Video View (ThruPlay)
**Purpose:** Ongoing belief management and objection handling through content. While ideal customers are being pitched, value-driven content stays in front of them.

**Effects:**
- Retains omnipresent effect
- Prevents conditioning to ignore ads from over-pitching
- Minimum: 8 additional content pieces (Videos 4-11)

## The Reverse Fly Trap

Source: Week 2 Lesson 17
Type: Budget-friendly content retargeting strategy
Best For: Lower budgets ($5K-$20K/month). Any business, any vertical.

### How It Differs from Venus Fly Trap

| | Venus Fly Trap | Reverse Fly Trap |
|---|---------------|-----------------|
| Order | Content FIRST, then direct response | Direct response FIRST, then content |
| Budget | Mid to high | Low ($5K-$20K/month) |
| When content appears | Before the pitch | After they visit landing page and do NOT convert |

### The Process

1. **Start with direct response ads** (cold or warm traffic)
   - CTA for webinars, trust funnels, opt-in pages, application funnels
   - Drive to landing page

2. **If they opt in:** treat them like a customer (onboarding content, framing before calls)

3. **If they do NOT convert:** This is the focus of the Reverse Fly Trap
   - Retarget with a **content cycle bin** (no CTAs, pure content)
   - Simultaneously retarget with **direct response retargeting ads**

### Why Content Works Better Than Ads Alone

Content gets past the **amygdala**. People are naturally defensive against being sold. When scrolling Facebook/Instagram:
- People are open to suggestions, mindlessly scrolling, being entertained
- Content looks like what they are there to see -- they are open to viewing it
- Ads have call-to-action buttons and headlines -- people recognize them and skip
- Even with "sponsored" label, content gets more consumption than ads

**Proof:** Check Facebook Ads Manager "video engagement" column. Content videos show steady viewing with many people reaching the end. Ad videos show steep drop-off below 10 seconds.

### Content Cycle Bin Strategy

What should go in the bin:
- **Authority framing:** Be known as the person/product to go to
- **Social proof:** Testimonials, product reviews (use as content, not ads)
- **Applied psychology:** Leverage cognitive biases for framing
- **Objection handling:** Address concerns without being sales-y

### Best Use Cases

- **Application funnels:** Person does not fill out application -> content frames them -> retargeting ad -> they come back and fill it out
- **Trust funnels:** Multi-video series
- **Webinars:** Someone does not opt in -> content with webinar clips -> retargeting -> higher opt-in probability
- **E-commerce:** Proven at scale by Harmon Brothers (Purple mattresses, Lumen deodorant, Poo-Pourri, Squatty Potty)

### Key Advantages
- Not asset-intensive (most businesses already have content)
- Easy to execute
- Works for any business, any vertical
- Designed for lower budgets
- Proven across dozens of industries from student feedback

## Strategy Selection Guide

| Budget | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| $5K-$20K/month | Reverse Fly Trap |
| $10K-$50K/month | Venus Fly Trap or Forester |
| $50K+/month | Tornado, Harvester, or layered approach |

| Content Volume | Strategy |
|---------------|----------|
| Limited (< 20 pieces) | Reverse Fly Trap or VFT |
| Moderate (20-100 pieces) | Forester or VFT |
| High (100+ pieces) | Tornado |

| Control Need | Strategy |
|-------------|----------|
| Ordered sequence | Venus Fly Trap |
| Random exposure | Forester |
| Maximum saturation | Tornado |
| Post-landing-page only | Reverse Fly Trap |
