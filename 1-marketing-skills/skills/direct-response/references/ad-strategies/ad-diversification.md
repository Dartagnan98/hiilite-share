## The Andromeda Algorithm Shift

In July 2025, Meta rolled out the Andromeda algorithm update. This fundamentally changed how creative performs in the ad platform.

### The Old Approach (Dying)
- Create interchangeable creative components (independent hook, body, CTA)
- Film 10-15 different hooks, edit onto the same body/CTA
- Swap components freely
- Still has some effectiveness if producing 50+ creatives per week

### The New Reality (July 2025+)
- Hook-swapping lasts for much shorter duration
- Exposes you to significant volatility unless maintaining 50+ creatives/week output
- **What works now:** Film entire ad from start to finish as one cohesive unit
- **Key insight:** Hook, body, and CTA must be completely aligned around ONE specific reason someone would be interested
- Unique ads around specific reasons have much longer lifespan and better performance

### Real Example: Sober Living Homes
During July (Andromeda rollout), costs did not fluctuate once. Maintained consistent $40-50 cost per qualified call while spending ~$40K.

**The critical factor:** 15 completely unique creatives, each built around a different reason someone would be interested. Each filmed start to finish with hook, body, CTA all aligned.

#### Ad 1 - Business Opportunity Angle
Hook: "You ever heard of 9 people paying rent on one single family home legally and having the government help you fill it?"
Body: Business model focus -- sober living strategy, not Airbnb or Section 8, regular house conversion, 2-3 people per bedroom, government support

#### Ad 2 - Social Problem Angle
Hook: "Let's be real. America has a drug problem. But what happens after someone gets sober?"
Body: Social issue focus -- rehab ends, insurance stops, people have nowhere to go, sober living as the solution

Completely different psychological triggers. Same business, completely different reasons to be interested.

## The Two Paths Forward

### Path 1: High Volume (50+ Creatives Per Week)
- Producing 50+ creatives per week
- Some ecom brands went from 3-4 to 50+/week and maintained stability
- Does not necessarily need wide range of diverse messaging
- Sheer volume overcomes algorithm changes
- Requires complete overhaul of creative production (~200/month)
- Need to relaunch campaigns and follow ad fatigue protocols weekly

### Path 2: Diverse Messaging (15-20 Unique Ads/Month) [RECOMMENDED]
- 15-20 completely unique ads per month with wide creative diversification
- Each ad built around a different core reason someone would be interested
- Accounts implementing this in July 2025 experienced NO cost inflation
- More manageable for most businesses
- Often produces better results with less resource investment
- Film 15 videos once or twice a month
- 3X less work than Path 1

## The Core Principle: Creative Controls Targeting Now

**The single most important concept:** Creative controls targeting in the current algorithm environment.

- Without diverse messaging, you get less reach regardless of volume
- Need diverse reasons and messaging angles to reach more people effectively
- Diverse messaging tapping different psychological triggers gives the algorithm more ways to match ads with different audience segments
- Better reach AND better performance than high volume alone

## Key Takeaways

1. **Creative controls targeting now** -- the single most important concept
2. **Diverse messaging > Volume** for most businesses (Path 2 recommended)
3. **Each ad must be a cohesive unit** -- hook, body, CTA all aligned around one specific reason/angle
4. **15-20 unique ads per month** is the sweet spot
5. **The 100 hooks** give psychological variety to feed Andromeda
6. **True diversification** = different core reasons to care about your offer, not just different people saying the same thing

## Practical Implementation

### Step-by-Step: Creating 15 Diverse Ads

1. **List all reasons someone would be interested in your offer** (aim for 20+)
2. **Group into clusters** of similar psychological triggers
3. **Select 15 that span the widest range** of emotional/logical appeals
4. **For each reason, write a complete ad** (hook, body, CTA all aligned)
5. **Film each as a standalone piece** -- do not reuse body copy across ads
6. **Test all 15 simultaneously** in the same campaign
7. **Monitor which angles perform best** and iterate on winners

### Example: 15 Angles for a Marketing Agency

1. Revenue growth angle: "Your competitors are outspending you 3:1"
2. Time freedom angle: "Stop being the bottleneck in your own business"
3. Social proof angle: "347 businesses scaled past $1M with this approach"
4. Fear angle: "The algorithm change that's killing small advertisers"
5. Authority angle: "What I learned managing $50M in ad spend"
6. Process angle: "The 4-step system behind every 7-figure campaign"
7. Objection handling angle: "Why your last agency failed you"
8. Identity angle: "Built for founders who refuse to settle"
9. Urgency angle: "Q4 is 90 days away -- your competitors are already building"
10. Contrarian angle: "Why most marketing advice is designed to keep you broke"
11. Specificity angle: "From $2.3M to $8.7M in 11 months -- here's the playbook"
12. Escape angle: "Stop trading hours for dollars in your agency"
13. Community angle: "Join 200+ founders who scaled past the plateau"
14. Education angle: "The metric that matters more than ROAS"
15. Story angle: "I almost quit advertising until I discovered this"

### Production Workflow

**Twice a month, schedule a 2-3 hour filming session:**
- Review the 15 angles
- Write complete scripts for each (30-90 seconds each)
- Film each as a self-contained ad
- Multi-cam setup for variation creation
- Edit within 48 hours
- Deploy all 15 within the same week

### Measuring Success

Track these metrics per ad (not just per campaign):
- Cost per result by ad
- CTR by ad
- Reach by ad (high reach = majority hook, low reach = minority hook)
- Frequency by ad
- Video view retention curves

The ads with highest reach at lowest frequency are your majority hooks. Double down on those angles with variations.
