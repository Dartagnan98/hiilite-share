# The Forester Ad Strategy

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing
Type: Content-First Audience Warming Strategy
Best For: Any company with good content and all funnel types. Works with lower ad spend budgets, giving room to scale up. Personal brands, companies, anyone with high quality social media content.

---

## Core Concept

Get future buyers who do not know you (cold audience) into an audience aware of your brand for less than $0.01 (warm audience). Then convert that newly warm audience with direct response ads and more content to keep CPMs down and get a high volume of excited buyers.

**Key Mechanism:** Cold audience -> content with no pitches/CTAs -> engagement + video view campaigns -> Facebook audience tool captures video viewers and page/post interactors -> warm audience -> direct response ads.

---

## Why It Works

- FB tracks interactions and factors lower CPM costs for previously engaged traffic
- CPMs for warm traffic are lower than ice cold traffic
- Building retargetable list for up to 1 year from last interaction
- Higher buyer volumes for less cost from gaming the CPM system
- FB puts algorithmic weight on past interaction when targeting

## Difference from Venus Fly Trap

VFT runs audience through a SEQUENCE of content before direct response. Forester's goal is simply to get warm audience created from people watching 10 seconds of 1 video out of 5-12 total -- THEN hit with direct response. Not a sequence but a spider web where people can get caught into becoming aware.

---

## The Process

### Step 1: Content Cycle Bin 1 (Cold Audience)

Take 5-12 pieces of content that capture attention, frame the brand right, and get people interested.

**Campaign Setup:**
- Objective: Engagement
- 5-12 ad sets within one campaign
- Ad Set Budget Optimization (ABO)
- Each ad set: same cold audience targeting (lookalike stack of past customers, qualified leads, email list. If unavailable, use cold targeting for ideal buyer profile)
- Budget: $5/day to however much per ad set
- 1 video per ad set

**Duplicate campaign** and change objective to video views optimized for ThruPlay.

**Goal:** Get them to watch 10 seconds so you can capture that audience and hit with direct response.

**Trust FB algorithms:** FB tracks 52,000 average data points per user. Let FB decide which person sees which content rather than forcing human viewpoints.

### Step 2: Content Cycle Bin 2 + Direct Response (Warm Audience)

**Warm audience definition:** People who watched 10 seconds of Bin 1 videos, or interacted with social profiles (FB and IG).

**Select ALL warm audience options:**
- Visited social profile
- Engaged with post or ad
- Pressed ad button
- Saved post
- Messaged page or account
- 10-second video viewers

**Audience retention:** 365-day buckets (widens audience, lowers costs).

**Direct Response Campaign:** Conversion objective for desired outcome (purchase, lead, scheduled call, etc.)

**Content Cycle Bin 2:**
- 5-12 ad sets with 1 video each
- Different content from cold -- focused on framing:
  - Supporting the sales cycle
  - Establishing right beliefs about product and brand
  - Establishing biases (credibility, authority, social proof)
  - Handling future objections
- Budget: same amount per ad set as cold

**Also run cold direct response** alongside warm. See the difference.

### Cold vs Warm Performance Data

| Campaign | Results | Reach | Freq | Cost/Result | Spent |
|----------|---------|-------|------|-------------|-------|
| Warm - Complete Registration | 80 | 60,622 | 2.20 | $14.09 | $1,127 |

**Key findings:**
- Warm audience: HALF the cost per result
- More than double the booked call volume
- Buyer volume also more than double the closing rate of cold
- Cold audience more skeptical but still bought profitably

## Scaling Rules

1. **Scale Content Cycle Bin 1 FIRST** -- fills warm audiences for Bin 2 and direct response
2. Bin 2 and direct response only scale when more audience volume from scaling Bin 1
3. Keeps costs extremely low for building audiences

## Content Selection

### Bin 1 (Cold Content)
- Goal is NOT framing -- get them into warm audience at extremely low cost
- Focus on attention-capturing content that gets 10+ second views
- Lower the cost to acquire into warm audience

### Bin 2 (Warm Content)
- Focus on framing -- averages frequency of 3-5 pieces per month
- Establish right beliefs about product/brand
- Establish biases (credibility, authority, social proof)
- Handle future objections
- Support the sales cycle

---

## Key Stats

- Cost per engagement in Bin 1: $0.01 average, as low as $0.0001
- Warm audience cost per result: typically half of cold
- Warm audience buyer volume: typically 2x+ cold closing rate
- Bin 2 frequency: averages 3-5 pieces per month per user

## Detailed Audience Setup

### Warm Audience Selection (All Options)

When creating warm audiences, select EVERY available option:
- Visited social profile
- Engaged with post or ad
- Pressed ad button
- Saved post
- Messaged page or account
- 10-second video viewers

Combine all interaction options into a single audience to make targeting easier.

### Audience Retention Considerations

**365-day audiences (recommended):**
- Widens audience size
- Lowers costs
- Captures people who may be ready to buy outside a 30-day window

**30-day audiences (alternative):**
- Limits audience to newer warm people
- Spend less on warm targeting
- Downside: miss people warmed up who might be ready to buy outside the window

### Why Both Engagement AND ThruPlay Campaigns

The Facebook algorithm reaches two DIFFERENT types of people based on the campaign objective:
- Engagement campaigns reach people who are more likely to interact (like, comment, share)
- ThruPlay campaigns reach people who are more likely to watch videos

Same audience, different behavioral segments. Running both maximizes your reach into the warm bucket.

## Content Production Guidelines

### How Many Content Pieces to Create

**Minimum:** 5 pieces per bin (10 total for Bin 1 + Bin 2)
**Recommended:** 12 pieces per bin (24 total)
**Maximum:** As many as you have -- FB AI will serve the right ones

### Content Format Requirements

- Post content directly to your Facebook and Instagram pages
- Use "existing post" at the ad level (not uploading new creative)
- This preserves organic engagement (likes, comments, shares) which boosts social proof
- Do NOT use copyrighted music if you plan to promote as ads

### What Makes Good Cold Content (Bin 1)

- Captures attention in first 1-2 seconds
- Does NOT pitch or include CTAs
- Establishes one of: authority, curiosity, relatability, entertainment value
- Optimized for getting 10+ second views
- Short format preferred (under 3 minutes)
- Topics that are broadly interesting, not niche-specific

### What Makes Good Warm Content (Bin 2)

- Addresses specific beliefs needed for purchase decision
- Establishes credibility and authority in your specific domain
- Handles the top 5-10 objections your prospects have
- Showcases customer results and success stories
- Demonstrates your unique process or methodology
- Longer format acceptable (3-10 minutes)
- More niche-specific and targeted to your ideal buyer

## Budget Allocation Framework

### Scaling Priority
1. Scale Bin 1 FIRST (this feeds everything downstream)
2. Scale Bin 2 proportionally to Bin 1 growth
3. Scale direct response proportionally to warm audience growth

### Budget Split Example ($10K/month)
- Bin 1 Cold Content: $4,000 (40%)
- Bin 2 Warm Content: $2,000 (20%)
- Warm Direct Response: $2,500 (25%)
- Cold Direct Response (comparison): $1,500 (15%)

### Budget Split Example ($30K/month)
- Bin 1 Cold Content: $12,000 (40%)
- Bin 2 Warm Content: $6,000 (20%)
- Warm Direct Response: $7,500 (25%)
- Cold Direct Response (comparison): $4,500 (15%)
