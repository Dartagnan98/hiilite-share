# Ad Fatigue: The Silent Killer of ROAS

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing
Type: Ad Creative Management & Fatigue Prevention
Application: Any ad platform, any spend level. Critical at $5K+/day.

---

## What Is Ad Fatigue?

When video or image ad creatives run for too long, the ad channel punishes you by:
- Lowering result volume
- Increasing cost per result

The platforms do this so users do not get targeted with the same ads day in and day out. The problem only gets worse over time.

---

## How to Identify It

Ad fatigue is happening when, **over 72 hours:**
- Result volume continues to drop
- Cost per result continues to increase

If both are trending the wrong direction for 72+ hours, fatigue is the likely cause.

---

## The Only Prevention: Creative Surplus

Have videos and images on standby to swap in when fatigue hits.

Most clients do not keep a surplus. Agencies press them AFTER fatigue has already become an issue. **The worst time to prevent a problem is after it already happened.** Like needing a glass breaker after your car is already sinking.

## Real-World Case Study: $7,500/Day Campaign

### The Decline

| Day | Calls | Cost/Call | Change from Day 1 |
|-----|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | 63 | $119 | Baseline |
| 2 | 61 | $122 | +2.5% |
| 7 | 37 | $202 | +70% |
| 14 | 25 | $300 | +152% |
| 21 | 21 | $357 | +200% |
| 30 | 20 | $375 | +215% |
| 37 | 14 | $535 | +350% |

**After just 1 week at $7,500/day:** Almost 90% spike in cost per call and almost half the result volume.

**From Day 1 to Day 37:** 63 calls at $119 down to 14 calls at $535. A 350% increase in cost and 78% drop in volume.

The client was warned about fatigue but delayed getting new creative.

### The Recovery: New Ads Launched

Only **two new videos** were needed to relaunch. Three others kept in reserve.

| Day (New Campaign) | Calls | Cost/Call |
|--------------------|-------|-----------|
| Day 1 | 78 | $96 |
| Day 2 | 281 | $26 |
| Day 3 (half day) | 138 | $54 |

Performance went back to (and exceeded) pre-fatigue levels. Sometimes new ads perform better than old ads.

## How to Overcome Ad Fatigue (The Process)

1. **Duplicate** the campaign with fatigued ads
2. Go to **ad level** of the new campaign
3. **Change** old video/image ads for new ones
4. **Wait** for new campaign to get approved
5. **Then shut off** the old campaign

**Why not just shut off or lower spend?**
- Any calls are better than no calls while waiting for new creative
- Some businesses can tolerate higher costs (e.g., $75,000 AOV can absorb $535/call)
- Lowering spend does not fix the fundamental issue -- audience has seen creative too many times

---

## The Tire Analogy

A car getting 50 mpg one day, 25 mpg seven days later, trending worse. The only fix is having fresh tires on standby to replace the old ones causing the efficiency loss.

## Creative Refresh Schedules by Spend Level

| Spend Level | Refresh Cycle | Backup Creatives Needed |
|------------|---------------|------------------------|
| $1K-$5K/day | Every 2-4 weeks | 2-3 ready |
| $5K-$20K/day | Every 1-2 weeks | 5-7 ready |
| $20K-$50K/day | Every 3-7 days | 10+ ready |
| $50K+/day | Every 1-3 days | 20+ in constant production |

### Frequency Monitoring Guide

| Frequency | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Under 2 | Probably fine, watch for changes |
| 2-3 | Start preparing fresh creative |
| Above 3 | Fatigue likely coming, get ready to refresh |
| Above 5 (and ROAS dropping) | Swap immediately |

**Note:** If profitable and scaling, high frequency does not matter until performance drops.

## Majority vs Minority Hooks and Fatigue

### Majority Hooks (Long Life)
- Appeal to largest segment of target audience
- High reach relative to audience size
- Frequency stays low even with significant spend
- Broad emotional appeal across demographics

**Example:** "Most people will never build wealth because they don't understand this one investing strategy"

### Minority Hooks (Short Life, Fast Fatigue)
- Appeal to small segment of audience
- Low reach relative to audience size
- Frequency climbs quickly (above 2-3)
- High performance initially, then rapid decline

**Example:** "If you're a nurse tired of night shifts, this real estate strategy can replace your income"

### The Data Signals
- Good: High reach relative to audience, frequency stays low
- Bad: Low reach relative to audience, frequency climbs fast = minority hook problem

## Multi-Cam Creative Production System

To stay ahead of fatigue, build a system for rapid creative production:

### The Setup
- Primary talking head shot
- Secondary angle from different position
- Wide shot showing full body/environment
- Close-up shots for emphasis
- B-roll footage relevant to each hook

### From 15 hooks + multi-cam, produce 100+ unique creatives:
- Standard talking head with captions
- Multi-angle cuts with captions
- Different B-roll overlays on each version
- Various lengths by changing cut points
- Different caption styles and positioning

### Hook Iteration (When a Hook Works)

Original: "Baby boomers are dying and their businesses will die with them"

Variations:
- "75 million baby boomers need someone to take over their businesses"
- "Successful business owners are looking for someone to keep their legacy alive"
- "There's a massive opportunity in businesses whose owners are ready to retire"
- "Business owners would rather sell to you than let their business die"
- "The largest wealth transfer in history is happening right now"

Same core concept, different emotional triggers and angles. Create 5-10 variations of any successful hook within 24 hours.

### Three Iteration Methods

**Core Concept Method:** Identify the concept, create variations hitting it from different angles.

**Emotional Angle Method:** Same factual hook through different emotional lenses:
- Fear: "Businesses will die"
- Opportunity: "Biggest business opportunity in history"
- Urgency: "Limited window to capitalize"
- Social proof: "Smart investors already capitalizing"

**Audience Segment Method:** Customize for different segments:
- General: "Baby boomers dying, businesses dying"
- Employees: "While stuck in 9-5, boomers looking for successors"
- Entrepreneurs: "Forget starting from scratch -- buy established business"
- Investors: "Best ROI isn't stocks -- it's boomer businesses"

## Key Takeaways

1. **Ad fatigue is real** -- always have creative surplus in reserve
2. **Do not tolerate fatigue longer than 72 hours** -- makes all the difference to ROAS
3. **Only 2 new videos** were needed to relaunch and surpass pre-fatigue performance
4. **Keep 3-5 creatives in reserve** for next fatigue cycle
5. **Simple fix:** Duplicate campaign, swap creatives, wait for approval, shut off old
6. **Never wait for fatigue to happen before preparing new creative**
7. **Monitor cost per result over 72-hour windows**
8. **Clients must understand ongoing creative production** -- not a one-and-done process
9. **Build multi-cam production systems** to generate 100+ creatives from single shoots
10. **Use majority hooks for longevity**, minority hooks for quick wins

## Budget Allocation for Creative Production

### The Creative Pipeline

To stay ahead of fatigue, build a pipeline:

**Monthly Production Schedule:**
- Week 1: Shoot 10-20 hooks with multi-cam setup
- Week 2: Edit and produce 50-100+ unique creatives from footage
- Week 3: Deploy first batch, begin shooting next month's content
- Week 4: Rotate in fresh creative as fatigue hits, audit performance

**Budget for Creative Production:**
- Allocate 10-15% of ad spend toward creative production
- At $50K/month ad spend: $5K-$7.5K for creative
- At $100K/month: $10K-$15K for creative
- This investment pays for itself many times over in maintained performance

### The Campaign Duplication Method (Detailed)

When fatigue hits:

1. **Do not turn off the fatigued campaign yet**
2. Go to campaign level, click "Duplicate"
3. In the new campaign, navigate to ad level
4. Replace every fatigued creative with fresh creative
5. Ensure all other settings (audiences, budgets, placements) remain identical
6. Submit the new campaign for review
7. **Wait for approval** (typically 15 minutes to 24 hours)
8. Once new campaign is active and delivering, THEN turn off old campaign
9. Monitor new campaign for 24-48 hours to ensure performance

**Why duplicate instead of editing in place:**
- Editing existing ads resets the learning phase
- Duplicating preserves campaign structure and pixel data
- The new campaign inherits the historical optimization
- Cleaner data separation between old and new performance

## Fatigue Warning Signs (Before the 72-Hour Window)

Catch fatigue BEFORE it becomes a 72-hour trend:

### Early Warning Indicators
- CPM starting to creep up (even 5-10% increase)
- CTR starting to decline (even slight drops)
- Frequency approaching your threshold for that spend level
- Reach plateauing while spend remains constant
- Cost per click trending upward over 48 hours

### The Monitoring Cadence

| Spend Level | How Often to Check |
|------------|-------------------|
| $1K-$5K/day | Every 2-3 days |
| $5K-$20K/day | Daily |
| $20K-$50K/day | Twice daily |
| $50K+/day | Multiple times per day |

### Key Columns to Monitor in Ads Manager
- Frequency (primary indicator)
- Cost per result (trending direction matters more than absolute number)
- CPM (rising CPM with flat creative = fatigue)
- Reach (plateauing reach = audience saturation)
- CTR (declining = creative losing effectiveness)
- Amount spent (ensure budget is being consumed -- underspend can indicate issues too)
