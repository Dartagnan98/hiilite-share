# The Perfect Client Traits

Source: Megalodon Marketing (MIM ecosystem)

---

## Core Concept
Wrong clients drain energy, destroy margins, and kill your agency. Right clients pay more, stay longer, refer others, and are a joy to work with.

---

## The 8 Traits of a Perfect Client

### 1. They Have Money (Not Their Last Dollar)
- Making $10-20K/mo minimum, ideally $50-100K+/mo
- Investing 5-10% of revenue in marketing = healthy
- Investing 50%+ = ticking time bomb
- Broke clients are desperate, demanding, and blame you for everything
- Always ask about revenue during sales

### 2. They Have a Proven Offer
- Already sold their product/service and know it works
- Have customers, testimonials, proof of concept
- "I just need traffic to test this idea" = RUN
- Your job: pour gasoline on a fire that already exists
- Ask: "How many units/clients in the last 90 days?" Zero = not ready

### 3. They Understand Marketing Takes Time
- Know about testing phases, learning, optimization cycles
- Don't expect 10x ROI in week one
- Red flag: "I need results by Friday"
- Good clients give 60-90 days minimum
- Set these expectations DURING sales

### 4. They Are Responsive and Communicative
- Respond within 24 hours
- Show up on time
- Provide assets when asked
- Red flag: ghosting, missing calls, no assets
- Non-responsive clients create bottlenecks YOU get blamed for

### 5. They Trust Your Expertise
- Let you do your job without micromanaging
- Give input but don't override professional judgment
- Red flag: "My nephew said the ad should say THIS"
- Establish authority early with data-backed reasoning

### 6. They Have Realistic Expectations
- Understand what their budget can and can't do
- Know industry benchmarks or are open to learning
- Red flag: "Spend $500/month, make $100K"
- Present realistic projections during sales
- Underpromise. Overdeliver. Always.

### 7. They Are In a Scalable Niche
- High LTV businesses: dental, real estate, coaching, legal, home services, med spas
- Single customer worth $500-$5,000+ is ideal
- Red flag: $10-$20 average sale (math doesn't work for paid ads)
- Higher LTV = more acquisition spend = easier job

### 8. They Are Likeable
- You genuinely enjoy talking to them
- Life is too short for clients who drain you
- Red flag: rude during sales = nightmare as client
- Difficult in the honeymoon phase = 10x worse later
- Trust your gut

## 5 Red Flag Client Types

### 1. The Micromanager
- Approves every creative, changes mind constantly, creates bottlenecks
- Solution: don't take them, or set boundaries / fire them

### 2. The Broke Gambler
- Spending money they can't afford, expects ads to save failing business
- Will dispute charges and demand refunds
- Solution: qualify financials during sales

### 3. The Know-It-All
- Thinks they know more than you, sends random articles, overrides recommendations
- Solution: establish expertise early; if they won't defer, walk away

### 4. The Ghost
- Disappears for weeks, doesn't provide feedback/assets, then blames you
- Solution: set clear SLAs during onboarding; pause campaigns if they miss deadlines

### 5. The Scope Creeper
- Hired for Facebook ads, now wants email + SEO + website + social media
- "Can you just quickly..."
- Solution: document scope in contracts; new work = new invoice

## 10 Qualifying Questions for Sales

1. What's your current monthly revenue?
2. How many customers/clients per month?
3. Average customer lifetime value?
4. Current marketing spend?
5. Worked with an agency before? How did it go?
6. What does success look like in 90 days?
7. Are you the decision maker?
8. What's your budget?
9. How quickly do you respond to emails/messages?
10. Open to trusting our expertise and letting us run strategy?

---

## The Pricing Filter
- Your pricing is your first filter for bad clients
- Too cheap = broke clients who are hardest to work with
- Premium pricing attracts premium clients
- $5,000/mo client is easier than $500/mo client
- Don't race to the bottom. Race to the top.

---

## Client Retention Rules
1. Monthly reporting with clear ROI
2. Proactive communication (tell them first, don't wait for them to ask)
3. Quick wins early (result in first 30 days)
4. Set expectations accurately (#1 reason clients leave = misaligned expectations)
5. Be honest about bad months -- address before they notice, come with a plan
6. Add value beyond scope -- insights, trends, ideas
7. Quarterly business reviews -- deep dive on performance and strategy
8. Make them feel like your only client
