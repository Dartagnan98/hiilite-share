# 5-Point Client Retention Star

Source: Jeremy Haynes - Jeremy's Inner Circle SOP

**Department:** Client Account Manager/Advertiser
**Time:** 5-10 hours/month
**Goal:** Retain clients for multi-year relationships

---

## The 5 Points

1. Onboarding
2. Communication
3. Results
4. Reporting
5. Added Value

---

## 1. Onboarding

Goal: Make client feel your process is trusted and smooth. Get everything needed to start producing results FAST.

### Steps
1. **Sign agreement** via DocuSign (scope, terms, expectations)
2. **Send onboarding email** with instructions:
   - Approved images/video in Google Drive or Dropbox
   - Logins: Clickfunnels, CRM, Zapier, Calendly, Typeform
   - Admin access to Facebook page
   - Business Manager access (create if needed)
   - Add to Slack/Telegram for team communication
3. **Get all logins upfront** -- avoid going back and forth later
4. **Admin access to all ad channels** -- don't settle for limited permissions
5. **Full sweep** -- verify all access and assets before starting work
6. **Pro tip:** Create video walkthroughs explaining each onboarding step
7. **Video + form combo** -- extract key info (demographics, goals) via form
8. **Speed is key** -- complete onboarding within 1 hour of charging
9. **Get started same day** -- update audiences, write copy, set up automation, launch

### Encourage Creatives Before Onboarding
- Have client provide creative assets BEFORE signing
- Allows launching ads immediately after charging
- Don't wait weeks for content after taking payment

## 2. Communication

### Best Practices
1. **Real-time messaging over weekly calls** -- Slack, text, Telegram
2. **Video updates** -- Loom videos replace redundant weekly calls
3. **Phone calls for deep dives only** -- strategy sessions and complex discussions
4. **Be friendly and informal** -- emojis, voice notes, GIFs, memes. Make the client your friend
5. **Follow their social media** -- stay current on their life (travel, family, events)
6. **Remember your role** -- you're hired to make revenue. Updates center on that
7. **Be direct** -- don't sugarcoat hard truths. Clients respect honesty
8. **Be honest and solution-oriented** -- when things go wrong, use data to find the real issue
9. **Data over assumptions** -- "past data" claims need current evidence
10. **Stay cool** -- emotional clients need your strength, not your collapse
11. **Team effort** -- don't let blame fall on one party. Alliance together
12. **Be in the same world** -- discrepancies in data or strategy cause friction and kill deals

### Why Clients Become Overbearing
They lose confidence in your ability. Good communication prevents this. When they trust you, they leave you alone.

---

## 3. Results

Results are the bedrock. They hired you to produce revenue.

### Best Practices
1. **Keep your sword sharp** -- constantly learn new strategies
2. **Make clients believe in the results** -- positive ROI they don't believe in doesn't count
3. **30-day cycles** -- you have 30 days to turn $1 into $2
4. **Appear confident** -- lost a $300K/mo client because they didn't feel confident in agency's ability to scale further
5. **Prioritize results over effort** -- clients don't care about hours invested, only ROI
6. **Step up when closers suck** -- bad closers = bad ROI. Train them, improve them, or push to replace them
7. **Don't omit responsibility** -- anything impacting ROI is your problem
8. **72-hour rule** -- 10 different 72-hour tests per month to improve funnel conversions
9. **Stay focused on revenue** -- don't get sidetracked by non-revenue activities

## 5. Added Value

### Actions
1. **Social media stalking (good kind)** -- wish happy birthdays, anniversaries, congratulate milestones
2. **Send books** -- every 2-3 months, send business books to client and staff
3. **Branded swag** -- agency merchandise via swag.com
4. **Network introductions** -- connect clients with valuable people in your network
5. **Educational resources** -- share books, strategy docs, training libraries
6. **Handwritten anniversary cards** -- yearly thank you for their business
7. **Free month on negative ROI** -- don't charge after a bad month (when financially able)
8. **Random gifts** -- cookies, personalized gifts for client's family/kids
   - "I once bought a client a commercial grade slurpee machine for his two kids. We've had that client for over 6.5 years."
