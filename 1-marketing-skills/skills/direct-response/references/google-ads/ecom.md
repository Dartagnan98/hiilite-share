# Ecommerce Google Ads Strategy

Source: MIM Week 4 - Google Ads for Ecommerce with Chasen Gersh (Lessons 16-24)

---

## Ecom vs Lead Gen

- Totally different worlds for campaign building
- Ecom is product-centered with feed management, shopping network, and product data
- Different campaign types, different strategies, different bidding models
- The agency manages ~$1M/month in ecom spend with defined, proven strategies

## Primary Campaign Types for Ecom (Priority Order)

### 1. PMax (50-60% of Budget)
- Most scalable campaign type for ecom
- Omnipresent: shows on Shopping, Search, Display, YouTube, and more
- Replaced traditional Shopping campaigns for scalability
- Can spend $20-30K/month on a single PMax campaign
- Algorithm-driven: feed it good assets and it finds buyers
- Start most ecom accounts with PMax + Search

### 2. Search
- Different segmentations targeting lower-hanging fruit
- Keyword-intent driven (broad vs long-tail)
- Very consistent when it works
- Requires horizontal scaling (build new campaigns, don't just increase budget)

### 3. Remarketing
- Capitalize on people who visited your store but didn't convert
- **RLSA campaigns** (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads) -- show search ads to past visitors
- **YouTube remarketing** -- video ads to past visitors
- **Display remarketing** -- banner ads to past visitors
- Fixed audiences you've already paid for -- milk them for conversions
- Best part: you know these audiences are relevant because they already visited

### 4. YouTube
- Extremely scalable for ecom but hardest to crack
- Requires different assets (VSLs, high-quality video)
- Can't always repurpose Facebook videos -- different user behavior
- Worth the investment when it works

## Ecom Bidding Models

### Start: Maximize Conversion Value
- Recommended starting bid strategy for almost all ecom campaigns
- Google's algorithm is strong enough to find buyers without historical data
- Google has data on shopping habits from other stores you don't even know about
- No need for manual warm-up period anymore (algorithm has gotten that smart)
- Lets Google optimize for who is most likely to buy at the highest value

### Later: Target CPA (tCPA)
- Only use after accumulating significant conversion data
- Makes the targeting net too tight in most cases
- Prevents Google from spending on traffic it thinks will convert
- Can reduce spend and miss opportunities
- Use when you want precise cost control and have enough flow conversions

### Key Bidding Principles
- Not all traffic is created equal -- let Google find the buyers
- Give Google leverage early, tighten later
- Maximize Conversion Value lets Google use its back-end data advantage
- Switch to targeted bidding only with enough conversion volume to justify it

## Ecom Scaling

- PMax usually takes 50-60% of total budget when scaling
- Search handles the precise, intent-driven portion
- Remarketing captures missed conversions across all channels
- YouTube adds scale once the other channels are optimized
- Scale horizontally: build new campaigns rather than dumping budget into one

## Tracking and Feed Management

### Why Tracking Matters
- Foundation of how your account runs
- Wrong data = wrong decisions + wrong algorithm optimization
- Google shows ads to wrong people if fed bad data
- Cannot optimize without accurate tracking

### Ecom Tracking vs Lead Gen
- Lead gen: simple phone calls and form fills
- Ecom: high-level data -- conversion events, purchase values, order amounts
- Average order value tells you if campaigns are profitable at scale
- If order values come in wrong, all ROI calculations are incorrect in the platform
- Good news: ecom tracking is actually easier to set up than lead gen tracking

### Google Merchant Center
- Controls the product feed going from your store into Google
- Integrations make setup simple but feed quality matters
- **Clean titles are critical** -- Google uses them to decide who sees your ads
- **Clean descriptions are critical** -- also used for targeting decisions
- Google looks at product titles and descriptions to determine ad targeting
- Wrong titles/descriptions = wrong audience = wasted spend

### Feed Management Best Practices
1. Ensure product titles are keyword-rich and accurate
2. Descriptions should match what people actually search for
3. Use proper product categories
4. Keep feed data clean and updated
5. Test different title structures for performance
6. Monitor feed errors and fix them immediately

## PMAX for Ecom Setup

### Asset Requirements
- Strong image assets (product photos, lifestyle images)
- YouTube video assets
- Well-written search ad copy (headlines + descriptions)
- The more assets you provide, the more the algorithm can optimize
- Better assets = more data for the algorithm = better traffic

### Audience Signals
- Give PMax audiences as starting indicators
- Converter lists (people who already bought from your site)
- In-market audiences
- Affinity audiences
- Custom audiences
- These aren't hard targets -- they're signals to help the algorithm start smarter

### How PMax Leverages the Algorithm
- Google's competition to Facebook's algorithm
- Once it identifies who converts and who's worth sending to your store, it keeps finding more
- Massive scalability with minimal manual work
- Unlike Search (constant building), PMax algorithm does the work
- You just give it the right assets to work with

## Merchant Center Setup

- Required for any ecom advertiser running Shopping or PMax
- Connect your store to Google Merchant Center
- Product data feeds from your store through Merchant Center into Google Ads
- Integrations available for Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, etc.
- Verify and claim your website
- Ensure all product data is accurate and complete

## Key Ecom Principles

1. Start with PMax + Search, add remarketing and YouTube as you scale
2. Use Maximize Conversion Value bidding from day one
3. Feed quality directly impacts targeting quality
4. Product titles and descriptions are targeting levers, not just content
5. Remarketing is non-negotiable -- capture missed conversions
6. Scale horizontally across campaign types
7. YouTube is powerful but requires dedicated video assets
8. Track order values accurately or all reporting is wrong
9. PMax will likely take 50-60% of budget at scale
10. The algorithm works for you when you give it good data and assets
