## What is PPC / Search

- PPC = Pay Per Click -- you only get charged when someone clicks your ad
- Target specific search terms on Google
- Someone searches a keyword, your text ad appears at the top of results
- If they click, they go to your website to convert (buy product or become a lead)
- The "sniper approach" -- precise, intent-driven, higher cost per click but higher conversion rate

## Why Google Search Stands Out

- **Consistency** -- unlike TikTok/Facebook where campaigns spike and crash, Google Search is stable
- Once you find something that works, it stays working
- You build on top of campaigns (horizontal scaling)
- Algorithm is auction-based: best ads + most budget = top position
- Google rewards advertisers who play into the algorithm

## The Pyramid of Converting Traffic

### Bottom (Foundation): Low-Hanging Fruit
- Highest intent keywords
- Most likely to convert
- Start here first
- Example: "iPhone 12 Pro Max" (they know exactly what they want)

### Top: Broader Traffic
- Higher risk, more scalability
- Less intent but larger audience
- Example: "cell phone" (they want one but don't know which)

### Strategy
- Maximize each level before moving up
- Staircase up the amount of traffic you can scale into

## Horizontal Scaling Strategy

1. Find what works in one campaign
2. Let it run consistently
3. Build the next campaign
4. Let that run
5. Keep building on top of consistent campaigns
6. Result: strong foundation of revenue from multiple stable campaigns

## Keyword Intent -- Ecommerce

### Broad Keywords
- General product terms: "cell phone," "running shoes"
- Lower intent, higher volume
- User still exploring options

### Long-Tail Keywords
- Specific product terms: "iPhone 12 Pro Max," "Nike Air Max 90 White Size 11"
- Higher intent, lower volume
- User knows exactly what they want
- Just looking for a reputable seller

### Keyword Tools for Ecom
- Google Keyword Planner (inside Google Ads)
- Direct data from Google on search volume, cost, competition
- Shows what terms people search, how often, and what they cost
- Use for forecasting and keyword research

## Keyword Intent -- Local Business

### High Intent Local Keywords
- "[Service] near me"
- "[Service] in [City]"
- "Best [service provider] [location]"
- "Emergency [service] [city]"

### Medium Intent
- "[Service] cost"
- "[Service] reviews"
- "How to find a [service provider]"

### Tools for Local Keyword Research
- Google Keyword Planner
- Search volume by geographic area
- Cost estimates by location
- Competitive landscape analysis

## Key Search Campaign Principles

1. **Search = sniper approach** -- precise targeting, higher CPC, better conversion
2. **Start with low-hanging fruit keywords** -- highest intent first
3. **Scale horizontally** -- don't dump all budget into one campaign
4. **Long-tail keywords convert better** -- more specific = more intent
5. **Google's algorithm rewards quality** -- good ads + good landing pages = lower costs
6. **Track everything** -- conversion tracking is the foundation
7. **Quality over quantity** -- you're paying more per click but getting better traffic
8. **Local keywords need geographic targeting** -- radius and location-specific terms
