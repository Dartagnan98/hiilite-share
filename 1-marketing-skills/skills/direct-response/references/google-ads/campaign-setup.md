## Google Ads Fundamentals

### What is PPC
- Pay Per Click: you only get charged when someone clicks your ad
- Powerful because you target intent-based traffic
- Consistent: once something works on Google, it stays working (unlike Facebook/TikTok volatility)
- Auction-based system: best ads + most budget = top position
- Algorithm rewards quality advertisers with better positions and lower costs

### Google vs Facebook: The Key Difference
- **Google = Sniper approach** -- precise, intent-driven, higher cost but higher conversion
- **Facebook = Shotgun approach** -- broad reach, lower cost but less intent
- Google lets you isolate variables and focus budget on what converts
- Google traffic is more consistent; Facebook is more volatile

## Opening a Google Ads Account

### Step-by-Step
1. Go to ads.google.com
2. Log in with a Gmail that has NOT opened an ad account before (for $500 promo credit)
3. Click "Start Now"
4. **Select "Google Ads Account"** -- NOT Smart Campaign (Smart locks you into basic mode that burns money like boosting posts on Facebook)
5. Set your country
6. Set your timezone (important: affects when campaigns run)
7. **Set currency -- THIS CANNOT BE CHANGED LATER** -- if you need non-USD, change it now
8. Invite users (optional, can do later)
9. Verify identity (captcha/Gmail verification)
10. Click Save and Continue

### Billing Setup
- Create billing profile with legal business name
- **Use correct legal name** -- Google will trigger identity verification later
- Wrong business info = potential account suspension
- Credit card: instant verification
- Bank account: takes longer (Google sends small deposits to verify)
- Once billing is set, click Submit to access your new account

### The $500 Promo Credit
- Only available on new Gmail accounts that haven't had an ad account
- Must spend $500 first to get $500 free credit
- Worth it if you're running ads anyway

## Navigating the Platform

### Key Areas (Where You'll Spend Most Time)

#### 1. Campaigns Dashboard (Left Sidebar)
- Shows all active, paused, and historical campaigns
- Filter by campaign type: All, Search, Performance Max, Display, YouTube
- Filtering is essential when managing large accounts with many campaigns
- Click into any campaign to see ad groups, ads, keywords

#### 2. Tools & Settings

**Conversions**
- Set up and manage conversion actions
- Track what's actually driving results
- Foundation of all campaign optimization

**Access & Security**
- Add team members by email
- Permission levels:
  - **Admin** -- full control of everything
  - **Standard** -- can see everything and make changes
  - **Read Only** -- can see data but can't make changes (good for audits)

**Manager Accounts**
- Link manager accounts using your 10-digit account number
- Managers can access your account from their dashboard
- Common for agencies managing client accounts

**Billing & Setup**
- Manage payment methods
- View billing activity and documents
- Change credit cards or bank accounts

#### 3. Audience Manager
- Build audiences from your data
- Create cold and warm audiences
- **Your Data** -- website visitors, customer lists, app users
- **Combined Segments** -- mix multiple audience types
- **Custom Segments** -- build from keywords, URLs, or Google data
- Set up audience sources (pixel/tag on your website)

#### 4. Keyword Planner
- Direct data from Google on search behavior
- Shows what terms people search, search volume, and costs
- Forecast tools for budget planning
- Primary tool for keyword research
- Use before building any Search campaign

## The Four Campaign Types

### 1. Search (Traditional PPC)
- Text ads at the top of Google search results
- Headline + description format
- Target specific keywords
- **Sniper approach**: high intent, high conversion rate
- Start here for most accounts

### 2. Display
- Visual/banner ads across Google's partner network (thousands of websites)
- Primary use: **remarketing** (retarget website visitors)
- Weakness: lack of control -- ads show on random websites and apps
- **Shotgun approach**: broad reach, lower quality traffic
- Easy to burn money -- use with specific audiences only
- Use targeted audiences to minimize waste

### 3. YouTube
- Pre-roll ads (before videos) and Discovery ads (homepage suggestions)
- Endless scalability (not capped by search volume)
- Free branding if viewers skip before threshold
- Requires strong video assets
- Hybrid between Google intent and Facebook broad targeting

### 4. Performance Max (PMax)
- Omnipresent: shows on Search, Display, YouTube, Shopping, Gmail, Maps, Discover
- Replaced Shopping campaigns for scalability
- Asset-driven: the more creative assets you give it, the better it optimizes
- **Audience signals** guide the algorithm (not hard targets, just hints)
- Takes ~50-60% of budget in scaled accounts
- Algorithm does the heavy lifting -- less manual work than Search
- Google's answer to Facebook's algorithm-driven campaigns
- Supply: images, videos, headlines, descriptions, audience signals
- The algorithm finds the right traffic across all Google properties

## Bidding Overview

### For Ecom
- Start with **Maximize Conversion Value** -- let Google find buyers using its data
- Move to **Target CPA (tCPA)** later once you have enough conversion data
- Google's algorithm is strong enough to work without historical account data

### For Lead Gen
- Start with **Maximize Conversions**
- Move to **Target CPA** once you have conversion volume
- Monitor lead quality alongside cost per lead

### General Bidding Principles
- Google has back-end data on user behavior across the internet
- Let the algorithm use that data early (automated bidding)
- Tighten with manual/target bidding only after accumulating your own data
- Not all traffic is equal -- automated bidding helps find the valuable traffic

## Key Setup Principles

1. Never use Smart Campaigns -- always select full Google Ads Account
2. Set currency correctly on day one (cannot change later)
3. Use legal business name for billing (identity verification comes later)
4. Set up conversion tracking BEFORE launching any campaigns
5. Use Keyword Planner before building Search campaigns
6. Start with Search (highest intent), then add PMax, then Display/YouTube
7. Get comfortable with the platform by clicking around -- it comes naturally
8. Filter campaigns by type when managing multiple campaigns
9. Give team members appropriate permission levels
10. Build audiences from day one to use for remarketing later
