# Performance Max (PMax) Strategy

Source: MIM Week 4 - Google Advertising with Chasen Gersh (Lessons 15, 24, 31)

---

## What is PMax

- Relatively new campaign type (last ~2 years)
- Replaced Shopping campaigns for scalability
- Omnipresent: shows ads across ALL Google networks simultaneously
- Google's answer to Facebook's algorithm-driven ad system
- Typically takes 50-60% of budget in scaled accounts

## Where PMax Shows Ads

1. **Shopping Network** -- product listings (for ecom)
2. **Search** -- text ads on search results
3. **Display** -- banner ads across partner websites
4. **YouTube** -- video ads
5. **Gmail** -- sponsored emails
6. **Google Maps** -- local business listings
7. **Discover** -- Google Discover feed

## Why PMax is Powerful

- **Omnipresent** -- assets appear wherever they'll perform best
- **Algorithm-driven** -- Google's machine learning finds the right traffic
- **Less manual work** -- unlike Search where you constantly build new campaigns
- **Scalable** -- once it hooks into who converts, it keeps finding more
- The algorithm does the work -- you just provide the right inputs

## PMax is Asset-Driven

The more creative assets you provide, the better PMax performs:
- **Images** -- product photos, lifestyle images, brand graphics
- **Videos** -- YouTube-ready video assets
- **Headlines** -- multiple headline variations
- **Descriptions** -- multiple description variations
- **Logos** -- brand logos in various sizes
- **Final URLs** -- landing pages for each asset group

### The Asset Equation
More assets = more the algorithm can utilize = more optimization opportunities = better results

## Audience Signals

Audience signals are NOT hard targeting -- they're indicators that help the algorithm start smarter.

### Types of Audience Signals
1. **Converter lists** -- people who already bought (your first-party data)
2. **In-market audiences** -- people actively shopping for products like yours
3. **Affinity audiences** -- people with interests related to your products
4. **Custom audiences** -- built from keywords, URLs, or Google data
5. **Customer match lists** -- uploaded email/phone lists

### How Signals Work
- Give the algorithm a starting point (not a limitation)
- Google uses signals to identify who might convert
- Then expands to find similar users across its network
- Better signals = faster optimization = better early results

## PMax for Ecom

### Setup
1. Connect Google Merchant Center (product feed)
2. Create asset groups with strong creative
3. Add audience signals from customer data
4. Set budget and Maximize Conversion Value bidding
5. Launch and let the algorithm optimize

### Ecom-Specific Benefits
- Shows products on Shopping network (visual product ads)
- Feeds product data to Search, Display, and YouTube
- Can spend $20-30K/month on a single PMax campaign
- Usually dominates budget allocation (50-60% of total spend)

### Budget Allocation Example
- PMax: 50-60% of budget
- Search: 20-30% of budget
- Remarketing: 10-15% of budget
- YouTube: 5-10% of budget (added later)

## Local PMax

### What Local PMax Does
- Optimized for local business objectives
- Shows on Google Maps, local Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail
- Drives phone calls, direction requests, store visits
- Uses Google Business Profile data

### Local PMax Setup
1. Connect Google Business Profile
2. Set geographic targeting (radius or city-based)
3. Provide local creative assets (photos of business, team, services)
4. Write locally-relevant ad copy
5. Set call and direction conversion tracking
6. Add audience signals (past customers, local demographics)
7. Set budget and bidding strategy

### Local PMax Benefits
- Google Maps visibility is critical for local businesses
- Algorithm leverages Google's massive local intent data
- Less manual management than individual Search + Display campaigns
- Captures demand across the entire local search ecosystem
- One campaign covers multiple Google surfaces

## PMax vs Search

| Aspect | PMax | Search |
|--------|------|--------|
| Manual work | Low (algorithm-driven) | High (constant building) |
| Scalability | Very high | Limited by search volume |
| Control | Less (Google decides placements) | High (you choose keywords) |
| Networks | All Google networks | Search only |
| Budget share | 50-60% at scale | 20-30% at scale |
| Consistency | High once optimized | Very high |
| Starting strategy | Good from day one | Essential from day one |

## Key PMax Principles

1. **Feed it assets** -- images, videos, headlines, descriptions. The more, the better
2. **Audience signals accelerate learning** -- always add them
3. **Let the algorithm work** -- don't over-manage or make daily changes
4. **Start with Maximize Conversion Value** -- let Google find the money
5. **PMax complements Search** -- run both, not either/or
6. **Clean product feeds matter** -- titles and descriptions affect targeting
7. **Give it time** -- the algorithm needs data to optimize (don't kill too early)
8. **For local businesses** -- connect Google Business Profile for Maps visibility
9. **Monitor performance by asset group** -- identify what creative works
10. **Scale by adding new asset groups** -- not just increasing budget
