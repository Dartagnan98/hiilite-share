# Lead Gen Google Ads Strategy

Source: MIM Week 4 - Google Ads for Lead Gen with Chasen Gersh (Lessons 25-34)

---

## Lead Gen vs Ecom

- Completely different strategy, campaign types, and bidding approaches
- Lead gen focuses on generating phone calls, form fills, and booked appointments
- Simpler tracking than ecom (form fills and phone calls vs purchase values)
- Different keyword intent categories (service-based vs product-based)

## Lead Gen Campaign Strategy

### Primary Campaigns
1. **Search** -- high-intent keyword targeting for local and national services
2. **PMax (Local)** -- omnipresent local campaigns across Google's network
3. **YouTube** -- video ads for broader awareness and retargeting
4. **Display Remarketing** -- retarget website visitors who didn't convert

### Campaign Priority Order
1. Start with Search targeting lowest-hanging-fruit keywords
2. Add Local PMax to expand reach across Google properties
3. Layer in remarketing to capture missed conversions
4. Scale with YouTube for broader awareness

## Local Targeting

### Geographic Targeting Options
- Radius targeting around business location
- City/zip code targeting
- DMA (Designated Market Area) targeting
- Custom radius with exclusions

### Local Keyword Intent Categories

#### High Intent (Start Here)
- "[Service] near me"
- "[Service] in [City]"
- "Best [service] [city]"
- "Emergency [service] [city]"
- "[Service] open now"
- These people are ready to buy/book NOW

#### Medium Intent
- "[Service] cost/pricing"
- "[Service] reviews"
- "How much does [service] cost"
- Researching but closer to a decision

#### Low Intent (Scale Into Later)
- General service terms without location
- "How to [related topic]"
- Educational/informational queries
- Higher volume but lower conversion rate

### Keyword Research Tools for Local
- Google Keyword Planner (primary tool)
- Shows local search volume by geographic area
- Cost per click estimates by location
- Competition levels for local terms
- Forecast tools for budget planning

## Local PMax Campaigns

### What Local PMax Does
- Shows your business across Search, Maps, Display, YouTube, Gmail, and Discover
- Optimized for local objectives (calls, directions, store visits)
- Uses business profile information from Google Business Profile
- Algorithm finds potential customers across all Google properties

### Local PMax Setup
- Connect Google Business Profile
- Provide strong creative assets (images, videos, logos)
- Write compelling ad copy (headlines, descriptions)
- Set geographic targeting
- Define budget and bidding strategy
- Add audience signals for the algorithm

### Local PMax Benefits
- Omnipresent in local search ecosystem
- Shows on Google Maps (critical for local businesses)
- Algorithm leverages Google's local intent data
- Less manual management than traditional Search campaigns
- Captures demand across multiple touchpoints

## Lead Gen Tracking and Bidding

### Tracking Setup
- Simpler than ecom: track form fills and phone calls
- Use Google Tag Manager for event tracking
- Set up conversion actions in Google Ads
- Track phone calls (both from ads and website)
- Track form submissions as conversion events

### Google Tag Manager Setup
1. Create GTM account and container
2. Install GTM code on website
3. Set up conversion tracking tags
4. Configure triggers for form submissions
5. Configure triggers for phone call clicks
6. Test all tracking before launching campaigns
7. Connect to Google Ads conversion actions

### Setting Up Conversion Actions
1. Go to Tools > Conversions in Google Ads
2. Create new conversion action
3. Choose type: website, phone calls, or app
4. Set conversion value (estimated lead value)
5. Set counting method (one per click for leads)
6. Set conversion window
7. Link to GTM tags

### Bidding for Lead Gen
- Similar principles to ecom: start with automated bidding
- Maximize Conversions is common starting point
- Target CPA once you have enough conversion data
- Monitor cost per lead closely
- Adjust bids based on lead quality, not just volume

## Lead Gen Scaling

### Scaling Strategy
1. Max out high-intent Search keywords first
2. Expand to medium-intent keywords
3. Add Local PMax for omnipresent coverage
4. Layer remarketing across all channels
5. Test YouTube for broader reach
6. Monitor lead quality at every scaling step

### Key Scaling Metrics
- Cost per lead (CPL)
- Cost per qualified lead
- Cost per booked appointment
- Show rate on booked calls
- Close rate on showed calls
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Return on ad spend (ROAS)

## Key Lead Gen Principles

1. Start with highest-intent keywords in your local area
2. Speed to lead matters enormously -- contact leads within 5 minutes
3. Track phone calls AND form submissions as conversions
4. Google Tag Manager is essential for proper tracking
5. Local PMax puts you on Google Maps + Search + Display + YouTube
6. Scale by expanding keyword intent levels, not just increasing budgets
7. Lead quality matters more than lead quantity
8. Remarketing captures the leads who visited but didn't convert
9. Set up conversion actions properly before launching any campaigns
10. Test and optimize landing pages alongside ad campaigns
