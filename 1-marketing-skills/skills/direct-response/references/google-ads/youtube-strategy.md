# YouTube Ads Strategy

Source: MIM Week 4 - Google Advertising with Chasen Gersh (Lesson 14)

---

## Why YouTube Ads

- One of the most exciting parts of Google's platform due to endless scalability
- Unlike Search (capped by search volume), YouTube has unlimited targeting options
- Unlike Display (which goes everywhere), YouTube keeps ads focused on YouTube's network
- A hybrid between Google and Facebook: intent-driven but broader than Search
- Harder to convert than traditional PPC but highly scalable once you find converting audiences

## YouTube Targeting Options

1. **People watching certain videos** -- target specific video placements
2. **Channels they visit** -- target subscribers or viewers of competitor/related channels
3. **Interest-based targeting** -- audience interests and affinities
4. **Demographic targeting** -- age, gender, income, parental status
5. **Custom intent audiences** -- people who recently searched specific keywords on Google
6. **In-market audiences** -- people actively researching products/services like yours

## Key YouTube Placements

### 1. Pre-Roll Ads (In-Stream)
- Plays before the video someone searched for
- Viewer sees your ad before their chosen content
- "Skip" button appears after a few seconds
- **If they skip before the threshold, you get FREE branding** -- no charge for that impression
- Best for awareness and direct response

### 2. Discovery Ads (In-Feed)
- Shows on YouTube homepage as suggested videos
- Appears before anyone chooses what to watch
- Viewer can click your video thumbnail to watch
- Good for reaching people in browse/discovery mode

## Asset Requirements

- Strong video creative is essential -- you need purpose-built YouTube video ads
- Cannot always repurpose Facebook video ads for YouTube (different user behavior)
- Sometimes need VSL-style videos for ecom
- Need landing pages/assets designed to convert this traffic type

## YouTube for Ecom

- Extremely scalable for ecommerce
- One of the hardest campaign types to crack for ecom
- Requires different assets than other channels
- VSLs often needed
- High video quality required
- People interact with YouTube ads differently than Facebook ads
- Worth the investment once cracked -- massive scale potential

## YouTube Remarketing

- Retarget people who visited your store but didn't convert
- Part of the remarketing stack alongside RLSA and Display remarketing
- Self-explanatory setup: create audiences from site visitors, show them YouTube ads

## Scaling with YouTube

- When you find audiences that convert, scalability is massive
- Takes work to find the right audience/creative combination
- Not capped by search volume like Search campaigns
- More control than Display but broader than Search

## Key Principles

1. YouTube requires strong video assets -- invest in quality
2. Test multiple audiences to find what converts
3. Pre-roll is the workhorse placement for direct response
4. Discovery is great for top-of-funnel awareness
5. Free branding from skipped ads is a unique advantage
6. Different creative needed vs. Facebook/Instagram
7. YouTube remarketing should be part of every account's retargeting stack
