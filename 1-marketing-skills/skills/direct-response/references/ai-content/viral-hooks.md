## The 5-Point Prompting Framework for Hooks

When creating viral hooks with AI, follow this framework:

1. **Desired Output** -- specify exactly what the hook should accomplish
2. **Constraints** -- word count, tone, format requirements
3. **Context** -- your offer, audience, platform
4. **Examples** -- 5-70 examples of winning hooks (THE MOST IMPORTANT INPUT)
5. **Formatting** -- hook structure, CTA format, platform-specific endings

---

## Viral Hook Structure

Every hook video follows this structure:

### 1. Hook (0-3 seconds)
- Ultra-compelling promise that forces continued watching
- Pattern interrupt: visual or verbal surprise
- Curiosity gap: open a loop that demands resolution
- Example: "This AI tool replaces the need for professional portraits. Watch this."

### 2. Explainer (3-20 seconds)
- Deliver on the hook's promise
- Show the tool/technique/result
- Keep it fast and visual
- Use "but" or "therefore" storytelling to create contrast

### 3. Ending Line (20-25 seconds)
- Wrap up the value
- Reinforce the key takeaway
- Create shareability moment

### 4. CTA (last 5 seconds)
- Platform-specific call to action
- Instagram: "Follow for more"
- TikTok: "Follow for more" or "Link in bio"
- Different CTAs for different platforms in the same batch

## Hook Example Patterns

### Pattern 1: "This [tool/method] does [impressive thing]"
- "This AI tool replaces the need for professional portraits. Watch this."
- "This secret website helps me stay ahead of people in my niche."

### Pattern 2: Problem + Shocking Stat
- "You're burning $50K monthly on video ads while your CPMs hit $45+"
- "98% of businesses waste their ad budget on this one mistake"

### Pattern 3: Curiosity Gap
- "I found something that changes everything about [topic]..."
- "Nobody is talking about this, but it could [benefit]"

### Pattern 4: Before/After
- "6 months ago I was [bad state]. Today, [good state]. Here's what changed."
- "My client went from [problem] to [result] in [timeframe]"

### Pattern 5: Contrarian/Surprising
- "Stop doing [common practice]. Here's what actually works."
- "Everything you've been told about [topic] is wrong."

## Finding Viral Content for Inspiration

### Where to Find Winning Hooks
- Your own top-performing content (by views, engagement, shares)
- Competitor accounts in your niche
- Cross-industry viral content (adapt formats to your niche)
- TikTok trending page
- YouTube Shorts trending
- Instagram Reels explore page

### What to Look For
- Videos with disproportionate views vs follower count
- High share counts (shareability is the #1 viral signal)
- Comments asking "how?" or "where?" (demand signals)
- Content that uses familiar formats with novel information

## Retention Mechanisms

Build these into every hook script:

### But/Therefore Storytelling
- Create contrast: "I was doing X, BUT then I discovered Y, THEREFORE now I get Z"
- Avoids "and then, and then, and then" boring sequences
- Forces narrative tension and resolution

### Visual Pattern Interrupts
- Unexpected scene changes
- Speed changes (slow to fast, fast to slow)
- Text overlays at key moments
- Split screen comparisons

### Shareability Elements
- Interesting/unusual visuals
- Cliffhangers that make people want to share
- Surprise endings
- Relatable moments that trigger "tag someone who needs this"

## Scaling Hook Production

### The 50-Hook System (VEO 3 Method)
1. Generate 50 hook ideas with Claude (using offer context + examples)
2. Convert each to VEO 3 video prompts
3. Generate video clips with Google Flow
4. Improve audio with ElevenLabs
5. Assemble in CapCut with captions
6. Attach each hook to a proven ad body
7. Result: 50 unique ad creatives in 1-2 hours

## Key Principles

1. The hook IS the ad -- if the hook fails, nothing else matters
2. Load 50+ examples into your prompt for best results
3. Test 5-10 hooks per ad concept
4. Different platforms need different CTAs
5. But/therefore storytelling > and/then storytelling
6. Shareability is the #1 viral signal
7. Speed up b-roll for retention
8. Always add captions (85% of social video is watched on mute)
9. First 1-3 seconds determine everything
10. Volume wins: 50 hooks tested > 5 hooks perfected
