# The Email Matrix

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing - Master Internet Marketing

---

## Core Philosophy

Email is the highest-ROI marketing channel. For every $1 spent, average return is $36-42. The Email Matrix ensures you never leave money on the table by having a system for every stage of the customer journey.

---

## Layer 1: Indoctrination Sequence (New Subscriber)

Triggered immediately when someone joins your list. Goal: build relationship, set expectations, deliver value.

**Email 1: Welcome + Delivery (Immediate)**
- Deliver what they opted in for
- Introduce yourself briefly
- Set expectations for future emails
- Quick win: something actionable they can do right now
- CTA: Consume the lead magnet

**Email 2: Your Story (Day 1)**
- Share your origin story (struggle -> transformation)
- Make it relatable to their situation
- Subtle connection to your product/solution
- CTA: Reply with their biggest challenge (drives engagement + deliverability)

**Email 3: Quick Win / Value Bomb (Day 2)**
- Standalone piece of value they can implement today
- Should get a result that builds trust in your expertise
- CTA: Implement the tip and reply with results

**Email 4: Social Proof / Case Study (Day 3)**
- Client/student success story with specific numbers and timeline
- Show the transformation journey
- Connect success to your system/method
- CTA: Soft pitch or curiosity builder for your offer

**Email 5: Belief Shift (Day 4)**
- Challenge a common belief in your industry
- Present your alternative viewpoint with proof
- Position your solution as the better way
- CTA: Move toward your offer

**Email 6: First Offer (Day 5)**
- Transition from value to pitch
- Present offer with clear benefits + social proof
- Address top 1-2 objections
- Strong CTA with link to sales page/VSL

**Email 7: FAQ / Objection Handling (Day 6)**
- Address top 3-5 objections/questions
- Use testimonials to address each one
- Restate the guarantee
- CTA: Direct to offer

## Layer 2: Engagement Sequences

### The Segmentation Email
- Send early in the relationship
- Ask subscribers to self-identify (biggest challenge?)
- Tag and segment based on response
- Deliver targeted content to each segment
- Dramatically improves open rates and conversions

### Re-Engagement Sequence (Cold Subscribers)
- Trigger: No opens/clicks in 30-60 days
- Email 1: "Are you still interested in [topic]?"
- Email 2: Deliver unexpected value (your best content)
- Email 3: Direct question: "Should I remove you?"
- Email 4: Final notice: "This is goodbye unless you click"
- After sequence: Remove non-responders (improves deliverability)

## Layer 3: Broadcast Emails (Ongoing)

### Content Emails (2-3x per week)
- 80/20 rule: 80% value, 20% pitch
- Formats: tips, stories, case studies, industry news, contrarian takes
- Always include a CTA (even "reply" or "read more")
- Write like you're emailing one person, not a list

### Promotional Emails (during launches)
- More frequent during launch windows (daily or 2x daily)
- Vary angles: benefit-focused, story-focused, proof-focused, urgency-focused
- Always honest about deadlines
- Multiple CTAs per email

## Layer 4: Launch Sequences

### Pre-Launch (1-2 Weeks Before)
- Tease what's coming without revealing the offer
- Build anticipation with behind-the-scenes content
- Share the story of why you created this
- Create open loops the product will close

### Launch Week (5-7 Day Window)

**Day 1: Launch Announcement** -- Full breakdown, bonuses, CTA
**Day 2: Deep Dive on Benefits** -- Transformation focus, detailed case study
**Day 3: Social Proof Day** -- 3-5 testimonials, different backgrounds and results
**Day 4: Objection Handling** -- Top 3-5 objections, FAQ format, testimonials for each
**Day 5: Bonus Stack** -- Launch-exclusive bonuses, value stack, urgency
**Day 6: Last Day Warning** -- Urgency, full recap, "X people have already joined"
**Day 7: Final Day (Multiple Emails)**
- Morning: Final day reminder
- Afternoon: "A few hours left"
- Evening: "Last call / Doors closing at midnight"
- Focus on what they'll MISS, not what they'll get

## Layer 5: Post-Purchase Sequences

### Onboarding (New Buyers)
- Email 1 (Immediate): Welcome + access + first step
- Email 2 (Day 1): Quick start guide / fastest path to first win
- Email 3 (Day 3): Check-in + common early questions
- Email 4 (Day 7): Success story of someone at this stage
- Email 5 (Day 14): Advanced tips + community resources
- Email 6 (Day 30): Results check-in + next offer teaser

### Upsell Sequence (7-14 Days After Purchase)
- Email 1: Segue from main product to next level
- Email 2: Case study of someone who upgraded
- Email 3: The offer + special pricing for existing customers
- Email 4: FAQ/objections for the upgrade
- Email 5: Deadline or limited availability

---

## Layer 6: Event-Based Sequences

### Cart Abandonment
- Email 1 (1 hour): "Did something go wrong?" + link
- Email 2 (24 hours): Address top objection + testimonial
- Email 3 (48 hours): Urgency + potential bonus
- Email 4 (72 hours): Final reminder + scarcity

### Trial Expiration
- 7 days before: Value recap + what they'll lose
- 3 days before: Case study of trial-to-paid success
- 1 day before: Urgency + special offer
- Day of: Last chance
- Day after: "We saved your spot" (short grace period)

## Email Copywriting Principles

### Subject Line Formulas
- Curiosity: "The weird trick that doubled my revenue"
- Benefit: "How to get 50% more leads this week"
- Urgency: "[Name], this expires at midnight"
- Social proof: "How Sarah went from $0 to $10K/month"
- Story: "The $50K mistake I made last Tuesday"
- Question: "Are you making this common [topic] mistake?"
- Direct: "Your [product] is ready"
- Personal: "Quick question for you, [Name]"

**Subject Line Rules:**
- Under 50 characters for mobile
- Don't mislead (kills trust and deliverability)
- Test emojis (can increase opens 15-20% or decrease them)
- Personalization (first name) boosts opens
- A/B test on every broadcast

### Body Copy Frameworks

**AIDA:**
- Attention: Hook with the first line
- Interest: Story or insight
- Desire: Make them want the outcome
- Action: Clear CTA

**PAS:**
- Problem: Call out the problem
- Agitation: Twist the knife
- Solution: Present your product as the fix

### Writing Style Rules
- Write at 5th-8th grade reading level
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Use their language, not jargon
- One email = one main idea
- Conversational tone (like texting a smart friend)
- Read it out loud before sending
- Always include at least one clear CTA
- Multiple CTAs for sales emails (top, middle, bottom)

### Preview Text
- Second most important element after subject line
- Complement the subject line, don't repeat it
- 40-100 characters is sweet spot

## Deliverability

### Domain Reputation
- Warm up new sending domains gradually
- Start with small sends to most engaged subscribers
- Increase volume 20-30% per day during warmup
- Never buy email lists
- Clean list regularly

### Avoiding Spam Filters
- Avoid spam trigger words (or test carefully)
- No all caps or excessive punctuation
- Include plain text version
- Healthy text-to-image ratio (more text)
- Always include unsubscribe link
- Authenticate domain (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)

### List Hygiene
- Remove hard bounces immediately
- Suppress soft bounces after 3 consecutive failures
- Re-engagement campaigns for 60-90 day inactive
- Remove non-responders after re-engagement sequence
- Segments: hot (30 days), warm (30-60), cold (60-90), dead (90+)

## Segmentation Strategy

### Behavior-Based
- Buyers vs. non-buyers
- Product A vs. Product B buyers
- High engagement vs. low engagement
- Click behavior (reveals interests)
- Purchase recency and frequency

### Self-Identified
- Survey responses
- Opt-in source (which lead magnet/page)
- Webinar/event attendance
- Content preferences

### Value-Based
- High LTV customers (VIP treatment)
- One-time buyers (upsell sequence)
- Repeat buyers (loyalty, referral asks)
- Refund requests (different communication)

## Key Metrics

### Performance
- **Open Rate**: Target 30%+ with good hygiene (industry avg 20-25%)
- **CTR**: Target 5%+ (industry avg 2-3%)
- **CTOR**: CTR / Open Rate (email content effectiveness)
- **Unsubscribe Rate**: Under 0.5% per email
- **Spam Complaint Rate**: Under 0.1%

### Revenue
- Revenue per email sent
- Revenue per subscriber
- Conversion rate (sales / clicks)
- Average order value from email

### What to Automate vs. Broadcast

**Automate:** Welcome sequence, post-purchase onboarding, cart abandonment, re-engagement, birthday emails, lead magnet delivery, upsell sequences

**Broadcast:** Weekly content, launch campaigns, timely promotions, personal stories, flash sales
