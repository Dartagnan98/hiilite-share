# Creative Production System
**Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing -- Ad Scaling Framework + Ad Fatigue SOP + Ad Diversification Mastery**

---

## Core Truth: Creative Is Your Biggest Bottleneck

At scale, your ability to produce fresh, effective creative becomes your primary limitation. Everything else -- targeting, bidding, audiences -- is secondary to having enough quality creative to feed the algorithm.

---

## Multi-Cam Setup

### The Standard Setup
Have talent shoot with at least two camera angles:
- Primary talking head shot
- Secondary angle from a different position

### Advanced Setup (For $20k+/day)
- Primary talking head angle
- Secondary angle from different position
- Wide shot showing full body/environment
- Close-up shots for emphasis
- B-roll footage relevant to each hook
- Veo3 ads for new video intros
- Veo3 full-length ads with same talking head scripts

### What Multi-Cam Produces
From two camera angles alone:
- Standard talking head with captions
- Multi-angle cuts with captions
- Different B-roll overlays on each version
- Various lengths by changing cut points
- Different caption styles and positioning

> "I've had clients create 175+ unique creatives from just 15 original hooks using this multi-cam approach."

## Hook Iteration Strategy

When you find a hook that works, don't run it until it dies. Create multiple versions of the same core concept.

### The Core Concept Method
Identify the core concept, then create variations hitting it from different angles:

**Original:** "Baby boomers are dying and their businesses will die with them"
**Core concept:** Opportunity in business succession

Variations:
- "75 million baby boomers are retiring and need someone to take over their businesses"
- "Successful business owners are looking for successors who can keep their legacy alive"
- "There's a massive opportunity in businesses whose owners are ready to retire"
- "Business owners would rather sell to you than let their business die"
- "There's a massive opportunity hiding in plain sight in every city"

### The Emotional Angle Method
Same factual hook, different emotional lenses:

- **Fear:** "Baby boomers are dying and their businesses will die with them"
- **Opportunity:** "Baby boomers are creating the biggest business opportunity in history"
- **Urgency:** "You have a limited window to capitalize on retiring baby boomers"
- **Social proof:** "Smart investors are already capitalizing on baby boomer retirements"

### The Audience Segment Method
Successful hook customized for different segments:

- **General:** "Baby boomers are dying and their businesses will die with them"
- **For employees:** "While you're stuck in your 9-5, baby boomers are looking for successors"
- **For entrepreneurs:** "Forget starting from scratch -- buy a baby boomer's established business"
- **For investors:** "The best ROI opportunity isn't stocks -- it's baby boomer businesses"

**Target:** Create 5-10 variations of any successful hook within 24 hours.

## Creative Refresh Schedules by Spend Level

| Daily Spend | Refresh Cycle | Monitor Frequency | Backup Creatives Ready |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| $1K-$5K/day | 2-4 weeks | Weekly | 2-3 |
| $5K-$20K/day | 1-2 weeks | Every 2-3 days | 5-7 |
| $20K-$50K/day | 3-7 days | Daily | 10+ |
| $50K+/day | 1-3 days | Multiple times/day | 20+ in constant production |

**Key:** Stay ahead of fatigue rather than reacting to it. By the time you see performance decline, you've already lost money.

## Ad Fatigue: The Silent Killer of ROAS

### Definition
When video/image creatives run too long, the ad channel punishes you by:
- Lowering result volume
- Increasing cost per result

### How to Identify
Over the course of **72 hours**:
- Result volume continues to drop
- Cost per result continues to increase
- The problem only gets worse over time

### Real-World Example: $7,500/Day Campaign

| Day | Calls | Cost/Call | Change |
|-----|-------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | 63 | $119 | Baseline |
| 2 | 61 | $122 | +2.5% |
| 7 | 37 | $202 | +70% |
| 14 | 25 | $300 | +152% |
| 21 | 21 | $357 | +200% |
| 30 | 20 | $375 | +215% |
| 37 | 14 | $535 | +350% |

**From 63 calls at $119 to 14 calls at $535 -- 350% cost increase, 78% volume drop.**

### The Recovery
Only 2 new videos needed to relaunch. Results after new creative:
- Day 1: 78 calls at $96/call
- Day 2: 281 calls at $26/call
- Day 3 (half): 138 calls at $54/call

New campaign EXCEEDED pre-fatigue performance.

### The Fatigue Fix Process
1. Duplicate the campaign with fatigued ads
2. Go to ad level of newly duplicated campaign
3. Change old videos/images for new ones
4. Wait for new campaign to get approved
5. THEN shut off old campaign

**Why not just shut off?** Any calls are better than no calls while waiting for new creative. High-AOV businesses ($75K+ per sale) can tolerate higher CPA temporarily.

### The Only Prevention
Having a **creative surplus** of videos and images on standby. The worst time to prevent a problem is after it's already an issue.

> "Don't deal with ad fatigue longer than 72 hours -- it makes all the difference to your ROAS."

## Frequency Monitoring Guide

### At Mid-Level Spend ($10K-$50K/day)
- **Under 2**: Probably fine, watch for changes
- **2-3**: Start preparing fresh creative, performance may decline soon
- **Above 3**: Fatigue likely setting in, ready to refresh

### At High Spend ($50K+/day)
- Prepare fresh creative when frequency hits 5 AND ROAS diminishes
- Have backup creative ready at all times
- Can't wait until campaigns fatigue to start producing

> "If I'm profitable and scaling, I do not care about frequency being high. It doesn't matter until it matters."

### Reach vs Audience Size Analysis
Best way to identify minority hook problems before they kill campaigns:
- **Good:** High reach relative to audience size, frequency stays low
- **Bad:** Low reach relative to audience size, frequency climbs quickly
- If targeting 10M but only reaching 100K with high frequency = hook problem (message not broad enough)

## Monthly Production Cadence

### For Most Businesses (Path 2)
- Film 15 unique videos once or twice per month
- Each video built around a different core reason to buy
- Use multi-cam setup for maximum variation output
- Each shoot should produce 100+ unique creatives from 15 hooks

### For High-Spend ($50K+/day)
- Plan for talent to shoot 10-20 different hooks every other week
- This sounds like a lot, but at $50K/day you burn through creative fast
- Build an always-running production pipeline

---

## B-Roll Integration

Having a library of relevant B-roll allows even more variations:
- Same talking head footage + different B-roll = completely different feeling ads
- Shoot B-roll relevant to your hooks and audience
- Real estate: properties, neighborhoods, people looking at houses
- Fitness: gym footage, meal prep, transformations
- Business: office scenes, team meetings, client wins

---

## Key Rules

1. **Always have 3-5 creatives in reserve** for the next fatigue cycle
2. **Never wait for fatigue to start producing** -- have creative ready in advance
3. **Monitor cost per result over 72-hour windows** -- trending up = fatigue likely
4. **Only 2 new videos** can be enough to relaunch and surpass pre-fatigue performance
5. **Clients must understand ongoing creative production** -- this is not one-and-done
6. **The solution is always the same:** Duplicate campaign, swap creatives, wait for approval, shut off old
