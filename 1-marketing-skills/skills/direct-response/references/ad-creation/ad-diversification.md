# Ad Diversification Mastery
**Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing -- Ad Diversification Mastery SOP + Week 3 Majority Hook Mastery**

## Core Principle: Creative Controls Targeting Now

The single most important concept in modern advertising. Your creative IS your targeting. The algorithm uses your ad content to decide who sees it. Diverse messaging = diverse reach.

---

## The Andromeda Algorithm Shift (July 2025+)

### The Old Approach (Dying)
- Create interchangeable components: independent hook, body, CTA
- Swap hooks freely onto the same body copy
- Film 10-15 different hooks, edit onto same body
- Still technically works if you can produce 50+ creatives/week
- Hook-swapping lasts much shorter duration now

### The New Reality
- Hook, body, and CTA must be completely aligned around ONE specific reason someone would be interested
- Film entire ad start to finish as one cohesive unit
- Creating unique ads around specific reasons has much longer lifespan and better performance
- Accounts using diverse messaging in July 2025 experienced zero cost inflation

## Real Example: Andrew's Sober Living Homes

During Andromeda rollout (entire month of July):
- Costs didn't fluctuate once
- Maintained consistent $40-50 cost per qualified call
- Spending approximately $40,000
- Consistent leads, consistent quality, consistent cost per call, NO volatility

**Critical factor:** 15 completely unique creatives, each built around a different reason someone would be interested.

### Ad 1 -- Business Opportunity Angle:
- Hook: "You ever heard of 9 people paying rent on one single family home legally and having the government help you fill it?"
- Body: Business model focus -- sober living strategy, regular house conversion, 2-3 people per bedroom, rent through agencies/disability/social security

### Ad 2 -- Social Problem Angle:
- Hook: "Let's be real. America has a drug problem. But what happens after someone gets sober?"
- Body: Social issue focus -- rehab ends, insurance stops, nowhere to go, sober living as the solution

**Completely different psychological triggers. Same business.**

## The Two Paths Forward

### Path 1: High Volume (50+ Creatives/Week)
- Some ecommerce brands successfully went from 3-4 to 50+ per week
- Sheer volume overcomes algorithm changes
- Requires ~200 creatives per month
- Must relaunch campaigns and follow fatigue protocols weekly
- Demands significant resources and production systems
- Doesn't necessarily need diverse messaging

### Path 2: Diverse Messaging (15-20 Unique Ads/Month) [RECOMMENDED]
- Each ad built around a different core reason someone would be interested
- Accounts implementing this experienced no cost inflation
- Once or twice a month, film 15 videos
- Dynamic creative for individual ads
- 3X less work than Path 1
- More manageable for most businesses
- Often produces better results with less resource investment

## Majority vs Minority Hook Strategy

### Majority Hooks
- Appeal to the largest segment of your target audience
- Broad emotional appeal transcending specific demographics
- Run longer with less fatigue (resonate with more people)
- High reach relative to audience size
- Frequency stays low even with significant spend
- Consistent performance across different segments

Example: "Most people will never build wealth because they don't understand this one investing strategy for accumulating real estate with other people's money."
- Appeals to anyone who wants wealth, regardless of situation
- Uses the "Grandfather Rule": if your grandfather tells you something is legit, trust and awareness are at extreme levels

### Minority Hooks
- Appeal to a small segment of your target audience
- Very high conversion rates within that segment
- Fatigue FAST because they only resonate with small percentage
- Low reach relative to audience size
- Frequency climbs quickly (above 2-3)
- High performance initially, then rapid decline

Example: "If you're a nurse tired of working night shifts, this real estate strategy can replace your income in 12 months"
- Only appeals to nurses working night shifts who are unhappy

### The Data Signals
- **Majority hook**: High reach relative to audience, low frequency, stable performance
- **Minority hook**: Low reach relative to audience, frequency climbs fast, performance declines rapidly
- If targeting 10M people but only reaching 100K with high frequency = HOOK PROBLEM (message not broad enough)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Creative controls targeting now** -- the single most important concept
2. **Diverse messaging > Volume** for most businesses (Path 2 recommended)
3. **Each ad must be a cohesive unit** -- Hook, body, CTA all aligned around one specific reason/angle
4. **15-20 unique ads per month** is the sweet spot
5. **True diversification** = different core reasons to care about your offer, not just different people saying the same thing
6. The Dog Food Brainstorm: map out EVERY possible reason someone would buy before creating ads
