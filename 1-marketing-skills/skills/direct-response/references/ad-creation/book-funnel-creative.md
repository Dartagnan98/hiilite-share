# Book Funnel Ad Creative
**Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing -- Book Funnel Ad Creative SOP**

---

## The Book Funnel Model

- Offer a physical or digital book for free (customer pays shipping, typically $7-9.95)
- The book serves as a lead magnet and authority builder
- Backend offers (courses, coaching, masterminds) generate the real profit
- The book positions you as THE expert in your space
- The book is a loss leader -- track 30/60/90-day ROAS including backend

---

## Hook Formulas That Work for Book Funnels

### 1. The "Free Book" Hook
Lead with the free offer: "Get my new book FREE (just cover shipping)"

### 2. The Curiosity Hook
Tease a specific secret from the book: "The #1 mistake [audience] makes with [topic]"

### 3. The Story Hook
Share a transformation story the book teaches: "How I went from [before] to [after] using this one strategy"

### 4. The Authority Hook
Lead with credibility: "After helping [number] people [achieve result], I wrote the book on it"

### 5. The Contrarian Hook
Challenge conventional wisdom: "Everything you've been told about [topic] is wrong. Here's why..."

## Video Ad Structure (Recommended Format)

| Segment | Timing | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Hook | 0-3 sec | Pattern interrupt. Stop the scroll. Bold claim, surprising stat, or visual disruption |
| Problem Agitation | 3-15 sec | Describe the pain point. Make them feel understood |
| Solution Introduction | 15-30 sec | Introduce the book as the answer. What's inside and why it matters |
| Credibility/Social Proof | 30-45 sec | Results, testimonials, authority markers |
| Offer Reveal | 45-60 sec | Free + shipping. Explain exactly what they get |
| CTA | Final 5-10 sec | Clear, direct. "Click the link below to get your free copy" |

## Image Ad Best Practices

- Show the physical book (even if digital, create a mockup)
- Use bold, readable text overlay
- Include "FREE" callout prominently
- Show the author (builds trust and recognition)
- Use contrasting colors that stand out in the feed
- Include a clear value proposition in the image

## Ad Copy Frameworks

### Formula 1: Problem/Solution
1. Start with the problem (2-3 sentences)
2. Introduce the book as the solution
3. List 3-5 key takeaways from the book
4. Social proof element
5. CTA with free + shipping offer

### Formula 2: Story-Based
1. Open with a personal or client story
2. Describe the transformation
3. Connect the transformation to the book content
4. Offer the book as the roadmap
5. CTA

### Formula 3: List-Based
1. "Inside this book you'll discover:"
2. Bullet point 5-7 key benefits/secrets
3. Each bullet should create curiosity
4. Close with the free offer and CTA

### Formula 4: Testimonial-Led
1. Open with a powerful customer quote
2. Expand on the result they achieved
3. Connect it back to the book
4. Offer details
5. CTA

## Creative Testing Strategy

### Phase 1: Hook Testing (3-5 days)
- Create 3-5 different hooks for the same offer
- Use the same body copy and CTA
- Identify winning hook based on CTR and CPL

### Phase 2: Body Copy Testing
- Take winning hook
- Test 3-4 different body copy angles
- Vary between short (<125 words), medium (125-250), and long form (250+)
- Test different proof elements

### Phase 3: Format Testing
- Test winning copy across formats: video, image, carousel
- Test vertical (9:16) vs square (1:1) vs horizontal (16:9)
- Test UGC-style vs polished production

### Phase 4: Scale Winners
- Take top 2-3 performing ads
- Scale budget 20-30% every 2-3 days
- Monitor frequency and cost metrics
- Refresh creative when frequency hits 3.0+

## Platform-Specific Tips

### Facebook/Instagram
- Lead with value, not the pitch
- Native-looking content outperforms polished ads
- Use the Facebook Ad Library to study competitors
- Carousel ads work well for showing book contents/chapters
- Reels placement can drive cheap traffic

### YouTube
- First 5 seconds must hook (they can skip after 5)
- Longer form (2-5 min) works for higher-ticket backend offers
- Include the book cover on screen
- End screen with clear CTA

### TikTok
- Raw, authentic content outperforms polished
- Show the physical book in hand
- "BookTok" style reviews can work
- Fast-paced, high-energy delivery
- Hook must be immediate (first 1-2 seconds)

## Targeting Strategy

### Warm Audiences
- Website visitors (30, 60, 90, 180 days)
- Email list
- Social media engagers
- Video viewers (25%, 50%, 75%, 95%)

### Lookalike Audiences
- 1% lookalike of book buyers
- 1% lookalike of backend buyers (most valuable)
- 1% lookalike of email list
- Stack multiple lookalikes in one ad set

### Cold Audiences
- Stack relevant interests, behaviors, demographics
- Target competitor audiences
- Target related authors and thought leaders
- Broad targeting (let the algorithm optimize)

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| CPC (Cost Per Click) | Under $1-2 for cold traffic |
| CTR (Click-Through Rate) | 1%+ for link clicks |
| Landing Page Conversion | 20-40% for free + shipping |
| Cost Per Acquisition | Under $15-20 for book buyer |
| Backend Conversion Rate | Where profit comes from |
| ROAS | Track 30-day, 60-day, 90-day including backend |

---

## Budget Recommendations

- **Testing Phase**: $20-50/day per ad set, test for 3-7 days
- **Optimization Phase**: $50-100/day on winning ad sets
- **Scaling Phase**: Increase 20-30% every 2-3 days on winners
- **Kill Rule**: If an ad set doesn't get results after spending 2-3x your target CPA, kill it

---

## Common Mistakes

1. Focusing on the book itself instead of the transformation
2. Not testing enough creative variations
3. Scaling too fast (more than 20-30% budget increase per day)
4. Not tracking backend conversions (the book is a loss leader)
5. Using generic stock images instead of the actual book
6. Writing copy that's too clever instead of clear
7. No strong hook in first 3 seconds
8. Ignoring mobile optimization (60%+ of traffic is mobile)
9. Not refreshing creative before fatigue sets in
10. Targeting too narrow on cold audiences
