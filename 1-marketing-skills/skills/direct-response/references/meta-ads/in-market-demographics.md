# In-Market Demographic Mastery
**Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing -- Week 3 Lesson 7 + Direct Response Ad Creation SOP**

---

## The 3 Audience Tiers

### Tier 1: In Market (3-4% of total addressable audience)
- Already BELIEVE in the broad category/vehicle you're selling
- Don't need to be told WHY the industry makes sense
- Need WHICH approach or WHO to pick
- Shortest sales cycles, highest ROAS
- Lowest friction, lowest cost to convert

### Tier 2: Needs Convinced (30% of market)
- Want the general outcome ("I want a second income")
- Not 100% sure your method is correct
- Need more "why" content
- Higher cost per conversion
- Higher no-show and churn risk

### Tier 3: Everyone Else (Cold Mass)
- Not initially on the table
- Ultra-expensive to convert
- Causes chaotic lead quality

**Rule:** Start with Tier 1. Master it. Exploit it to the maximum. Only then expand.

> "If you're not speaking to that 3-4% who already want it, you're gonna inflate your costs."
> "Communicate like they're already sold -- no mention of 'why real estate at all?'. Just assume they know."

---

## In-Market Sub-Segments

Not all in-market buyers are identical. Example (real estate):

| Sub-Segment | Motivation | Messaging |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Current STR investors | Optimize or expand quickly | Efficiency, scale, ROI maximization |
| Long-term rental investors | Curious if STR has better returns | Comparison, returns data |
| Tax burden people | Know RE reduces taxes | Tax strategy, specific property types |
| Ambitious newcomers (still in-market) | Actively searching for program/mentor | Credibility, proof, path to first deal |

Each segment has its own motivations, language, and circumstances. Create separate copy/ad sets for each.

## Messaging for In-Market Audiences

### What They Want to Hear
1. Your approach is FASTER or EASIER than the standard
2. Your approach is MORE PROFITABLE or LESS RISKY
3. Your approach is COMPATIBLE with their time, lifestyle, or resources

### What They DON'T Need
- Being sold on why the category exists
- Basic education about the industry
- Convincing that the opportunity is valid

### The Key Shift
- "Needs convinced" messaging: "Did you know real estate can build wealth?"
- In-market messaging: "You already know real estate builds wealth. Here's the approach that adds 5-figure monthly cash flow without the overhead."

## Targeting In-Market Audiences

### Interest Stacking
Combine related interests to find in-market people:
- Real estate + investing + entrepreneurship
- Short-term rentals + Airbnb + VRBO
- Trading + options + market analysis

### Custom Audiences (Best for In-Market)
- Lookalikes of your best customers (1%)
- Lookalikes of your purchasers
- Email subscriber lookalikes
- Video viewer lookalikes (people who watched 75%+ of your content)

### Behavioral Targeting
- People who have purchased similar products
- People who engage with competitor content
- People who follow industry thought leaders

### Qualification Inside the Ad
Light gatekeeping to filter in-market from needs-convinced:
- "If you already own at least one property..."
- "This isn't for people who think real estate is too risky..."
- "For investors already doing 5+ deals per year..."

This helps the pixel learn who your actual buyers are.

## Real-Time Contextual Targeting

Platforms deliver ads based on recent user behavior. If your buyer just browsed "best real estate investing strategies," the algorithm can show your relevant ad.

**Incorporate contextual lines:**
- "Still scrolling for the fix to your day-trading headaches?"
- "Already hitting your watchlist daily and not seeing results?"

The platform sees your ad matches what they just engaged with = lower CPM + higher watch rates.

## The Audience Progression Framework

### Phase 1: In-Market Only
- Target the 3-4% who already want what you offer
- Use hooks that assume they're sold on the industry
- Measure: show-up rates, lead quality, sales cycle length

### Phase 2: Expand to Warm Needs-Convinced
- After maximizing in-market
- Test angles for "maybe this approach is good?"
- Higher budget needed, accept higher CPA

### Phase 3: Broader Market
- Only at significant scale ($50K+/day)
- Test mass market angles
- Expect much higher costs and longer sales cycles

### Quality Indicators That In-Market Messaging Is Working
- Higher show-up rates
- Fewer leads ghosting
- Shorter sales cycles
- Lower CPA
- Better close rates

## Broad Targeting (Modern Approach)

Modern platforms favor broad targeting with algorithm optimization:

1. Start with broad, relevant audiences (10M+ people)
2. Optimize for actual business outcome (purchases, calls, leads)
3. Let the algorithm learn from conversion patterns
4. Focus optimization on creative and landing pages

**Facebook's Advantage Detailed Targeting** automatically expands beyond your selected audiences to find converters. In many cases, you reach warm audiences even when targeting cold.

---

## Key Principles

1. **In-market first, always** -- master this before expanding
2. **Sub-segment your in-market** -- different motivations need different messages
3. **Assume they're sold** on the category, pitch the METHOD
4. **Qualify inside the ad** -- filter for in-market, deter needs-convinced
5. **Use contextual hooks** -- reference what they're already thinking about
6. **Watch quality signals** -- show rates and close rates matter more than CPL
