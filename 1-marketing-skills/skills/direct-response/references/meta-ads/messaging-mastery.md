# Messaging Mastery: How to Attract the Right People
**Source: Jeremy Haynes / Megalodon Marketing -- Week 3 Lessons 9-10 + Direct Response Ad Creation SOP**

---

## Core Principle

Your messaging determines who responds. The hook, body, and CTA must align to attract the RIGHT people -- not just any people. Wrong messaging = expensive leads that never convert.

---

## The Three Communication Styles (Circles, Triangles, Squares)

### Circle People (Visionaries, Decision-Makers)
- Want the big picture FAST
- Don't want data dumps or disclaimers
- Respond to broad vision statements
- Example: "This new method can add 5-figure monthly cash flow without endless phone calls"
- Most common among your ideal buyers

### Triangle People (Doers, Executors)
- Want the step-by-step process
- Respond to "how it works" messaging
- More detail-oriented but still action-driven

### Square People (Analysts, Researchers)
- Want all the data, proof, specifics
- Respond to case studies, statistics, ROI calculations
- Take longest to convert but often highest LTV

**For direct response ads:** Lead with Circle Talk (big picture), include enough for Triangles (how), and provide Square content in retargeting/nurture sequences.

---

## Messaging for In-Market Prospects

### What They Already Believe
- The category/industry works (real estate, trading, fitness, etc.)
- They want the general outcome
- They're evaluating which approach or expert to choose

### What Your Messaging Must Do
1. Acknowledge their existing belief in 1-2 lines
2. Introduce your unique angle or approach
3. Show why your method is faster, easier, more profitable, or less risky
4. Qualify them (filter for in-market, deter tire-kickers)

### Message Structure for In-Market
- **Hook:** Reference the belief or situation they already hold
- **Body:** Justify why your unique approach outperforms the mainstream
- **CTA:** "Tap here to learn how we do it" or "Press below to get started"
- ZERO time convincing them the category is valid

---

## Qualifying vs Disqualifying in Your Messaging

### Why It Matters
- Qualifies the right people for your pixel
- Improves downstream funnel performance
- Reduces wasted ad spend on tire-kickers

### Qualifying Statements
- "If you already own at least one property and want more monthly income..."
- "For investors already doing 5+ deals per year..."
- "If you have at least $10K to invest..."

### Disqualifying Statements
- "This isn't for people who think real estate is too risky or unproven"
- "If you're still trying to figure out IF this works, this isn't for you"
- "This is for people who are past the 'should I?' stage"

---

## The Wrong Messaging Trap

### Needs-Convinced Messaging (Expensive)
- "Did you know real estate can build wealth?"
- "Let me show you why stocks are a safe bet!"
- "If you aren't sure trading works..."

This explodes cost per lead, lowers show-up rates, and creates chaos on sales calls.

### In-Market Messaging (Profitable)
- "You already know real estate builds wealth. Here's how to add 5-figure cash flow."
- "You've tried day trading. Here's a simpler approach."
- "Your job pays six figures but you want $3-5K more per month."

## Messaging Across the Funnel

### Cold Traffic (First Touch)
- Use in-market hooks
- Assume they already believe in the category
- Focus on your unique angle
- Keep it short (30-60 seconds for video)

### Warm Traffic (After Content Exposure)
- Can go deeper into the how/why
- Reference content they've already seen
- More specific claims and proof points
- Address specific objections

### Hot Traffic (Post-Opt-In)
- Direct and specific
- Reference actions they've taken ("You scheduled a call...")
- Address the remaining objections
- Social proof and testimonial-heavy
- Urgency and scarcity elements

### Retargeting (Didn't Convert)
- Call out their specific situation
- "You saw this offer but didn't take action..."
- Different hooks addressing different buying reasons
- 20+ variations with 20+ different angles

## Messaging by Buying Motive

People buy for different reasons. Map out ALL buying motives and create messaging for each.

### The Lamborghini Example
Up to 25 different reasons someone buys a Lamborghini -- not just "go fast":
- Status and prestige
- Investment (appreciating asset)
- Networking tool
- Reward for achievement
- Childhood dream
- Engineering appreciation
- Social media content
- Business marketing
- Lifestyle signaling
- Community/club membership

### Application to Any Product
Map out 20+ buying motives for YOUR product, then create:
- Individual ads around each motive
- Retargeting sequences using different motives
- Content pieces addressing each motive
- Different hooks targeting each motive

## The Rotisserie Chicken Analogy

A grocery store doesn't cater to one type of buyer. The rotisserie chicken serves:
- The busy professional who wants quick dinner
- The budget-conscious who want value
- The meal prepper who needs protein
- The family that wants easy feeding

Your product serves multiple types of buyers too. Create messaging for each type.

## Key Messaging Rules

1. **Lead with their belief, not your pitch** -- acknowledge what they already know
2. **Speak to the 3-4% in-market first** -- they're the cheapest to convert
3. **Qualify inside the ad** -- help the pixel learn who your buyers are
4. **Circle talk for cold traffic** -- big picture, not details
5. **20+ angles in retargeting** -- people buy for different reasons
6. **Context over generic pitches** -- reference what's happening in their world
7. **Never educate when you should be closing** -- in-market people don't need to be convinced the category works
8. **Test messaging changes before audience changes** -- usually the message is the problem, not the audience
