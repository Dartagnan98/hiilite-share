## How to Give IG Ads a Native Feeling

### The Research Process
1. Launch Instagram ads
2. Within first 72 hours, people interact (likes, comments)
3. Click through their profiles (if not private)
4. See what they're posting
5. Go to their FOLLOWING tab
6. Click through profiles of accounts they follow
7. Study what those accounts post
8. Study any brands they follow
9. Watch some stories from these accounts
10. Apply these insights into your ad creatives

> "You'd be shocked of how incredible this is, just to get a good idea of what people are actively looking at."

### Why This Works
- Most people skip ads immediately when they see "Sponsored"
- Native-feeling ads blend into what they're used to seeing
- Increases probability of attention vs being ignored
- Makes the ad feel like content, not an interruption

## Context Trumps Content

### The Peloton / Ryan Reynolds Example
- Peloton ran a controversial holiday ad (2019) that went viral
- Ryan Reynolds' gin company hired the SAME actress
- Showed her distraught at a bar: "Well, at least the gin's good"
- The ad ONLY worked because of the context (everyone knew the Peloton ad)
- Then at Art Basel: $120K banana taped to wall sold. Reynolds taped a gin bottle to a wall: "Are we doing this right?"
- Massive viral effect -- all from leveraging context

### How to Use Context
- There's always something contextual going on in your specific market
- More relatable than generic direct response pitches
- Reference current events, trends, industry shifts in your hooks
- What's happening in your market RIGHT NOW that people are talking about?

## Tap into Culture

### What "Culture" Means for Ads
- The feel of content your audience is used to seeing
- How people in that space dress
- How they talk to each other
- Specific vocab terms they use
- Influencers they follow

### Example: Luxury Clothing Brand
- Target people interested in Louis V, Dior, Supreme, Louboutin
- Study influencers wearing those brands (like rapper Fabulous, OBJ)
- Use actors/models who fit that demographic profile
- Also narrow targeting: interest in brands + influencers + top 5% income

### Implementation
- Use influencers or create content that matches the culture
- Match the dress, language, vibe, and aesthetic
- If you match the culture, high probability of being accepted by that audience

## Optimizing Your Instagram Profile for Ads

### Business Profile Required
- Can't run ads from personal profiles
- Convert personal to business in Instagram settings
- Enables: Instagram Shopping, Instagram Ads, Instagram Insights, Promote Post button
- Connect profile to Business Manager (business.facebook.com)
- Or connect through Instagram app: Settings > Connected Accounts

### Username Best Practices
- Simple, recognizable, easy to find
- If business name is taken: use business name + location or abbreviation
- Your username search ranking depends on the searcher's network
- The NAME field (separate from username) is what appears in search results

### Profile Picture
- Business: use your logo with strong color psychology
- Personal brand: quality photo with contrasting colors
- Consider dark mode: colored logos/photos pop on black backgrounds
- Consider light mode: needs to stand out against white
- Profile picture shows next to every comment and post/ad

### Bio Optimization
- 150 character limit
- Keep everything above the fold (no "...see more")
- Include: summary of what you do + key benefit + call to action
- CTA usually points to link in bio
- Don't stack too much text

### Connect to Business Manager
Two methods:
1. Business Manager > left sidebar > Instagram tab > click Add > login
2. Instagram app > Settings > Connected Accounts > link Facebook page + ad accounts

## What Works Best: CPA Ranges

### Define Your Tiers
| Tier | Action |
|------|--------|
| Great CPA | Scale 20-30% daily |
| Good/Average CPA | Scale 10% daily |
| Poor/Intolerable CPA | Cut immediately |

### Beyond CPA: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- CPA is cost per lead/action
- CAC is cost per actual paying customer
- A $200 CPL selling a $3,000 product might be great if close rate is high
- Always factor in CAC before cutting campaigns

### Performance Columns (3 Quality Signals)
1. **Quality Ranking** -- perceived ad quality vs same-audience competitors
2. **Engagement Rate Ranking** -- expected engagement vs competitors
3. **Conversion Rate Ranking** -- expected conversion rate vs competitors

All three low = update creatives or try new audiences.
Use Facebook Ad Library to study competitor ads.

### CTR Benchmarks
- Above 2%: Good
- Above 5%: Great
- Below 2%: Creative/copy not resonating

Good CTR + low conversions = funnel problem, not ad problem.

## Test, Learn, Optimize (72-Hour Cycles)

1. Launch Instagram ads
2. Review data daily
3. Make decisions every 72 hours
4. Test new creatives based on learnings
5. Consider what's performing + what to vary
6. Get new creatives made and launched
7. Repeat indefinitely

> "It's very rare that a specific ad lasts more than a few weeks because we're constantly running tests."

## Mobile-First Rule

- 100% of Instagram ad inventory is on mobile
- Desktop viewing exists but mobile dominates
- Always adjust for mobile aspect ratios
- Test vertical (9:16) for maximum screen real estate
- Ensure all landing pages are mobile-optimized
