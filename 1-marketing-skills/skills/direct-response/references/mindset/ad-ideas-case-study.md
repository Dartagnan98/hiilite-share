# Ad Ideas Case Study: Sober Living Homes

Source: MIM - Andrew Lamb Case Study

---

## Niche Overview

### Why Sober Living is a Great Ad Niche
- **High LTV:** $500-$2,500+/month rent, 3-12+ month stays ($2,400-$12,000 LTV)
- **Recurring revenue model** (monthly payments)
- **Constant demand** (addiction doesn't stop)
- **Emotional buying decision** (families pay whatever it takes)
- **Low ad competition** (most homes rely on referrals only)
- **Two buyer personas** (person in recovery AND their family)

---

## The Two Audiences

### Person in Recovery
- Looking for a safe, supportive environment for themselves
- Needs: hope, understanding, community, structure
- Age: typically 18-45

### Family/Loved One
- Parent, spouse, or sibling seeking help for someone they love
- Needs: reassurance, trust, safety, peace of mind
- Age: typically 40-65 (parents)
- Often in crisis mode -- speed to lead is critical

---

## 12 Ad Angles

### For the Person in Recovery

**1. Fresh Start**
- "Ready For A Fresh Start? [City] Sober Living Has A Bed For You"
- Focus: new beginnings, clean slate

**2. Community & Brotherhood/Sisterhood**
- "You Don't Have To Do This Alone"
- Focus: support system, housemates who understand

**3. Structure Without Institutionalization**
- "Structure That Supports Recovery Without Feeling Like An Institution"
- Key differentiator: this is a HOME, not a facility

**4. Accountability**
- "Accountability That Actually Works"
- Drug testing, curfews, meetings as features (not restrictions)

**5. Success Stories**
- "6 Months Ago, [Name] Walked Through Our Door. Today, [Result]"
- Real testimonials, before/after (emotional, not physical)

**6. Location-Specific**
- "Sober Living in [City] -- Steps From [Beach/Downtown/Nature]"
- Location as selling point

**7. Employment & Life Skills**
- "Get Sober AND Get Your Life Back"
- Job placement, life skills, financial literacy

### For the Family

**8. Peace of Mind for Parents**
- "Finally, A Safe Place For Your Son/Daughter"
- Staff supervision, drug testing, structure

**9. "What Comes After Rehab"**
- "Rehab Was Step 1. Now What?"
- Bridge between rehab and independent living
- Stats: relapse rates highest in first 90 days

**10. Intervention/Crisis**
- "If Your Loved One Needs Help Right Now, We Have Availability"
- Urgency-based, immediate availability, 24/7 contact

**11. Insurance & Affordability**
- "Quality Sober Living Your Family Can Afford"
- Payment plans, insurance, scholarships

**12. Education/Awareness**
- "5 Things Every Parent Should Know About Sober Living"
- Lead magnet approach: value first, offer second

## Creative Types

### 1. Video Tour
- Walk through house (bedrooms, common areas, kitchen)
- Staff member narrates
- Under 60 seconds, warm lighting, homey feel

### 2. Testimonial Video
- Real resident/alumni, 60-90 seconds
- Raw and authentic (iPhone filming > professional)
- Include before/after context

### 3. Image Carousel
- 4-6 photos: home, amenities, local area
- Each slide has benefit callout
- Last slide = CTA

### 4. Single Image + Long Copy
- Strong image + 500-1,000 word story
- Works extremely well for family audience (they read every word)

### 5. Staff Introduction Video
- House manager or clinical staff introduces themselves
- Humanizes the brand, builds trust

## Targeting Strategies

### Person in Recovery
- Interests: AA, NA, recovery, sobriety, sober living
- Custom audiences: rehab website visitors (via pixel)
- Lookalikes: based on past residents
- Location: city + 25-50 mile radius + cities people relocate from

### Family
- Interests: Al-Anon, addiction help, family support groups
- Income: middle to upper-middle class
- Location: can be broader (families relocate loved ones)
- Lookalikes: based on past family inquiries

### Retargeting
- Website visitors who didn't apply
- Video viewers (25%, 50%, 75%)
- Page engagers
- Message: testimonials, FAQs, "we have beds available"

---

## Landing Page Structure
1. Hero: home photo + headline + CTA
2. Trust bar: accreditations, certifications, years, beds filled
3. About: mission, why you started
4. Program details: what's included
5. Virtual tour: video or photo gallery
6. Testimonials: 3-5 reviews
7. Pricing: transparent or "starting at"
8. FAQ: top 8-10 questions
9. CTA: application form or click-to-call
10. Footer: phone, address, social links

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Cost per lead | $15-$50 |
| Cost per tour/visit | $50-$150 |
| Cost per move-in | $200-$800 |
| Lead to tour rate | 20-30% |
| Tour to move-in rate | 40-60% |
| Average monthly rent | $800-$2,000 |
| Average stay | 3-6 months |
| LTV per resident | $2,400-$12,000 |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Underserved niche with high LTV and emotional buying triggers
2. Split messaging between person in recovery and family
3. Authenticity > production quality
4. Video tours and testimonials convert highest
5. Retargeting critical (longer decision cycle for families)
6. Transparent pricing builds trust
7. Availability messaging creates urgency
8. Location is a selling point
9. Speed to lead matters enormously (families in crisis)
10. Follow up relentlessly but compassionately
