## TikTok Creative Fundamentals

### The Hook is Everything
- First 1-3 seconds determine if someone watches or scrolls
- Must be a scroll-stopping moment (visual or verbal)
- Pattern interrupts work: unexpected visuals, bold claims, curiosity gaps
- Test 5-10 different hooks per ad concept

### Native Content Wins
- Ads that look like organic TikTok content outperform polished production
- Use iPhone filming, natural lighting, casual tone
- Avoid looking like a corporate advertisement
- Mirror the format of trending TikTok content

### Safe Zone Compliance
- TikTok UI overlays parts of the screen (username, buttons, description)
- Keep important text and faces in the center safe zone
- Don't put key info at the bottom 20% of the screen
- Test on mobile before launching

### Creative Volume is Key
- TikTok creative fatigues faster than any other platform
- Plan to produce 5-10 new creatives per week minimum
- Variations of winning concepts: different hooks, different angles, different CTAs
- A/B test relentlessly

### Video Structure
1. **Hook** (0-3 seconds): grab attention
2. **Problem/agitation** (3-10 seconds): relate to their pain
3. **Solution** (10-20 seconds): present your product/service
4. **Social proof** (20-30 seconds): results, testimonials, numbers
5. **CTA** (last 5 seconds): clear next step

### Content Types That Work
- UGC (user-generated content) style testimonials
- Before/after demonstrations
- "Day in my life" using the product
- Reaction/response videos
- Trending sound/format adaptations with product integration
- Unboxing and first impression videos

### What to Avoid
- Overly polished, corporate-looking ads
- Long intros before getting to the point
- Static images or slideshows
- Ignoring trends and current cultural moments
- Same creative running for weeks without refresh
