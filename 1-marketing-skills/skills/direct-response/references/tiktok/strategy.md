## TikTok in the MIM Ecosystem

TikTok is referenced throughout Jeremy Haynes' MIM course as a comparison platform to Facebook and Google:

### TikTok vs Google vs Facebook
- **Google:** Sniper approach, intent-driven, very consistent once working
- **Facebook:** Broader reach, algorithm-driven, more volatile
- **TikTok:** Most volatile of all three, campaigns spike and crash frequently
- Google is the most consistent; TikTok/Facebook see more volatility

### When to Use TikTok
- After mastering Facebook/Google (not a beginner platform)
- When you have strong video creative (TikTok is video-first)
- For brands targeting younger demographics
- For ecom brands with visually compelling products
- When you need additional scale beyond Facebook/Google

### TikTok Creative Principles
- Native-feeling content outperforms polished ads
- UGC-style creative works best
- Vertical video (9:16) is mandatory
- First 1-3 seconds determine everything (the hook)
- Safe zone compliance: keep text/faces away from UI overlay areas

### TikTok Campaign Considerations
- Different bidding models than Facebook/Google
- Requires high creative volume (creative fatigue is fast)
- Attribution can be challenging
- TikTok Shop integration for ecom
- Smart+ campaigns (TikTok's version of advantage+ / PMax)
