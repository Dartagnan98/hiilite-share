# Handling Inbound Leads Who Only Filled Out a Form

Source: MIM Bonus - Sales Training with Jeremy Haynes (Lesson 3)

---

## The New Reality of Consumer Behavior

The old model: leads book a call, watch a sales video, and wait for your call.
The new model: leads fill out a form and expect text-first communication.

### Why Phone Calls Don't Work Like They Used To
- Most people have **Do Not Disturb** on permanently
- iPhone feature "Silence Unknown Callers" is default for many users
- Years of political calls, scammers, and spam have destroyed phone trust
- Nobody goes through missed calls from random numbers and calls them back
- Even if someone fills out your form, they're often in the middle of something (DND on)

---

## The Inbound Lead Protocol

### Step 1: Text With Your Name First
- Send a text BEFORE calling: "Hey, it's Jeremy"
- iPhone trick: when you then call, the phone displays "Maybe: Jeremy" under the random number
- Much higher answer rate when a name is associated with the call

### Step 2: Call Twice
- First call on DND = goes straight to voicemail
- Second call on DND = rings through (iPhone feature for repeat callers)
- Always call at least twice back-to-back

### Step 3: Text an Open-Ended Question (Reduce Your No's)

**Bad approach (50/50 odds):**
"Hey, do you have 5 minutes?"
- 50% yes, 50% no. Binary question.

**Good approach (75/25 odds):**
"Hey Leo, it's Jeremy. Saw you just filled out a form of ours online. If you're open in the next 5 minutes, I can call now. If not, I have time later today. Or you can just text me your questions right here."
- Three positive options = 75% chance of positive response
- Only 25% chance of a flat "no"

### The Ratio Rule
- Every additional positive option you give reduces the probability of "no"
- Binary question = 50% no
- Three options = 25% no
- Goal: reduce no's as low as possible in every communication

## Speed to Lead

### The Stats
- Texting or calling within 5 minutes increases close/communication probability by 50%
- This is validated by massive data sets, not anecdotal
- First person to contact wins 78% of the time

### The Reality
- You may already have 4-8 scheduled calls in your calendar
- Some will no-show, creating windows to reach new leads
- If you're a small team handling all inbound, you'll get overwhelmed
- Prioritize speed when you have availability
- Even if you can't reach out in 5 minutes, do it at the first opportunity

## The Complete Inbound Lead Sequence

1. Lead fills out form
2. **Immediately:** Text with your name
3. **Immediately:** Call twice back-to-back
4. **If no answer:** Send open-ended text with 3+ response options
5. **Within 1 hour:** Follow up via text with value content
6. **Same day:** Try calling again during business hours
7. **Next day:** Value-driven text + call attempt
8. **Day 3:** "Just checking in" + case study or testimonial
9. **Day 5-7:** Final direct outreach attempt
10. **After 7 days:** Move to automated nurture sequence

---

## Text-First Communication

### Why Text Beats Phone
- People actually read texts (even from unknown numbers)
- Lower barrier to respond
- Can respond on their own time
- Less confrontational than a phone call
- Allows them to engage at their comfort level

### Texting Rules
1. Always include your name
2. Keep messages short (2-4 sentences)
3. End with an open question
4. Don't send links in the first text
5. Don't use formal/corporate language
6. Text like a normal person
7. Respond quickly when they reply
8. Move to phone only when they're engaged

---

## Key Principles

1. Bias towards text-first communication in today's DND world
2. Always text your name before calling (iPhone "Maybe: [Name]" trick)
3. Call twice to get through Do Not Disturb
4. Give 3+ response options to reduce your no's
5. Speed to lead = 50% higher probability of conversion
6. Don't give up after one attempt -- sequence your outreach over days
7. Value-driven follow-up beats "are you ready to buy?" every time
8. Respect that people are busy -- offer flexible communication options
9. The first person to make meaningful contact wins
10. If overwhelmed with leads, use the gaps between scheduled calls to reach new ones
