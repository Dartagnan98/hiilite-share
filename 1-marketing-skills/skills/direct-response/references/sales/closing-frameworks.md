# Closing Frameworks & Sales Team Systems

Source: MIM Bonus - Sales Training with Jeremy Haynes (Lessons 4-10)

---

## Sales Team Structures

### Structure 1: One-Call Close (Solo Closer)
- Lead books call, closer handles everything
- Works for smaller operations
- Closer does qualification + presentation + close
- Pro: simple, one person handles full cycle
- Con: closer wastes time on unqualified leads

### Structure 2: SDR + Closer (Two-Step)
- SDR qualifies and books
- Closer presents and closes
- SDR acts as filter and interest-builder
- Pro: closers only talk to qualified leads
- Con: requires managing SDR quality and handoff

### Structure 3: SDR + Closer + Cleaner (Three-Step)
- SDR qualifies and books
- Closer presents and attempts to close
- Cleaner follows up on non-closes

## The Cleaner Role

### What a Cleaner Does
- Follows up with leads who didn't close on the first call
- Handles post-call objections and questions
- Keeps the conversation warm between call attempts
- Sends additional value and trust assets
- Re-books leads for follow-up calls

### Why You Need a Cleaner
- Most revenue is hiding in follow-up
- Closers hate following up (they want fresh leads)
- A dedicated cleaner catches the deals closers leave on the table
- Closers focus on first-call closes, cleaner handles the rest

### Cleaner Best Practices
- Follow up within 1 hour of the initial call
- Reference specific things discussed on the call
- Send personalized value (case studies, testimonials, educational content)
- Multiple touchpoints: call, text, email, video
- 3-5 follow-up attempts minimum before giving up
- "Value-first" approach: educate, don't pressure

## Using Sales Videos

### Pre-Call Videos
- 10-20 minute VSL between booking and call
- Educates, builds authority, handles common objections
- People who watch full VSL close at 2-3x higher rates
- Track who watched and for how long

### Types of Sales Videos
1. **Authority/credibility video** -- who you are, results you've gotten
2. **Case study compilation** -- multiple client success stories
3. **Objection handler videos** -- specific videos addressing common concerns
4. **Behind-the-scenes** -- show the team, process, culture
5. **"What to expect" video** -- walk through the call experience

### When to Send
- Confirmation page: embed multiple videos
- Email sequences: one per email
- SDR follow-up: send relevant video based on conversation
- Pre-call day: reminder with authority video

## Selfie Follow-Up Video Texts

### Why Selfie Videos Work
- Personal, authentic, impossible to ignore
- Shows real human behind the business
- Creates connection before/after the call
- Pattern interrupt in a sea of text messages
- Higher response rate than any other follow-up method

### How to Do It
1. Use an iPhone (better video quality)
2. Film a 15-30 second selfie video
3. Address the person BY NAME
4. Reference something specific about their situation
5. Keep it casual and genuine
6. Send via text message (not email)

### Script Template
"Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] with [Company]. Just wanted to reach out -- I saw you [booked a call / filled out a form] and I'm really looking forward to chatting with you about [their specific situation]. Just wanted to put a face to the name. Talk soon!"

### When to Send
- After booking but before the call
- After a no-show (to reschedule)
- After a call where they didn't close (to follow up)
- When re-engaging cold leads

## Getting Leads Back on the Phone

### Value-Driven Follow-Up
- Don't just call and say "are you ready to buy?"
- Provide VALUE in every follow-up touchpoint
- Send educational content relevant to their situation
- Share client results and case studies
- Position follow-up as helping, not selling

### Follow-Up Sequence
**Day 1 (same day as missed call/no-show):**
- Call + text + email within 5 minutes
- Selfie video text

**Day 2:**
- Value email with case study
- Text with helpful resource

**Day 3:**
- Call attempt
- "No worries, life happens" text

**Day 5:**
- Email with educational content
- Social proof (testimonial video)

**Day 7:**
- Final direct attempt
- "Held a spot for you" angle

**Day 14+:**
- Move to nurture sequence
- Re-engage with new value content

## Texting Leads Manually

### Why Manual Texting Matters
- Automated texts feel automated
- Manual texts feel personal
- Higher response rates
- Can adapt tone to the individual
- Shows you actually care

### Texting Best Practices
1. Use their name
2. Reference something specific about them
3. Keep it short (2-3 sentences max)
4. End with an open-ended question
5. Don't use corporate language
6. Text like a normal person, not a business
7. Include your name so they know who's texting
8. Text before calling (iPhone shows "Maybe: [Name]")

### What NOT to Do
- Don't send walls of text
- Don't use all caps or excessive punctuation
- Don't be pushy or salesy
- Don't send links in the first text
- Don't automate what should be personal

## Key Principles

1. The Cleaner role catches the revenue closers leave behind
2. Sales videos do 50% of the selling before the call
3. Selfie video texts are the highest-converting follow-up method
4. Value-driven follow-up outperforms pressure-based follow-up every time
5. Manual texting > automated texting for response rates
6. Follow up 5-7 times minimum before giving up
7. Every touchpoint should provide value, not just ask for the sale
8. Track who watches videos -- they close at 2-3x higher rates
9. Speed to lead is the foundation of everything
10. The best sales teams have clear roles: SDR, Closer, Cleaner
