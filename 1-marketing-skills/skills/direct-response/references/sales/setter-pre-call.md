# SDR Best Practices & Pre-Call Systems

Source: MIM Bonus - Backend Selling Systems (Lessons 6-8) + Sales Training (Lesson 9)

---

## The SDR's Real Job

Most people think SDRs exist to qualify and schedule. That's the perceived job. The REAL job:
1. Qualify leads
2. Increase show rate probability
3. **Get leads the information they need to increase interest level**
4. Input data into CRM for tailored closer communication

### The Restaurant Analogy
A fancy Michelin restaurant treats the reservation confirmation call as part of the experience. Internet marketing businesses treat SDR calls as a selfish filter. That initial interaction IS the experience -- treat it accordingly.

---

## Interest Level Framework

Leads come in at different interest levels:

| Level | Description | SDR Action |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Curious** | Just exploring, filled out a form | Send tailored info to increase interest |
| **General Interest** | Knows about the offer, has questions | Answer questions, send case studies |
| **High Interest** | Actively wants to solve the problem, few remaining questions | Qualify financials, book call |
| **Conviction** | Ready to buy, just needs final push | Pass directly to closer |

### The Money Zone
Deals get done between Conviction and Sold. Everything below that = no money collected.

### The Critical Mistake
SDRs develop **comparison bias**: they compare high-interest leads to lower-interest leads and DQ the lower ones. But lower interest does NOT mean unqualified. It means they need more information.

**Information is what gets people to act. Information leads to action.**

---

## Comparison Bias in SDRs

- SDRs naturally favor high-interest leads (less work, easier handoff)
- Over time, financially qualified leads at lower interest levels get canceled
- Cancellation rates creep up month over month
- This is NOT because lead quality dropped -- it's because SDR bias grew

### The Fix
- Recognize that curious/general interest leads may be financially qualified
- SDR's job becomes: identify what info gap exists and fill it
- The skin cancer analogy: a general statistic doesn't lead to action, but a dermatologist pointing out a specific spot on YOUR arm does
- Tailored, specific information leads to action. Generic info doesn't.

## Tailored Information System

### For Marketers: Build a Resource Library
Organized by objection/question category:
- How the business model works (SOPs, FAQ docs, videos, podcasts, testimonials)
- ROI and results proof (case studies, screenshots, testimonials)
- Trust and credibility (media appearances, credentials, client logos)
- Process and timeline (step-by-step guides, onboarding videos)

### For SDRs: Match Resources to Lead Needs
After the SDR conversation:
1. Identify which questions/concerns the lead has
2. Select appropriate resources from the library
3. Send tailored content (not mass blasts)
4. Options: "I have videos, written FAQs, a longer podcast, and case studies. Which do you prefer?"

### CRM Automation
- Create dropdown fields in CRM for common interest-gap reasons
- SDR selects the reason, CRM triggers contextual marketing automation
- Automated emails + manual SDR outreach = double coverage
- Joint strategy dramatically increases interest level before the call

## Modern Show Rate Best Practices

### Consumer Reality
- Most people have Do Not Disturb on permanently
- iPhone silences unknown callers by default
- Years of spam calls have trained people to ignore phone calls
- People won't call back random missed numbers

### The Inbound Lead Protocol
When a lead fills out a form:

1. **Text with your name first** -- iPhone trick: if you text "Hey, it's Jeremy" then call, the phone shows "Maybe: Jeremy" on the incoming call
2. **Call twice** -- if DND is on, first call goes to voicemail, second call rings through
3. **Text an open-ended question** -- reduce odds of "no"

### Reducing Your No's (The Ratio Method)

**Bad (50/50):** "Hey, can you jump on the phone?"
- 50% chance of yes, 50% chance of no

**Better (75/25):** "Hey Leo, it's Jeremy. Saw you filled out a form. If you're open in the next 5 minutes I can call now, or I have time later today. You can also text me your questions right here."
- Three options = 75% chance of positive response, 25% chance of no

### Speed to Lead Stats
- Contacting within 5 minutes = 50% higher probability of closing or communicating
- Not a made-up stat -- validated by massive data sets
- If you can't reach out in 5 minutes, do it at the first available opportunity

## SDR Incentive Structures

### Common Problem
- SDRs on base pay with no commission tied to outcomes
- No incentive to do extra work on lower-interest leads
- Results in "one and done" -- reach out once, cancel if not immediately qualified

### Better Structure
- Base pay + commission based on closes within ideal sales cycle
- Example: $50-100 bonus if lead closes within 2-3 days
- Reduced bonus if lead closes within 7 days
- Creates incentive to both qualify AND nurture leads
- Makes sending tailored information financially worthwhile

### Key Principle
If SDRs are one-and-done-ing leads with no follow-up after cancellation, you're wasting all the money spent to generate those leads.

## Pre-Call Best Practices (What Salespeople Should Do)

### Before the Call
1. Review the lead's application/survey answers
2. Check CRM notes from SDR interactions
3. Review any engagement data (did they watch the VSL? Open emails?)
4. Prepare personalized talking points
5. Have relevant case studies ready to reference

### Building Trust Rapidly
- Send a personalized video before the call
- Reference something specific from their application
- Show your face, be human, be real
- Position yourself as already understanding their situation
- "I looked over your application and noticed X -- excited to talk about that"

### The Pre-Call Sequence Stack
1. Ad (first impression)
2. VSL/landing page (education + authority)
3. Application/booking (investment + qualification)
4. Confirmation page (videos, next steps)
5. Value-dense email sequences (6/day "hammer them")
6. SDR outreach (qualification + tailored info)
7. Closer manual outreach (trust building)
8. The actual sales call

---

## Key Principles

1. SDRs make or break revenue -- treat the role seriously
2. Lower interest =/= unqualified. It means information gap.
3. Comparison bias is the #1 SDR management problem
4. Text-first communication in today's Do Not Disturb world
5. Reduce the probability of "no" with multiple response options
6. Build a categorized resource library for tailored sending
7. Use CRM automation as a safety net for manual outreach
8. Incentivize SDRs based on closes, not just bookings
9. Speed to lead is validated by data -- 5 minutes or less
10. Constantly reiterate organizational beliefs to SDR teams (beliefs drive behavior)
