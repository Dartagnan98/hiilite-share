# Market Awareness, Sophistication & Mass Desire

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Master Internet Marketing, 5th Cohort Week 5 Live Class
Framework: The three critical components of copywriting from Eugene Schwartz's "Breakthrough Advertising" (1950s). Still the foundation of all direct response copywriting today.

---

## The 3 Critical Components

1. Mass Desire
2. Market Awareness
3. Market Sophistication

Combine all three and you have a solid understanding of the frame you want to be communicating to your ideal customers in.

---

## Component 1: Mass Desire

### What Is Mass Desire?

Mass desire is the pre-existing want, need, fear, or desire that already lives inside your target market. You do NOT create desire. You channel it. You tap into what people already want and direct that energy toward your offer.

### Key Principles

1. **You cannot create desire.** You can only channel, direct, and focus it. The desire must already exist.

2. **Your job as a copywriter** is to connect your product/service to an existing desire.

3. **Mass desire has three dimensions:**
   - **Urgency/Intensity** -- How badly do they want it? How much pain are they in?
   - **Staying Power** -- Is this a fleeting desire or a deep, long-term need?
   - **Scope** -- How many people share this desire? The broader, the bigger the market.

4. **The strongest desires are primal:**
   - Health / avoiding death
   - Wealth / financial security
   - Love / relationships / belonging
   - Status / recognition / respect
   - Comfort / pleasure / avoiding pain
   - Freedom / independence

5. **Every product must satisfy a mass desire.** If it does not connect to something people already want, no amount of clever copy will save it.

### How to Identify Mass Desire

- What keeps your ideal customer up at night?
- What are they complaining about in forums, groups, and comment sections?
- What do they search for on Google at 2 AM?
- What results are they desperately trying to achieve?
- What pain are they trying to escape?

### Examples by Market

| Market | Mass Desire |
|--------|------------|
| Weight loss | Look good, feel confident, avoid health problems |
| Real estate agents | More listings, more closings, more commission |
| Business owners | More revenue, more freedom, less stress |
| Parents with addicted children | Safety for their child, peace of mind |
| Local service businesses | More customers, reliable revenue, less hustle |

## Component 2: Market Awareness

### What Is Market Awareness?

How much your audience already knows about: (1) their own problem, (2) your product/solution, (3) you and your brand.

### The 5 Levels of Awareness (Eugene Schwartz)

#### Level 1: Unaware
- They do not even know they have a problem
- Your job: make them aware of the problem first, THEN present the solution
- Approach: education-first, story-based ads, problem-agitation content

#### Level 2: Problem Aware
- They know they have a problem but do not know solutions exist
- Your job: agitate the problem and introduce the category of solution
- Approach: "Are you struggling with X? Here is what is causing it..."

#### Level 3: Solution Aware
- They know solutions exist but do not know about YOUR specific solution
- Your job: differentiate yourself from other solutions
- Approach: unique mechanism, social proof, specific claims

#### Level 4: Product Aware
- They know about YOUR product but have not bought yet
- Your job: overcome objections, build urgency, make offer irresistible
- Tools: testimonials, guarantees, bonuses, scarcity

#### Level 5: Most Aware
- They know your product, love it, ready to buy (or buy again)
- Your job: just make the offer
- Approach: "Hey, it is back in stock" or "New version" or "Special deal for you"

### The IT Guys Story (What NOT to Do)
- Jeremy's IT guys tried to sell him something for his data rack
- Said it was "standard for this type of rack to have"
- Communicated as if he should already know what it is -- they assumed his awareness level and were wrong
- He could not even remember the product name
- Result: no sale
- **Lesson: Never assume your audience's awareness level.**

### The Majority Rule

- When a majority of an audience is at one awareness level and some are at lower levels, pick whichever is the bigger half
- If you pick the smaller half, you willingly shrink your total buyer pool
- You never reach everyone -- only people probable to convert
- **Always set yourself up to speak to the majority**

### The Exception: Niche/Rich Audiences

- Selling to rich people: smaller pool makes sense
- Jeremy's YouTube videos are for business owners/marketers making $100K+/month minimum
- He speaks directly to big dogs and they know it
- Fewer people, but they pay well -- offsets the smaller audience
- If you do not consider this, you get chaos, randomness, or artificially inflated costs

## Component 3: Market Sophistication

### What Is Market Sophistication?

How many times your ideal customer has already been pitched on what you are selling. How "burned" or "experienced" they are with offers like yours.

### The 4 Stages

#### Stage 1: Never Been Pitched
- Audience has never seen an offer like yours
- Simply make the claim: "I can help you master internet marketing"
- Simple, direct claim is enough
- Example: first person to offer Facebook ads management in a small town

#### Stage 2: Pitched Twice
- A competitor has already made the same basic claim
- Still just make the claim, but OUTSPEND other businesses
- Louder voice wins at this stage

#### Stage 3: Pitched Six Times
- Must introduce and market a **unique mechanism** (differentiator)
- The specific way you accomplish the outcomes you help people with
- **Ice cream unique mechanisms:** N2 (nitrogen ice cream), rolled ice cream (Thai-style), classic soft serve done right, gelato done better
- Your unique mechanism separates you from everyone else making the same claim

#### Stage 4: Pitched Dozens of Times
- Must articulate your full PROCESS to result
- Be transparent and clear about how you produce outcomes
- Not enough to just claim or differentiate -- show the entire journey
- Example: Agency owners pitching "let me run your paid ads" at Stage 1 level have a bad time. Businesses have been pitched multiple times a week AND burned by previous agencies. Clear Stage 4.

### How to Determine Your Stage

| Times Pitched | Stage |
|--------------|-------|
| 0 times | Stage 1 |
| 1-2 times | Stage 2 |
| 3-6 times | Stage 3 |
| 6+ times | Stage 4 |

### The Hastens Bed Example (Stacking All 4 Stages)

Hastens: beds costing $20K to $400K+ USD. They understand Stage 4 and stack every layer:

| Stage | How Hastens Covers It |
|-------|----------------------|
| Stage 1 (Claim) | "We make the world's best beds" |
| Stage 2 (Outspend) | Easy -- massive revenue from high-priced beds |
| Stage 3 (Unique Mechanism) | Horse hair and organic materials. Exploded bed view showing natural material layers |
| Stage 4 (Process) | Craftsmen videos, material sourcing, heritage narrative |

## Combining All 3 Components

### Example: Sober Living Home for Businessmen

**Headline:** "How Businessmen Can Stop Struggling With Alcohol Without Going To Rehab"

| Component | Application |
|-----------|------------|
| Mass Desire | "Stop Struggling With Alcohol" -- the pre-existing desire |
| Sophistication | "Without Going To Rehab" -- differentiator for Stage 3 (only seen rehab/AA) |
| Awareness | "Businessmen" calls out identity. Solution Aware (Level 3) |

## Four Rules for Writing Headlines

### Rule 1: Get Self-Interest Into Every Headline
The headline must answer "what is in it for me?" People are selfish. Use that.

**Example:** Wake Up Warrior -- "ATTENTION: Searching for a select few entrepreneurs that are as serious about their marriage and family, as they are their empire"

### Rule 2: Suggest Something They Want
Imply a desirable outcome is available. Do not state features -- imply transformation.

### Rule 3: Leverage a Bias to Inspire Action
Use cognitive biases: urgency, scarcity, social proof, authority, reciprocity.

### Rule 4: The Headline's ONLY Job Is to Capture Attention
Get them to read the NEXT line. Not to sell. Not to explain everything. If the headline fails, nothing else matters.

### Headline Examples (News Announcement Format)
- "Real Estate Agents Are Redefining How They Show Homes"
- "Real Estate Agents Are Using MLS In A Newly Discovered Way To Get More Deals!"
- "The Vacuum Cleaner Redefined? This New Feature Is A Game Changer"

### Curiosity Warning
- Avoid headlines that MERELY provoke curiosity
- Curiosity + news or self-interest = excellent
- Curiosity alone = seldom enough
- Always combine curiosity with substance

## Key Takeaways

1. You do not create desire, you channel it. Find what people already want.
2. Awareness level determines HOW you talk. Unaware needs education. Aware needs offers.
3. Always speak to majority awareness level unless strategic reason not to.
4. Market sophistication determines WHAT you say. Stage 1 needs a claim. Stage 4 needs full process.
5. Best messaging combines all three: taps mass desire, matches awareness, accounts for sophistication.
6. Headlines have one job: get them to read the next line.
7. Read "Breakthrough Advertising" by Eugene Schwartz.
8. Every piece of copy should be filtered through these three lenses before publishing.

## Quick Decision Matrix

| Awareness Level | Sophistication Stage | Copy Approach |
|----------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Unaware + Stage 1 | Educate on problem, simple claim | Story-based, problem-agitation |
| Problem Aware + Stage 2 | Agitate problem, outspend competitors | Direct claim with bigger reach |
| Solution Aware + Stage 3 | Differentiate with unique mechanism | Process-focused, "why we are different" |
| Product Aware + Stage 4 | Overcome objections, show full process | Testimonials, guarantees, methodology |
| Most Aware + Any Stage | Just make the offer | "It is back" / "New deal for you" |
