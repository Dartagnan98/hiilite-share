# The 13 Emotional Manipulators

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Master Internet Marketing, Week 1 Lessons 8-15
Framework: Applied psychology triggers that drive buying behavior. Use strategically and ethically -- these are not tricks, they are deep human motivations that already exist. Your job is to connect your product to them.

---

## Overview

The 13 emotional manipulators are psychological hot spots that dictate human behavior and buying decisions. Most people are unaware these underlying drivers are influencing their actions. At the highest levels of ad spend in any industry, top marketers leverage these strategically.

**Rule:** Never use these in evil or vindictive ways. Use them to get positive outcomes for people. The manipulation is in the framing, not the intent.

**The Complete List:**
1. Lust / Sex Motivation
2. Escape (Greener Pastures)
3. Esteem & Inadequacy
4. Fear
5. Guilt
6. Affinity
7. Greed
8. Deep Anxiety
9. Persistent Worry
10. Feeling Impotent
11. Poor Sense of Self
12. Excess Concern Over What Others Think
13. Victimhood & Paranoia

---

## 1. Lust / Sex Motivation

**Definition:** People seeking approval, acceptance, and admiration. At their evolutionary core, humans are driven to elevate their status within a social group to attract higher quality partners.

**Core Drivers:**
- Power -- opposite of the skinny weakling who gets kicked in his face at the beach
- Popularity -- desired by others, looked up to
- Status elevation within a specific social chamber
- Exceptionally strong desire for something

**Gender Split:**
- Male thought: "Buy this improvement, I'll get laid more"
- Female thought: "Buy this improvement, I'll be more attractive"

**Real-World Examples:**
- Rolex watches -- worn for a couple weeks then shelved. The purchase is a status symbol, not utility. People think they'll be seen as higher status and richer
- Los Angeles cosmetic industry -- 70-80% of women Jeremy interacted with in LA had lip injections and at least one minor cosmetic surgery. Investment to become more attractive
- Med spas doing Botox on lunch breaks
- Cool sculpting, true sculpting to remove body fat in specific places

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Link purchasing your product to the probability of being seen as more attractive by the opposite sex
- Connect to higher status within the prospect's echo chamber
- Show that buying = approval, acceptance, elevated status
- NOT about putting half-naked people in your ads -- it is about associating product ownership with being more desirable

**Ad Copy Application:**
If you can connect the purchase to "I'll be seen as more attractive" or "I'll have higher status" or "I'll get approval and acceptance in my social circle," you have incredible odds of selling at a higher rate.

## 2. Escape (Greener Pastures)

**Definition:** The applied version of "the grass is greener on the other side." When people don't have what they can see on the other side of the fence, they are drawn to it and will invest in anything that lets them vicariously live through the result they want.

**Core Concept:** Pleasure from living vicariously through another's experiences. When lacking something in real life, people seek the experience through consumption.

**Key Terms:** Liberation, liberty, freedom from X, out from under control of X, escapism

**Real-World Examples:**
- Food Network -- Jeremy watched it most when he had the least money. Once he could afford great food, he stopped watching
- Martha Stewart, Oprah -- "porn for women" (lifestyle escape)
- Hustle porn -- "porn for entrepreneurs"
- Grand Theft Auto -- one of the highest-usage video games worldwide because you can do anything: millions of dollars, great houses, cars, illegal activities. In real life, you're constrained
- Tai Lopez -- constantly communicates from multiple luxury properties, with beautiful women, in exotic cars, showing exclusive shoes. His demographic looks at that and says "I want that too"

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Show the lifestyle outcome your product enables
- Let people see themselves in the role your product creates
- Communicate from the environment your prospect aspires to
- Make your content the escape route -- people will vicariously live through you while they don't have the result yet
- Build aspiration into every touchpoint

**Ad Copy Application:**
If you're able to market in a way where people can see themselves inside of that specific role, you have a much higher probability of them buying. The environment you communicate from matters as much as what you say.

## 3. Esteem & Inadequacy

**Definition:** People like to feel good about themselves, feel respected, have positive emotional attributes. This manipulator targets their desire for self-respect, confidence, ownership, status, elitism, and prestige.

**The Social Totem Pole:** Every social echo chamber has a hierarchy. Rich, famous, attractive people at the top. People take actions to climb this ladder and feel adequate.

**Core Drivers:**
- Self-respect and confidence
- Status within a social chamber
- Being part of the "in crowd"
- Revenge of the nerds -- "they all laughed until I proved them wrong"
- Feeling superior to others / fear of feeling inferior
- Nobody wants to be a loser, ignorant, weak, embarrassed

**Real-World Examples:**
- Influencer marketing -- celebrities associate with products, fans buy to feel like they're at that level
- Jacob & Co watches -- celebrity collaborations (Messi), wealthy fans buy, status of product elevated
- Birkin bags -- $15K-$100K+. Walk into Hermes without purchase history and they won't sell to you. Call weeks later offering colors you didn't ask for. People buy anyway to be part of the in crowd
- Cartier bracelets -- levels from basic gold to fully iced out at $50K. "General badges" that show what financial bracket you're in

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Communicate toward the identity they want to embody
- Use language like "nobody wants to be a loser" paired with "these types of people are using it"
- Link product purchase to elevated status
- Show what bracket of people already own your product
- Make buying feel like joining an exclusive group
- Use "revenge" framing: "prove them wrong"

**Ad Copy Application:**
When you communicate in copy that enables them to feel what they're actually feeling, and it's coming from somebody else, they feel understood. Like someone is mind-reading their situation. That has a higher draw to filling out an application or getting on a call.

## 4. Fear

**Definition:** People have deep-seated fears that drive buying decisions -- fear of loss, danger, being left out, criticism, and bad decisions.

**Five Types of Fear in Marketing:**

### Fear of Loss
- Losing money, status, respect
- Example: Jeremy in penthouse with three offices, constantly investing in things to feel secure he could keep funding payroll, rent, software

### Fear of Danger, Hazard, and Threat
- Example: Anti-radiation phone cases and organite minerals after learning about 5G frequencies
- Security systems -- massive recurring revenue businesses. Probability of a real break-in is low, but people invest to feel safe and mitigate fear

### Fear of Being Left Out or Left Behind
- Communicate the downside of not taking action
- Show what happens to people who miss the window
- People fear being stuck where they are while others advance

### Fear of Criticism, Negative Opinions, and Gossip
- People who obsess over one hater when 999 people love them
- Drives investment in reputation management, lawyers, community managers monitoring social networks
- Software that mitigates against negative reputation

### Fear of Bad Decisions
- Creates paralysis -- people won't take any action
- Call it out in copy: acknowledge they're afraid of making the wrong choice, then show why this isn't a risk

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Fear is the most commonly leveraged manipulator because it's the easiest for people to recognize in themselves
- Name the specific fear: "worried about losing X?"
- Show the cost of inaction
- Position your product as the safety net
- FOMO works for being left behind
- For fear of bad decisions: address it directly, provide guarantees, show proof

## 5. Guilt

**Definition:** People carry guilt about past unresolved issues, present failures, disappointing others, and not living up to expectations. This guilt drives purchasing decisions.

**Types of Guilt:**

### Past Unresolved Issues / Trauma
- Targeting people who went through rehab: "You have undealt with, unresolved past experiences affecting your present self"
- Therapists can use this ethically to get people into treatment

### Present Failures
- Family man who promises Disney World but never goes -- next opportunity, guilt drives him to finally buy
- Built-up guilt becomes a weight that forces investment to alleviate it

### Disappointing Others (Spouses, Family, Teams)
- Missing payroll, not training staff properly
- Not providing enough for partner
- Over-the-top gifts to compensate (flowers after a mistake, new car to make up for years of under-delivering)

### Not Living Up to Expectations
- Societal pressure: people feel guilty for not going to college
- Not hitting work metrics -- guilt about letting managers down, drives investment in self-improvement

### Poor Behavior
- Being fat, procrastinating, making bad decisions
- Call out the bad decisions and what they produce as outcomes
- Fitness industry masters this every January: "it's not your fault, the time to turn it around is now"

### Guilty for True Desires
- People are programmed from youth to feel guilty about wanting wealth, success, certain lifestyles
- **Power of the permission slip:** People will invest in you because you give them permission to behave how they secretly want to but won't allow themselves to
- Being the permission slip is a massive sales driver

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Acknowledge the guilt directly without shaming
- Free them from past failures
- Position the product as the fresh start
- Be the permission slip for their desires
- Fitness example: "It's not your fault you gained weight over the holidays. Invest with us and we'll help you turn it around"

## 6. Affinity

**Definition:** People buy products because they internally identify with specific characteristics. "By association, I am." If someone identifies as rich, they buy things rich people buy to validate that internal characteristic.

**Core Mechanism:** Buying decisions reinforce internal identity. People purchase to validate who they believe they are or want to be.

**Real-World Examples:**
- Jeremy turning around at the airport after accidentally buying coach -- doesn't associate with flying economy. First class validates "I am rich"
- Lamborghini/Ferrari purchases -- identify as rich, exotic car owner, someone with fun money
- Strip clubs throwing $10K -- internal identity of big spender
- Vegan juice -- not just vegan food, but the highest quality vegan food. Identity = "I eat the highest quality food"
- Choosing premium marketing services at $10K+/month -- buyers feel smarter for choosing the highest caliber option

**The Trust Factor:** People prefer buying where others have already bought (safety in numbers). Most people are not first movers. They want to feel better about an investment because other people have made it too.

**Key Phrases:** Prestige and status. "By investing in X, I am X."

**Identity Framing:** Man or mouse? Smart or dumb? Winner or loser? Make the person self-identify with a side that plays into the identity they get when buying from you.

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Link your product to a specific identity or characteristic
- Show what kind of person buys your product
- Make them feel like buying = joining an identity group
- Use social proof heavily -- "these types of people use this"
- Position the purchase as self-validation
- Most powerful manipulator to leverage, but hardest to apply

## 7. Greed

**Definition:** Appeals to the desire for excess, abundance, and having what others don't. The sin of sloth -- living like Caesar being fed grapes. The permission slip to have more and get even more than that.

**Core Drivers:**
- Secret of secrets -- something concealed or withheld from the public
- Possessed by others but not by them
- Joining the greedy few who reap the biggest rewards
- Gluttony -- insatiable appetite, never enough
- Desire to trump others -- motivated by competition more than personal goals

**Real-World Examples:**
- Buffets -- leveraging "you can have more"
- Information products using the "hidden secrets" frame (ethically or unethically)
- Healthcare/biohacking -- access to drugs or nootropics that make you smarter than others
- Competitive billionaires (Peter Thiel, Bill Gates) -- openly admit insatiable appetite for more
- "I did it to prove them wrong" success stories

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Frame access to your product as getting what others don't have
- Appeal to competitive nature: "get ahead of everyone else"
- Position as the unfair advantage
- Use exclusive or limited access framing
- For competitive buyers: "sell them products that enable them to keep proving people wrong"
- For abundance seekers: "live in excess" messaging

## 8. Deep Anxiety

**Definition:** A pervasive, overwhelming sense of dread about the future or current circumstances that goes beyond normal worry. People with deep anxiety seek solutions that promise relief, stability, and control.

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Acknowledge the anxiety directly and specifically
- Position your product as the path back to stability
- Show that you understand the depth of their concern
- Offer concrete, tangible steps (not vague promises)
- Industries: financial services, health, insurance, coaching programs

---

## 9. Persistent Worry

**Definition:** Ongoing, nagging concern about a specific issue that won't go away. Unlike deep anxiety which is broad, persistent worry is targeted at a particular problem.

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Name the specific worry ("Can't stop thinking about X?")
- Show that others share this worry (normalize it)
- Present your solution as the way to finally resolve it
- Use repetition to mirror the persistent nature of the worry itself
- Industries: health monitoring, security, financial planning

---

## 10. Feeling Impotent

**Definition:** The sense of powerlessness -- feeling unable to control outcomes, change circumstances, or make an impact. Not just sexual impotence but a broad feeling of helplessness.

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Restore agency: "Take back control of X"
- Show transformation from helpless to empowered
- Provide tools and frameworks (not just motivation)
- Industries: business coaching, fitness, self-defense, career development

---

## 11. Poor Sense of Self

**Definition:** Low self-image, lack of confidence in who they are, disconnection from personal identity. People with poor sense of self are searching for products that help them define or elevate who they are.

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Offer identity formation through your product
- "Become the person you know you can be"
- Show before/after identity transformations
- Build community around a shared identity
- Industries: personal development, fitness, fashion, coaching

---

## 12. Excess Concern Over What Others Think

**Definition:** Paralyzing preoccupation with others' opinions that prevents action. These people make (or avoid) purchases based on how they think others will perceive them.

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Normalize the concern, then reframe it
- Show social approval for the purchase: "people will look at you differently"
- Or flip it: "stop caring what they think -- do this for you"
- Use social proof heavily (if everyone is doing it, it must be acceptable)
- Industries: cosmetics, fashion, luxury goods, career coaching

---

## 13. Victimhood & Paranoia

**Definition:** The belief that the world is against them, that systems are rigged, that others are actively working to keep them down. These people see themselves as victims of circumstance.

**How to Use in Ads:**
- Validate their feeling that the deck is stacked against them
- Position your product as the great equalizer
- "Level the playing field" messaging
- Show that you're the insider willing to reveal what's been hidden
- Be careful not to feed harmful conspiracy thinking -- use ethically
- Industries: financial education, business coaching, alternative health

## How to Apply in Ad Copy

1. **Identify the primary manipulator** for your market/offer
2. **Write the hook** to trigger that emotion (name the feeling, describe the situation)
3. **Agitate** -- make them feel the depth of the emotion
4. **Bridge** -- connect the emotional state to your product as the resolution
5. **Stack manipulators** -- most effective ads layer 2-3 manipulators together
6. **Never use all 13 at once** -- pick 2-3 that align with your product and target market
7. **Test which manipulators resonate most** -- different demographics respond to different ones
