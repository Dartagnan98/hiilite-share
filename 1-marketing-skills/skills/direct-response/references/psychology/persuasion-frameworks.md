# Persuasion Frameworks

Source: Jeremy Haynes / Master Internet Marketing, Week 1 Lessons 6-7, 16
Framework: The psychological foundations of marketing persuasion -- the Steps of Agreement and the three dimensions that drive human action.

## The Marketing Psychology Foundation

Marketing psychology is about understanding:
1. Ways to think and ways that people think
2. How they behave and why they behave that way
3. How they filter information
4. The cognitive biases and triggers that get people to take specific actions

## The Steps of Agreement

For every action (opt-in, purchase, booking), the buyer must agree to a series of facts before they agree to buy. Few marketers understand this.

**Pro Tip:** Jeremy prefers to reverse engineer -- start from the point of transaction and work backwards to the first exposure.

### The 11 Steps

#### Step 1: Possibility
The prospect must believe something is possible. Usually simple problem + solution.
- Identify a problem going on, propose a potential solution
- That IS possibility -- something can actually be solved

#### Step 2: Beneficial Nature
The prospect must believe the outcome is beneficial to them.
- What are the good possible outcomes from buying or investing?
- Address objections that naturally arise

#### Step 3: Attainability
The prospect must understand how attainable the outcome/product is.
- Sometimes keep things exclusive (out of reach)
- Sometimes show overabundance of stock creating a deal
- Not about making everything seem easy -- clarify what is realistic

#### Step 4: Personal Possibility
What is actually possible for THIS person on an individual level?
- Different from Step 1 which is broad
- Gets contextual to their specific situation
- If targeting multiple demographics, speak to each one

#### Step 5: Personal Attainability
What can this specific person attain?
- Personal versions of Steps 1 and 3
- First steps are broad (appeal to multiple demographics at once)
- Personal steps get contextual to specific segments

#### Step 6: Reasons Prior Failures Are Not Their Fault
Remove guilt about past failures so they are open to a new solution.
- Many prospects are jaded -- already tried something similar and failed
- Alleviate self-blame: "It wasn't your fault"
- When they become self-forgivable, it creates a void -- opportunity to fill with new solution

#### Step 7: Reasons This Is Different
Explain why your offer is unique. Critical for sophisticated markets.
- Stage 3-4 market sophistication requires this
- Phone case example: anti-radiation tech, built-in screen protectors, shock absorption, visual design -- each is a unique mechanism drawing different buyers
- Get people to agree that HOW it is different is beneficial for their specific customer wants

#### Step 8: Personal Beneficial Nature
Drill down on benefits specific to each demographic.
- Broader "beneficial nature" (Step 2) appeals to multiple segments
- This step contextualizes benefits for specific groups

#### Step 9: Advantages or Essential Timing
Why investing RIGHT NOW is advantageous.
- **First mover's advantage:** People get the most advantage when they do things first
- **Seasonal timing:** "Order by this date for Christmas delivery"
- **Life events:** Target based on anniversaries, moves, life changes
- **Overproduction deals:** "We overproduced -- get our best pricing." Supply and demand is easily understood

#### Step 10: Personal Advantageous or Essential Timing
Same as Step 9 but contextualized to each demographic.
- Why is it personally advantageous for THIS type of person to act now?
- Different demographics have different timing pressures

#### Step 11: Personal Appropriateness
This is the right thing for them. The final agreement.
- Drill down on why this is the right fit for this specific person
- By this point, they should have agreed to everything above
- This confirms: "Yes, this is appropriate for me"

### How to Use the Steps

**Long-Form Landing Pages:** Work through each step sequentially. One section per step.

**Ad Videos:** Hit key steps in condensed form -- possibility, beneficial nature, reasons different, timing.

**Webinars:** Structure around all 11 steps. Best webinars walk prospects through every agreement before the pitch.

**Short-Form Ads:** Focus on Steps 1-3 to get the click, let the landing page handle the rest.

**Amazon Listings:** All 11 steps are covered in a simple product description and display -- people just don't realize it.

**Reverse Engineering Approach:**
1. Start at the transaction -- what final agreement do they need?
2. Work backwards -- what needs to be true for them to make that agreement?
3. Keep going back until you reach the very first exposure
4. Now you have your agreement chain mapped out

## The Three Dimensions of Action

From Eugene Schwartz's Breakthrough Advertising. The three forces that drive ALL human behavior and buying decisions.

### Dimension 1: Desires

Wants, needs, cravings, thirsts that drive behavior.

**Key Principle:** You cannot create desire. You can only channel, direct, and focus existing desire toward your offer.

**What makes people read, want, believe, and buy:**
- Things they want to have
- Things they perceive they need
- Things they see themselves as in the future
- Products that make them feel like the version of themselves they aspire to

### Dimension 2: Identifications

Roles or characteristics your prospect wants to play in life. The traits they want to build.

**Your Task:** Put those traits directly behind your product.

**The Formula:** "By buying this, I am ___"

**Example:** Jeremy identified as "peak performance." After seeing his Whoop recovery scores, he spent $15,500 on recovery devices. Any product aligned to peak performance "almost immediately has a piece of my wallet."

**Application:**
- Make them feel part of a select group when they become a user
- Link your product to the characteristics they identify with
- Show that your product validates the identity they are building
- Use the law of association -- visually connect your product to the type of person they want to be

### Dimension 3: Beliefs

Opinions, prejudices, fragments of knowledge, and conceptions of reality your prospect lives by.

**Key Principle:** Beliefs dictate behavior almost as much as identifications. But beliefs are extremely hard to change.

**Critical Insight:** Do not try to change beliefs to convert customers. It is too expensive and too slow. Instead, identify a large group who ALREADY have beliefs that align with your product.

**How Beliefs Work as Filters:**
- Believing is a process of fitting new facts into established patterns of thought
- Beliefs form a filter through which your product information must pass or be rejected
- If your product contradicts their beliefs, it gets rejected before they even consider it

**Example:** Jeremy in New York -- believes street vendor food is low quality, so he never buys from them. He buys water only. No amount of marketing from a street vendor would change his belief about food quality.

**Application:**
1. Start with their existing beliefs as a base
2. Build up using their own logic
3. Prove your product satisfies their desires
4. Prove your product works
5. Prove their kind of people rely on your product
6. Typically do this VISUALLY through the law of association

## Combining Steps of Agreement with Three Dimensions

**Step 1 (Possibility)** maps to **Desires** -- tap into what they already want
**Steps 4-5 (Personal Possibility/Attainability)** maps to **Identifications** -- make it about their specific identity
**Step 6 (Prior Failures Not Their Fault)** maps to **Beliefs** -- work within existing belief system
**Step 7 (Reasons This Is Different)** maps to **Beliefs** -- fit new facts into established patterns
**Step 11 (Personal Appropriateness)** maps to **Identifications** -- "this is right for who I am"

### The Checklist for Any Marketing Asset

Before publishing any ad, landing page, or funnel:

- [ ] Does it connect to an existing desire (not try to create one)?
- [ ] Does it link to an identity the prospect already holds or aspires to?
- [ ] Does it work within their existing beliefs (not fight them)?
- [ ] Does it walk through enough steps of agreement for the action required?
- [ ] Are personal steps included for each target demographic?
- [ ] Are prior failures addressed and forgiven?
- [ ] Is the timing advantage clear?
- [ ] Is the final personal appropriateness obvious?
